<br />
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
Hunting has been my primary responsibility for the last several years.&nbsp;
Over this time I've done a lot of experimentation around different processes and methods of finding malicious activity in log data.&nbsp;What has always stayed true though is the need
for a solid understanding of the hypothesis you're using, familiarity with all the data you can take advantage of and a method to produce/analyze the results.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>For this post I'd
like to share one of the ideas I've been working on lately.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span></div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
I've previously
written a number of blog posts on beaconing.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>Over time I've refined much of how I go about looking for these
anomalous connections.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>My rule of thumb
for hunting beacons (or other types of malicious activity) is to ignore static IOC's
as those are best suited for detection.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>Instead, focus on behaviors or clusters of behaviors that will return higher confidence output.&nbsp; Here's how I'm accomplishing this in a single Splunk search.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<ol style="direction: ltr; font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal; margin-bottom: 0in; margin-left: .375in; margin-top: 0in; unicode-bidi: embed;" type="1">
<li style="margin-bottom: 0; margin-top: 0; vertical-align: middle;" value="1"><span style="font-family: Calibri; font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; font-size: 11.0pt; font-style: normal; font-weight: normal;">Describe what you are looking
     for in numbers.&nbsp; This will allow you to have much more control over your conditional statements which impacts the quality of your output.</span></li>
<li style="margin-bottom: 0; margin-top: 0; vertical-align: middle;"><span style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt;">Define those attributes that
     you are interested in and assign number values to them.&nbsp; These attributes will be your points of correlation.</span></li>
<li style="margin-bottom: 0; margin-top: 0; vertical-align: middle;"><span style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt;">Reduce your output to those
     connections that exhibit any of what you are looking for.&nbsp; This is the correlation piece where we can use the total score of all attributes identified within a src/dest pair.&nbsp; Higher sums translate to greater numbers of attributes identified.</span></li>
</ol>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
Below is a
screenshot of the search I came up with.&nbsp; This is again using the botsv3 data set from Splunk's Boss of the SOC competition.&nbsp; Thanks Splunk!</div>
<!--EndFragment--><br /><br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<!--StartFragment-->

<br />
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
The following is a
description of the fields in the output.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-dest: Based on the
data source, this field may include the ip address or domain name.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-src_ip: Source of
request</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-dest_ip:
Destination of request</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-bytes_out: Data
sent from src_ip.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-distinct_event_count:
The total number of connections per destination.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-i_bytecount: The
total count of bytes_out by src/dest/bytes_out. Large numbers may indicate
beaconing.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-t_bytecount: The
total count of connections between src/dest.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-avgcount:
i_bytecount / t_bytecount.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Beacon
percentage.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Values closer to 1 are more
indicative of a beaocn. </div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-distinct_byte_count:
Total count of bytes_out (used in determining percentages for beaconing).</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-incount: The count
of unique bytes_in values.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>When compared
with t_bytecount you may see the responses to beacons.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-time_count: The
number of hours the src/dest have been communicating.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Large numbers may indicate persistent
beaconing.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-o_average: The
average beacon count between all src/dests.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-above: The
percentage above o_average.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-beaconmult:<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>weight multiplier given to higher above
averages.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-evtmult: weight
multiplier given to destinations with higher volume connections.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-timemult: weight
multiplier given to connections that last multiple hours.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
-addedweight: The
sum of all multipliers. </div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
You can see from the search results that we reduced 30k+ events down to 1700 that exhibit some type of
behavior that we're interested in.&nbsp; This is good, but still not feasible to analyze every event individually.&nbsp; I have a couple of choices to reduce my output at this point.&nbsp; I can adjust my weighted condition to something like "|where weighted &gt; 100" which would have the effect of requiring multiple characteristics being correlated.&nbsp; My other choice is to use some type of anomaly detection to surface those odd connections.&nbsp; You can probably tell from the "ML" portion of the title which direction I'm going to go.&nbsp; So from here we need a method to pick out the anomalies as the vast majority of this data is likely legitimate traffic.&nbsp; For this I'll be inserting our results into a MySQL database.&nbsp; I don't necessarily need to for this analysis, but it's a way for me to keep the metadata of the connections for greater periods of time.&nbsp; This will allow me to do longer term analysis based on the data that is being stored.</div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjLjGLu1inyYrIxXUQcTX3B7U1onQ1NghUIlWSM8k02kQECE6tjEmRemwtlNANkPZuCb3H8mkJSPIqJe0FT-tL9BG49VPZjoTP8sctf7BznRwPwznKie9xx4snrtqOPHCuLH97ksxVOGw2S/s1600/Screen+Shot+2020-06-21+at+12.34.29+PM.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="263" data-original-width="1600" height="52" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjLjGLu1inyYrIxXUQcTX3B7U1onQ1NghUIlWSM8k02kQECE6tjEmRemwtlNANkPZuCb3H8mkJSPIqJe0FT-tL9BG49VPZjoTP8sctf7BznRwPwznKie9xx4snrtqOPHCuLH97ksxVOGw2S/s320/Screen+Shot+2020-06-21+at+12.34.29+PM.png" width="320" /></a></div>
<div style="font-family: Calibri; font-size: 11.0pt; margin: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<!--EndFragment--><br /><br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
Once it's in the database we can use python and various ML algorithms to surface anomalous traffic.&nbsp; For this I'll be using an Isolation Forest.&nbsp; I'll also be choosing fields that I think best represents what a beacon looks like as I don't want to feed every field through this process.<br />
<br />
distinct_event_count: Overall activity.<br />
time_count: How persistent is the traffic?<br />
above: How does the beacon frequency compare to all other traffic?<br />
addedweight: How many beacon characteristics does this traffic exhibit?<br />
<br />
The following screenshot contains the code as well as the output.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEin4koGjVDHy7pd9SInS8QxUbH0f1Nr1FKIbQm48o-hDbHdt4x7Qx6cyt2amyLP_cfhP2iIW6xrzqncbNpDG1gCaKfI6E3S3EJTCt3n4fkU2J0FThQPz50ZqNC7F3DPXQ4dAQ545Sf9lUKX/s1600/Screen+Shot+2020-06-22+at+8.13.05+AM.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="1073" data-original-width="1600" height="214" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEin4koGjVDHy7pd9SInS8QxUbH0f1Nr1FKIbQm48o-hDbHdt4x7Qx6cyt2amyLP_cfhP2iIW6xrzqncbNpDG1gCaKfI6E3S3EJTCt3n4fkU2J0FThQPz50ZqNC7F3DPXQ4dAQ545Sf9lUKX/s320/Screen+Shot+2020-06-22+at+8.13.05+AM.png" width="320" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
Looking at the top 3 tenths of 1 percent of the most anomalous src/dest pairs you can see that there are 4 destination ip addresses that may need investigating.&nbsp; If you've read my last 2 posts on beaconing the 45.77.53.176 ip should look familiar.&nbsp; This ip was definitely used for C2.&nbsp; The 172.16.0.178 ip is also interesting.&nbsp; Taking a quick look at the destination in the botsv3 data, you can see memchached injection that appears to be successful.&nbsp; Additional investigation of the src ip's in this output would definitely be justified.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
I will say that this method is very good at identifying beacons, but beacons are not always malicious.&nbsp; Greater work may be needed to surface those types malicious connections.&nbsp; Some additional ideas may be first seen ip's or incorporating proxy data where even more characteristics can be defined, scored and correlated.<br />
<br />
A large portion of hunting is experimentation so experiment with the data and see what you can come up with!<br />
