          <h1 class="entry-title ">Attacks against media in the Philippines continue</h1><div class="row-fluid tc-single-post-thumbnail-wrapper tc-singular-thumbnail-wrapper __after_content_title"><section class="tc-thumbnail span12"><div class=""><a class="tc-rectangular-thumb" href="https://www.qurium.org/alerts/philippines/attacks-against-media-in-the-philippines-continue/"><img width="1170" height="252" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/featured_image_default-1170x252.png" class="attachment-slider tc-thumb-type-thumb wp-post-image no-lazy wp-post-image" alt="" decoding="async" fetchpriority="high" /></a></div></section></div><hr class="featurette-divider __before_content">        </header>
                  <section class="entry-content ">
              <p class="entry-content-date">22 June 2021</p>
<p><strong>Note:</strong> This forensic report is a LIVE DOCUMENT, new findings are added to the report constantly. We are still processing and analyzing the attack data recorded. </p>



<p>During the past month, Qurium has received brief but frequent denial attacks against the Philippine alternative media outlets <a href="https://www.bulatlat.com">Bulatlat</a> and <a href="https://www.altermidya.net">Altermidya</a>, as well as the human rights group <a href="https://www.karapatan.org">Karapatan</a>.</p>



<p>This forensic report summarizes our findings and has been updated since the release of the first version to include the new findings. </p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading">Summary of attacks</h3>



<p>This section includes a brief summary of some of the attacks recorded during May &#8211; June 2021.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">What is OG2-PA?</h2>

<figure class="aligncenter size-large"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-17.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="562" height="181" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-17.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13352" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-17.png 562w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-17-300x97.png 300w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 562px) 100vw, 562px" /></a></figure></div><h2 class="wp-block-heading">Wikipedia Edits</h2>



<p>Another interesting finding is that the attacker&#8217;s IP address is present in the Edits of the Wikipedia page for &#8220;Chief of Army (Philippines)&#8221; and many other pages related to the Army. </p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-9.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="769" height="324" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-9.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13223" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-9.png 769w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-9-300x126.png 300w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 769px) 100vw, 769px" /></a></figure>



<figure class="wp-block-image"><img decoding="async" src="https://pbs.twimg.com/media/E4qFE2RXoAAvbDS?format=jpg&amp;name=large" alt="Image"/></figure>



<p>We could also reconfirm that other IP addresses using the same digital certificate had also make similar edits. </p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-16.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="1024" height="375" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-16-1024x375.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13349" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-16-1024x375.png 1024w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-16-300x110.png 300w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-16-768x282.png 768w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-16.png 1429w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Linking .42 and .43 addresses</h2>



<p>During our research we could confirm that (1) both address run similar hardware, Sophos XG and RED tunneling, (2) both boxes have digital certificates of the hardware supplier &#8220;IP Solutions Inc&#8221; and (3) that their very first certificates were issued 2015-07-31 (Source: RiskIQ)</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image-1.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="867" height="635" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image-1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13392" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image-1.png 867w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image-1-300x220.png 300w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image-1-768x562.png 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 867px) 100vw, 867px" /></a></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Attacks continue</h2>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>2021/05/20 13:05 Karapatan receives a small flood with user-agent AdobeUxTechC4-Async/3.0.12 (win32). The machine 188.63.78{.}119 crawls heavily media from the Philippines.</li>



<li>2021/06/16 06:42 Large flood on Altermidya and Bulatlat from multiple IP addresses. Floods include requests of the type /?u144642756919q213158629662aB2146578135208224622277442H and user agent: null</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large is-resized"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-5.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="897" height="716" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-5.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13193" style="width:673px;height:537px" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-5.png 897w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-5-300x239.png 300w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-5-768x613.png 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 897px) 100vw, 897px" /></a></figure>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>2021/06/22 22:50 &#8211; 2021/06/23 &#8211; 03:00 Attacks against Bulatlat and Altermidya for several hours.  During the DDOS attacks a pen testing against the sites was conducted.</li>
</ul>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large is-resized"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-10.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="892" height="673" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-10.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13237" style="width:669px;height:505px" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-10.png 892w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-10-300x226.png 300w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-10-768x579.png 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 892px) 100vw, 892px" /></a></figure>



<p>The attacker launched a &#8220;Denial of Service&#8221; against bulatlat.com, flooding the site with requests of the type GET /?q=123456789. At the very same time the attacker run &#8220;Nikto&#8221; to pen test the site. The flood run 1000+ faster that the pen testing script. We have sample down 1/1000 the &#8220;Denial of Service&#8221; logs to visualize the attack.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="960" height="499" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13386" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image.png 960w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image-300x156.png 300w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/image-768x399.png 768w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 960px) 100vw, 960px" /></a><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Pen testing attack &#8220;Nikto&#8221; while running DDOS.</figcaption></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Abuse Handling and official reaction</h2>



<p>Days before the release of this report, we reached out to ops@pregi.net and denis@asti.dost.gov.ph to request more information about the IP addresses inside DOST-ASTI. Qurium also mailed to &#8220;IP Solutions Inc.&#8221; as suppliers of the Sophos XG Firewalls for extra information.</p>



<p>The mailbox of Denis F. Villorente, point of contact for Abuse cases (Abuse ASTIPH) bounced as mailbox was full. IP Solutions did not respond to our request for information.</p><div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-large"><a href="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-13.png" class="grouped_elements" rel="tc-fancybox-group13134"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="649" height="628" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-13.png" alt="" class="wp-image-13305" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-13.png 649w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2021/06/image-13-300x290.png 300w" sizes="auto, (max-width: 649px) 100vw, 649px" /></a><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Abuse contact details for the network</figcaption></figure></div><p>[29 June 2021] The public official response that we have read in the media:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Rowena Cristina Guevara (DOST-ASTI) told the <a href="https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1144854">PNA</a> that addresses were received from the Regional Registry (APNIC) and DOST provides IP space to other Governmental Agencies.</li>



<li>That Qurium traced the attack to the IP address does not mean the DOST involvement. </li>



<li>DOST-ASTI has not made public which Governmental Agency is behind the IP address.</li>



<li>The army spokesman Col. Ramon Zagala  <a href="https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/1450227/cyberattacks-on-red-tagged-news-sites-traced-to-dost-army">stated</a> that the Philippine Army &#8220;respects freedom of expression and per policy, will never infringe that freedom&#8221;.</li>



<li>In a Zoom interview for <a href="https://twitter.com/ANCALERTS/status/1410460315398152192">ABS-CBN</a>, DOST confirmed that the IP space belongs to an &#8220;Agency&#8221; which name can not be revealed not to burden DICT investigation. </li>
</ul>



<p>Our open questions still remain:</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>Which Organization or Institution operates behind the address 202.90.137{.}42?</li>



<li>Why such organization and their Abuse Handling details are not properly reflected in the APNIC Whois Database or any other public resource to speed up &#8220;Network Abuse&#8221; resolution?</li>



<li>Who is acepcionecjr @ army.mil.ph?</li>
</ol>
