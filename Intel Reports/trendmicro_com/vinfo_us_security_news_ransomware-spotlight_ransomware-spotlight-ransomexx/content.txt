no-margin-top">Recommendations</h2><div class="width-90-wide"><div class="wide-padding"><div class="grid-1-col-layout"><p>RansomEXX is not as active as it had been in 2020, when its consecutive attacks made it one of the newer ransomware families to watch out for. However, being a highly targeted and human-operated ransomware, its attacks affect its victims and their reputation significantly. The combination of memory-based techniques, legitimate Windows tools, and post-intrusion contribute a lot to RansomEXX’s successes.</p><p>Preventing the attacks from the outset is key to avoiding the worst of ransomware campaigns. Organizations should learn from past RansomEXX campaigns and be vigilant against initial access tactics. Users should be wary of enabling macros, and of documents that prompt them to do so.</p><p>To help defend systems against similar threats, organizations can establish security frameworks that can class="iconContainerHolder configure-icon"><br></div><h4>Configure and monitor</h4><ul><li>Manage hardware and software configurations.</li><li>Grant admin privileges and access only when necessary to a software allowlist that only executes legitimate applications.</li></ul></div><div class="grid-col-layout"><div class="iconContainerHolder patch-icon"><br></div><h4>Patch and update</h4><ul><li>Conduct regular vulnerability assessments.</li><li>Perform patching or virtual patching for operating systems and applications.</li><li>Update software and applications to their latest versions.</li></ul></div><div class="grid-col-layout"><div class="iconContainerHolder protect-icon"><br></div><h4>Protect and recover</h4><ul><li>Implement data protection, back up, and recovery measures.</li><li>Enable multifactor authentication (MFA).</li></ul></div><div class="grid-col-layout"><div class="iconContainerHolder secure-icon"><br></div><h4>Secure and defend</h4><ul><li>Employ sandbox analysis to block malicious emails.</li><li>Deploy the latest versions of security solutions to all layers of the system, including email, endpoint, web, and network.</li><li>Detect early signs of an attack such as the presence of suspicious tools in the system.</li><li>Use advanced detection technologies such as those powered by AI and machine learning.</li></ul></div><div class="grid-col-layout"><div class="iconContainerHolder test-icon"><br></div><h4>Train and test</h4><ul><li>Regularly train and assess employees on security skills.</li><li>Conduct red-team exercises and penetration tests.</li></ul></div></div><div class="grid-1-col-layout"><p>A multilayered approach can help organizations guard possible entry points into
