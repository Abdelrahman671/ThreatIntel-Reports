			<h1 class="entry-title">Trickbot Still Alive and Well</h1>
		<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="https://thedfirreport.com/2021/01/11/trickbot-still-alive-and-well/" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2021-01-11T02:37:17+00:00">January 11, 2021</time></a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

		
	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	<div class="entry-content">
		
<p>In October of 2020, the group behind the infamous botnet known as Trickbot had a bad few days. The group was under concerted pressure applied by US <a href="https://www.cyberscoop.com/trickbot-takedown-cyber-command-microsoft/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cyber Command</a> infiltrating the botnet, and allegedly, providing <a href="https://www.lawfareblog.com/persistently-engaging-trickbot-uscybercom-takes-notorious-botnet" target="_blank" rel="noopener">alternate configuration</a> files to break the bot&#8217;s connections to the larger network. At the same time, <a href="https://www.zdnet.com/article/microsoft-says-it-took-down-94-of-trickbots-command-and-control-servers/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Microsoft</a> along with other partners, secured <a href="https://www.noticeofpleadings.com/trickbot/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">court orders</a> to take over and take down Trickbot command and control servers.</p>
<p>While this did appear to have a short term effect on limiting the scope of the botnet operators, there have been reports on the limits of its&#8217; effectiveness. In our collection there was certainly a drop in overall Trickbot activity, but since the October disruption, we have seen it begin to rise again; this is a recent intrusion from late December.</p>
<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Case Summary</h2>
<p>The Trickbot threat actors used Cobalt Strike to pivot through-out the domain, dumping lsass and ntds.dit as they went. They used tools such as AdFind, Nltest, Net, Bloodhound, and PowerView to peruse the domain, looking for high privileged credentials to accomplish their mission. They used PowerShell, SMB, and WMI to move laterally.</p>
<p>After acquiring the necessary credentials, the threat actors used a technique called Overpass-the-hash to move to a backup server, before being kicked off the network. We believe if this attack had been allowed to continue, it would have ended in domain wide ransomware, specifically Ryuk.</p>
<h2><span style="color: #222222; font-family: Lato, serif; font-size: 24px;">MITRE ATT&amp;CK</span></h2>





<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a>Initial Access</a></h3>
<p>The original delivery mechanism was not found, but likely to have been a malicious email based on previous known Trickbot campaigns.</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading"><a>Execution</a></h3>
<p>Trickbot was manually executed on a single endpoint. Source: <a href="https://tria.ge/201221-tbmr9vdc86/behavioral1#report">Hatching Triage | Behavioral Report</a></p>



<h3>Privilege Escalation</h3>
<p>During the intrusion, we witnessed the threat actors elevate privileges on several systems using the built-in GetSystem named pipe privilege escalation tool in Cobalt Strike.</p>
<p><a href="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-5.png"><img fetchpriority="high" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1876" src="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-5.png" alt="" width="1379" height="389" srcset="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-5.png 1379w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-5-300x85.png 300w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-5-1024x289.png 1024w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-5-768x217.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1379px) 100vw, 1379px" /></a></p>
<h3><a>Defense Evasion</a></h3>
<p>After executing on the infected endpoint, the Trickbot executable injected itself into the Window Error Reporting Manager (wermgr.exe).</p>
<p><a href="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-1.png"><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1870" src="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-1.png" alt="" width="560" height="574" srcset="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-1.png 560w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-1-293x300.png 293w" sizes="(max-width: 560px) 100vw, 560px" /></a></p>
<p>Subsequent Trickbot command and control traffic then originated from the injected wermgr.exe process going forward.</p>
<p><a href="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-6.png"><img decoding="async" class="alignnone wp-image-1880 size-full" src="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-6.png" alt="" width="1143" height="200" srcset="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-6.png 1143w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-6-300x52.png 300w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-6-1024x179.png 1024w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/trick-21-6-768x134.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1143px) 100vw, 1143px" /></a></p>
<p>Using the YARA rule generated by <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.cobalt_strike" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Malpedia</a> we were able to locate Cobalt Strike injections in the following processes.</p>
