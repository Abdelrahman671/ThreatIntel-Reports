    <h1 class="text-center">Kazakhstan-associated YoroTrooper disguises origin of attacks as Azerbaijan</h1>

    <div class="text-center m-1">
        <div class="post-author">
            <span>By </span>
                <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/author/asheer-malhotra/">Asheer Malhotra</a>, 
                <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/author/vitor-ventura/">Vitor Ventura</a>
        </div>

        <br/>
        <time class="post-datetime" datetime="October 25, 2023 08:01">
            Wednesday, October 25, 2023 08:01
        </time>

        <div class="m-3">
                            <a href="/category/threats/" class="category primary-category">
                                Threats
                            </a>
                                                    <a href="/category/threat-spotlight/" class="category primary-category">
                                Threat Spotlight
                            </a>
                                                    <a href="/category/securex-3/" class="category primary-category">
                                SecureX
                            </a>
                                </div>
    </div>

    <section class="post-content-wrapper mt-5">
        <div class="post-content">
            <p></p><ul><li>Cisco Talos assesses with high confidence that <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/yorotrooper-espionage-campaign-cis-turkey-europe/">YoroTrooper, an espionage-focused threat actor</a> first active in June 2022, likely consists of individuals from Kazakhstan based on their use of Kazakh currency and fluency in Kazakh and Russian. The actor also appears to have a defensive interest in the website of the Kazakhstani state-owned email service and has rarely targeted Kazakh entities.</li><li>YoroTrooper attempts to obfuscate the origin of their operations, employing various tactics to make its malicious activity appear to emanate from Azerbaijan, such as using VPN exit nodes local to that region.&nbsp;</li><li>YoroTrooper’s targeting appears to be focused on Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) countries, and the operators have compromised multiple state-owned websites and accounts belonging to government officials of these countries between May and August 2023.</li><li>Our findings also indicate that, in addition to commodity and custom malware, YoroTrooper continues to rely heavily on phishing emails that direct victims to credential harvesting sites, an assessment that is in line with recent <a href="https://web-assets.esetstatic.com/wls/2023/05/eset_apt_activity_report_q42022_q12023.pdf">reporting from ESET</a>.</li><li>Recent retooling efforts by YoroTrooper demonstrate a conscious effort to move away from commodity malware and increasingly rely on new custom malware spanning across different platforms such as Python, PowerShell, GoLang and Rust.</li></ul><p></p><p></p><h1 id="yorotrooper-operators-likely-based-in-kazakhstan">YoroTrooper operators likely based in Kazakhstan</h1><p>Talos assesses with high confidence that YoroTrooper operators are likely based in Kazakhstan based on their language preferences, use of Kazakhstani currency and very limited targeting of Kazakhstani entities, which only included the government’s Anti-Corruption Agency.&nbsp;</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/JT3nlbB9Cb1QLM73Nr8DSdsMwIWC1tXHQlvu6fUyDE4QEvt-Uek3PDYblTeguNJtaJy3721cpA9U_yr7jBo2w9ExpiRurkt4DYV-YeeOn0cAikK-TyJMi91dgRxBx9_10oWPMUKL3kewxeILe1Z9l58" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="329"></figure><p></p><p>Our primary observation that points toward the actor being of Kazakh origin is that they speak Kazakh and Russian, both of which are official languages of Kazakhstan. YoroTrooper frequently visits websites written in Kazakh and has used Russian in debugging and logging messages in their custom Python remote access trojans (RATs). For example, we have seen phrases such as “Сохраняю в {save_dir}” or “Файл загружен!\nИмя” that translate to “I save in {save_dir}” and “File uploaded!\nName” respectively, and the translation of output of commands to CP866 — the code page for Cyrillic.&nbsp;</p><p></p><p>Starting in June 2023, we saw this actor using Uzbek in their implants, another popular language in Kazakhstan, enabling us to narrow down their country of origin. While this may be an attempt at generating false flags to masquerade as an Uzbek adversary, it is highly likely that YoroTrooper operators are simply well-versed in Kazakh, Russian and Uzbek languages.</p><p></p><p>A second observation that supports our assessment of YoroTrooper’s strong ties to Kazakhstan&nbsp; is the involvement of Kazakhstani currency in their operations. The threat actor primarily relies on using cryptocurrency to pay for operating infrastructure such as domains and servers for hosting their lures, payloads and decoys, and regularly checks for currency conversion rates between Kazakhstani Tenge (KZT), Kazakhstan’s official currency and Bitcoin (BTC) on Google.</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/J5dpaAQKE8azy6HBBLUfX14gkkiwfCGdD7R73DlCzVTq0TJfe_GO9pTxZxRSogUGTVz9PkmWaR9GAWZG8zby_akOWRZTTFLQl0As6SviD6z4pX28a8GbsaA06RzVvlnr48O8rTatXMSVcqQxDVERg8c" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="56"></figure><p></p><p>The threat actor also uses online exchanges, such as alfachange[.]com, which converts money from Kazakhstani Tenge to Bitcoin via their Visa and Mastercard cards:</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/fuYGTfZsony4c8n2b0OZsTJb6S_ar6vxXkUueFDEgg4--Xw8Z-upvzwOOOj0i5gnWmaasKglTgzWQK9YJjODkwXzE5yc84-uoOjODmS2wrmn04eUfoSyl-VMFAdMtmrzzghVU6e4s2LD2cugsPQfUFY" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="37"></figure><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/qtUA4-lKr30O5vt6UB2-Iv5CdMSSAL3_wA6k24ERiTiQMkZ7W6ktauJeI6gJ5Kf0g3vIwuPp33M0QIv3XOwZF9tdIa_nOK_UYdll61pWLaq0Rj_Q3x-KZIZMS1YNYQTkSpouqP_ztbHZGhx1tr8E8Ok" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="613" height="90"></figure><p></p><p>Talos’ research found that YoroTrooper has a special defensive interest in repeatedly evaluating the security posture of the website of the Kazakhstani state-owned email service, mail[.]kz. YoroTrooper will regularly conduct security scans of mail[.]kz but has never registered any look-a-like domains or created credential harvesting pages spoofing the site, tactics the threat actor commonly uses when attempting to target an online service or its users. The image below shows bookmarks pertaining to the evaluation of mail[.]kz’s security posture saved on a browser used by YoroTrooper, indicating that the threat actor frequently visits these links to monitor the website for potential security vulnerabilities.</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/DGKbfoSobq2OlAhm98RJQtf7ccfuvhZ_jufrda5Ie2Gs3gFnZ16MfFRvKyaj-mfVacSp1QgVa9npcSLWc9OFjtdtB5QvHTs8e6Wrb3eQyQoWMS8_JFjFFhTVcW7rTjeu8iMJGa8xs-5AEXOJI9559Bc" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="181"></figure><p></p><p>This monitoring activity indicates YoroTrooper values mail[.]kz, as they have conducted similar security scanning for their own malicious infrastructure to verify that it is not vulnerable to exploitation. For example, YoroTrooper queried IP address 168[.]100[.]8[.]242 on Shodan, which hosted domain mail[.]asco[.]az-link[.]email on July 11, 2022 that was used by YoroTrooper to target entities in Azerbaijan in November 2022:</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/sSRtJcze9993QWq_8ZftRA6pgf7rpnNyoRE21D8yyTTxBtG8Zh6vXH1BVsaIbOJg0vjrYe5uhnYkXcG_biWBpiwWSCo8_w2zns6j61yGJJkwtr6cQua34NsutP3G9_yaqqMzWBBousn_HHISjWyKCI8" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="583" height="103"></figure><p>YoroTrooper checking one of their own IP addresses in Shodan.</p><p></p><p>Finally, Talos’ analysis of YoroTrooper’s victimology found that the only institution targeted in Kazakhstan was the government’s Anti-Corruption Agency. YoroTrooper facilitated this attack by creating a malicious subdomain mail[.]antikor[.]gov[.]kz[.]openingfile[.]net, that spoofed the legitimate government domain antikor[.]gov[.]kz:.</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/RYbopmITg_lWw0qNAF5EHgwZ--9_raAWIYypYIyGFNE_FoKNIrqzMD1PC-OyFQWPwzTrwh_OPvoFIO1pD9yzS0k_LClWpGYbOGscCbY7d-6AIUl-nCuT31T1Vu24CjzH04E-ADtBrK0sbaYHuWIp8o4" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="611" height="765"></figure><p> </p><h2 id=""> <br></h2><h1 id="threat-actor-attempts-to-masquerade-as-azerbaijani">Threat actor attempts to masquerade as Azerbaijani</h1><p>We assess with high confidence that YoroTrooper made numerous efforts to disguise their origin by hosting a majority of their infrastructure in Azerbaijan while still targeting institutions in Azerbaijan, using malicious sub-domains such as:</p><p></p><ul><li>mail[.]economy[.]qov[.]az-link[.]email</li><li>mail[.]gov[.]az-link[.]email</li><li>mail[.]mfa[.]az-link[.]email</li></ul><p></p><p>YoroTrooper employs numerous tactics to obfuscate the origin of their activity, attempting to appear as if they are located in Azerbaijan. We observed that most of YoroTrooper’s operations are routed via Azerbaijan, though notably, the threat actor does not appear to speak the Azerbaijani language. Intelligence obtained by Talos indicates the adversary regularly translates information from Azerbaijani to Russian, the second official language in Kazakhstan.</p><p></p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh5.googleusercontent.com/9sUT0W4r8hbY1CyssBfqUZTwGwmdBrNqopZeeOqjKFkOuxddM8DINfIax_AGZUP6AhTm2KH3mmTsRXWIACYURb9jBjo0wFqKNAT3mQHU_0UVlLuSC4jB8OGkAvLCngQZae0Hquznnw0TCF3u-U1eAf4" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="83"></figure><p>YoroTrooper using Google Translate to convert text from Azerbaijani to Russian for an account verification message.</p><p></p><p>Furthermore, the operator drafts lures in Russian and then translates them to Azerbaijani to use in their phishing attacks:</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/avH5fX6k68DDzPsmTCoDHb3PkQhOILnzdaA4tWuyXaqJe9wLrndgVs8vr4cR-kPKehOW1clUViz-zyBrctmBcFZI3z9InU0lH88hgeSOSQJQbppNBW5i-Qc3QXEGrVn8AXm9TCi-ypLtCDhRsm-wI5k" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="408"></figure><p></p><p>YoroTrooper makes an effort to have their operations appear as if they originate from Azerbaijan, looking to use VPN exit nodes in the country:</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/8OX1Pv0pTC047k-fc-RuUHvkjikkZY37rFSQidJ1LchfKphLeDJcBndFNAzy7oFcan5XzXVukUL7V1hPkAa_hyt_-QtdzKxtloOSNopdHOgXz_TOv_JqOyTUg4Rp2ZiLikhXZKZ8cD1FWRm8q94MfFk" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="73"></figure><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/OPIgyOMN66aiLzmka6GBJCWF3xIzk6ES5-a5X-rsLXjxXJFterBTF6UxH0K8wWPDvZgYO9vVxlvNkcoeqRGJAaAfoltCiajLjaWzGc9hJkhSO0cNf83Nx6nJd54zDCzBEJ_RRfpw-B6tEN6PqPD7UfM" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="99"></figure><p></p><p>Finally, we have also seen the threat actor searching for random contact information for Azerbaijani individuals, likely to use when setting up their infrastructure and tools:</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/dmy5gfEtisxVA26KMP4Vm6scM06EvZSbbPRZXP4dXxfR0UkZz06OrAW8kPuIonObP8PRT_EYThEs-XbyCKI6NDLxcZYdaopfvFiyp--8a88K-n1C3eWeZneTT25VsljT6J1Fkb09hFyJdsVvye1Rlg0" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="57"></figure><p></p><p></p><h2 id="targeting-activity-focuses-on-prominent-government-officials-and-organizations-in-cis-countries"><br>Targeting activity focuses on prominent government officials and organizations in CIS countries</h2><p>YoroTrooper modified and expanded their tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs) after Talos’ <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/yorotrooper-espionage-campaign-cis-turkey-europe/">seminal disclosure</a> on this threat actor in March 2023, and continued their targeting efforts against Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) countries with these new TTPs starting in June 2023. Some of these tactics included:&nbsp;</p><p></p><ul><li>Porting their Python-based implant to PowerShell.</li><li>Increasingly adopting the use of custom implants and abandoning previously used commodity malware.</li></ul><p></p><p>YoroTrooper’s targeting of government entities in these countries may indicate the operators are motivated by Kazakh state interests or working under the direction of the Kazakh government. It is also possible, however, that the actors are simply motivated by financial gain achieved by selling restricted state information. Talos is pursuing further research on YoroTrooper’s intelligence collection goals to ascertain the group’s potential state sponsorship.&nbsp;</p><p></p><p>A number of prominent and successful YoroTrooper intrusions took place in recent months, beginning in June 2023 when the adversary compromised a Tajiki national. Although we could not determine the identity of the victim, Talos assesses that the victim is associated with the Tajik government, based on the nature of the data that YoroTrooper exfiltrated from them, which amounted to 165MB of documents. Many of these documents consisted of government certificates and affidavits, appearing to belong to someone who has visibility into government personnel management and welfare.</p><p></p><p>YoroTrooper consistently relies on vulnerability scanners such as Acunetix and open-source data from search engines such as Shodan to locate and infiltrate a target’s infrastructure. This exercise turned out to be extremely fruitful for YoroTrooper, who from May to July 2023, successfully compromised three state-owned Tajiki and Kyrgyzstani websites and hosted malware payloads on them, with some malware still being hosted as of September 2023. The first website compromised in May 2023 was tpp[.]tj, which is managed by the country’s Chamber of Commerce and Industry of the Republic of Tajikistan.</p><p></p><p>Subsequently, in July, YoroTrooper compromised and hosted malware on akn[.]tj, another state-owned website belonging to the<strong> </strong>Drug Control Agency under the President of the Republic of Tajikistan, as well as kyrgyzkomur[.]gov[.]kg, which belongs to Kyrgyzstan's state-owned coal enterprise.YoroTrooper also compromised a user from the Ministry of Transport and Roads of the Kyrgyz Republic, successfully harvesting some browser credentials from the user.</p><p></p><p>YoroTrooper began its campaign to target Uzbeki government entities as early as January 2023. About eight months of aggressive attack attempts yielded success in August 2023, when YoroTrooper successfully compromised a high-ranking official from the Uzbek Ministry of Energy. While Talos confirmed the compromise, we could not determine what data was stolen from this individual.</p><p></p><p>The following timeline provides an updated view and details of various geographies targeted since June 2023.&nbsp;</p>

The sample consists of a mere 13 lines of Python code packed into a 6MB PyInstaller binary:</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/d56eVtwdNiU_lRFa6S1O730Pgf72dJaIbOCAxCj2nRLSAHmfK7JaiWtIswRIpaQtcpFIknukVapBtu_ysEeEbIj7SkrrSeCnzVSZ3AbG-96rcPLfTvNqqWz8Sf5FQr2u8vhGG6xNIZhdSr53tYXlM50" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="297"></figure><p></p><p>The malware payload downloaded is the <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/yorotrooper-espionage-campaign-cis-turkey-europe/">Python-based RAT</a> that YoroTrooper has used for quite some time now, and is the same RAT that was recently ported by the actors to PowerShell.</p><p> </p><h2 id="-8"> <br></h2><p></p><h3 id="rust-and-golang-based-implants">Rust and Golang-based implants</h3><p>As recently as September 2023, YoroTrooper began using a Rust-based implant that opens an interactive reverse-shell via the command:</p><p>cmd.exe /d /c &lt;command_from_C2&gt;</p><p>Their Golang-based implants are ports of the Python-based RAT that uses Telegram channels for file exfiltration and C2 communication. So far we have seen the Python-based RAT already being ported to two other languages, PowerShell and Golang.</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh4.googleusercontent.com/cq5Y5oeSU4YpQqCgecCGPULpfZQ4wqAErUgs-UJf4cLtcRw6ikQs7_RybZg9xJcIF4deyx2daIsFTr3hJ9x2VULW_b6GJ2-1ATljJx1v4AhvxFWrdSs0lrhJqBttb8GId8LiCIVoE__VHBFrcet-TbU" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="411" height="351"></figure><p>GoLang implant checking for “/run” command from the C2.</p><p> </p><h2 id="-9"> <br></h2><h1 id="coverage">Coverage</h1><p>Ways our customers can detect and block this threat are listed below.</p><p></p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://lh6.googleusercontent.com/SVR6ZVqkQvWUDgHRBzJnGgI72HpvotHl4KgxexbLnK3vyqA2WG9wUtoajz728yzjgzZazytoDWkPMKMxLw01cwknCy1jwMzSr9mZ2xqMabximEKvH95Vx6TV-sZF4vgKP2baOKzkPyCoy1AbVAFslik" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="624" height="127"></figure><p></p><p><a href="https://www.cisco.com/c/en/us/products/security/amp-for-endpoints/index.html">Cisco Secure Endpoint</a> (formerly AMP for Endpoints) is ideally suited to prevent the execution of the malware detailed in this post.
