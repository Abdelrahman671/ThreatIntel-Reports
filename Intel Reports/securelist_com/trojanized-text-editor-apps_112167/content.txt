												<div class="c-wysiwyg">
													<p>&#8220;Malvertising&#8221; is a popular way of attracting victims to malicious sites: an advertisement block is placed at the top of the search results, increasing the likelihood of users clicking the link. Sites at the top of search results also tend to be more trusted by users. A year ago, our experts <a href="https://securelist.com/malvertising-through-search-engines/108996/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">discussed</a> a malvertising campaign that spread the <strong>RedLine</strong> stealer via Google Ads. Using <a href="https://encyclopedia.kaspersky.com/glossary/typosquatting/?utm_source=securelist&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=termin-explanation" target="_blank" rel="noopener">typosquatting</a> and other techniques, the attackers tried to make their resources look as similar as possible to the official websites of popular programs.</p>
<p>This time, a similar threat has affected users of one of the most popular search engines in the Chinese internet. We&#8217;ve discovered two related cases where modified versions of popular text editors were distributed in this system: in the first case, the malicious resource appeared in the advertisement section; in the second case, at the top of the search results. We have not yet been able to establish all the details of the threat, so this material may be updated later.</p>
<h2 id="malicious-sites-in-search-results">Malicious sites in search results</h2>
<p>The screenshots below show two searches which the search engine responds to with malicious links:</p>
<div id="attachment_112169" style="width: 1034px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-112169" class="size-large wp-image-112169" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-1024x352.png" alt="Malicious link in the advertisement section for the search notepad++ (left) and search results for vnote (right)" width="1024" height="352" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-1024x352.png 1024w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-300x103.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-768x264.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-1018x350.png 1018w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-740x254.png 740w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-814x280.png 814w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01-800x275.png 800w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11114834/Trojanized_Notepad_01.png 1428w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-112169" class="wp-caption-text">Malicious link in the advertisement section for the search notepad++ (left) and search results for vnote (right)</p></div>
<p>The malicious site found in the <strong>notepad++</strong> search is distributed through an advertisement block. Opening it, an attentive user will immediately notice an amusing inconsistency: the website address contains the line <strong>vnote</strong>, the title offers a download of <strong>Notepad&#8208;&#8208;</strong> (an analog of <strong>Notepad++</strong>, also distributed as open-source software), while the image proudly shows <strong>Notepad++</strong>. In fact, the packages downloaded from here contain <strong>Notepad&#8208;&#8208;</strong>.</p>

Although the attackers removed any direct mention of <strong>Geacon</strong> from their project, we found a large number of lines, names, and code fragments of functions and modules matching implementations of <a href="https://github.com/Z3ratu1/geacon_plus/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>geacon_plus</strong></a>, <strong>geacon_pro</strong>, and <a href="https://github.com/jas502n/BeaconTool" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>BeaconTool</strong></a>. For example, they have almost completely identical <strong>sysinfo</strong> modules, functions <strong>FirstBlood</strong>, <strong>EncryptedMetaInfo</strong>, <strong>PullCommand</strong>, and so on:</p>
<div id="attachment_112179" style="width: 1034px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-112179" class="size-large wp-image-112179" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11-1024x243.png" alt="Comparison of the list of functions of the sysinfo module of DPysMac64 (left) and an instance of geacon_pro (right)" width="1024" height="243" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11-1024x243.png 1024w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11-300x71.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11-768x182.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11-740x175.png 740w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11-800x190.png 800w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120633/Trojanized_Notepad_11.png 1143w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-112179" class="wp-caption-text">Comparison of the list of functions of the <strong>sysinfo</strong> module of DPysMac64 (left) and an instance of geacon_pro (right)</p></div>
<p>The backdoor has two launch options – normal and as a service. Communication with the C2 server <strong>dns[.]transferusee[.]com</strong> is carried out via HTTPS protocol. Interestingly, the attackers named the project which implements the functionality of executing remote commands <strong>spacex</strong>:</p>
<div id="attachment_112180" style="width: 658px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120705/Trojanized_Notepad_12.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-112180" class="size-full wp-image-112180" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120705/Trojanized_Notepad_12.png" alt="The name of the backdoor module contained in the lines of the DPysMac64 file" width="648" height="448" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120705/Trojanized_Notepad_12.png 648w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120705/Trojanized_Notepad_12-300x207.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120705/Trojanized_Notepad_12-506x350.png 506w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120705/Trojanized_Notepad_12-405x280.png 405w" sizes="(max-width: 648px) 100vw, 648px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-112180" class="wp-caption-text">The name of the backdoor module contained in the lines of the DPysMac64 file</p></div>

<h2 id="connection-between-infected-applications">Connection between infected applications</h2>
<p>While we cannot be certain about the files previously downloaded from <strong>vnote[.]info</strong>, we have discovered that the sources distributing applications on both sites are the same. It&#8217;s also worth mentioning another interesting detail that we found completely by chance during the examination of the modified <strong>NotePad&#8208;&#8208;</strong>. In the lines of the executable file, we found text resembling an <strong>About</strong> window, but instead of a link to the official project website, it contained a link to the suspicious resource <strong>vnotepad[.]com</strong>. Below is a screenshot of the <strong>About</strong> window in the program&#8217;s user interface:</p>
<div id="attachment_112181" style="width: 1034px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-112181" class="size-large wp-image-112181" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-1024x580.png" alt="About window of modified Notepad--" width="1024" height="580" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-1024x580.png 1024w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-300x170.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-768x435.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-618x350.png 618w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-740x419.png 740w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-494x280.png 494w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13-800x453.png 800w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11120750/Trojanized_Notepad_13.png 1456w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-112181" class="wp-caption-text">About window of modified Notepad&#8208;&#8208;</p></div>
<p>The link in the <strong>About</strong> window led us to a stub page:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141.png" alt="" width="2298" height="542" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-112235" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141.png 2298w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-300x71.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-1024x242.png 1024w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-768x181.png 768w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-1536x362.png 1536w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-2048x483.png 2048w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-1484x350.png 1484w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-740x175.png 740w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-1187x280.png 1187w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154800/Trojanized_Notepad_141-800x189.png 800w" sizes="(max-width: 2298px) 100vw, 2298px" /></a></p>
<p>We found it strange, so we tried to switch from HTTP to HTTPS, which made it possible to discover that this site is another copy of the <strong>VNote</strong> site, similar to the one we saw on <strong>vnote[.]info</strong>. Furthermore, when opening this site, the browser warned us that the certificate it was using was invalid because it was issued for <strong>vnote[.]info</strong>:</p>
<div id="attachment_112182" style="width: 1034px" class="wp-caption aligncenter"><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154934/Trojanized_Notepad_15.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-112182" class="size-large wp-image-112182" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2024/03/11154934/Trojanized_Notepad_15.png" alt="Certificate used by the site vnotepad[.]com" width="1024" height="453" /></a><p id="caption-attachment-112182" class="wp-caption-text">Certificate used by the site vnotepad[.]com</p></div>
<p>This indicates a definite connection between the two cases described, as well as the high probability that the purpose of the modified <strong>VNote</strong> editors is similar to that of <strong>NotePad&#8208;&#8208;</strong>, and involves delivering the next stage of infection.</p>
<h2 id="conclusion">Conclusion</h2>
<p>We&#8217;re continuing to study the threat described above and are searching for intermediate stages that have not yet been discovered. In addition, we&#8217;ve established that the changes in the Linux and macOS applications are identical, suggesting the possibility of a backdoor for Linux that is similar to the one we found for macOS.</p>
