<p>Welcome to the Threat Context Monthly blog series where we provide a comprehensive roundup of the most relevant cybersecurity news and threat information from KrakenLabs, Outpost24’s cyber threat intelligence team. Here’s what you need to know <a>from</a> September.</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-threat-actor-of-the-month-noname-ransomware"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="threat-actor-of-the-month-noname-ransomware"></span>Threat actor of the month: NoName (Ransomware)<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p><em>“<strong>NoName</strong>”</em>&nbsp;is a ransomware group that emerged in 2020 but gained prominence in 2023. Despite name similarities, it should not be confounded with the pro-Russian hacktivist of the same name.</p>



<p>As an initial attack vector, the group employs&nbsp;<strong>brute-force</strong>&nbsp;attacks and exploiting&nbsp;<a href="https://www.welivesecurity.com/en/eset-research/cosmicbeetle-steps-up-probation-period-ransomhub/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">known vulnerabilities</a>, such as&nbsp;<strong>EternalBlue</strong>&nbsp;(CVE-2017-0144) and&nbsp;<strong>Zerologon</strong>&nbsp;(CVE-2020-1472). The adversary employs different ransomware families, such as <strong>RansomHub</strong>, <strong>Scara</strong>, and <strong>ScRansom</strong>.</p>



<p><em>NoName</em> has notably attempted to <strong>leverage the reputation of the <em>LockBit</em></strong> <strong>Group</strong>, even creating a leak site called &#8220;NONAME&#8221; that closely resembles <em>LockBit</em>&#8216;s design. However, <em>NoName</em> primarily focuses on data encryption, they <strong>do not consistently exfiltrate data</strong>. Instead, they utilize their <strong>leak site</strong> <strong>to create a sense of urgency </strong>among victims.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-image size-large no-border"><img decoding="async" width="1024" height="507" src="//outpost24.com/wp-content/plugins/a3-lazy-load/assets/images/lazy_placeholder.gif" data-lazy-type="image" data-src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-1024x507.png" alt="" class="lazy lazy-hidden wp-image-6900" srcset="" data-srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-1024x507.png 1024w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-300x149.png 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-768x380.png 768w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-1536x761.png 1536w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1.png 1876w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /><noscript><img decoding="async" width="1024" height="507" src="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-1024x507.png" alt="" class="wp-image-6900" srcset="https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-1024x507.png 1024w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-300x149.png 300w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-768x380.png 768w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1-1536x761.png 1536w, https://outpost24.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/image1-1.png 1876w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px" /></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption"><em>NoName Data Leak Site</em></figcaption></figure>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-spotlight-threat-chromium-zero-day-cve-2024-7971"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="spotlight-threat-chromium-zero-day-cve-2024-7971"></span>Spotlight threat: Chromium zero-day (CVE-2024-7971)<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<p>Microsoft recently uncovered a&nbsp;<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2024/08/30/north-korean-threat-actor-citrine-sleet-exploiting-chromium-zero-day/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">new campaign</a>&nbsp;linked to the&nbsp;<strong>North Korean</strong>&nbsp;&#8220;<strong><em>Lazarus Group&#8221;</em></strong>, targeting the&nbsp;<strong>cryptocurrency sector</strong>&nbsp;for financial gain. The group exploited a&nbsp;<strong>zero-day vulnerability in the Chromium</strong>&nbsp;engine, designated as&nbsp;<a href="https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2024-7971" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>CVE-2024-7971</strong></a>.</p>



<p>In this attack, the&nbsp;<strong><em>Lazarus Group</em></strong>&nbsp;directed victims to an attacker-controlled exploit domain, possibly through&nbsp;<strong>social engineering</strong>&nbsp;techniques. Once a user connected, the&nbsp;<strong>zero-day exploit</strong>&nbsp;allowed the adversary to achieve&nbsp;<strong>remote code execution (RCE)</strong>, after which they deployed a&nbsp;<strong>sophisticated rootkit</strong>&nbsp;called<strong>&nbsp;<em>FudModule</em></strong>&nbsp;onto the compromised systems. This malware is capable of&nbsp;<strong>escaping browser sandboxes</strong>&nbsp;and tampering with&nbsp;<strong>Windows kernel security</strong>&nbsp;via&nbsp;<a href="https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2024-38106" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener"><strong>CVE-2024-38106</strong></a><strong>&nbsp;exploitation</strong>, allowing the threat actor to maintain long-term access to the target’s systems.</p>



<p>The attack is part of a&nbsp;<strong>broader strategy by North Korean</strong>&nbsp;nation-state threat actors to&nbsp;<strong>fund state operations</strong>&nbsp;by targeting vulnerable cryptocurrency platforms.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-krakenlabs-observed-highlights"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="krakenlabs-observed-highlights"></span>KrakenLabs observed highlights<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h2>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-vulnerabilities"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="vulnerabilities"></span>Vulnerabilities<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p><em>Critical Vulnerability</em>: The vulnerability&nbsp;<strong>CVE-2024-40766</strong>&nbsp;in&nbsp;<strong>SonicWall&nbsp;</strong>SonicOS firewall devices is being exploited by&nbsp;<strong><em>Akira&nbsp;</em>and other ransomware groups</strong>. This affects SSLVPN features and firewalls in Gen 5, 6, and 7. Attackers have&nbsp;<strong>disabled multi-factor authentication</strong>&nbsp;during breaches, and government agencies are mandated to patch by September 30. <a href="https://psirt.global.sonicwall.com/vuln-detail/SNWLID-2024-0015" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Learn more</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;/&nbsp;<a href="https://arcticwolf.com/resources/blog/arctic-wolf-observes-akira-ransomware-campaign-targeting-sonicwall-sslvpn-accounts/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">more</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;/<a href="https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/2024/09/09/etr-cve-2024-40766-critical-improper-access-control-vulnerability-affecting-sonicwall-devices/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">&nbsp;and more&nbsp;→</a></p>



<p><em>0-day</em>: The Chinese state-sponsored threat actor<em>&nbsp;&#8220;<strong>Volt Typhoon&#8221;</strong></em> has been actively exploiting a zero-day vulnerability (<a href="https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2024-39717" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">CVE-2024-39717</a>) in&nbsp;<strong>Versa Director servers</strong>, which are widely used by&nbsp;<strong>internet service providers (ISPs)</strong>&nbsp;and<strong>&nbsp;managed service providers (MSPs).</strong>&nbsp;This vulnerability allows attackers to escalate privileges and plant a&nbsp;<strong>custom web shell</strong>, enabling them to steal credentials and&nbsp;<strong>infiltrate targeted networks</strong>. <a href="https://www.darkreading.com/cyberattacks-data-breaches/china-s-volt-typhoon-actively-exploiting-now-patched-0-day-in-versa-director-servers" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Learn more</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;/&nbsp;<a href="https://versa-networks.com/blog/versa-security-bulletin-update-on-cve-2024-39717-versa-director-dangerous-file-type-upload-vulnerability/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">more</a>&nbsp;&nbsp;/<a href="https://krebsonsecurity.com/2024/08/new-0-day-attacks-linked-to-chinas-volt-typhoon/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">&nbsp;and more&nbsp;→</a></p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-trend"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="trend"></span>Trend<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p><em>Data exfiltration tactic</em>: Data exfiltration has become a common tactic in ransomware attacks, with groups like&nbsp;<strong><em>BianLian</em>&nbsp;</strong>and&nbsp;<strong><em>Rhysida&nbsp;</em></strong>using&nbsp;<strong>Azure Storage Explorer to steal sensitive data</strong>. These actors repurpose this legitimate tool as Azure Blob Storage allows efficient handling of&nbsp;<strong>large volumes of unstructured data</strong>. <a href="https://www.modepush.com/blog/highway-blobbery-data-theft-using-azure-storage-explorer" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Learn more →</a>&nbsp;</p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-law-enforcement"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="law-enforcement"></span>Law enforcement<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p><em>Law enforcement action</em>: French authorities have indicted Pavel Durov, the&nbsp;<strong>CEO and co-founder of Telegram</strong>. The charges include complicity in running an online platform that facilitates illegal activities such as child abuse, drug sales, and hacking. An increased scrutiny, may push Telegram to&nbsp;<strong>implement stricter content moderation</strong>&nbsp;or<strong>&nbsp;cooperate with law enforcement</strong>, forcing cybercriminal to find communication alternatives. <a href="https://intel471.com/blog/france-vs-telegram-what-does-it-mean-for-cybercrime" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Learn more →</a></p>



<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-emerging-threats"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="emerging-threats"></span>Emerging threats<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>



<p><em>Attack</em>: A new attack method has been identified where&nbsp;<strong>Amadey&nbsp;</strong>drops&nbsp;<strong>StealC&nbsp;</strong>alongside a&nbsp;<strong>credential flusher</strong>. The flusher forces the&nbsp;<strong>browser into kiosk mode</strong>, displaying a Google login page that prevents victims from closing or navigating away. Frustration compels victims to enter their credentials, which are then stolen from the browser&#8217;s credential store by StealC. <a href="https://research.openanalysis.net/credflusher/kiosk/stealer/stealc/amadey/autoit/2024/09/11/cred-flusher.html" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Learn more →</a></p>



<p><em>Supply chain</em>: Researchers have uncovered a new&nbsp;<strong>supply-chain attack</strong>&nbsp;technique called&nbsp;<strong>Revival Hijack</strong>&nbsp;that exploits&nbsp;<strong>PyPI</strong>&#8216;s package removal process, potentionally allowing attackers to&nbsp;<strong>re-register and hijack</strong>&nbsp;over<strong>&nbsp;22,000</strong>&nbsp;<strong>removed</strong>&nbsp;<strong>packages</strong>. This method manipulates PyPI&#8217;s system, enabling malicious actors to&nbsp;<strong>republish packages</strong>&nbsp;under the&nbsp;<strong>same name</strong>&nbsp;after the original owner removes them.&nbsp;<a href="https://jfrog.com/blog/revival-hijack-pypi-hijack-technique-exploited-22k-packages-at-risk/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">Learn more →</a></p>
