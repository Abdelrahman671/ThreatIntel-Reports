<h2 class="sec_title">How was Search.searchetan.com set in Google Chrome?</h2>
<p>Search.searchetan.com is configured by the Easy Television Access Now extension. This extension is promoted through adware programs that display pop-up advertisements. One of the advertisements that is displayed is for the Easy Television Access Now extension.</p>
<p>If Chrome's new tab page is configured to use Easy Television Access Now and you do not find it useful, you can <a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/tutorials/how-to-remove-a-google-chrome-extension/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">remove the extension</a> from Chrome. As this extension is commonly advertised by adware, you may want to use the removal guide below to scan for and remove unwanted programs for free as well.</p>

                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/internet-explorer/reset-confirmation.jpg" alt="Reset Confirmation" width="453" height="348"></p>
                            <p>In the reset dialog shown above, please put a check mark in <strong>Delete personal settings</strong> and then click on the <strong>Reset</strong> button. Internet Explorer will now erase all your personal data, browsing history, and disable all add-ons and toolbars. Your favorites, though, will remain intact and still be accessible.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/internet-explorer/reset-finished.jpg" alt="Reset finished" width="301" height="217"></p>
                            <p>Once the Reset process has been completed, click on the <strong>Close</strong> button. You will now be prompted to restart Internet Explorer to complete the reset. Once you have restarted Internet Explorer, you can continue with the rest of the instructions.</p>
                           </section><section id="section-firefox"><p>In order to remove <strong>Search.searchetan.com Chrome New Tab Page</strong> completely you will need to refresh Firefox back to its initial settings. It does this by removing all add-ons and personalized configuration settings. All of your bookmarks, though, will be preserved. </p>
                            <p>To reset Firefox, open the program and click on the Firefox menu<br>button (<img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/firefox/menu-button.jpg" alt="Firefox menu button" width="34" height="33" align="absmiddle">) in the top right-hand corner of the window. This will open the main menu for Firefox as shown below.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/firefox/firefox-menu.jpg" alt="Firefox Menu" width="650" height="577"></p>
                            <p>&nbsp;</p>
                            <p>Now click on the question mark button (<img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/firefox/help-button.jpg" alt="Help button" width="48" height="38" align="absmiddle">) as indicated by the arrow in the image above. This will open up the Firefox help menu.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/firefox/help-menu.jpg" alt="Help Menu " width="650" height="577"></p>
                            <p>Next click on the <strong>Troubleshooting Information</strong> option as indicated by the arrow in the image above. This will bring you to a Troubleshooting page.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/firefox/troubleshooting-information.jpg" alt="Troubleshooting Information Page" width="650" height="577"></p>
                            <p>To begin the refresh process click on the  <strong>Refresh Firefox..</strong> button. When you do this a confirmation will be shown asking if you wish to perform a Firefox refresh.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/firefox/refresh-confirmation.jpg" alt="Firefox Refresh Confirmation" width="306" height="154"></p>
                            <p>To refresh Firefox, click on the <strong>Refresh Firefox</strong> button. When the refresh process is finished you will be shown an Import window that will automatically close. When that closes, Firefox will be open and state that it has been refreshed.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/firefox/refresh-complete.jpg" alt="Refresh Complete" width="650" height="481"></p>
                            <p>You can now click on the <strong>Let's go!</strong> button to start using Firefox again.</p>
                           </section><section id="section-safari"><p>In order to completely remove <strong>Search.searchetan.com Chrome New Tab Page</strong> you will need to reset Safari back to its initial settings. Doing these steps will erase all configuration information from Safari such as your Top Sites, saved form information, browsing history, and cookies. This process will not erase your bookmarks or extensions, which will still be available after you reset Safari.</p>
                            <p>To reset Safari, open the program and click on the gear (<img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/safari/safari-options-gear.jpg" alt="Safari Options Gear" width="38" height="25" align="absmiddle">) in the top right-hand corner of the window. This will open the main menu for Safari as shown below.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/safari/reset-safari-menu-option.jpg" alt="Safari Options Menu" width="650" height="489"></p>
                            <p>&nbsp;</p>
                            <p>Now click on the menu option labeled <strong>Reset Safari</strong> as shown by the arrow in the picture above. This will open a window that allows you to select all the items you wish to reset.</p>
                            <p align="center"><img class="no_b_lazy" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/swr-guides/tools/reset-browsers/safari/reset-options.jpg" alt="Reset Options Window" width="464" height="438"></p>
                            <p>Keep the check marks in each option and then click on the <strong>Reset</strong> button. Safari will delete all of your personal data and then open a blank page, which means the process has finished.</p>
                            <p>It is important to note that this process does not delete your Bookmarks or any installed Safari Extensions. If you wish to remove your Safari Extensions as well, you can download <a href="https://download.bleepingcomputer.com/reset-browsers/safari/reset-safari.bat" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">this batch file</a>, which will reset Safari and delete all installed extensions, while still retaining your bookmarks.</p>
