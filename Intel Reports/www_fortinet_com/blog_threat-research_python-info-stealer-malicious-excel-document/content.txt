<p>In January 2024, FortiGuard Labs obtained an Excel document distributing an info-stealer. From the fingerprints in this attack, it is related to a Vietnamese-based group that was <a href="https://medium.com/@ic3_2k/an%C3%A1lisis-de-malware-en-python-servido-desde-gitlab-com-c90741e130a1" target="_blank">first reported</a> on in August 2023 and <a href="https://labs.guard.io/mrtonyscam-botnet-of-facebook-users-launch-high-intent-messenger-phishing-attack-on-business-3182cfb12f4d" target="_blank">again</a> in September. The attack stages before the info-stealer are simple downloaders that increase the difficulty of detection. This article introduces each stage in this attack and provides a glimpse into the world behind the malware campaign.</p>

  <h3><span style="font-weight: normal;">Test.vbs</span></h3>
<p>Test.vbs is roughly the same as the middle stage of the campaign reported in August 2023. It downloads three files:</p>
<table border="1" cellspacing="0" cellpadding="0">
<tbody><tr><td width="114" valign="top"><p>script.py</p>
</td>
<td width="377" valign="top"><p>Information stealer.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td width="114" valign="top"><p>Document.zip</p>
</td>
<td width="377" valign="top"><p>Python 3.11 with libraries for script.py.</p>
</td>
</tr><tr><td width="114" valign="top"><p>bypass.vbs</p>
</td>
<td width="377" valign="top"><p>Executes script.py with the downloaded Python.</p>
</td>
</tr></tbody></table>
<p>Script.py is obfuscated by PyObfuscate, which means it needs extra modules to be executed properly. Document.zip is downloaded to solve this problem.</p>
<p>In addition, test.vbs creates a value named “WinUpdater” within the registry key HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run to automatically run bypass.vbs when the victim logs in.</p>

<h2><span style="font-weight: normal;">Conclusion</span></h2>
<p>The threat actors separate the malware campaign into several simple downloaders and use open platforms to avoid detection. However, this also provided clues to identify the hacker group. From the information left in the files, we also found files abused in other campaigns and the SNS platforms being abused to distribute malware. Such services not only provide a convenient environment for people but are commonly used by threat actors.</p>
