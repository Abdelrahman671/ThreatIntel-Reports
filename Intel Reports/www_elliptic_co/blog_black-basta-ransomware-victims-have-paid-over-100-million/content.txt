             <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><h2 style="font-weight: normal;">Key takeaways</h2>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1">Joint research by Elliptic and Corvus Insurance has identified at least $107 million in Bitcoin ransom payments to the Black Basta ransomware group since early 2022.<br><br></li>
<li aria-level="1">Black Basta has infected over 329 victims, including Capita, ABB and Dish Network.&nbsp;<br><br></li>
<li aria-level="1">Analysis of blockchain transactions shows a clear link between Black Basta and the Conti Group - a Russian ransomware gang that ceased operations in 2022, around the time that Black Basta emerged.<br><br></li>
<li aria-level="1">Much of the laundered ransom payments can be traced onwards to Garantex, the sanctioned Russian crypto exchange.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<!--more-->
<p>Black Basta is a Russia-linked ransomware that emerged in early 2022. It has been used to attack more than 329 organizations globally and has grown to become the fourth-most active strain of ransomware by number of victims in 2022-2023. The group employs double-extortion tactics whereby they extort the victim by threatening to publish stolen data unless the victim pays a ransom.</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 11px; color: #000000;">Researchers have </span><a href="https://twitter.com/malwrhunterteam/status/1519301421958578177" style="font-size: 11px;"><span>suggested</span></a><span style="font-size: 11px; color: #000000;"> that Black Basta may be an offshoot of the Conti Group, one of the most prolific ransomware gangs of the past few years. Leaks of Conti’s online chats hinted at its </span><a href="https://www.wired.co.uk/article/conti-ransomware-russia" style="font-size: 11px;"><span>links</span></a><span style="font-size: 11px; color: #000000;"> to the Russian government and its support for the invasion of Ukraine, before the group dissolved in May 2022.</span><span style="font-size: 11px; color: #000000;"></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png?width=2500&amp;height=3639&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png" width="2500" height="3639" loading="lazy" alt="Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 2500px;" srcset="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png?width=1250&amp;height=1820&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png 1250w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png?width=2500&amp;height=3639&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png 2500w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png?width=3750&amp;height=5459&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png 3750w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png?width=5000&amp;height=7278&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png 5000w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png?width=6250&amp;height=9098&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png 6250w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png?width=7500&amp;height=10917&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-01_V3.png 7500w" sizes="(max-width: 2500px) 100vw, 2500px"></p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><em>Black Basta victims, by sector and country. (Data from the Black Basta leak site)</em></div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Black Basta targets businesses in a wide variety of sectors including construction (10% of victims), law practices (4%) and real estate (3%). In fact, Black Basta’s victimology closely resembles that of the Conti ransomware group, with an overlapping appetite for many of the same industries.</p>
<p>Black Basta has largely focused on US-based organizations, accounting for 61.9% of all victims, followed by Germany at 15.8%.</p>
<p>High-profile victims include Capita, a technology outsourcer with billions of dollars in UK government contracts, and industrial automation company ABB, which has revenues of over $29 billion. Neither company has publicly disclosed whether they paid a ransom<span style="font-size: 11px; color: #000000;">.</span></p>
<h2 style="font-weight: normal;">Identifying Black Basta ransom payments</h2>
<p>Despite the transparency of blockchains, it can be challenging to identify ransom payments made in cryptocurrency. First, ransomware groups rarely use a single wallet to receive payments, and victims rarely share details of the wallet they have paid ransoms to. This can make it difficult to track a ransomware group’s activity at scale. Second, these groups also employ complex laundering techniques to cover their blockchain tracks and conceal the illicit source of their profits.&nbsp;</p>
<p>However, our analysis of verified Black Basta cryptocurrency transactions using our crypto investigations tool – <a href="/solutions/crypto-investigations" rel="noopener">Elliptic Investigator</a>, – has uncovered unique patterns in the group’s activity. This has allowed us to identify a large number of Bitcoin ransoms paid to the group, with high confidence.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Our analysis suggests that Black Basta has received at least $107 million in ransom payments since early 2022, across more than 90 victims. The largest received ransom payment was $9 million, and at least 18 of the ransoms exceeded $1 million. The average ransom payment was $1.2 million.</p>
<p>It should be noted that these figures are a lower bound - there are likely to be other ransom payments made to Black Basta that our analysis is yet to identify - particularly relating to recent victims. Due to the overlap between the groups, some of these payments may also relate to Conti ransomware attacks.</p>
<p><img src="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png?width=2291&amp;height=1187&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png" width="2291" height="1187" loading="lazy" alt="Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 2291px;" srcset="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png?width=1146&amp;height=594&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png 1146w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png?width=2291&amp;height=1187&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png 2291w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png?width=3437&amp;height=1781&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png 3437w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png?width=4582&amp;height=2374&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png 4582w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png?width=5728&amp;height=2968&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png 5728w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png?width=6873&amp;height=3561&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-02_no_title.png 6873w" sizes="(max-width: 2291px) 100vw, 2291px"></p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-style: italic;">The number of reported Black Basta attacks, and ransoms paid, by month. The timing of the ransom payments correlates reasonably well with the timing of attacks, with peaks in payments following peaks in attacks. The dip in payments in Q1 of 2023 corresponds to a period when Black Basta is </span><a href="https://www.malwarebytes.com/blog/business/2023/02/ransomware-in-february-2023" rel="noopener" style="font-style: italic;">reported</a><span style="font-style: italic;"> to have paused operations.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div>&nbsp;</div>
<div>&nbsp;</div>
<p>Based on the number of known victims listed on Black Basta’s leak site through Q3 of 2023, our data indicates that at least 35% of known Black Basta victims paid a ransom. This is consistent with <a href="https://www.coveware.com/blog/2023/1/19/improved-security-and-backups-result-in-record-low-number-of-ransomware-payments#:~:text=FEWER%20Ransomware%20Victims%20Are%20Paying,76%25%20in%202019."><span>reports</span></a> that 41% of all ransomware victims paid a ransom in 2022.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png?width=2291&amp;height=1315&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png" width="2291" height="1315" loading="lazy" alt="Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 2291px;" srcset="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png?width=1146&amp;height=658&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png 1146w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png?width=2291&amp;height=1315&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png 2291w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png?width=3437&amp;height=1973&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png 3437w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png?width=4582&amp;height=2630&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png 4582w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png?width=5728&amp;height=3288&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png 5728w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png?width=6873&amp;height=3945&amp;name=Blackbasta_23_Graph-03_no_title.png 6873w" sizes="(max-width: 2291px) 100vw, 2291px"></p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><em>The number of reported Black Basta attacks, and ransoms paid, by month. The timing of the ransom payments correlates reasonably well with the timing of attacks, with peaks in payments following peaks in attacks.</em></div>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2 style="font-weight: normal;">Uncovering Black Basta’s financial links</h2>
<p>The Qakbot malware – which infects victims’ computers through email phishing attacks – was commonly used to deploy the Black Basta ransomware. This link between the groups is also visible on the blockchain, with portions of some victims’ ransoms sent to Qakbot wallets.</p>
<p>These transactions indicate that approximately 10% of the ransom amount was forwarded on to Qakbot, in cases where they were involved in providing access to the victim. Qakbot was <a href="https://www.justice.gov/usao-cdca/pr/qakbot-malware-disrupted-international-cyber-takedown"><span>disrupted</span></a> in August 2023 by a multinational law enforcement operation - perhaps explaining a marked reduction in Black Basta attacks in the second half of 2023.</p>
<p>The Black Basta operator appears to take an average of 14% of ransom payments. This is a typical split seen in ransomware-as-a-service operations.</p>
<p>Our analysis of Black Basta’s crypto transactions also provides new evidence of their links to Conti Group. In particular, we have traced Bitcoin worth several million dollars from Conti-linked wallets to those associated with the Black Basta operator. This further strengthens the theory that Black Basta is an offshoot or rebrand of Conti.</p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><i><img src="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/basta%20inv.png?width=4056&amp;height=2020&amp;name=basta%20inv.png" width="4056" height="2020" loading="lazy" alt="basta inv" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 4056px;" srcset="https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/basta%20inv.png?width=2028&amp;height=1010&amp;name=basta%20inv.png 2028w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/basta%20inv.png?width=4056&amp;height=2020&amp;name=basta%20inv.png 4056w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/basta%20inv.png?width=6084&amp;height=3030&amp;name=basta%20inv.png 6084w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/basta%20inv.png?width=8112&amp;height=4040&amp;name=basta%20inv.png 8112w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/basta%20inv.png?width=10140&amp;height=5050&amp;name=basta%20inv.png 10140w, https://www.elliptic.co/hs-fs/hubfs/basta%20inv.png?width=12168&amp;height=6060&amp;name=basta%20inv.png 12168w" sizes="(max-width: 4056px) 100vw, 4056px">A screenshot from Elliptic Investigator, showing transactional links between Conti, Qakbot and Black Basta.&nbsp;</i></div>
<pre style="text-align: center; font-size: 14px;">&nbsp;</pre>
<p>Elliptic Investigator also provides insights into how the ransom payments are being laundered, with millions of dollars worth of the group’s proceeds being sent to Garantex, a Russian cryptocurrency exchange. Garantex was <a href="/blog/5-billion-darknet-market-hydra-seized-by-german-authorities" rel="noopener"><span>sanctioned</span></a> by the US government in April 2022 for its role in laundering the proceeds of darknet marketplaces and ransomware gangs such as Conti.</p>
<h2 style="font-weight: normal;">Leveraging insights into Black Basta’s crypto activity</h2>
<p>This research provides important information on the cryptocurrency wallet infrastructure employed by one of the most prolific ransomware gangs in the world. This information can be used in two key ways:</p>
<ol>
<li aria-level="1">Cryptocurrency exchanges can use transaction screening tools such as <a href="/solutions/crypto-transaction-monitoring" rel="noopener">Elliptic Navigator</a> to identify any customer deposits originating from Black Basta wallets. By doing so they can help to prevent the ransom payments from being laundered, as well as providing law enforcement with timely intelligence.<br><br></li>
<li aria-level="1">Law enforcement agencies can “follow the money” using blockchain forensics tools such as <a href="/solutions/crypto-investigations" rel="noopener">Elliptic Investigator</a>, to aid with potential asset seizures and the identification of those responsible.<br><br></li>
</ol></span>
