            <ul><li>Cisco Talos is disclosing a new threat actor we deemed “Starry Addax” targeting mostly human rights activists associated with the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic (SADR) cause with a novel mobile malware.&nbsp;</li><li>Starry Addax conducts phishing attacks tricking their targets into installing malicious Android applications we’re calling “FlexStarling.”&nbsp;</li><li>For Windows-based targets<u>,</u> Starry Addax will serve credential-harvesting pages masquerading as login pages from popular media websites.&nbsp;</li></ul><p><em>Talos would like to thank the Yahoo! Paranoids Advanced Cyber Threats Team for their collaboration in this investigation.</em>&nbsp;</p><h2 id="starry-addax-has-a-special-interest-in-western-sahara">Starry Addax has a special interest in Western Sahara</h2><p>The malicious mobile application (APK), “FlexStarling,” analyzed by Talos recently masquerades as a variant of the<a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=info.spsrasd.app.sps"> <u>Sahara Press Service</u></a> (SPSRASD) App. The<a href="https://www.spsrasd.info/en/about-us"> <u>Sahara Press Service</u></a> is a media agency associated with the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic. <h2 id="starry-addax-likely-to-escalate-momentum">Starry Addax likely to escalate momentum&nbsp;</h2><p>Campaigns like this that target high-value individuals usually intend to sit quietly on the device for an extended period. All components from the malware to the operating infrastructure seem to be bespoke/custom-made for this specific campaign indicating a heavy focus on stealth and conducting activities under the radar. The use of FlexStarling with a Firebase-based C2 instead of commodity malware or commercially available spyware indicates the threat actor is making a conscious effort to evade detections and operate without being detected.&nbsp;</p><p>The timelines connected to various artifacts used in the attacks indicate that this campaign is just starting and may be in its nascent stages with more infrastructure and Starry Addax working on additional malware variants.&nbsp;</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/content/images/2024/04/data-src-image-f3a7dbee-b1a3-4d62-bdbf-17eb31f6aa3f.jpeg" class="kg-image" alt="" loading="lazy" width="1600" height="1166" srcset="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/content/images/size/w600/2024/04/data-src-image-f3a7dbee-b1a3-4d62-bdbf-17eb31f6aa3f.jpeg 600w, https://blog.talosintelligence.com/content/images/size/w1000/2024/04/data-src-image-f3a7dbee-b1a3-4d62-bdbf-17eb31f6aa3f.jpeg 1000w, https://blog.talosintelligence.com/content/images/2024/04/data-src-image-f3a7dbee-b1a3-4d62-bdbf-17eb31f6aa3f.jpeg 1600w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><h2 id="flexstarling-%E2%80%93-a-highly-capable-implant">FlexStarling – A highly capable implant&nbsp;</h2><p>The FlexStarling malware app requests a plethora of permissions from the Android OS to extract valuable information from the infected mobile device.
