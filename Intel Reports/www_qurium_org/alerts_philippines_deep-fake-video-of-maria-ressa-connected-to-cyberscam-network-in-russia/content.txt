The AI edited video was based on Ressa&#8217;s appearance in <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xpWevZ5yQz8">The Late Show with Stephen Colbert</a> from November 2022, where she was invited to discuss her insights about Democracy and freedom and the danger of living in a world dominated by social media.</p>



<p>The computer generated voice is quite similar to Maria&#8217;s, but her lips do not match the words she says. Anyone familiar with Maria Ressa and her tireless and fearless struggle against the tyrants in power and towards press freedom in Philippines, knows that this is not her words. The Philippine government has for years tried to bring Ressa down by accusing her for tax fraud, tax evasion, receiving money from the Central Intelligence Agency as well as arrested her a number of times. Although Ressa has been acquitted from all charges, the accusations will stay with her, <strong>that is how disinformation works</strong>. A seed is planted when a lie is spread, and if it is repeated enough times, it becomes a fact. As Ressa herself describes disinformation <em><strong>&#8220;a lie told a million times becomes a fact&#8221;</strong></em>. </p>



<p>The same logic applies to this deep fake scam. Although it is unlikely to believe that Maria Ressa has  made herself a fortune with Bitcoins, a seed has been planted that perhaps there is a glimpse of truth in that video. Perhaps the awarded and internationally known media mogul behind Rappler.com has not earned her position only by working hard as a journalist? </p>



<p></p>



<p>Despite the low quality of the deep fake, the video was promoted as an advertisement campaign in the Microsoft Network (MSN) targeting the Filipino audiences, with statements such as &#8220;<strong>The end for her</strong>?&#8221; and &#8220;<strong>Maria Ressa could be sued for her remarks on TV</strong>&#8220;.  </p><div style="height:44px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div><p>The greatest challenge of disinformation is not the lies, but the <strong>lack of accountability</strong> of those that disseminate the fake information and the silence support from platform providers like META and others that profit from it and allows the disinformation to thrive. </p>



<p>Qurium has investigated the deep fake video in an attempt to figure out what forces that were behind this attempt to defamation and which actors that were involved. </p>



<p>Our findings are the following:</p>



<ol class="wp-block-list">
<li>In early February 2024 the domain name ultimainv{.}website was used to distribute <strong>fake look-like articles from CNN and Rappler </strong>and a <strong>fake video</strong> of Maria Ressa promoting the bitcoin{.}method.</li>



<li>The articles were later promoted as Ads in the Microsoft Network in the Philippines using the title &#8220;The end for her?&#8221; with the motive to discredit Maria Ressa.</li>



<li>Meta data of the fake video indicates <strong>Russian editors.</strong></li>



<li>An error during the preparation of the defamation campaign against the journalist leaked in the newly registered domain a webpage offering a &#8220;Handy Heater&#8221; linked to a<strong> defunct Russian company</strong>. The page was quickly replaced by the malicious pages that were intended as part of the campaign.</li>



<li>The analysis of the different types of content of the domain ultimainv{.}website from January 2024 provides strong links with a network of malicious advertisers included in several cases of online fraud associated with ООО &#8220;МЕДИАР&#8221; (aka M1), a <strong>Russian CPA advertisement network</strong>.</li>
</ol><div style="height:28px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div><p><strong>Hence, Russian controlled infrastructure was used to set up the clone articles that featured the  deepfake of Maria Ressa promoting Bitcoins. Although the deepfake video followed the rule book of a regular scam where a celebrity was used to promote Bitcoins, it was made by Russians, and disseminated to a Philippine audience to maligning Maria Ressa. </strong></p><div style="height:47px" aria-hidden="true" class="wp-block-spacer"></div><hr class="wp-block-separator has-alpha-channel-opacity is-style-default"/>

<p>The disinformation campaign against Ressa served the deep fake video in two fake articles (hosted under <strong>ultimainv{.}website</strong>) with the graphical appearance of Rappler.com and CNN Philippines. <h2 class="wp-block-heading">What are TD Globus Contract and M1.top up to?</h2>



<p>It turns out that the Russian companies TD Globus Contract and M1.top are involved in an online shopping scam. In a Russian reputation <a href="https://reputaciy-otzyvy.com/org-ru-1187746227020">forum</a> the modus operandi of these network of fraudsters is explained: the companies advertise products online and the payment and shipment of goods is arranged by phone.  The victim of the fraud picks up the order at its local Postal Office and pays for the goods in person. In many cases, the packages are empty or contain a <a href="https://scam.su/moshenniki-pionerden-xfastcart-com.html">random</a> low cost product.  </p>

<p>Once we understood that m1{.}top acted as an intermediary between the scam sites and their victims, we were able to find more domain names used by M1 as Postback URLs. In a nutshell, M1 provides a set of links so affiliates can gain commissions if specific actions take place. </p>



<p>In all the advertisements that we reviewed, including the sites of the &#8220;Bitcoin Method&#8221; and the &#8220;Handy Heater&#8221;, the scammers only collect <strong>Name</strong> and <strong>Phone Number</strong> of the victims. This information is then forwarded to M1 by means of an API (api.m1.top) where the next stage of the scam takes place.</p>



<p>A call-center gathers the personal data of the victim, confirms the sale and ships the product to the nearest postal office of the victim. Payment takes place when the package is collected from the Postal Office by means of &#8220;cash on the delivery&#8221;. The victim does not receive the ordered product but a low quality piece of junk or a bag of sawdust.</p><div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" width="660" height="423" src="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/image-19.png" alt="" class="wp-image-22908" srcset="https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/image-19.png 660w, https://www.qurium.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/image-19-300x192.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 660px) 100vw, 660px" /></figure></div><h3 class="wp-block-heading">The role of M1</h3>



<p>In this scenario, M1 is responsible to pay those that help out promoting the content and lead to new scam sales.</p>



<p>Affiliate Website (Publisher) -&gt; M1.top (CPA) -&gt; Advertisers.</p>



<p>In order to remain unaccountable for fraud, the fraud scheme includes three different roles:</p>



<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li><strong>Affiliate Advertiser</strong> <strong>(Publisher)</strong>: Promotes the goods and forwards the Names and Phone numbers of potential victims to M1. In their website they make clear that they just advertise goods and they are not responsible of anything related to the merchandise.     </li>



<li><strong>M1 Shop (CPA):</strong> Receives the Names and Phone numbers from the publisher and hands over the information to the Advertiser. It is responsible to pay the Publishers for their &#8220;actions&#8221; and receives money from the Advertisers to play the intermediary role.</li>



<li><strong>Advertisers</strong>: They are responsible to create new offerings in the fraud network including nutrition goods (nutra) or cryptocurrency offers. They are responsible of delivering the goods or services to the clients. </li>
</ul>



<p>For this type of fraud to be effective, advertisers identities need to be protected by M1-Shop and publishers needs to be constantly renewed once their reputation has been compromised.  </p>



<p>Ultimately none takes responsibility for the fraud. The websites that promote the products are registered under fake companies and claim that they do not know the final product vendors, and the advertisement network claims that they do not monitor what is promoted in their platform etc. Something is guaranteed though, victims get scammed and everyone in their network gets paid for their &#8220;services&#8221;. </p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Conclusions</h2>



<p>During the early February, the domain name <strong>ultimainv{.}website</strong> was used to distribute fake look-like articles from CNN and Rappler and a fake video of Maria Ressa promoting the bitcoin{.}method.</p>



<p>Such articles were then promoted as Ads in the Microsoft Network in the Philippines using the title &#8220;The end for her?&#8221;</p>



<p>An error during the preparation of the defamation campaign against the journalist leaked in the newly registered domain a webpage offering a &#8220;Handy Heater&#8221;. The page was quickly replaced by the malicious pages that were intended as part of the campaign.    </p>



<p>The analysis of the different types of content of the domain  ultimainv{.}website from January 2024 provides strong links with a network of malicious advertisers included in several cases of online fraud associated with ООО &#8220;МЕДИАР&#8221; (aka M1), a Russian CPA advertisement network.</p>
