<p>In 2016, OurMine hacked the <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> accounts of <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a> co-founder <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Wales" title="Jimmy Wales">Jimmy Wales</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Twitter co-founder <a href="/wiki/Jack_Dorsey" title="Jack Dorsey">Jack Dorsey</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> CEO <a href="/wiki/Sundar_Pichai" title="Sundar Pichai">Sundar Pichai</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> co-founder <a href="/wiki/Mark_Zuckerberg" title="Mark Zuckerberg">Mark Zuckerberg</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Pinterest" title="Pinterest">Pinterest</a> was also hacked.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>  In addition to social media accounts, the group has hacked the website <a href="/wiki/TechCrunch" title="TechCrunch">TechCrunch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In October, <a href="/wiki/BuzzFeed_News" title="BuzzFeed News">BuzzFeed News</a> published an article linking the OurMine group to a <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabian</a> teenager using the name Ahmad Makki on social media. OurMine denied the allegations, claiming that Makki was only a "fan" of the group.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> One day after the article's publication, OurMine infiltrated <a href="/wiki/BuzzFeed" title="BuzzFeed">BuzzFeed</a>'s website and altered the content of several posts to read "Hacked By OurMine".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Other 2016 hacks include the <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> accounts of <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> President <a href="/wiki/Shuhei_Yoshida" title="Shuhei Yoshida">Shuhei Yoshida</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikimedia">Wikimedia</a> global account of <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Wales" title="Jimmy Wales">Jimmy Wales</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the Twitter accounts of <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marvel_Comics" title="Marvel Comics">Marvel</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the Twitter accounts of <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> Music Global;<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the Instagram accounts of <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="National Geographic (magazine)">National Geographic</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and the Twitter accounts of <a href="/wiki/National_Geographic_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="National Geographic (magazine)">National Geographic</a> Photography.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2017">2017</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=OurMine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2017"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In 2017, OurMine hacked into a <a href="/wiki/Medium_(website)" title="Medium (website)">Medium website</a> employee account. The account was part of a strategic partnerships team, allowing OurMine to hijack blogs belonging to <i><a href="/wiki/Fortune_(magazine)" title="Fortune (magazine)">Fortune</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Backchannel_(blog)" title="Backchannel (blog)">Backchannel</a>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Twitter accounts hacked in 2017 included that of <a href="/wiki/David_Guetta" title="David Guetta">David Guetta</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Times">New York Times</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/WWE" title="WWE">WWE</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones">Game of Thrones</a></i> (along with some other <a href="/wiki/HBO" title="HBO">HBO</a> TV shows, and HBO's own official account).<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian20170817_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian20170817-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They also hacked the Twitter and Facebook accounts of <a href="/wiki/PlayStation" title="PlayStation">PlayStation</a> (including a claimed leak of the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Network" title="PlayStation Network">PlayStation Network</a> databases),<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Real_Madrid" class="mw-redirect" title="Real Madrid">Real Madrid</a> (including their YouTube channel);<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> several Facebook accounts of <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">CNN</a> were also hacked.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Some <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> accounts were hacked by OurMine in 2017 included that of the Omnia Media network, gaining access to numerous channels;<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and various YouTube channels from the <a href="/wiki/Studio_71" class="mw-redirect" title="Studio 71">Studio71</a> network were also hacked.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On August 31, OurMine left a message on the homepage of <a href="/wiki/WikiLeaks" title="WikiLeaks">WikiLeaks</a>. "Hi, it’s OurMine (Security Group), don’t worry we are just testing your…. blablablab, oh wait, this is not a security test! Wikileaks, remember when you challenged us to hack you?" The messages went on to accuse Anonymous of trying to dox them with false information and included an exhortation to spread the #WikileaksHack tag on Twitter. The message was visible when the site was accessed from certain locations. At the time of publication, some visitors to the site were greeted with a message saying that WikiLeaks’ account has been suspended.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2017, OurMine claimed responsibility for hacking <a href="/wiki/Vevo" title="Vevo">Vevo</a> and publishing more than 3TB of their internal documents.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2020">2020</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=OurMine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2020"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>In January, OurMine compromised the Twitter, Facebook and Instagram accounts of the <a href="/wiki/NFL" class="mw-redirect" title="NFL">NFL</a> and 15 NFL teams.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In February, OurMine compromised Facebook's Twitter account.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Later that month, they also compromised the Twitter account of South Korean boy band, <a href="/wiki/NCT_127" title="NCT 127">NCT 127</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In May, the group OurMine invaded the Brazilian streaming platform <a href="/wiki/Globoplay" title="Globoplay">Globoplay</a> and sent a notification to all users of the Globoplay app.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>42<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
