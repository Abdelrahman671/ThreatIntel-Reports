<h1><strong><span data-contrast="auto">1. Artificial Intelligence, Cybersecurity and Large Language Models</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></h1>
<h2><strong><span data-contrast="auto"><img src="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/LLM.jpeg?width=500&amp;height=500&amp;name=LLM.jpeg" width="500" height="500" loading="lazy" alt="LLM" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 500px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/LLM.jpeg?width=250&amp;height=250&amp;name=LLM.jpeg 250w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/LLM.jpeg?width=500&amp;height=500&amp;name=LLM.jpeg 500w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/LLM.jpeg?width=750&amp;height=750&amp;name=LLM.jpeg 750w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/LLM.jpeg?width=1000&amp;height=1000&amp;name=LLM.jpeg 1000w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/LLM.jpeg?width=1250&amp;height=1250&amp;name=LLM.jpeg 1250w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/LLM.jpeg?width=1500&amp;height=1500&amp;name=LLM.jpeg 1500w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px"></span></strong></h2>
<p style="font-size: 14px; text-align: center;"><em>Image created with DALL·E 3</em></p>
<p><strong style="font-size: 1.125rem; background-color: transparent;"><span data-contrast="auto">AI and Cybersecurity</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}" style="font-size: 1.125rem; font-weight: bold; background-color: transparent;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">2023 marked significant advancements in AI, with a surge in tools developed for text, audio, and image generation that allowed a wider audience to create content in ways previously unseen. ChatGPT from OpenAI stood out, as it took 5 days for the AI chat service to reach 1 million users and about 1 month to reach 100 million users. </span><span>[<a href="#a1" rel="noopener">1</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT are designed to understand and generate human-like text. They are trained on vast amounts of data, enabling them to generate coherent text, answer questions, summarize, and translate languages. While they can be fine-tuned for specific tasks, they also have limitations in how they produce information.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">With AI's widespread deployment, misuse and abuse is spreading. Malicious AI tools and jailbroken legitimate AI tools are being used to facilitate cyberattacks. As AI becomes more integrated into various IT systems, gradually becoming their nexus, the future of hacking will increasingly revolve around AI-centric cyberattack strategies and tactics. While AI offers immense potential, its misuse, especially in hacking with the support of generative AI tools, poses significant threats to organizations and individuals. Opportunities are also arising as a consequence of the creation and implementation of AI driven cybersecurity tools. The arms race between AI driven cybersecurity offense and defense underscores the importance of research, global collaboration, and vigilance in the AI domain.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<h2><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Large Language Models</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></h2>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">ChatGPT is a Large Language Model. Large language Model is a type of artificial intelligence (AI) model designed to understand and generate human-like text based on the data it has been trained on. LLMs fall under the umbrella of deep learning models using recurrent neural networks or transformers. The Transformer architecture, introduced in 2017, revolutionized natural language processing with its unique self-attention mechanism. Unlike traditional recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and long short-term memory (LSTM) networks, Transformers process data in parallel, enhancing training efficiency. This design underpins state-of-the-art models like Google’s BERT and OpenAI’s GPT, Meta’s BART and Baidu’s ERNIE, as well as many other less widely used LLMs. </span><span>[<a href="#a2" rel="noopener">2</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">LLMs are termed "large" due to their billions of parameters. Parameters are the configuration variables internal to the model that are learned from the training data. More parameters often mean better understanding and more fluent, accurate responses. For instance, GPT-3 has 175 billion parameters, GPT-4 is estimated to have approximately 1.76 trillion. </span><span>[<a href="#a3" rel="noopener">3</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> GPT-5 is currently in the preparatory phase of development, details on capabilities and release date are currently unavailable, but it is expected to be much more powerful than its predecessors. </span><span>[<a href="#a4" rel="noopener">4</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto">&nbsp;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">LLMs are trained on vast amounts of text data from a large variety of written content. The idea is to expose the model to as much diverse language as possible so it can observe and learn grammar,</span><span>&nbsp;</span><span data-contrast="auto"> reasoning abilities, and even some level of common sense. Tokens are the basic units of text like characters, words or phrases, they enable the model to break down and analyze language into manageable units for processing. The ability to dissect and understand language is fundamentally dependent on the effective use of tokens.</span><span>[<a href="#a5" rel="noopener">5</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> Once trained, LLMs can generate coherent and contextually relevant text over long passages, answer questions based on the information they've been trained on, summarize articles or documents and translate between languages to a similar level&nbsp;expected from a university freshman.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">One of the powerful features of LLMs is their ability to be tuned for specific tasks. For instance, a model trained on a general dataset can be further trained on medical literature to answer medical-related questions.</span><span>[<a href="#a6" rel="noopener">6</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> At EclecticIQ we are developing an LLM-powered Cyber Threat Intelligence tool, EclecticIQ analysts are currently in the fine-tuning process and details will be released soon.&nbsp;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">LLMs have limitations. They don't comprehend text in the same way humans do, they generate responses based on patterns in the data they've been fed. They can also produce incorrect or biased information if the training data had parallel biases or errors. </span><span>[<a href="#a7" rel="noopener">7</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> They do not have the ability to think critically or possess consciousness. </span><span>[<a href="#a8" rel="noopener">8</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<h1 style="text-align: left;"><strong><span data-contrast="auto">2. Aligned Language Models</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></h1>
<h2><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}"><img src="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/guardrails.jpeg?width=500&amp;height=500&amp;name=guardrails.jpeg" width="500" height="500" loading="lazy" alt="guardrails" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 500px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/guardrails.jpeg?width=250&amp;height=250&amp;name=guardrails.jpeg 250w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/guardrails.jpeg?width=500&amp;height=500&amp;name=guardrails.jpeg 500w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/guardrails.jpeg?width=750&amp;height=750&amp;name=guardrails.jpeg 750w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/guardrails.jpeg?width=1000&amp;height=1000&amp;name=guardrails.jpeg 1000w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/guardrails.jpeg?width=1250&amp;height=1250&amp;name=guardrails.jpeg 1250w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/AI-Powered%20Cyber%20Threats/guardrails.jpeg?width=1500&amp;height=1500&amp;name=guardrails.jpeg 1500w" sizes="(max-width: 500px) 100vw, 500px"></span></h2>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}"><em>Image created with DALL·E 3</em></span></p>
<h2><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">Guardrails in AI: Safeguarding Against Detrimental Content in Language Models</span></h2>
<p><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">Aligned language models, a subset of these AI-driven systems, are engineered with a distinct emphasis on operating within predefined ethical and safety guidelines, often referred to as "guardrails." LLMs like OpenAI´s GPT and Google´s BERT are so-called Aligned Language Models. Guardrails encompass a comprehensive framework of principles, rules, and constraints, placed above the data training stack, that further steer the behavior of language models during the process of text generation. By focusing on generating content that is precise, respectful, unbiased, and compliant with human-defined values, aligned models are able to address the ethical concerns arising from AI-generated text.</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The core principles these guardrails encompass are:</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Accuracy:</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> Aligned language models prioritize the accuracy of the information they generate. Through inherent fact-checking mechanisms and an understanding of reliable sources, these models strive to provide information that is trustworthy and factually correct.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Respectfulness:</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> The importance of generating text that is respectful and inclusive of diverse perspectives cannot be understated. Aligned models are programmed to use language that respects cultural sensitivity, avoids derogatory language, and refrains from promoting any form of discrimination.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Unbiased Behavior:</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> Bias is an intrinsic challenge in AI development, and aligned model developers take significant efforts to mitigate this issue. Training on diverse and balanced datasets minimizes the presence of biases in generated text, ensuring that responses are fair and unbiased.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Compliance with Human Values:</span></strong><span data-contrast="auto"> Guardrails for aligned models are rooted in human-defined values that comprise ethical standards. These models are engineered to refrain from generating content that promotes hate speech, misinformation, or harmful ideologies; aligning with the values upheld by human societies. </span><span>[<a href="#a9" rel="noopener">9</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The establishment of guardrails for aligned language models is a nuanced process that involves two key phases: pre-training and fine-tuning. In pre-training, language models learn foundational patterns from datasets, without explicit ethical guidelines. </span><span>[<a href="#a10" rel="noopener">10</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> Fine-tuning trains the model on specific datasets to align its output with desired ethical guidelines by differentiating between appropriate and inappropriate content. </span><span>[<a href="#a11" rel="noopener">11</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">At the moment, the governance of guardrails is left to the individual AI companies. Not having the correct guardrails in place can pose a variety of unexpected risks in the form of objectionable content that encourages violence and even death in some extreme cases. In late 2021, a man was encouraged by a chatbot created on Replica to kill Queen Elizabeth II, </span><span data-contrast="auto">he later </span><span data-contrast="auto">scaled the walls of Windsor Castle where he was apprehended with a loaded crossbow. </span><span>[<a href="#a12" rel="noopener">12</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> In March 2023, a Belgian man took his own life, encouraged by Eliza chatbot after a 6-week conversation. </span><span>[<a href="#a13" rel="noopener">13</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>

<h2 style="text-align: left;">Ethical Boundaries Breached: The Risks of Manipulating Aligned Language Models&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;</h2>
<p><span>Jailbreaks are a type of adversarial attack and refer to scenarios where an aligned language model's behavior deviates from its intended ethical and safety constraints as a consequence of an adversarial attack. Adversarial attacks on AI involve the deliberate creation of specialized input data with the goal of causing AI models to produce incorrect or unintended outputs. This way, attackers can steer the model towards generating outputs that align with their malicious intent, thereby gaining an advantage in achieving their objectives.&nbsp;<br><br>These deviations can enable the model to generate content that is malicious, inappropriate, or otherwise undesirable. Jailbroken models escape the controlled boundaries established during fine-tuning and produce outputs that may pose risks to individuals, organizations, or society at large. Output risks can include generating harmful misinformation, hate speech, phishing emails, malicious code or other forms of malicious content.&nbsp;<br></span></p>

<h2><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Generative AI Exacerbates Social Divisions with Rapid Disinformation Content Production</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></h2>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The content created by generative AI tools has the potential to exacerbate social divisions. Hate speech, incitement to violence, and content that stirs discord are often produced at an alarming rate, contributing to the polarization of online communities.&nbsp;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Generative AI tools are proficient at generating content that can be used to spreads false information and misleading narratives. By automating the production of fake news articles, fabricated images, misleading videos, and provocative social media posts, these tools contribute to the manipulation of public opinion and erode trust in reliable sources. Notable instances include AI-generated news stories that perpetuate false claims and fabricated images that portray events that never occurred. </span><span>[<a href="#a18" rel="noopener">18</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> Other examples include AI-generated social media posts that promote extremist ideologies and contribute to harassment campaigns against targeted individuals or groups. </span><span>[<a href="#a19" rel="noopener">19</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<h2><strong><span data-contrast="auto">The Role of Generative AI in the Disinformation Space During Recent Conflicts</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></h2>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The late events in Israel and Gaza have seen an overwhelming amount of content on social media, but there is currently some disagreement among experts as to how much AI-generated content was used and its actual impact on the target audience.</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-contrast="auto">A recent publication by the Harvard’s Misinformation Review suggests that generative AI might have a limited impact from a quantitative and qualitative standpoint due to, among other reasons, an already content-saturated social media environment and an inherently finite attention span of the target audience. </span><span>[<a href="#a20" rel="noopener">20</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> On the other hand, Hany Farid, professor at the UC Berkeley School of Information, states that the presence of Generative AI content in relation to the events in Israel and Gaza is “far more prevalent than it was at the outset of the Russian invasion of Ukraine.” </span><span>[<a href="#a21" rel="noopener">21</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<h2><strong><span data-contrast="auto">Tackling the Rapid Rise of Malicious AI Content</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></h2>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Detecting and mitigating generative AI tools pose significant challenges. The line between genuine and AI-generated content is becoming increasingly blurred, making it difficult for traditional content moderation techniques to keep up. The speed and scale at which these tools produce content further compound the problem, as manual moderation becomes impractical.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The creation and deployment of malicious content via generative AI tools raise ethical concerns. The responsibility and accountability for the harm caused by such tools are complex issues. Striking a balance between freedom of expression and preventing the spread of harmful content is a delicate challenge. Efforts to curb malicious content should not infringe upon legitimate discourse.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">To address the threat posed by generative AI tools, several strategies are being explored, from digital watermarking to the development of advanced/AI-driven content verification algorithms that can distinguish between genuine and AI-generated content. Additionally, educating users about the potential for AI-driven content manipulation and cultivating critical thinking skills can empower individuals to recognize and question the authenticity of the content they encounter. </span><span>[<a href="#a22" rel="noopener">22</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto">&nbsp;</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<h2><strong><span data-contrast="auto">AI-Driven Acceleration: Simplifying the Cyber Threat Campaign Process</span></strong><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></h2>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Large Language Models simplify the Cyber Threat Campaign process by supporting a variety of attack vectors, from crafting convincing phishing emails to creating malicious payloads.</span><span>[<a href="#a23" rel="noopener">23</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> AI can therefore lower the barrier to entry, allowing new actors to enter the cyber threat landscape. AI also works as a force multiplier for existing actors by making all the processes involved in the campaign easier and quicker to execute. Malicious AI-generated content is designed to bypass traditional security measures, making it harder for humans and machines to detect and stop.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">2023 has seen the widespread development of malicious generative AI tools. Unlike Legitimate LLM tools with guardrails in place that need to be jailbroken to produce malicious content, malicious generative AI tools are designed specifically with the goal of enabling cyberattacks. Examples include: WormGPT, FraudGPT, PoisonGPT, Evil-GPT, XXXGPT, WolfGPT, DarkBART. </span><span>[<a href="#a24" rel="noopener">24</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The two most popular malicious generative AI tools are WormGPT&nbsp; and FraudGPT.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">WormGPT is a blackhat alternative to GPT models, designed specifically for malicious activities. It's based on the GPTJ language model developed in 2021 and offers features like unlimited character support, chat memory retention, and code formatting. WormGPT was trained on various data sources, especially malware-related data. Tests revealed its potential to generate highly persuasive emails for phishing or BEC attacks, showcasing the significant threat posed by such generative AI technologies. </span><span>[<a href="#a25" rel="noopener">25</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">FraudGPT is a new AI tool designed specifically for offensive actions, such as crafting spear phishing emails, creating cracking tools, carding, and more. The tool is currently available for purchase on various Dark Web marketplaces and on Telegram. Like WormGPT, FraudGPT allows threat actors to draft emails that have high likelihood of enticing recipients to click on malicious links. This capability is particularly useful for business email compromise (BEC) phishing campaigns targeting organizations. The tool aids in identifying the most targeted services/sites to further deceive victims. Some of the features of FraudGPT are: writing malicious code, creating hard to detect malware, finding non-VBV bins, creating phishing pages, creating hacking tools, finding groups/sites/markets, writing scam pages/letters, discovering leaks/vulnerabilities, learning to code/hack, finding cardable sites. </span><span>[<a href="#a26" rel="noopener">26</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">As described in the jailbreak section, legitimate LLMs with strong ethical guardrails in place such as ChatGPT can still be manipulated via jailbreaks to create potentially harmful content, in essence turning a legitimate LLM into a malicious generative AI tool.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>

<h2><span data-contrast="auto" xml:lang="EN-US" lang="EN-US"><span>Countering Disinformation in the Age of AI Generative Tools: A Strategic Approach to Detection and Regulation</span></span></h2>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">A holistic approach to counter the threat posed by AI generative tools suggests the use of multiple detection tools and applying Goodhart’s law “when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure”. Goodhart's law posits that once a metric is used as a target, it loses its effectiveness as a measure. For instance, the detection of deep fakes often relies on spotting facial movement discrepancies. Initially, this is an effective method to differentiate authentic from manipulated content. However, as this metric becomes a focal point for detection, malicious creators refine their technology to specifically counteract these movement anomalies, rendering the original detection methods less effective. Consequently, those refining AI detection tools may fall into the trap of over-focusing on this single aspect, neglecting broader strategies necessary for identifying synthetic media, and thereby inadvertently facilitating the task of those aiming to deceive the detection systems. </span><span>[<a href="#a27" rel="noopener">27</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">On a strategic level, addressing the threat of AI generative tools requires collaboration among AI developers, researchers, policymakers, and online platforms. All must work together to create a strong and utilitarian product. Establishing ethical guidelines and frameworks for the responsible development and deployment of AI tools is essential in order to benefit society at large. Ongoing discussions about the regulation of AI and its content aim to prevent malicious use while preserving the positive aspects of the technology. In his Executive Order of late October 2023 “New Standards for AI Safety and Security”, US President Biden mandated guidelines targeting, among other things, AI-generated content&nbsp;to “Protect Americans from AI-enabled fraud and deception by establishing standards and best practices for detecting AI-generated content and authenticating official content.” </span><span>[<a href="#a28" rel="noopener">28</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> The European Parliament is proposing similar legislation with the EU AI Act drafted in 2021 and expected to be approved soon. </span><span>[<a href="#a28" rel="noopener">29</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> This sets a strong precedent for other nations to follow.</span></p>
<h2><span data-contrast="auto"></span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}"><span data-contrast="auto" xml:lang="EN-US" lang="EN-US">Malicious Generative AI: A Force Multiplier in Cyberattacks</span>&nbsp;</span></h2>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">EclecticIQ analysts expect Malicious Generative AI tools designed to facilitate cyberattacks to become more widespread and popular. This further lowers the barrier to entry for new cybercriminals to craft better cyberattacks and helps scale quantitative and qualitative capabilities for existing cybercriminals and APT groups. </span><span>[<a href="#a30" rel="noopener">30</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto"> Analysts expect legitimate Generative AI tools to be jailbroken via adversarial attacks with the purpose of turning them into Malicious Generative AI tools.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">The use of Malicious Generative AI tools works as a force multiplier, both from a quantitative and from a qualitative standpoint. It will pose a serious challenge to the defenders community, which will need to quickly adapt responses, likely using AI-powered security tools. Initiatives such as the DARPA AI Cyber Challenge, announced in August 2023 at Black Hat USA </span><span>[<a href="#a31" rel="noopener">31</a>]</span><span data-contrast="auto">, will help bring together the brightest minds in Cybersecurity and Artificial Intelligence with the aim of building AI-driven cyber defense tools. </span><span>[<a href="#a32" rel="noopener">32</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>

<p><span data-contrast="auto">EclecticIQ analysts expect new attack vectors pivoting off of AI and exploiting vulnerabilities as AI systems are being integrated into the technology stack of organizations. </span><span>[<a href="#a33" rel="noopener">33</a>]</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:259}">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">EclecticIQ analysts have seen adversarial attacks against Large Language Models, but adversarial attacks can be launched against a variety of AI systems, not just LLMs like GPT.
