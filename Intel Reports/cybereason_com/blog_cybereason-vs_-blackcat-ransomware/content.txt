      <h1><span id="hs_cos_wrapper_name" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="text">Cybereason vs. BlackCat Ransomware</span></h1>
      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">
      <div id="hubspot-author_data" class="hubspot-editable cr-mln__post-meta" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author">
        
          <span class="descriptor">Written By</span>
          <p><span class="author">Cybereason Nocturnus</span></p>
         
      </div>
    </div>
     
    </div>
    
    <!-- Sticky Author and Social Box -->
    
    <!-- END Sticky Author and Social Box -->
      <div class="container-is-blog columns is-multiline page-center cr-mln__blog-post--body">
        
        <div class="column is-7-fullhd is-7-desktop is-10-tablet is-10-mobile is-offset-1-fullhd is-offset-1-desktop is-offset-1-tablet is-offset-1-mobile">
          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p><span style="color: #070707;">Since its first emergence in November 2021, the Cybereason Nocturnus team has been tracking the BlackCat Ransomware (aka ALPHV), which <a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/alphv-blackcat-this-years-most-sophisticated-ransomware/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">has been </a></span><a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/alphv-blackcat-this-years-most-sophisticated-ransomware/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>called</span></a><span style="color: #070707;"><a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/alphv-blackcat-this-years-most-sophisticated-ransomware/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"> “2021’s most sophisticated ransomware”</a>.&nbsp;</span></p>
<!--more-->
<p><span style="color: #070707;">BlackCat ransomware gained notoriety quickly leaving a trail of destruction behind it, among its recent victims are German </span><a href="https://www.zdnet.com/article/blackcat-ransomware-implicated-in-attack-on-german-oil-companies/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>oil companies</span></a><span style="color: #070707;">, an </span><a href="https://www.secureblink.com/cyber-security-news/moncler-group-becomes-the-first-victim-of-alphv-(blackcat)-raas-following-the-data-leak" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Italian luxury fashion brand</span></a><span style="color: #070707;"> and a </span><a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/blackcat-alphv-claims-swissport-ransomware-attack-leaks-data/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Swiss Aviation company</span></a><span style="color: #070707;">.&nbsp;</span></p>
<div class="hs-embed-wrapper" data-service="fast.wistia" data-script-embed="true" style="position: relative; overflow: hidden; width: 100%; height: auto; padding: 0px; max-width: 791px; min-width: 256px; display: block; margin: auto;"><div class="hs-embed-content-wrapper"><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/embed/medias/rvpkt0od5q.jsonp" async>&nbsp;</script><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/assets/external/E-v1.js" async></script><div class="wistia_responsive_padding" style="padding:56.25% 0 0 0;position:relative;"><div class="wistia_responsive_wrapper" style="height:100%;left:0;position:absolute;top:0;width:100%;"><span class="wistia_embed wistia_async_rvpkt0od5q popover=true popoverAnimateThumbnail=true videoFoam=true" style="display:inline-block;height:100%;position:relative;width:100%">&nbsp;</span></div></div><script src="https://fast.wistia.net/assets/external/E-v1.js" async></script></div></div>
<p style="font-size: 16px; text-align: center;"><em><span style="color: #070707;">The Cybereason XDR Platform Detects and Blocks BlackCat Ransomware</span></em></p>
<p><span style="color: #070707;">Since its recent emergence, BlackCat has attacked various industries, including telecommunication, commercial services, insurance, retail, machinery, pharmaceuticals, transportation, and construction industries. Among the affected regions are Germany, France, Spain, the Philippines, and the Netherlands, with the most victims being located in the US.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #070707;">The ransomware was given the name “BlackCat” due to the favicon of a black cat being used on every victim's Tor payment site. The operators of BlackCat have been using the names “alphv” and “ransom” in Cybercrime forums (</span><span style="color: #070707;">ramp_v2, exploit.in</span><span style="color: #070707;">) in order to recruit affiliates.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #070707;">The operators of the ransomware appear to be from Russian speaking regions. Like many others, BlackCat uses a RaaS model (Ransomware-as-a-service). Affiliates of BlackCat are offered between 80-90% of the ransom payment, and once approved, are given access to a control panel that manages access:</span></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #070707;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png?width=1144&amp;name=image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png" alt="image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM" width="1144" loading="lazy" style="width: 1144px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png?width=572&amp;name=image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png 572w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png?width=1144&amp;name=image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png 1144w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png?width=1716&amp;name=image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png 1716w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png?width=2288&amp;name=image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png 2288w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png?width=2860&amp;name=image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png 2860w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png?width=3432&amp;name=image6-Feb-28-2022-06-37-23-10-PM.png 3432w" sizes="(max-width: 1144px) 100vw, 1144px"></span><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #859da6;">Leaked document from BlackCat Leaks website</span></em></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #070707;">One of the unique elements of the BlackCat ransomware is that it is written in </span><a href="https://www.rust-lang.org/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Rust</span></a><span style="color: #070707;">, which is not a common coding language for malware and ransomware. </span><em><span style="color: #070707;">“Rust is a multi-paradigm, general-purpose programming language designed for performance and safety.</span></em><span style="color: #070707;">”&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #070707;">Because of Rust’s emphasis on performance, the process of encryption is very fast, and in addition, Rust is cross-platform, which makes it easier to create variants for both Windows and Linux.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #070707;">The operators of BlackCat </span><a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/blackcat-alphv-ransomware-linked-to-blackmatter-darkside-gangs/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>confirmed</span></a><span style="color: #070707;"> that they are affiliates of <a href="/blog/cybereason-vs-darkside-ransomware" rel="noopener" target="_blank">DarkSide/BlackMatter</a> ransomware gang. They </span><a href="https://therecord.media/an-alphv-blackcat-representative-discusses-the-groups-plans-for-a-ransomware-meta-universe/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>claim</span></a><span style="color: #070707;"> to be apolitical in regards to geopolitical relations and to refrain from attacking medical institutions and hospitals.&nbsp;</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #070707;">The group has adopted the popular double extortion paradigm, which means that in addition to encrypting files, they also steal them and later threaten to publish stolen data unless the ransom is paid. In some cases even, triple extortion is used - threatening to perform DDOS attacks.&nbsp;</span></p>
<h2 style="font-size: 30px;">Key Details</h2>

<li aria-level="1"><strong>Sophisticated Ransomware:</strong> BlackCat has been called “2021’s most sophisticated ransomware</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>High Severity</strong>: The <a href="/blog/authors/cybereason-nocturnus" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Cybereason Nocturnus Team</span></a> assesses the threat level as HIGH given the destructive potential of the attacks.</li>

<h2 style="font-size: 30px;">Technical Analysis</h2>
<h3 style="font-size: 24px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #434343;">Breaking down BlackCat Ransomware</span></h3>
<p>The BlackCat ransomware has both Windows and Linux variants. The ransomware includes multiple execution flags which grant its operators control over operations like whether to stop executions of virtual machines or if the ransomware should change the desktop wallpaper or not:</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png?width=766&amp;name=image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png" alt="image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM" width="766" loading="lazy" style="width: 766px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png?width=383&amp;name=image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png 383w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png?width=766&amp;name=image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png 766w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png?width=1149&amp;name=image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png 1149w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png?width=1532&amp;name=image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png 1532w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png?width=1915&amp;name=image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png 1915w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png?width=2298&amp;name=image9-Feb-28-2022-06-39-31-88-PM.png 2298w" sizes="(max-width: 766px) 100vw, 766px"><span style="font-size: 20px;"></span><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #859da6;">BlackCat help menu</span></em></span></p>
<p>In order to execute properly, BlackCat must be executed with the “--access-token” flag, although the value of the string that is passed on to it can be any string.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Upon execution, BlackCat may attempt to perform Privilege escalation in the following manners:</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1218/003/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>UAC bypass by abusing the Connection Manager Admin API Helper for Setup COM interface (cmstplua.dll)</span></a></li>
<li aria-level="1">Abusing <a href="https://cve.mitre.org/cgi-bin/cvename.cgi?name=CVE-2016-0099" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>CVE-2016-0099</span></a> (Secondary Logon Service exploit)</li>
<li aria-level="1">Adjusting access token token privileges&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p>Next, BlackCat checks the UUID (universally unique identifier) of the machine by running a WMI command, which is used later for the recovery URL in the ransom note:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><em>wmic csproduct get UUID</em></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400;">BlackCat enables local and remote </span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symbolic_link" style="background-color: transparent;" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><em>symbolic links</em></a><span style="background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400;"> on the infected machine. A symbolic link is a type of file that contains a reference to another file. This is probably done to make sure that the ransomware is able to follow shortcuts on the machine in order to find the original file to encrypt:</span></p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><em>fsutil behavior set SymlinkEvaluation R2L:1</em></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><em>fsutil behavior set SymlinkEvaluation R2R:1</em></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400;">BlackCat also attempts to stop Internet services on the infected machine using the iisreset.exe:</span></p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><em>iisreset.exe /stop</em></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="background-color: transparent; font-weight: 400;">The ransomware changes the number of outstanding requests that can be maintained.&nbsp;</span>An outstanding request is a request that is still waiting for a response. These are used when performing SMB requests, the change is probably done to raise the number of possible PsExec requests the machine could make so the ransomware may spread:</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1">&nbsp;<em>reg add HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\LanmanServer\Parameters /v MaxMpxCt /d 65535 /t REG_DWORD /f</em></li>
</ul>
<p>Then, it deletes the shadow copies from the infected machine using both “vssadmin” and “wmic”:</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><em>vssadmin.exe delete shadows /all /quiet</em></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1">wmic shaodwcopy delete</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #859da6;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png?width=1370&amp;name=image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png" alt="image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM" width="1370" loading="lazy" style="width: 1370px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png?width=685&amp;name=image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png 685w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png?width=1370&amp;name=image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png 1370w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png?width=2055&amp;name=image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png 2055w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png?width=2740&amp;name=image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png 2740w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png?width=3425&amp;name=image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png 3425w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png?width=4110&amp;name=image5-Feb-28-2022-06-40-59-18-PM.png 4110w" sizes="(max-width: 1370px) 100vw, 1370px"></span></em></span><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #859da6;">BlackCat Execution as seen in the Cybereason XDR Platform</span></em></span></p>

</ul>
<p>Finally all snapshots of the virtual machines are deleted:</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><em>for i in `vim-cmd vmsvc/getallvms| awk '{print$1}'`;do vim-cmd vmsvc/snapshot.removeall $i &amp; done</em></li>
</ul>
<h2 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #434343;">BlackCat and LockBit Connection</span></h2>
<p>The Nocturnus team observed interesting overlaps between tools and infrastructure used by BlackCat ransomware and LockBit ransomware. The Nocturnus team analyzed a .NET written launcher named “setup.exe” that is used to download and execute BlackCat ransomware.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The launcher contains the following PDB path:</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1">&nbsp;“<em>D:\my\Documents\Visual Studio 2019\setup\obj\Release\setup.pdb</em>”.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<p>When searching for files that share the PDB, we encountered several additional malware with the same name that have remarkable similarities to the BlackCat launcher. When examining the code and Infrastructure of these malware, we see overlaps between BlackCat infrastructure and LockBit infrastructure.&nbsp;</p>
<h4 style="font-size: 24px; line-height: 1.75; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #666666;">BlackCat Launcher</span></h4>
<p>The launcher downloads the BlackCat executable from the C2 and executes it using the “--access-token” argument, which is required in order to run BlackCat:</p>

<h4 style="font-size: 24px; line-height: 1.75; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #666666;">LockBit Profiler Tool</span></h4>
<p>The Nocturnus team discovered striking similarities with the BlackCat launcher and a profiler associated with LockBit ransomware. The profiler variants which are linked to LockBit use almost the same code as the BlackCat launcher, except for slight variations.&nbsp;</p>
