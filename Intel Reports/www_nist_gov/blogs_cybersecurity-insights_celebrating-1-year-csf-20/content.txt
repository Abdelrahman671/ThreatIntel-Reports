Stakeholders are a very important force behind NIST’s cybersecurity and privacy programs. We want to recognize and express gratitude to all who provide direct feedback to NIST, implement the CSF, or promote awareness of the CSF and its resources in service of helping organizations of all sizes and sectors improve their cybersecurity posture.&nbsp;</span><span>&nbsp;</span><br><br><span lang="EN-US"><strong>What’s New in 2025?&nbsp;</strong></span><span>&nbsp;</span></p></div><div class="OutlineElement Ltr SCXW221601094 BCX0"><p class="Paragraph SCXW221601094 BCX0"><span lang="EN-US">The CSF team is excited to announce additional resources designed to provide different audiences with tailored pathways to implement a stronger cybersecurity posture using CSF 2.0, making the Framework easier to put into action. </span><br><br><span lang="EN-US"><strong>Ensuring cybersecurity risk management is supporting the organization’s mission and objectives</strong></span><span>&nbsp;</span></p></div><div class="OutlineElement Ltr SCXW221601094 BCX0"><p class="Paragraph SCXW221601094 BCX0"><span lang="EN-US">One of the major updates to CSF 2.0 is the focus on cybersecurity governance to highlight the importance of ensuring cybersecurity capabilities support the broader mission through Enterprise Risk Management (ERM). implementation of risk management programs to meet the requirements of the Federal Information Security Modernization Act (FISMA), NIST released a draft mapping of NIST SP 800-37, <em>Risk Management Registration will open in the coming months.&nbsp;</span></em><span>&nbsp;</span></p></li></ul></div><div class="ListContainerWrapper SCXW221601094 BCX0"><ul type="disc"><li><p class="Paragraph SCXW221601094 BCX0"><span lang="EN-US">August 20, 2025 (2:00 - 3:00 PM EST): Deep Dive into the CSF 2.0 Ransomware Profile. </span><em><span lang="EN-US">Save the date! He is also program manager for the </span></span><a href="http://checklists.nist.gov"><span lang="EN"><span>National Checklist Program</span></span></a><span><span> and the </span></span><a href="https://csrc.nist.gov/Projects/Cybersecurity-Framework/Informative-Reference-Catalog"><span lang="EN"><span>National Online Informative Reference (OLIR)</span></span></a><span lang="EN"><span> programs at NIST.&nbsp; </span></span><span><span>He is a co-originator of the NIST </span></span><a href="http://nvd.nist.gov/scap.cfm"><span lang="EN"><span>Security Content Automation Protocol (SCAP)</span></span></a><span><span>.</span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span><span><span><span><span>Stephen was named to the “Federal 100” by the trade publication Federal Computer Week (FCW) and received the Department of Commence Gold Medal Award for his work in automating security protocols for applications. He also received the Federal CIO Council Leadership award for related work.</span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p><span lang="EN"><span><span>Prior to joining NIST, Steve worked in the private sector as a consultant to the Department of Defense and large commercial outsourcings with Wall Street banking firms and insurance companies. Specifically, he comes from an operational background, having owned two companies that provided service offering for vulnerability assessments, designing security architectures, code development, risk management, certifications and accreditations, and ST&amp;Es. His research experience and practitioner experience includes managing and remediating risks specific to computer viruses/malware, intrusion detection systems (IDSs), vulnerability/misconfiguration identification, categorization, and remediation.</span></span></span></p></div>
