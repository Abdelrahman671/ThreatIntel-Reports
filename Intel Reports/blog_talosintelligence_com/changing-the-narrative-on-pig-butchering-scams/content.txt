    <h1 class="text-center">Changing the narrative on pig butchering scams</h1>

    <div class="text-center m-1">
        <div class="post-author">
            <span>By </span>
                <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/author/hazel-burton/">Hazel Burton</a>
        </div>

        <br/>
        <time class="post-datetime" datetime="February 13, 2025 14:05">
            Thursday, February 13, 2025 14:05
        </time>

        <div class="m-3">

                            Threat Source newsletter
                        </a>
                            </div>
    </div>

    <section class="post-content-wrapper mt-5">
        <div class="post-content">
            <p>Welcome to this week’s edition of the Threat Source Newsletter.</p><p>Love is in the air this week. Wait, is that love? Or is it some tech bro with a housing development company (that would totally love to meet in person but can’t this week) emailing you about an investment opportunity in his cryptocurrency scheme?</p><p>You may be seeing a lot of ‘Beware of romance/ pig butchering scams’ articles around Valentines Day. This isn’t really one of those. Although, if said tech bro initiates a course of love bombing mixed in with wire transfer requests, report that dude quicker than the roadrunner declares “meep meep”.&nbsp;</p><p>I recently came across an article on&nbsp;<a href="https://thehackernews.com/2024/12/interpol-pushes-for-romance-baiting-to.html"><u>The Hacker News</u></a>&nbsp;that talked about how Interpol is pushing for a “linguistic shift” when it comes to pig butchering scams. They’re advocating for the term to be replaced by ‘romance baiting’.</p><p>In a statement, Interpol explained their reasoning:</p><p><em>"The term 'pig butchering' dehumanizes and shames victims of such frauds, deterring people from coming forward to seek help and provide information to the authorities,"</em></p><p>Pig butchering originates from a Chinese phrase. Its meaning is derived from “fattening a pig before the slaughter”. When we put that in the context of online scams, the emphasis is on the victim, with some not so nice connotations (and a certain sense of inevitability attached to it).&nbsp;</p><p>By flipping the script and renaming pig butchering as romance baiting, Interpol suggests this could have a positive effect on the psychological nature of being targeted:</p><p><em>"Words matter. We've seen this in the areas of violent sexual offences, domestic abuse, and online child exploitation. We need to recognize that our words also matter to the victims of fraud," INTERPOL Acting Executive Director of Police Services Cyril Gout said.</em></p><p><em>"It's time to change our language to prioritize respect and empathy for the victims, and to hold fraudsters accountable for their crimes."</em></p><p>I wholeheartedly agree. Victim blaming only causes more harm. The more we can do to encourage people to report perpetrators, without feeling a sense of shame, the better.&nbsp;</p><p>What do you think? Will you be changing the narrative the next time you talk about romance scams? Are there any other terms in our industry that potentially put more focus on the victim than the adversary?</p><div class="kg-card kg-header-card kg-v2 kg-width-regular " style="background-color: #000000;" data-background-color="#000000">

        </div><h3 id="the-one-big-thing">The one big thing</h3><p>In the&nbsp;<a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/small-praise-for-modern-compilers-a-case-of-ubuntu-printing-vulnerability-that-wasnt/"><u>latest Talos Vulnerability Deep Dive</u></a>, the team picked out something that had caught their attention during an earlier investigation of the macOS printing subsystem:&nbsp;IPP over USB specification, which defines how printers that are available over USB can only still support network printing via Internet Printing Protocol (IPP). During this new investigation, Talos decided to look at how other operating systems handle the same functionality.&nbsp;</p><p>The result? Some pretty good news actually. Although the potential vulnerability Talos discusses in&nbsp;<a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/small-praise-for-modern-compilers-a-case-of-ubuntu-printing-vulnerability-that-wasnt/"><u>this article</u></a> is very real, mitigating circumstances make it less severe. The vulnerability is discovered and made unexploitable by modern compiler features, and we are highlighting this as a rare win.</p><h3 id="why-do-i-care">Why do I care?</h3><p>We often hear of all the failings of software and vulnerabilities and mitigation bypasses, and we felt we should take this opportunity to highlight the opposite. In this case, modern compiler features, static analysis via -Wstringop-overflow and strong mitigation via FORTIFY_SOURCE, saved the day.</p><h3 id="so-now-what">So now what?</h3><p>The modern compiler features detailed above should always be enabled by default.
