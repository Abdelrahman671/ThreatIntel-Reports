			<span class="rt-reading-time" style="display: block;"><span class="rt-label">Estimated reading time: </span><span class="rt-time">8</span><span class="rt-label"> minutes</span></span><p><span data-contrast="auto">Seqrite Labs APT-Team has recently discovered multiple campaigns involving fake PayPal lures. These are targeting individuals around the globe with a new variant of ransomware known as Cronus. In this case, the malware is developed in PowerShell and is executed directly in memory without writing any malicious content to disk.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">This blog will explore the sophistication and technical details of the campaign we encountered during our telemetry analysis. We will examine the various stages of this campaign, starting with the initial document payload. We will then proceed to extract and analyze the malicious VBA script and finally review the PowerShell payload that executes the ransomware using reflective DLL loading.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p aria-level="2"><b><span data-contrast="none">Initial Findings</span></b><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134245418&quot;:true,&quot;134245529&quot;:true,&quot;335559738&quot;:40,&quot;335559739&quot;:0}"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">On 14th July 2024, our team discovered a malicious document on </span><a href="https://www.virustotal.com/gui/file/9ebf60ad31f0eb1fa303e0b00f9cc605c5013ea30771e6b14409cb70af7416cb/details" data-wpel-link="external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow"><span data-contrast="none">VirusTotal</span></a><span data-contrast="auto">, depending on the hunting rules we use for files containing malicious embedded VBA Macros.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Upon initial analysis, we found that the malicious lure aimed to mimic a mere receipt document known as </span><span data-contrast="auto">paypal_charges.doc. </span><span data-contrast="auto"> Then, upon extracting the malicious macros from the malicious streams, we found that the malicious VBA was downloading the second stager PowerShell-based loader known as</span> <span data-contrast="auto">8eef4df388f2217caec3dc26.ps1</span><span data-contrast="auto">, which loaded the ransomware-based DLL using the reflective loading technique. </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Ransomware groups, such as NETWALKER and many others, have previously used this technique to deploy ransomware.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p aria-level="3"><b><span data-contrast="none">Infection Chain</span></b></p>
<p aria-level="3"><img class="size-full wp-image-6323 aligncenter" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-1.png" alt="" width="579" height="243" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-1.png 579w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-1-300x126.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 579px) 100vw, 579px" /></p>
<p aria-level="2"><b><span data-contrast="none">Technical Analysis </span></b><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134245418&quot;:true,&quot;134245529&quot;:true,&quot;335559738&quot;:40,&quot;335559739&quot;:0}"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">We will break down the analysis into three different parts.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p aria-level="3"><b><span data-contrast="auto">Initial Infection &#8211; Malicious Document</span></b><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">We initially observed that the initial infection was caused by a malicious Word document, which primarily spread through a Phishing vector. We, therefore, decided to deep dive into the lure&#8217;s contents before diving into the malicious streams of macros.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6324" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-2.png" alt="" width="1756" height="875" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-2.png 1756w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-2-300x149.png 300w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-2-768x383.png 768w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/Image-2-1024x510.png 1024w" sizes="(max-width: 1756px) 100vw, 1756px" /></p>
<p><span class="TextRun SCXW2979228 BCX0" lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US" data-contrast="auto"><span class="NormalTextRun SCXW2979228 BCX0" data-ccp-parastyle="Normal (Web)">The document file depicted the maturity of the threat actor as the lure is just a blank Word document. Therefore, we decided to deep-dive into the evil streams of this malicious document and quickly extract it for further analysis.</span></span><span class="EOP SCXW2979228 BCX0" data-ccp-props="{&quot;134233117&quot;:true,&quot;134233118&quot;:true}"> </span></p>
