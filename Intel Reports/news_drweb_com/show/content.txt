<p><b>January 30, 2023</b></p>

<section class="margTM margBM" id="main">
  <p><newslead>An analysis of Dr.Web anti-virus detection statistics for December 2023 revealed a 40.87% increase in the total number of threats detected, compared to November. The number of unique threats also increased by 24.55%. Once again, most commonly detected were adware trojans and unwanted adware programs as well as malicious programs that are distributed with other threats to make them more difficult to detect. In email traffic, phishing documents of various formats were most frequently found.</newslead></p>
  <p>The number of user requests to decrypt files affected by encoder trojans decreased by 27.95%, compared to November. Most often, victims of these encrypting trojans encountered <a href="https://vms.drweb.com/search/?q=Trojan.Encoder.26996&lng=en"><b>Trojan.Encoder.26996</b></a>, <a href="https://vms.drweb.com/search/?q=Trojan.Encoder.3953&lng=en"><b>Trojan.Encoder.3953</b></a>, and <b>Trojan.Encoder.37369</b>, which accounted for 21.76%, 20.73%, and 4.14% of all recorded incidents, respectively.</p>
  <p>In December, Doctor Web’s specialists discovered yet other malicious programs on Google Play. Also found were new websites that cybercriminals were using to distribute fake crypto-wallet software for the Android and iOS operating systems.</p>

  <div class="paddXM paddYM bg_ocean_1 white">

      <li>A decrease in the number of user requests to decrypt files affected by encoder trojans</li>
      <li>The discovery of new malicious apps on Google Play</li>

<section class="margTM margBM" id="formobile">
  <h2 class="alignCenter">Malicious and unwanted programs for mobile devices</h2>
  <p>According to detection statistics collected by Dr.Web for Android, in December, users were most often attacked by <a href="https://vms.drweb.com/search/?q=Android.HiddenAds&lng=en"><b>Android.HiddenAds</b></a> adware trojans. At the same time, the activity of these malicious apps decreased, compared to the previous month. The number of banking trojan and spyware trojan attacks also decreased.</p>
  <p>Over the course of December, Doctor Web’s virus analysts discovered other fake apps from the <a href="https://vms.drweb.com/search/?q=Android.FakeApp&lng=en"><b>Android.FakeApp</b></a> family on Google Play. In addition, our specialists found new websites which cybercriminals use to distribute fake crypto-wallet software for Android and iOS-based devices.</p>

  <p>The following December events involving mobile malware are the most noteworthy:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>A decrease in the activity of <a href="https://vms.drweb.com/search/?q=Android.HiddenAds&lng=en"><b>Android.HiddenAds</b></a> adware trojans,</li>
    <li>A decrease in banking trojan and spyware trojan activity,</li>
    <li>The discovery of new malicious programs on Google Play,</li>
    <li>The discovery of new websites, through which fake crypto-wallet software is distributed.</li>
  </ul>

  <p>To find out more about the security-threat landscape for mobile devices in December, read our <a href="https://news.drweb.com/show/review/?lng=en&i=14796" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">special overview</a>.</p>
