	<h1 class="article-details__title">Examining A Sodinokibi Attack </h1>
	<p class="article-details__description">Sodinokibi was behind several notable attacks last year. In this entry, we describe its attack process using some of the examples we encountered.</p>
	<p class="article-details__author-by">By: Trend Micro Research
		
			<time class="article-details__date">January 26, 2021</time>
		
		
		<span>Read time:&nbsp;</span><span class="eta"></span> (<span class="words"></span> words)

		<div>
			<p>Sodinokibi was first detected in April 2019 and linked to the retired GandCrab. From that point on, Sodinokibi launched <a href="https://www.trendmicro.com/vinfo/tmr/?/us/security/news/cybercrime-and-digital-threats/-sodinokibi-ransomware-group-adds-malvertising-as-delivery-technique">several high-profile attacks</a> that continued throughout 2020, thus making a name for itself as one of the <a href="https://www.trendmicro.com/vinfo/tmr/?/us/security/definition/ransomware">ransomware</a> families that should be watched out for. Here we describe Sodinokibi’s typical attack process.</p>
<p><span class="body-subhead-title">Technical analysis </span></p>
<p>The threat actors behind Sodinokibi typically hire a variety of affiliates for their initial access. Their attacks often begin with familiar techniques like malspam emails with spear-phishing links or attachments, RDP access that uses valid accounts, compromised websites, and exploits. They also use techniques that indicate their targeted approach.</p>
<p><b><i>Initial access </i></b></p>
<p>We observed the use of several of these initial access techniques. For example, as with campaigns, we saw the use of the <a href="https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2019-2725" target="_blank">CVE-2019-2725</a> vulnerability and observed an instance where Sodinokibi was loaded in the memory of PowerShell through reflective-load instead of binary execution. We also saw malspam that led to the use of a macro to download and execute the malware.  </p>
<p><a href="https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2018-13379" target="_blank">CVE-2018-13379</a> and <a href="https://nvd.nist.gov/vuln/detail/CVE-2019-11510" target="_blank">CVE-2019-11510</a> are also used by the malware, as well as compromised valid accounts. This allows the threat actors to drop and execute other components like the anti-antivirus, exfiltration tools, and finally Sodinokibi itself. </p>
<p><b><i>Lateral movement and evasion tactics </i></b></p>
<p>Sodinokibi, like many ransomware families known today, have a targeted approach with regard to their campaigns. In line with this, we observed the use of RDP and PsExec for lateral movement — a sign of targeted attacks — to drop and execute other components and the ransomware itself.  </p>
<p>We also observed that PC Hunter and Process Hacker are used to terminate services or processes, especially those services and processes that are related to antivirus software. </p>
<p>Once the system is infected, Sodinokibi sends a report and system information to its command-and-control (C&amp;C) server. It generates a pseudorandom URL based on a fixed format and generation to add to a list of domains in its configuration.  </p>
<p><span class="body-subhead-title">Security recommendations </span></p>
<p>Sodinokibi has been known to target high-profile entities and uses notable evasive tactics. Organizations should, therefore, be wary of its techniques. For now, here are some best practices to prevent similar ransomware attacks:</p>
<ul>
<li><span class="rte-circle-bullet"><span class="rte-red-bullet" style="font-weight: normal;">Avoid opening unverified emails or clicking on their embedded links, as these can start the ransomware installation process. </span></span></li>
<li><span class="rte-circle-bullet"><span class="rte-red-bullet" style="font-weight: normal;">Back up your important files using the 3-2-1 rule: Create three backup copies on two different file formats, with one of the backups in a separate location. </span></span></li>
<li><span class="rte-circle-bullet"><span class="rte-red-bullet" style="font-weight: normal;">Regularly update software, programs, and applications, to ensure that your apps are current, with the latest protections from new vulnerabilities.</span></span></li>
</ul>
<p>If you believe that your organization has been affected by this campaign, visit <a href="https://success.trendmicro.com/solution/000284483" target="_blank">this page</a> for the available Trend Micro solutions that can help detect and mitigate any risks from this campaign. </p>
