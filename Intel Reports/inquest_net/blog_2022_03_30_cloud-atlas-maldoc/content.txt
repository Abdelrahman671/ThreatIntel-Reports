					<h1 class="entry-title">Cloud Atlas Maldoc</h1>					<div class="single-post-meta">
													<div class="single-post-authors">
														<span>By:
							<a href="#author">Dmitry Melikov</a>							</span>
							</div>
													<div class="single-post-date">
							March 30, 2022						</div>
					</div>
											<div class="single-post-thumbnail"><img width="1000" height="518" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201000%20518'%3E%3C/svg%3E" class="attachment-post-thumbnail size-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="Cloud Atlas logo" decoding="async" data-lazy-src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/cloug_atlas-e1702405757442.png" /><noscript><img width="1000" height="518" src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/cloug_atlas-e1702405757442.png" class="attachment-post-thumbnail size-post-thumbnail wp-post-image" alt="Cloud Atlas logo" decoding="async" /></noscript></div>
									</div>
				
<p>For several weeks, eyes around the world have been set on the war in Ukraine and events that have transpired as a result.</p>



<p>The economic sanctions affecting Russian banks and enterprises are some of many consequences that persist as main talking points across international media outlets. This presented yet another opportunity for attackers to leverage this subject for targeted attacks and/or phishing campaigns.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>We uncovered a very interesting document that was observed impersonating the United States Securities and Exchange Commission. It is our assumption with a high degree of probability that an attacker called Cloud Atlas is responsible for this malicious campaign.</p>



<figure class="wp-block-table text-monospace"><table><tbody><tr><td>File Type&nbsp;</td><td>Microsoft Excel 2007&nbsp;</td></tr><tr><td>Sha-256</td><td><a href="https://labs.inquest.net/dfi/sha256/8df0d05c36a64b13869343917076ba8f65604ea1ecde50292f361ad4e34b4b09">8df0d05c36a64b13869343917076ba8f65604ea1ecde50292f361ad4e34b4b09&nbsp;</a></td></tr></tbody></table></figure>



<p>The document appears to be a request to obtain data from Russian clients.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img width="706" height="507" decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20706%20507'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" class="wp-image-1592" data-lazy-src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/1_3.png"/><noscript><img width="706" height="507" decoding="async" src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/1_3.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1592"/></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Image 1. Visual lure.</figcaption></figure></div>


<p></p>



<p>On the date observed, the document had very low detection rates. Contained in the graph below, you can see how document detection changed day by day.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img width="1194" height="371" decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201194%20371'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" class="wp-image-1593" data-lazy-src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/2_1.png"/><noscript><img width="1194" height="371" decoding="async" src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/2_1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1593"/></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Image 2. VT Detection Graph</figcaption></figure></div>


<p>Let&#8217;s dive into the analysis of this document to examine the payload as well as what techniques are in use.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img width="1404" height="712" decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%201404%20712'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" class="wp-image-1594" data-lazy-src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/image3_1.png"/><noscript><img width="1404" height="712" decoding="async" src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/image3_1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1594"/></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Image 3. Disassembled P-code</figcaption></figure></div>


<p>The purpose of the first stage of the attack, as is often the case, is to retrieve and execute the payload of the next stage. In the image above we find a URL address which points to a ZIP file containing compressed suspicious data to be executed on the system.</p>



<p>2 Stage &gt; hxxps://cvg[.]org/wp-content/uploads/2020/document.zip&nbsp;</p>



<figure class="wp-block-table"><table><tbody><tr><td>FileType</td><td>MSI installer package file</td></tr><tr><td>Sha 256</td><td>ff06cffedc00b97f82005c9768951d0e8c18c63ba36e584aef3c7c9e845e62e0</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>



<p>This sample also shows a low rate of detection after uploading to VirusTotal.&nbsp;</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img width="788" height="281" decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20788%20281'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" class="wp-image-1596" data-lazy-src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/Capture.png"/><noscript><img width="788" height="281" decoding="async" src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/Capture.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1596"/></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Image 4. VirusTotal Graph&nbsp;</figcaption></figure></div>


<p>ff06cffedc00b97f82005c9768951d0e8c18c63ba36e584aef3c7c9e845e62e0</p>



<p>After downloading this installation package, the program starts installing the executable components. The installation file contains a few legitimate files along with one malicious library (main) that is installed in the following directory.&nbsp;</p>



<p>%LOCALAPPDATA%\EdgeTools\edgecef.dll</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><img width="445" height="167" decoding="async" src="data:image/svg+xml,%3Csvg%20xmlns='http://www.w3.org/2000/svg'%20viewBox='0%200%20445%20167'%3E%3C/svg%3E" alt="" class="wp-image-1597" data-lazy-src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/5_1.png"/><noscript><img width="445" height="167" decoding="async" src="https://inquest.net/wp-content/uploads/5_1.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1597"/></noscript><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Image 5. Contents of the installation package.</figcaption></figure></div>


<p>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; </p>



<p>We believe that this is one of the malicious tools of the Cloud Atlas APT group. It beacons out to a remote server, waiting for further commands. Initially, this sample collects information about the system it is running on, which is then exfiltrated to the remote server.</p>



<p>This attacker has been active for many years, identified in 2014, the group is known for using documents to infect government organizations such as embassies or organizations affiliated with the aerospace industry.</p>



<p>Indicators:</p>



<figure class="wp-block-table text-monospace"><table><tbody><tr><td>Type&nbsp;</td><td>Indicator</td></tr><tr><td>Document</td><td>8df0d05c36a64b13869343917076ba8f65604ea1ecde50292f361ad4e34b4b09</td></tr><tr><td>MSI</td><td>ff06cffedc00b97f82005c9768951d0e8c18c63ba36e584aef3c7c9e845e62e0</td></tr><tr><td>x86 Dll</td><td>4eb85a5532b98cbc4a6db1697cf46b9e2b7e28e89d6bbfc137b36c0736cd80e2</td></tr><tr><td>C2</td><td>driverwindowsupdate[.]at&nbsp; &nbsp; 212.193.48.150</td></tr><tr><td>C2&nbsp;</td><td>windowsdriverupdate[.]at&nbsp; &nbsp; 212.193.48.150</td></tr></tbody></table></figure>
