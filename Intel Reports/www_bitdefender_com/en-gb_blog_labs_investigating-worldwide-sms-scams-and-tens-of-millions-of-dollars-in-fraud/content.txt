This combination of features makes them incredibly useful for criminals who use the technology as a stepping stone in their never-ending campaigns. And if you think that the new <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rich_Communication_Services">RCS messaging standard</a> will offer any protection, you would be wrong. These types of scams will continue to spread regardless of the messaging standard used.</p><p>SMS scams are everywhere, and attackers are always looking for a social or political issue to exploit for profit. As scams get more creative, whether it's a package delivery, a government refund or a banking credential issue, anybody can become a victim.</p><p>We looked at SMS campaigns worldwide to determine which scams are most prevalent and where they are primarily located. Our research spans from the beginning of <strong>September </strong>to the<strong> </strong>beginning of <strong>December.</strong></p><p><strong>Key findings:</strong></p><!--kg-card-begin: markdown--><ul>
<li>We estimate that attackers have taken in around $40 million in just three months, and that's a very conservative number.</li>
<li>SMS scams span the globe and most malicious messages are about package deliveries, banking, government, social media and other platform accounts, and fake prizes.</li>
<li>Attackers take time to customize messages and build campaigns that take advantage of each country's political or socio-economic landscape.</li>
</ul>
<!--kg-card-end: markdown--><h3 id="estimated-financial-impact">Estimated Financial Impact</h3><p></p><p>Based on our telemetry and by determining the likely behavior of non-Bitdefender users over a three-month period, we approximate that 15% of SMS will result in a person clicking a URL. From this cohort, we expect that 10% will actually enter personal details that may result in money loss that averages to $1000. From the above data, we estimate it's likely attackers made at least <strong>$40 million</strong> in three months, which is a conservative number.</p><p>Keep in mind the likely user behavior used to calculate the attackers' profit doesn't apply to people using Bitdefender's security solutions. The software displays warnings when the user receives a malicious SMS, which makes it less likely for the user to become a victim.</p><p>It’s also worth noting that the profits are not going to a single group of attackers, as the campaigns described in this research likely stem from multiple groups.</p><h3 id="heatmaps">Heatmaps</h3><p></p><p>Using a novel Bitdefender technology that lets us group apparently unrelated SMS messages from different campaigns and track their worldwide evolution, we plotted a heatmap of the regions with the largest numbers of scams per user. Most of our attack insights came from the specific areas outlined on the map.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/2024/01/image-16.png" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="2000" height="986" srcset="https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/size/w600/2024/01/image-16.png 600w, https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/size/w1000/2024/01/image-16.png 1000w, https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/size/w1600/2024/01/image-16.png 1600w, https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/2024/01/image-16.png 2203w" sizes="(min-width: 720px) 720px"></figure><p>The scams are diverse and always focus on getting people to share their data. Our new technology discovered major scam attack campaigns in most countries, ranging from typical package delivery messages to government-related ones.</p><p></p><h3 id="country-profiles">Country profiles</h3><p></p><p>Looking at areas of higher scam density, we notice a few regions with a greater prevalence of scams, such as the ones described below.</p><p>In <strong>South Korea</strong>, most SMS scams contain invitations to join Telegram or Kakaotalk channels, which include investment and stock information or receiving fake prizes or payments.</p><p><strong>Australia</strong>, another popular country for scammers, has received plenty of fake package delivery messages and government and banking-related attacks. In the <strong>United States</strong>, users receive many spam messages concerning political surveys, donations, job openings, or service reviews. Apart from this, plenty of banking attacks have also been identified.</p><p>In <strong>Turkey</strong>, SMS messages get delivered from betting and casino websites, some of which may be spam. Looking at the European region, we notice that most countries have a prevalence for scams, with the highest percentage being <strong>Turkey</strong>, Ireland, Germany, France and the United Kingdom.</p><p>Compared to the European average value of messages received per user, in Germany, France and the United Kingdom, users received twice as much as most Europeans have in this period.</p><p>In <strong>Turkey</strong>, most messages are casino-related, but in other European countries, scammers prefer messages related to banking, package delivery and government issues.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/2024/01/image-17.png" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="714" height="723" srcset="https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/size/w600/2024/01/image-17.png 600w, https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/2024/01/image-17.png 714w"></figure><p>Regarding the northern and southern American regions, the average value of scams per user is four times larger than the European number. The most significant number of scams have been identified in the United States, followed by Colombia, Chile and Canada.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/2024/01/image-18.png" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="573" height="750"></figure><h3 id="key-sms-trends-by-countries">Key SMS Trends by Countries</h3><p></p><p>We delimited the scam campaigns popular in most countries and placed them on a map showing the incidence of certain scams in a specific country.</p><p>We analyzed countries with the highest incidence of scam campaigns and split them into categories, comparing keywords and domains of the attacks. Even though most scams can be seen in many countries, where delivery is the most prevalent scam worldwide, the incidence of each scam category varies by country.</p><p>Shifting our focus to the European countries where campaigns are widespread, we have analyzed the most significant categories in each country.</p><p>While the names of most scams are self-explanatory, such as banking-related or package delivery failure, media streaming scams stand out. In this case, users are warned they might lose access to their account due to a missed payment, missing credit information, or any variation of this message.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/2024/01/sms_scams_map_europe_average.svg" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="14000" height="12000"></figure><p></p><h3 id="campaigns-in-the-americas">Campaigns in the Americas</h3><p></p><p>In northern and southern America, the trends are similar to those in Europe, apart from the United States, where most received SMS are spam related to political candidates, jobs or surveys. We noticed a high incidence of banking scams in southern American countries.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card"><img src="https://blogapp.bitdefender.com/labs/content/images/2024/01/sms_scams_map_america_average.svg" class="kg-image" alt loading="lazy" width="13500" height="12000"></figure><h3 id="types-of-sms-scams">Types of SMS Scams</h3><p></p><p>Taking a further look, we distinguish five major categories SMS scams typically fall into:</p><!--kg-card-begin: markdown--><ul>
