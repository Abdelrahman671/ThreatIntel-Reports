              <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p style="font-size: 16px;"><em>Original research by Pritam Salunkhe and Shilpesh Trivedi</em></p>
<p style="font-size: 16px;">&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Uptycs Threat Research team has observed several malicious binaries in our threat intelligence systems using LOLBins in their attack kill chain. LOLBins (short form for Living Off the Land Binaries), are non-malicious native operating system or known software binaries used for performing malicious activities and evading cyber defenses.&nbsp;</p>
<!--more-->
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Uptycs Threat research team has created over 300 rules covering different techniques used by LOLBins in the MITRE ATT&amp;CK framework.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">In this post, we’ll take a look at the LOLBins used by the attackers and how you can use Uptycs <a href="/products/attack-surfaces/endpoints" rel="noopener" target="_blank">EDR</a> detection capabilities to find if these have been used in your environment.<br><br></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="#MITREMapLOLBin" rel="noopener">Click here to see the LOLBins MITRE map</a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>LOLBins &amp; Uptycs EDR Coverage</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Living off the Land binaries exploit the trusted utilities for achieving malicious objectives. They are mostly used by threat actors to stay under the radar and continue malicious activities undetected. In Windows, most of the malware families are taking leverage of LOLBins for a wide variety of phases in the attack kill chain.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Uptycs EDR has a robust coverage for all <a href="https://lolbas-project.github.io/"><span>LOLBAS (Living off the Land Binaries and Scripts)</span></a> techniques in the wild. Using the data from our customer telemetry and threat intelligence systems, the Uptycs Threat research team has created over 300 rules covering 8 different tactics used by LOLBins in the MITRE ATT&amp;CK framework. The distribution of these rules with the techniques is shown below (see Figure 1).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://f.hubspotusercontent00.net/hubfs/2617658/Figure1.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure1.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure1.png" alt="Figure1" width="700" loading="lazy" style="width: 700px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure1.png?width=350&amp;name=Figure1.png 350w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure1.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure1.png 700w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure1.png?width=1050&amp;name=Figure1.png 1050w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure1.png?width=1400&amp;name=Figure1.png 1400w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure1.png?width=1750&amp;name=Figure1.png 1750w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure1.png?width=2100&amp;name=Figure1.png 2100w" sizes="(max-width: 700px) 100vw, 700px"></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>April - July 2021 LOLBins &amp; MITRE ATT&amp;CK Mapping</h2>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Using the data from our in-house threat intelligence systems and customer telemetry, we created a monitoring dashboard of all observed LOLBins. From April 2021 through July 2021, we have observed 26 binaries mostly used as LOLBins by several malware groups. The prevalence of the malicious binaries using the LOLBins is shown below (see Figure 2).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://f.hubspotusercontent00.net/hubfs/2617658/Figure2.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure2.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure2.png" alt="Figure2" width="700" loading="lazy" style="width: 700px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure2.png?width=350&amp;name=Figure2.png 350w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure2.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure2.png 700w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure2.png?width=1050&amp;name=Figure2.png 1050w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure2.png?width=1400&amp;name=Figure2.png 1400w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure2.png?width=1750&amp;name=Figure2.png 1750w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure2.png?width=2100&amp;name=Figure2.png 2100w" sizes="(max-width: 700px) 100vw, 700px"></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">These LOLBins were identified to be exclusively used in the Defense Evasion and Execution phase of the MITRE ATT&amp;CK framework. The distribution of the different ATT&amp;CK tactics used by the attackers leveraging Windows utilities from April 2021 through July 2021 is shown below (see Figure 3).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://f.hubspotusercontent00.net/hubfs/2617658/Figure3.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure3.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure3.png" alt="Figure3" width="700" loading="lazy" style="width: 700px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure3.png?width=350&amp;name=Figure3.png 350w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure3.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure3.png 700w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure3.png?width=1050&amp;name=Figure3.png 1050w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure3.png?width=1400&amp;name=Figure3.png 1400w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure3.png?width=1750&amp;name=Figure3.png 1750w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure3.png?width=2100&amp;name=Figure3.png 2100w" sizes="(max-width: 700px) 100vw, 700px"></a></p>

<h2>LOLBins Observations</h2>
<p>Based on the data we obtained from April 2021 through July 2021, we identified the following:</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1">Most of the LOLBin alerts we have identified have been triggered via decoy macro documents.</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>regsvr32.exe and rundll32.exe</strong> have the highest number of counts as these utilities. These utilities were used exclusively by Qbot and IcedID malwares from the beginning of January 2021, as detailed in our previous <a href="https://www.uptycs.com/blog/icedid-campaign-spotted-being-spiced-with-excel-4-macros"><span>blog</span></a>.</li>
<li aria-level="1">We have also seen a significant number of Loki and Agent Tesla malware samples exploiting a Microsoft Equation Editor (EE) vulnerability in the EQNEDT32.</li>
</ul>
<p>We will now cover interesting examples of LOLBins and their corresponding MITRE ATT&amp;CK tactics.</p>

<h2>LOLBin - Chrome.exe</h2>

<p><em>Hash: eae1b54ba4168e16e951fde291520078d8a5f8b98447cedf5663ae62b9069127</em></p>

During June 2021, our threat intelligence systems detected a document “Resume.docx '' which spawned a new process of chrome.exe via command line. <p>We identified a word document named “contract.docm”, which launches powershell to download the uncompiled C# code. After download is complete, csc.exe compiles the same executable code on the fly as shown below (see Figure 6).&nbsp;</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>LOLBin - netsh.exe</h2>
<h3>Tactic: Persistence</h3>
<p><em>Hash: 36b891924e7259d7b517a5f16a108e63aca927da3610b1dcb4dee79a4ccd2223</em></p>
<p>Netsh is a command-line scripting utility that allows you to display or modify the network configuration. Netsh also has an option to add helper DLLs to extend functionality of the utility.</p>
<p>We identified an excel document that called wmic to create a new process of netsh to register the malicious DLL as the helper DLL as shown below (see Figure 8).</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://f.hubspotusercontent00.net/hubfs/2617658/Figure8.png" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure8.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure8.png" alt="Figure8" width="700" loading="lazy" style="width: 700px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure8.png?width=350&amp;name=Figure8.png 350w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure8.png?width=700&amp;name=Figure8.png 700w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure8.png?width=1050&amp;name=Figure8.png 1050w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure8.png?width=1400&amp;name=Figure8.png 1400w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure8.png?width=1750&amp;name=Figure8.png 1750w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Figure8.png?width=2100&amp;name=Figure8.png 2100w" sizes="(max-width: 700px) 100vw, 700px"></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The path of the DLL is also entered into Windows Registry at <span style="font-size: 10px; color: #1c2226; background-color: #e6e6e6;">HKLM\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Netsh</span>. This allows adversaries to maintain persistence and the execution of the DLL would take place whenever netsh is launched.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>The Uptycs Threat Research team continues to see an increase in the LOLBins used in various stages of the MITRE ATT&amp;CK framework. As most of these utilities are often used for daily activities, it becomes a challenge for traditional security solutions that do not monitor process behavior.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Uptycs’ EDR functionality with suspicious parent/child process relationships, correlation and Threat intelligence provides comprehensive detection and visibility to identify and detect LOLBins malicious activity generically.</p>
