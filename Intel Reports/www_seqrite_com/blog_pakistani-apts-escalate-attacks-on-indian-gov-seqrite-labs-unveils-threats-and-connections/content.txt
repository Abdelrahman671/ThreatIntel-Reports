			<span class="rt-reading-time" style="display: block;"><span class="rt-label">Estimated reading time: </span><span class="rt-time">13</span><span class="rt-label"> minutes</span></span><p>In the recent past, cyberattacks on Indian government entities by Pakistan-linked APTs have gained significant momentum. Seqrite Labs APT team has discovered multiple such campaigns during telemetry analysis and hunting in the wild. One such threat group, SideCopy, has deployed its commonly used AllaKore RAT in three separate campaigns over the last few weeks, where two such RATs were deployed at a time in each campaign. During the same events, its parent APT group Transparent Tribe (APT36) continuously used Crimson RAT but with either an encoded or a packed version. Based on their C2 infrastructure, we were able to correlate these APTs, proving their sub-divisional relation once again. This blog overviews these campaigns and how a connection is established by looking at their previous attacks.</p>
<p>India is one of the most targeted countries in the cyber threat landscape where not only Pakistan-linked APT groups like <a href="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/sidecopys-multi-platform-onslaught-leveraging-winrar-zero-day-and-linux-variant-of-ares-rat/" data-wpel-link="internal" target="_self" rel="follow">SideCopy</a> and APT36 (Transparent Tribe) have targeted India but also new spear-phishing campaigns such as Operation <a href="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/operation-rusticweb-targets-indian-govt-from-rust-based-malware-to-web-service-exfiltration/" data-wpel-link="internal" target="_self" rel="follow">RusticWeb</a> and <a href="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/operation-flightnight-indian-government-entities-and-energy-sector-targeted-by-cyber-espionage-campaign" data-wpel-link="external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">FlightNight</a> have emerged. At the same time, we have observed an increase in the sale of access to Indian entities (both government and corporate) by initial access brokers in the underground forums, high-profile ransomware attacks, and more than 2900 disruptive attacks such as DDoS, website defacement and database leaks by 85+ Telegram Hacktivist groups in the first quarter of 2024.</p>
<p><strong>Threat Actor Profile</strong></p>
<p>SideCopy is a Pakistan-linked Advanced Persistent Threat group that has been targeting South Asian countries, primarily the Indian defense and government entities, since at least 2019. Its arsenal includes Ares RAT, Action RAT, AllaKore RAT, Reverse RAT, Margulas RAT and more. Transparent Tribe (APT36), its parent threat group with the same persistent targeting, shares code similarity and constantly updates its Linux malware arsenal. Active since 2013, it has continuously used payloads such as Crimson RAT, Capra RAT, Eliza RAT and Oblique RAT in its campaigns.</p>
<p><strong>SideCopy</strong></p>
<p>So far, three attack campaigns with the same infection chain have been observed, using compromised domains to host payloads. Instead of side-loading the Action RAT (DUser.dll) payload, as seen previously, two custom variants of an open-source remote agent called <a href="https://github.com/maickonn/AllaKore_Remote" data-wpel-link="external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">AllaKore</a> are deployed as the final payload.</p>
<p><img class="size-full wp-image-6153 aligncenter" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig1.png" alt="" width="992" height="1120" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig1.png 992w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig1-266x300.png 266w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig1-768x867.png 768w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig1-907x1024.png 907w" sizes="(max-width: 992px) 100vw, 992px" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Fig. 1 – Attack Chain of SideCopy</em></p>
<p><strong>Infection Process</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Spear-phishing starts with an archive file containing a shortcut (LNK) in a double-extension format.</li>
<li>Opening the LNK triggers the MSHTA process, which executes a remote HTA file hosted on a compromised domain. The stage-1 HTA contains two embedded files, a decoy and a DLL, that are base64 encoded.</li>
<li>DLL is triggered to run in-memory where the decoy file is dropped &amp; opened by it. As <a href="https://ti.qianxin.com/blog/articles/Analysis-of-SideCopy-Group%27s-Recent-Attacks-Using-Indian-Ministry-of-Defense-Documents-as-Lures-EN/" data-wpel-link="external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">previously</a> seen, the DLL creates multiple text files that mention the name “Mahesh Chand” and various other random texts.</li>
<li>Later, the DLL will download two HTA files from the same compromised domain to begin its second stage process.</li>
<li>Both the HTA contain embedded files, this time an EXE and two DLLs.
<ol start="6">
<li>One of the DLLs is executed in-memory, which drops the remaining two files into the public directory after decoding them. Persistence on the final payload is set beforehand via the Run registry key. One example:</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
<p>REG ADD &#8220;HKCU\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run&#8221; /V &#8220;issas&#8221; /t REG_SZ /F /D &#8220;C:\Users\Public\issas\issas.exe&#8221;</p>
<p><img class="size-full wp-image-6154 aligncenter" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig2.png" alt="" width="359" height="534" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig2.png 359w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/fig2-202x300.png 202w" sizes="(max-width: 359px) 100vw, 359px" /></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Fig. 2 – Files dropped in one of the campaigns</em></p>
<ol>
<li>Lastly, both the final payloads, which is AllaKore RAT, are executed and connected with the same IP but different port numbers for C2 communication. The final DLL is not side-loaded but is completely legitimate and old file.</li>
</ol>
<p>An in-depth analysis of each stage can be checked in our previous <a href="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/sidecopys-multi-platform-onslaught-leveraging-winrar-zero-day-and-linux-variant-of-ares-rat/" data-wpel-link="internal" target="_self" rel="follow">blogs</a> and <a href="https://www.seqrite.com/resources/double-action-triple-infection-and-a-new-rat-sidecopys-persistent-targeting-of-indian-defence" data-wpel-link="internal" target="_self" rel="follow">whitepapers</a>. It contains timers for timeout, reconnection, clipboard, and separate sockets for desktop, files, and keyboard. The functionality of AllaKore includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Gathering system information</li>
<li>Enumerating files and folders</li>
<li>Upload and execute files</li>
<li>Keylogging</li>
<li>Steal clipboard data</li>
</ul>
