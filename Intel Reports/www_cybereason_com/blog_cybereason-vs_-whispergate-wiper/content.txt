      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">
      <div id="hubspot-author_data" class="hubspot-editable cr-mln__post-meta" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author">

          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p>As geopolitical tensions are escalating between Russia and Ukraine, the cyberwar that has been going on since 2013 recently had another round of escalation. For the last couple of months, there have been a wave of cyberattacks targeting Ukrainian interests involving <a href="/blog/ukraine-government-and-embassy-websites-attacked" rel="noopener" target="_blank">website defacements</a> and <a href="/blog/the-fog-of-cyberwar" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>DDOS attacks.</span></a></p>
<!--more-->
<p>The most recent discovery of sophisticated multi-stage attacks that delivered a highly destructive wipers dubbed <a href="https://www.zdnet.com/article/cisa-fbi-warn-us-orgs-of-whispergate-and-hermeticwiper-malware/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">WhisperGate&nbsp;and HermeticWiper</a>. Cybereason detects and blocks both of these destructive malware strains. See below for a demo that shows Cybereason blocking the WhisperGate variant.<span></span></p>
<h2 style="font-size: 30px;">WhisperGate Wiper</h2>
<p><a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-fake-ransomware-targets-ukraine-in-data-wiping-attacks/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><em><span>WhisperGate</span></em></a> is masquerading as ransomware and has paralyzed numerous Ukrainian organizations.&nbsp;This is not the first time a destructive malware makes its way to Ukrainian organizations by the Russians. A similar attack was conducted in 2017 when thousands of Ukrainian businesses were targeted with the <a href="https://www.wired.com/story/notpetya-cyberattack-ukraine-russia-code-crashed-the-world/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>NotPetya</span></a> ransomware, which was attributed to the elite Russian APT group known as <em>Sandworm</em>.</p>
<p>Even though the NotPetya attacks started as attacks targeting only Ukraine, it later “spilled” worldwide, causing massive collateral damage across Europe, Asia and the US.&nbsp;Based on history, it is not an unlikely scenario that the spillage will happen again, where WhisperGate or similar wipers will eventually cause damage in other countries, potentially causing mass disruption.</p>
<h2 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: bold;">Cybereason Detects and Blocks the WhisperGate Wiper</h2>
<p><span>WhisperGate</span> is delivered through of a multi-stage infection chain with two main malware components:&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li style="color: #000000;" aria-level="1"><strong>Stage 1</strong>: A <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_boot_record" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Master Boot Record</span></a><span>&nbsp;(MBR) locker used to overwrite the operating system's MBR, which effectively prevents the operating system from loading successfully</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Stage 2:</strong> A disk-wiper used to wipe and destroy files on the target machine.</li>
</ul>
<p>While the wiper was not attributed to a specific Russian APT group, <a href="https://thehackernews.com/2022/01/ukrainian-government-officially-accuses.html" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>Ukrainian officials publicly attributed</span></a> the attack to Russia, potentially a step of “preparing the ground” for an upcoming military operation.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Cybereason Anti-Ransomware and Anti-MBR corruption technology in the <a href="/platform/xdr" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Cybereason XDR Platform</a> detects and prevents the WhisperGate wiper, as well as every other <a href="/fundamentals/what-is-ransomware" rel="noopener" target="_blank">ransomware </a>and wiper strain:</p>

<h2 style="font-size: 30px;"><strong>Security Recommendations:</strong></h2>
<ul>
<li style="color: #000000;" aria-level="1"><strong>Enable the Anti-Ransomware Feature on <a href="/fundamentals/what-is-ngav" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Cybereason NGAV</a>:</strong> Set Cybereason Anti-Ransomware protection mode to <em>Prevent </em>with MBR protection set to <span style="font-style: italic;">O</span><em>n</em> - <a href="https://nest.cybereason.com/user/login?destination=/documentation/product-documentation/190/anti-ransomware-settings#set-protection-mode" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>more information for Cybereason customers can be found here</span></a></li>
<li style="color: #000000;" aria-level="1"><strong>Enable Anti-Malware Feature on Cybereason NGAV:</strong> Set Cybereason Anti-Malware mode to <em>Prevent </em>and set the detection mode to <em>Moderate </em>and above - <a href="https://nest.cybereason.com/user/login?destination=/documentation/product-documentation/190/anti-malware-settings" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span>more information for Cybereason customers can be found here</span></a></li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Keep Systems Fully Patched:</strong> Make sure your systems are patched in order to mitigate vulnerabilities</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Regularly Backup Files to a Remote Server:</strong> Restoring your files from a backup is the fastest way to regain access to your data</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Use Security Solutions:</strong> Protect your environment using organizational firewalls, proxies, web filtering and mail filtering.</li>
</ul>
