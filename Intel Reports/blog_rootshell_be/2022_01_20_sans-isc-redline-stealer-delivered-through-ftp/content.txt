The difference, in this case, is that the payload is delivered through FTP! Itâ€™s pretty unusual because FTP is today less and less used for multiple reasons (lack of encryption by default, complex to filter with those passive/active modes). Support for FTP has even been disabled by default in Chrome starting with version 95! ButÂ FTP remains a common protocol in the IoT/Linux landscape with malware families like Mirai. My honeypots still collect a lot of Mirai samples on FTP servers. I don&#8217;t understand why the attacker chose this protocol because, in most corporate environments,Â FTP is not allowed by default (and should definitely not be!)&#8230; [Read <a href="https://isc.sans.edu/forums/diary/RedLine+Stealer+Delivered+Through+FTP/28258/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">more</a>]</p>
