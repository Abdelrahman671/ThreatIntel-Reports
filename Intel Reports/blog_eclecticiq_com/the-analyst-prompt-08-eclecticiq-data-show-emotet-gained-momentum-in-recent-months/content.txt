<h2>EIQ Intelligence Center Data: Emotet Gains Momentum in Recent Months</h2>
<p>EclecticIQ Researchers identified continued growth of the Emotet botnet’s activity based on reported botnet command and control (C2) nodes and unique payloads observed from November 2021 through April 2022.</p>
<p>Figure 1 below illustrates new botnet C2 nodes discovered daily and indicates Emotet operators appear to be adding new botnet nodes daily, with growth accelerating in March and April. The increase in C2 nodes likely indicates the continued growth of the botnet. It is also likely that with more nodes the botnet will become more stable, meaning takedown efforts will be difficult and require significant cooperation between law enforcement and private organizations.</p>
<p><img src="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/bot_per_day.png?width=502&amp;name=bot_per_day.png" alt="bot_per_day" width="502" loading="lazy" style="width: 502px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/bot_per_day.png?width=251&amp;name=bot_per_day.png 251w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/bot_per_day.png?width=502&amp;name=bot_per_day.png 502w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/bot_per_day.png?width=753&amp;name=bot_per_day.png 753w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/bot_per_day.png?width=1004&amp;name=bot_per_day.png 1004w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/bot_per_day.png?width=1255&amp;name=bot_per_day.png 1255w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/bot_per_day.png?width=1506&amp;name=bot_per_day.png 1506w" sizes="(max-width: 502px) 100vw, 502px"><span style="text-align: center; background-color: transparent;"></span></p>
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 12px;"><span style="text-align: center; background-color: transparent;">Figure 1 Emotet bots reported per day</span></p>
<p>Figure 2 illustrates that Emotet operators regularly compile new payloads to be delivered via phishing. The number of payloads observed in the given timeframe, at times more than 1,000 unique payloads in a 24-hour period, likely indicates an automated method for compiling payloads. Changing payloads can make it more difficult for network defenders to identify infections and respond quickly.</p>
<p><img src="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/payloads_per_day.png?width=501&amp;name=payloads_per_day.png" alt="payloads_per_day" width="501" loading="lazy" style="width: 501px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/payloads_per_day.png?width=251&amp;name=payloads_per_day.png 251w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/payloads_per_day.png?width=501&amp;name=payloads_per_day.png 501w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/payloads_per_day.png?width=752&amp;name=payloads_per_day.png 752w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/payloads_per_day.png?width=1002&amp;name=payloads_per_day.png 1002w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/payloads_per_day.png?width=1253&amp;name=payloads_per_day.png 1253w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2022/payloads_per_day.png?width=1503&amp;name=payloads_per_day.png 1503w" sizes="(max-width: 501px) 100vw, 501px"></p>
<p style="font-size: 12px; text-align: center;">Figure 2 Emotet payloads reported per day</p>
<p>EclecticIQ Researchers assess it is likely the increasing number of C2 nodes combined with regularity of newly observed payloads that Emotet will continue to re-establish and stabilize its operations after past takedowns, while expanding the botnet’s reach and sophistication to avoid future take down attempts. Organizations should consider Emotet a growing threat especially with its previous associations with Colbalt Strike Beacons and ransomware operations.</p>
<h2>New and Noteworthy: Ransomware Operators may Target Agriculture Organizations during Critical Operating Periods</h2>
<p>The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) issued a warning to the US Food and Agriculture sectors during planting and harvesting seasons that ransomware actors may be more likely to attack during critical planting and harvesting seasons<a href="#a1" rel="noopener"> [1]</a>. Attacks during these periods would likely cause significant disruptions to operations, causing financial losses, and possibly disrupt the food supply chain. The FBI notes that ransomware operators may perceive these critical periods as factors that will increase the willingness to pay the ransom quickly to avoid significant disruptions.</p>
<p>It is likely that ransomware operators have also targeted other industries during critical periods and holidays to apply pressure to pay ransoms. The Covid-19 pandemic caused significant stress on the shipping and logistics sectors. Some ransomware operators utilized this pretext to apply additional pressure to victims to pay ransoms and recover quickly <a href="#a2" rel="noopener">[2]</a>. CISA released two alerts between August and November 2021 for organizations to “stay vigilant” of ransomware attacks during the holiday periods <a href="#a3" rel="noopener">[3]</a> <a href="#a4" rel="noopener">[4]</a>.</p>
<p>EclecticIQ Researchers assess it is likely ransomware operators will utilize critical operating periods and holidays to target organizations on an increasing basis. It is important that businesses in all industries anticipate these days and prepare accordingly. This may include extra security awareness training to all employees for potential phishing lures, reviewing vulnerabilities and patch managements, and ensuring disaster recovery plans have been tested recently.</p>
<h2>Exploit Tools and Targets: Zero-days Exploited in 2021 more than Double</h2>
<p>According to Mandiant zero-day exploitation more than doubled in 2021 over the year prior <a href="#a5" rel="noopener">[5]</a>. Research found that state sponsored espionage groups primary based out of China were the most likely to utilize zero-day exploits in cyber-attacks. Financially motivated actors including ransomware groups account for about one-third of the observed exploits. Mandiant suggests that with the continued growth of the of the exploit market zero-day exploitation will continue to increase in coming years.</p>
<p>The increasing threat of zero-day exploitation poses a risk across most industries and sectors. However, EclecticIQ Researchers assess these exploits are most likely to be used when targeting governments, software supply chains, critical infrastructure, or large financial institutions. These organizations are more likely to have mature security practices that make using a zero-day more necessary and the kind of payoffs that are lucrative enough for the threat actor to utilize it.</p>
<p>It is important that organizations adopt a layered secured approach, adhere to best practices, and quickly patch vulnerabilities as quickly as possible. These measures can limit the impact from zero-day exploits.</p>
