<div class="text text__pt-50 aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12">
<div data-cmp-is="text" class="cmp-text" data-cmp-data-layer="{&#34;text-889ad73d58&#34;:{&#34;@type&#34;:&#34;akamai/components/text&#34;,&#34;repo:modifyDate&#34;:&#34;2023-10-03T00:16:52Z&#34;,&#34;xdm:text&#34;:&#34;&lt;p>Magecart skimmers are here to stay, and they’re becoming more sophisticated, more creative, and harder to detect. In this post, we reveal a new skimmer infrastructure that targets ecommerce sites all over the world with advanced methods of detection evasion and obfuscation.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>In thorough tests, Akamai &lt;a href=\&#34;/content/akamai/en/products/client-side-protection-compliance.html\&#34; target=\&#34;_self\&#34;>Client-Side Protection &amp;amp; Compliance&lt;/a>&amp;nbsp;was shown to be effective protection against the skimmer attacks described in this post. Client-Side Protection &amp;amp; Compliance&amp;nbsp;successfully detected the read attempts from the sensitive input fields and the data exfiltration, and events were created successfully in our Akamai Control Center console.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;h2>Detecting Magecart skimming&amp;nbsp;&lt;/h2>\r\n&lt;p>Magecart skimmers are becoming increasingly advanced in their ability to steal personal information from ecommerce sites. Let’s walk through a successful detection of Magecart skimming — one in which the attacker had used some of the most elaborate evasion techniques ever seen — and determine why it had worked for so long.&amp;nbsp;&amp;nbsp;&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>Although skimmers seem to be able to dodge the traditional protection systems running on even the biggest ecommerce sites, hope is not lost for website owners. We’ll explain how Akamai &lt;a href=\&#34;/content/akamai/en/products/client-side-protection-compliance.html\&#34; target=\&#34;_self\&#34;>Client-Side Protection &amp;amp; Compliance&lt;/a>&amp;nbsp;monitors for abnormal behavior and can alert you to any unexpected activity, malicious code, and outstanding vulnerabilities — making you better able to stop skimmers immediately.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;h2>What is Magecart skimming?&amp;nbsp;&lt;/h2>\r\n&lt;p>A Magecart attack refers to a type of cyberattack that targets ecommerce sites by injecting malicious code into checkout pages, allowing bad actors to “skim” user credit card details input to the HTML form. The credit card information then goes directly to a server, where it’s either used by the attacker or sold on the dark web. Magecart attacks have increased in regularity over the past few years, and high-profile targets have included Ticketmaster and British Airways. In the British Airways attack, the existing JavaScript (JS) on the checkout page was modified — a move that went undetected by both administrators and users — and more than 380,000 credit card details were stolen. Magecart attacks pose a substantial risk to ecommerce sites, and any other site that collects user information, as highly sophisticated attackers use obfuscation methods to disguise malicious code and use geofencing to obscure their own whereabouts.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;h3>How we protected our customer&lt;/h3>\r\n&lt;p>In late October 2021, we came across a Magecart attack on &lt;a href=\&#34;https://scufgaming.com/\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34;>SCUF Gaming International&lt;/a>, a leading manufacturer of custom-built PC and console controllers, in which credit card details from more than 32,000 customers were stolen. The skimmer was detected and removed on March 16 after a month of running on the targeted site. What we found out in this instance, turned out to be the tip of the iceberg that led us to investigate and expose a very sophisticated, broadly deployed Magecart skimmer infrastructure. This brand of skimming attack is still active today on many ecommerce websites.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>Like most Magecart attacks, the main goal of the scufgaming.com attack was to steal credit card information. Many of the attacked websites were built with Magento, an open-source platform used to build ecommerce sites. We assume the attackers exploited known security vulnerabilities on Magento to gain access and inject their malicious script loaders on the targeted websites.&amp;nbsp;&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>As Magecart protections keep growing, we see more and more attacks that implement sophisticated anti-detection and anti-research mechanisms to bypass protections. These traditional protection mechanisms were running on all the targeted websites we tracked, and yet this evolved attack method went undetected.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;h3>How to track skimmers&amp;nbsp;&lt;/h3>\r\n&lt;p>One of the main techniques this skimmer uses to evade detection is the registration of new unique domain names for every (or almost every) targeted website. This is an unusual anti-detection technique that makes it harder to track a skimmer. It also gives the skimmer the flexibility to deactivate the attack when it’s exposed on a specific website while keeping the attack active on others.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>In our case study of scufgaming.com, the response returned from the skimmer’s domain was an innocent JavaScript code related to a known logger library, without any malicious code. We found that the skimmer&#39;s command and control (C2) server responds with clean code when running on nonsensitive pages, and only sends the malicious code if it runs on checkout pages, where credit card information can be found. This ability to hide malicious code and only execute it when needed is another example of Magecart’s increasing sophistication.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>The first step of our investigation focused on determining the skimmer’s code and looking for additional targeted websites. We traced after the skimmer and found — and notified — three more websites (&lt;a href=\&#34;http://www.whitemountainshoes.com/\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34;>whitemountainshoes.com&lt;/a>, &lt;a href=\&#34;http://www.goldboutique.com/\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34;>goldboutique.com&lt;/a>, &lt;a href=\&#34;http://www.nafnaf.com/\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34;>nafnaf.com&lt;/a>) that contacted the same resource as the one we found on scufgaming.com.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>&amp;nbsp;&lt;/p>\r\n&#34;}}">
    <p>Magecart skimmers are here to stay, and they’re becoming more sophisticated, more creative, and harder to detect. In this post, we reveal a new skimmer infrastructure that targets ecommerce sites all over the world with advanced methods of detection evasion and obfuscation.</p>
<p>In thorough tests, Akamai <a href="/products/client-side-protection-compliance" target="_self">Client-Side Protection &amp; Compliance</a> was shown to be effective protection against the skimmer attacks described in this post. Client-Side Protection &amp; Compliance successfully detected the read attempts from the sensitive input fields and the data exfiltration, and events were created successfully in our Akamai Control Center console.</p>
<h2>Detecting Magecart skimming </h2>
<p>Magecart skimmers are becoming increasingly advanced in their ability to steal personal information from ecommerce sites. Let’s walk through a successful detection of Magecart skimming — one in which the attacker had used some of the most elaborate evasion techniques ever seen — and determine why it had worked for so long.  </p>
<p>Although skimmers seem to be able to dodge the traditional protection systems running on even the biggest ecommerce sites, hope is not lost for website owners. We’ll explain how Akamai <a href="/products/client-side-protection-compliance" target="_self">Client-Side Protection &amp; Compliance</a> monitors for abnormal behavior and can alert you to any unexpected activity, malicious code, and outstanding vulnerabilities — making you better able to stop skimmers immediately.</p>
<h2>What is Magecart skimming? </h2>
<p>A Magecart attack refers to a type of cyberattack that targets ecommerce sites by injecting malicious code into checkout pages, allowing bad actors to “skim” user credit card details input to the HTML form. The credit card information then goes directly to a server, where it’s either used by the attacker or sold on the dark web. Magecart attacks have increased in regularity over the past few years, and high-profile targets have included Ticketmaster and British Airways. In the British Airways attack, the existing JavaScript (JS) on the checkout page was modified — a move that went undetected by both administrators and users — and more than 380,000 credit card details were stolen. Magecart attacks pose a substantial risk to ecommerce sites, and any other site that collects user information, as highly sophisticated attackers use obfuscation methods to disguise malicious code and use geofencing to obscure their own whereabouts.</p>
<h3>How we protected our customer</h3>
<p>In late October 2021, we came across a Magecart attack on <a href="https://scufgaming.com/" target="_blank">SCUF Gaming International</a>, a leading manufacturer of custom-built PC and console controllers, in which credit card details from more than 32,000 customers were stolen. The skimmer was detected and removed on March 16 after a month of running on the targeted site. What we found out in this instance, turned out to be the tip of the iceberg that led us to investigate and expose a very sophisticated, broadly deployed Magecart skimmer infrastructure. This brand of skimming attack is still active today on many ecommerce websites.</p>
<p>Like most Magecart attacks, the main goal of the scufgaming.com attack was to steal credit card information. Many of the attacked websites were built with Magento, an open-source platform used to build ecommerce sites. We assume the attackers exploited known security vulnerabilities on Magento to gain access and inject their malicious script loaders on the targeted websites. </p>
<p>As Magecart protections keep growing, we see more and more attacks that implement sophisticated anti-detection and anti-research mechanisms to bypass protections. These traditional protection mechanisms were running on all the targeted websites we tracked, and yet this evolved attack method went undetected.</p>
<h3>How to track skimmers </h3>
<p>One of the main techniques this skimmer uses to evade detection is the registration of new unique domain names for every (or almost every) targeted website. This is an unusual anti-detection technique that makes it harder to track a skimmer. It also gives the skimmer the flexibility to deactivate the attack when it’s exposed on a specific website while keeping the attack active on others.</p>
<p>In our case study of scufgaming.com, the response returned from the skimmer’s domain was an innocent JavaScript code related to a known logger library, without any malicious code. We found that the skimmer's command and control (C2) server responds with clean code when running on nonsensitive pages, and only sends the malicious code if it runs on checkout pages, where credit card information can be found. This ability to hide malicious code and only execute it when needed is another example of Magecart’s increasing sophistication.</p>
<p>The first step of our investigation focused on determining the skimmer’s code and looking for additional targeted websites. We traced after the skimmer and found — and notified — three more websites (<a href="http://www.whitemountainshoes.com/" target="_blank">whitemountainshoes.com</a>, <a href="http://www.goldboutique.com/" target="_blank">goldboutique.com</a>, <a href="http://www.nafnaf.com/" target="_blank">nafnaf.com</a>) that contacted the same resource as the one we found on scufgaming.com.</p>

    <h2>Akamai’s solution </h2>
<p>Akamai tested our web skimming protection product, <a href="/products/client-side-protection-compliance" target="_self">Client-Side Protection &amp; Compliance</a>, against the described attack. Client-Side Protection &amp; Compliance successfully detected the read attempts from the sensitive input fields and the data exfiltration. Events were created successfully in our Control Center console that led to fast and effective mitigations. </p>
<p>As skimmers improve their attacking abilities, we will need to improve our defensive abilities. That’s why the effectiveness of <a href="/products/client-side-protection-compliance" target="_self">Client-Side Protection &amp; Compliance</a> is not restricted to this particular case of web skimming. The software was designed from the beginning to detect behavior anomalies in general and to intelligently assess risk so that website owners can take appropriate actions. </p>
<h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>As we have seen, not only is Magecart skimming alive and well, but skimmers are evolving and becoming even more sophisticated. Attackers have developed new and complex methods to evade detection of their malicious code and themselves. Many existing protection solutions rely on predefined allowlists/blocklists, scanning, and/or sandboxing, all of which skimmers are dodging in order to steal information.</p>
<p>However, there are solutions that skimmers are unable to bypass. The ability to detect changes or vulnerabilities in script gives website owners more control and visibility. As a result, they will be alerted to any potential skimmers almost immediately. Akamai <a href="/products/client-side-protection-compliance" target="_self">Client-Side Protection &amp; Compliance</a> ensures enhanced security and faster action in the case of an attack. </p>
<p>This interesting cat-and-mouse game will continue to evolve for the foreseeable future and it will require powerful, flexible, intuitive solutions to keep ahead of bad actors and their evolving strategies.</p>
