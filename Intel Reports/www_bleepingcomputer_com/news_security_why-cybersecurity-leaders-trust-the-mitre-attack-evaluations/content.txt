<p>In today&rsquo;s dynamic threat landscape, security leaders are under constant pressure to make informed choices about which solutions and strategies they employ to protect their organizations.</p>

<p>The &ldquo;MITRE Engenuity ATT&amp;CK&nbsp;Evaluations: Enterprise&rdquo; stand out as an essential resource for cybersecurity decision makers to navigate this challenge. Unlike other independent assessments, MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations simulate real-world threats to assess how competing cybersecurity vendors detect and respond to real-world threats.</p>

<p>As soon as the highly anticipated 2024 MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluation results are released, <a href="https://go.cynet.com/mitre_2024?utm_source=BleepingComputer&amp;utm_medium=sponsored_article&amp;utm_campaign=Q4-sponsored-content&amp;utm_content=MITREATT&amp;CK2024">this webinar</a> will distill key findings for cybersecurity leaders.</p>

<p>The webinar is hosted by Cynet, the cybersecurity vendor whose <a href="https://www.cynet.com/platform?utm_source=BleepingComputer&amp;utm_medium=sponsored_article&amp;utm_campaign=Q4-sponsored-content&amp;utm_content=MITREATT&amp;CK2024">All-in-One Platform</a> made MITRE ATT&amp;CK history in 2023. For the first time ever, a vendor achieved <a href="https://www.cynet.com/mitre-attck-results/?utm_source=BleepingComputer&amp;utm_medium=sponsored_article&amp;utm_campaign=Q4-sponsored-content&amp;utm_content=MITREATT&amp;CK2024">100% Visibility and 100% Analytic Coverage</a> &mdash; with no configuration changes.</p>

<p>To prepare for the 2024 edition, let&rsquo;s examine what makes MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations unique &mdash; and opportunities for cybersecurity leaders to leverage its results and reduce risk for their organizations.</p>

<h2><a href="http://go.cynet.com/mitre_2024?utm_source=BleepingComputer&amp;utm_medium=sponsored_article&amp;utm_campaign=Q4-sponsored-content&amp;utm_content=MITREATT&amp;CK2024" target="_blank">Why Cybersecurity Leaders Trust the MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations</a></h2>

<p>As soon as the 2024 MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluation (currently under strict embargo) is released publicly<em>,&nbsp;</em>Cynet CTO Aviad Hasnis will&nbsp;distill key takeaways&nbsp;from the 2024 results and provide&nbsp;practical advice you can use to assess the performance of competing cybersecurity vendors.</p>
<a class="fl_button" href="https://go.cynet.com/mitre_2024?utm_source=BleepingComputer&amp;utm_medium=sponsored_article&amp;utm_campaign=Q4-sponsored-content&amp;utm_content=MITREATT&amp;CK2024" rel="nofollow noopener" target="_blank">Register for webinar</a></div>
</div>

<h2>How do the MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations work?</h2>

<p>The MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations are rigorous, independent assessments that test how cybersecurity products detect, respond to, and report various attack techniques.</p>

<p>The Evaluation based on the globally recognized <a href="https://www.cynet.com/network-attacks/quick-guide-to-mitre-attck-matrices-tactics-techniques-more/?utm_source=BleepingComputer&amp;utm_medium=sponsored_article&amp;utm_campaign=Q4-sponsored-content&amp;utm_content=MITREATT&amp;CK2024">MITRE ATT&amp;CK framework</a> &mdash; a comprehensive knowledge base categorizing adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs). By organizing TTPs in stages, the framework gives organizations a structured, standardized way to understand potential threats, and to assess the performance of platforms for detecting and countering them.</p>

<p>During the Evaluation, well-known attack scenarios are recreated in a controlled setting. This allows vendors to test their cybersecurity solutions against emulated adversary behaviors across several stages of the attack lifecycle, providing valuable insights into real-world performance.</p>

<h2>What Differentiates the MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations?</h2>

<p>Several key factors set MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations apart from other independent analyst assessments, making them particularly valuable for security leaders:</p>

<ol><li>Real-World Conditions: Unlike other assessments, MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluations are based on simulated TTPs by specific threat actors. This helps leaders understand how well a security platform could perform in realistic scenarios.</li>
	<li>Transparent Results: The MITRE ATT&amp;CK methodology allows cybersecurity leaders to see in detail how each platform reacts to various TTPs. MITRE doesn&rsquo;t assign scores or rank vendors, encouraging security teams to determine which solution best meets their organization&rsquo;s unique needs.</li>
	<li>Alignment with the MITRE ATT&amp;CK Framework: Since the results align with the well-respected MITRE ATT&amp;CK framework, security teams can easily integrate findings with their existing threat models. This continuity helps to find and fix potential detection or response capability gaps.</li>
	<li>Broad Participation: 31 vendors participated in the 2023 MITRE ATT&amp;CK Evaluation, giving security leaders a diverse view of available options in today&rsquo;s cybersecurity ecosystem.</li>
</ol><h2>What to expect for 2024?</h2>

<p>MITRE says their 2024 Evaluations &ldquo;will incorporate multiple, smaller emulations for a more nuanced and targeted evaluation of defensive capabilities.&rdquo;</p>

<p>Vendor solutions will be pitted against two adversary focus areas: adaptable ransomware-as-a-service variants targeting Linux and Windows; and North Korea state-sponsored tactics to breach macOS.</p>

<p>Whether parsing the Evaluation themselves or <a href="https://go.cynet.com/mitre_2024?utm_source=BleepingComputer&amp;utm_medium=sponsored_article&amp;utm_campaign=Q4-sponsored-content&amp;utm_content=MITREATT&amp;CK2024">watching expert guidance</a> to interpret its results, cybersecurity leaders would be wise to track their tools&rsquo; strengths and weaknesses, refine their defenses, and bolster their resilience against emerging threats.</p>

function cz_strip_tags(input, allowed) {
  allowed = (((allowed || '') + '')
    .toLowerCase()
    .match(/<[a-z][a-z0-9]*>/g) || [])
    .join(''); // making sure the allowed arg is a string containing only tags in lowercase (<a><b><c>)
  var tags = /<\/?([a-z][a-z0-9]*)\b[^>]*>/gi,
    commentsAndPhpTags = /<!--[\s\S]*?-->|<\?(?:php)?[\s\S]*?\?>/gi;
  return input.replace(commentsAndPhpTags, '')
    .replace(tags, function($0, $1) {
      return allowed.indexOf('<' + $1.toLowerCase() + '>') > -1 ? $0 : '';
    });
}
function cz_br2nl(str) {
	var regex = /<br\s*[\/]?>/gi;
    //var pure_str = str.replace(regex,"\n");
    var pure_str = str.replace(regex,"");
    return cz_strip_tags(pure_str,'');
}
