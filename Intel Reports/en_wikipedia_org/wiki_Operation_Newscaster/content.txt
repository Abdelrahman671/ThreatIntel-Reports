					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Newscaster.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8c/Newscaster.jpg/220px-Newscaster.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="84" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8c/Newscaster.jpg/330px-Newscaster.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/8c/Newscaster.jpg/440px-Newscaster.jpg 2x" data-file-width="510" data-file-height="195" /></a><figcaption>Logo designed by <i>iSIGHT Partners</i></figcaption></figure>
<p>"<b>Operation Newscaster</b>", as labelled by American firm <i>iSIGHT Partners</i> in 2014, is a <a href="/wiki/Cyberwarfare" title="Cyberwarfare">cyber espionage</a> <a href="/wiki/Covert_operation" title="Covert operation">covert operation</a> directed at military and political figures using <a href="/wiki/Social_networking" class="mw-redirect" title="Social networking">social networking</a>, allegedly done by <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a>. The operation has been described as "creative",<sup id="cite_ref-Washington_Post_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Post-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> "long-term" and "unprecedented".<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> According to <i>iSIGHT Partners</i>, it is "the most elaborate cyber espionage campaign using <a href="/wiki/Social_engineering_(security)" title="Social engineering (security)">social engineering</a> that has been uncovered to date from any nation".<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="ISight's_perceptions"><span id="ISight.27s_perceptions"></span>ISight's perceptions</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Operation_Newscaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: ISight&#039;s perceptions"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:NewsOnAir.org.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/d/dd/NewsOnAir.org.jpg/220px-NewsOnAir.org.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="236" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/d/dd/NewsOnAir.org.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="305" data-file-height="327" /></a><figcaption>A screenshot from <i>NewsOnAir.org</i></figcaption></figure>
<p>On 29 May 2014, <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">Texas</a>-based cyber espionage research firm <i>iSIGHT Partners</i> released a report, uncovering an operation it labels "Newscaster" since at-least 2011, has targeted at least 2,000 people in <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Britain</a>, <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">Saudi Arabia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Syria" title="Syria">Syria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iraq" title="Iraq">Iraq</a> and <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">Afghanistan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Washington_Times_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Times-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The victims who are not identified in the document due to security reasons, are senior U.S. military and diplomatic personnel, congresspeople, journalists, lobbyists, think tankers and defense contractors, including a <a href="/wiki/Admiral_(United_States)" title="Admiral (United States)">four-star admiral</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Washington_Times_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Times-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The firm couldn’t determine what data the hackers may have stolen.<sup id="cite_ref-Washington_Times_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Times-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>According to the <i>iSIGHT Partners</i> report, hackers used 14 "elaborated fake" personas claiming to work in journalism, government, and defense contracting and were active in <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a>, <a href="/wiki/LinkedIn" title="LinkedIn">LinkedIn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Google%2B" title="Google+">Google+</a>, <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blogger_(service)" title="Blogger (service)">Blogger</a>. To establish trust and credibility, the users fabricated a fictitious journalism website, <i>NewsOnAir.org</i>, using content from the media like <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a>, <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> and populated their profiles with fictitious personal content. They then tried to befriend target victims and sent them "friendly messages"<sup id="cite_ref-Washington_Post_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Post-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Spear-phishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Spear-phishing">Spear-phishing</a> to steal <a href="/wiki/Email" title="Email">email</a> passwords<sup id="cite_ref-Al_Jazeera_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and attacks and infecting them to a "not particularly sophisticated" malware for data exfiltration.<sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Washington_Times_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Times-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The report says <i>NewsOnAir.org</i> was registered in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a> and likely hosted by an Iranian provider. The <a href="/wiki/Persian_language" title="Persian language">Persian</a> word "Parastoo" (<span title="Persian-language text"><span lang="fa" dir="rtl">پرستو</span></span>; meaning <i>swallow</i>) was used as a <a href="/wiki/Password" title="Password">password</a> for malware associated with the group, which appeared to work during business hours in <a href="/wiki/Tehran" title="Tehran">Tehran</a><sup id="cite_ref-Reuters_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reuters-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> as they took Thursday and Friday off.<sup id="cite_ref-Washington_Post_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Washington_Post-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <i>iSIGHT Partners</i> could not confirm whether the hackers had ties to the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_government" class="mw-redirect" title="Iranian government">Iranian government</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Al_Jazeera_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Analysis">Analysis</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Operation_Newscaster&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Analysis"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>According to <i><a href="/wiki/Al_Jazeera_Media_Network" title="Al Jazeera Media Network">Al Jazeera</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Cyberwarfare_in_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyberwarfare in China">Chinese army's cyber unit</a> carried out scores of similar <a href="/wiki/Phishing" title="Phishing">phishing</a> schemes.<sup id="cite_ref-Al_Jazeera_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Morgan_Marquis-Boire" title="Morgan Marquis-Boire">Morgan Marquis-Boire</a>, a researcher at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Toronto" title="University of Toronto">University of Toronto</a> stated that the campaign "appeared to be the work of the same actors performing malware attacks on <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iranian</a> dissidents and journalists for at least two years".<sup id="cite_ref-Al_Jazeera_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Franz-Stefan Gady, a senior fellow at the <a href="/wiki/EastWest_Institute" title="EastWest Institute">EastWest Institute</a> and a founding member of the Worldwide Cybersecurity Initiative, stated that “They’re not doing this for a quick buck, to extrapolate data and extort an organization. They’re in it for the long haul. Sophisticated human engineering has been the preferred method of state actors”.<sup id="cite_ref-Al_Jazeera_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Al_Jazeera-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
