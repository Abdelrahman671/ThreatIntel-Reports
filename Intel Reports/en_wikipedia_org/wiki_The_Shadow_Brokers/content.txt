					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Hacker group</div>
<p>
<b>The Shadow Brokers</b> (<b>TSB</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Hacker_group" title="Hacker group">hacker group</a> who first appeared in the summer of 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> They published several leaks containing hacking tools, including several <a href="/wiki/Zero-day_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Zero-day (computing)">zero-day exploits</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> from the "<a href="/wiki/Equation_Group" title="Equation Group">Equation Group</a>" who are widely suspected to be a branch of the <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Agency" title="National Security Agency">National Security Agency</a> (NSA) of the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ConfirmsNSA_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ConfirmsNSA-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Specifically, these exploits and vulnerabilities<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pastebin.com_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pastebin.com-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> targeted enterprise <a href="/wiki/Firewall_(computing)" title="Firewall (computing)">firewalls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antivirus_software" title="Antivirus software">antivirus software</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> products.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Shadow Brokers originally attributed the leaks to the Equation Group threat actor, who have been tied to the NSA's <a href="/wiki/Tailored_Access_Operations" title="Tailored Access Operations">Tailored Access Operations</a> unit.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ConfirmsNSA_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ConfirmsNSA-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Name_and_alias">Name and alias</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Name and alias"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Several news sources noted that the group's name was likely in reference to a character from the <i><a href="/wiki/Mass_Effect_(series)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mass Effect (series)">Mass Effect</a></i> video game series.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Matt_Suiche" title="Matt Suiche">Matt Suiche</a> quoted the following description of that character: "The Shadow Broker is an individual at the head of an expansive organization which trades in information, always selling to the highest bidder. The Shadow Broker appears to be highly competent at its trade: all secrets that are bought and sold never allow one customer of the Broker to gain a significant advantage, forcing the customers to continue trading information to avoid becoming disadvantaged, allowing the Broker to remain in business."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Leak_history">Leak history</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Leak history"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Equation_Group_leaks">Equation Group leaks</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Equation Group leaks"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>While the exact date is unclear, reports suggested that the preparation of the <a href="/wiki/Internet_leak" title="Internet leak">leak</a> started at least in the beginning of August,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and that the initial publication occurred August 13, 2016 with a Tweet from a <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> account "@shadowbrokerss" announcing a <a href="/wiki/Pastebin.com" title="Pastebin.com">Pastebin</a> page<sup id="cite_ref-pastebin.com_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pastebin.com-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and a <a href="/wiki/GitHub" title="GitHub">GitHub</a> repository containing references and instructions for obtaining and decrypting the content of a file supposedly containing tools and exploits used by the <a href="/wiki/Equation_Group" title="Equation Group">Equation Group</a>. The initial response to the publication was met with some uncertainty about its authenticity.<sup id="cite_ref-businessinsider_2016_NSA_TSB_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businessinsider_2016_NSA_TSB-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On October 31, 2016, The Shadow Brokers published a list of servers supposedly compromised by the Equation Group, as well as references to seven supposedly undisclosed tools (DEWDROP, INCISION, JACKLADDER, ORANGUTAN, PATCHICILLIN, RETICULUM, SIDETRACK AND STOICSURGEON) also used by the threat actor.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On April 8, 2017, the <a href="/wiki/Medium_(website)" title="Medium (website)">Medium</a> account used by The Shadow Brokers posted a new update.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The post revealed the password to encrypted files released the previous year, which allegedly had more NSA hacking tools.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This posting explicitly stated that the post was partially in response to President Trump's <a href="/wiki/2017_Shayrat_missile_strike" title="2017 Shayrat missile strike">attack against a Syrian airfield</a>, which was also used by Russian forces.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="April_14_hacking_tool_leak">April 14 hacking tool leak</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: April 14 hacking tool leak"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>On April 14, 2017, The Shadow Brokers released, amongst other things, the tools and exploits codenamed: DANDERSPRITZ, ODDJOB, FUZZBUNCH, DARKPULSAR, ETERNALSYNERGY, ETERNALROMANCE, <a href="/wiki/ETERNALBLUE" class="mw-redirect" title="ETERNALBLUE">ETERNALBLUE</a>, EXPLODINGCAN and EWOKFRENZY.<sup id="cite_ref-Ars_Technica_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ars_Technica-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>21<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The leak was suggested to be the "...most damaging release yet"<sup id="cite_ref-Ars_Technica_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ars_Technica-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and CNN quoted Matthew Hickey saying, "This is quite possibly the most damaging thing I've seen in the last several years".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>22<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Some of the exploits targeting the Windows operating system had been patched in a Microsoft Security Bulletin on March 14, 2017, one month before the leak occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>23<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>24<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some speculated that Microsoft may have been tipped off about the release of the exploits.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>25<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading4"><h4 id="EternalBlue">EternalBlue</h4><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: EternalBlue"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/EternalBlue" title="EternalBlue">EternalBlue</a></div>
<p>Over 200,000 machines were infected with tools from this leak within the first two weeks,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>26<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and in May 2017, the major <a href="/wiki/WannaCry_ransomware_attack" title="WannaCry ransomware attack">WannaCry ransomware attack</a> used the ETERNALBLUE exploit on <a href="/wiki/Server_Message_Block" title="Server Message Block">Server Message Block</a> (SMB) to spread itself.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>27<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The exploit was also used to help carry out the <a href="/wiki/2017_NotPetya_cyberattack" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 NotPetya cyberattack">2017 NotPetya cyberattack</a> on June 27, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>28<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>ETERNALBLUE contains kernel shellcode to load the non-persistent <a href="/wiki/DoublePulsar" title="DoublePulsar">DoublePulsar</a> <a href="/wiki/Backdoor_(computing)" title="Backdoor (computing)">backdoor</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>29<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This allows for the installation of the PEDDLECHEAP payload which would then be accessed by the attacker using the DanderSpritz Listening Post (LP) software.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>30<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>31<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Speculations_and_theories_on_motive_and_identity">Speculations and theories on motive and identity</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Speculations and theories on motive and identity"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="NSA_insider_threat">NSA insider threat</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: NSA insider threat"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/James_Bamford" title="James Bamford">James Bamford</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Matt_Suiche" title="Matt Suiche">Matt Suiche</a> speculated<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>32<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> that an insider, "possibly someone assigned to the [NSA's] highly sensitive <a href="/wiki/Tailored_Access_Operations" title="Tailored Access Operations">Tailored Access Operations</a>", stole the hacking tools.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>33<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>34<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In October 2016, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">The Washington Post</a></i> reported that <a href="/wiki/Harold_T._Martin_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Harold T. Martin III">Harold T. Martin III</a>, a former contractor for <a href="/wiki/Booz_Allen_Hamilton" title="Booz Allen Hamilton">Booz Allen Hamilton</a> accused of stealing approximately 50 terabytes of data from the <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Agency" title="National Security Agency">National Security Agency</a> (NSA), was the lead suspect. Martin had worked with the NSA's Tailored Access Operations from 2012 to 2015 in a support role. He pleaded guilty to retaining national defense information in 2019, but it is not clear whether the Shadow Brokers obtained their material from him. The Shadow Brokers continued posting messages that were cryptographically-signed and were interviewed by media while Martin was detained.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>35<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Theory_on_ties_to_Russia">Theory on ties to Russia</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=The_Shadow_Brokers&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Theory on ties to Russia"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Edward_Snowden" title="Edward Snowden">Edward Snowden</a> stated on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> on August 16, 2016 that "circumstantial evidence and <a href="/wiki/Conventional_wisdom" title="Conventional wisdom">conventional wisdom</a> indicates Russian responsibility"<sup id="cite_ref-twitter_Snowden_2016_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twitter_Snowden_2016-36"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>36<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and that the leak "is likely a warning that someone can prove responsibility for any attacks that originated from this malware server"<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>37<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> summarizing that it looks like "somebody sending a message that an escalation in the attribution game could get messy fast".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>38<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>39<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> put the incident in the context of the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_National_Committee_cyber_attacks" title="Democratic National Committee cyber attacks">Democratic National Committee cyber attacks</a> and hacking of the <a href="/wiki/Podesta_emails" title="Podesta emails">Podesta emails</a>. As US intelligence agencies were contemplating counter-attacks, the Shadow Brokers code release was to be seen as a warning: "Retaliate for the D.N.C., and there are a lot more secrets, from the hackings of the State Department, the White House and the Pentagon, that might be spilled as well. One senior official compared it to the scene in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Godfather" title="The Godfather">The Godfather</a></i> where the head of a favorite horse is left in a bed, as a warning."<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>40<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, David Aitel, a computer scientist formerly employed by the NSA, summarized the situation with: "I don't know if anybody knows other than the Russians. And we don't even know if it's the Russians. We don't know at this point; anything could be true."<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>41<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
