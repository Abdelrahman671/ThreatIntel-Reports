  <p>On a bi-weekly basis, FortiGuard Labs gathers data on ransomware variants of interest that have been gaining traction within our datasets and the OSINT community. <h2>Dark Power Ransomware</h2>
<p><b>Overview</b></p>
<p>Dark Power is a relatively new ransomware launched in early February 2023. This is a rare ransomware breed in that it was written in the Nim programming language.</p>
<p><b>Dark Power Ransomware Infection Vector</b></p>
<p>Information on the infection vector used by this group is not currently available. However, it is not likely to differ significantly from other ransomware groups.</p>

<h2>PayMe100USD Ransomware</h2>
<p><b>Overview</b></p>
<p>PayMe100USD is a new ransomware written in Python that was discovered in March 2023. The malware has basic functionality and performs ordinary ransomware activities.</p>
<p><b>PayMe100USD Ransomware Infection Vector</b></p>
<p>The PayMe100USD ransomware samples that FortiGuard Labs collected have a Microsoft Bing logo and a file name of “newbing.exe”. As such, the ransomware was likely distributed via fake Bing installers.</p>
<p><b>PayMe100USD Ransomware Execution</b></p>
<p>Once the PayMe100USD ransomware is executed, it encrypts files in the D, E, and F drives and the user directory in the C drive. It adds a “.PayMe100USD” file extension to the affected files. The ransomware avoids encrypting files with the following file extensions:</p>


</div>
<div class="cmp cmp-text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12">

<h2>Best Practices include Not Paying a Ransom</h2>
<p>Organizations such as CISA, NCSC, the <a href="https://www.fbi.gov/scams-and-safety/common-scams-and-crimes/ransomware">FBI</a>, and HHS caution ransomware victims against paying a ransom partly because payment does not guarantee that files will be recovered. According to a <a href="https://home.treasury.gov/system/files/126/ofac_ransomware_advisory_10012020_1.pdf">U.S. Department of Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) advisory</a>, ransom payments may also embolden adversaries to target additional organizations, encourage other criminal actors to distribute ransomware, and/or fund illicit activities that could potentially be illegal. For organizations and individuals affected by ransomware, the FBI has a Ransomware Complaint <a href="https://www.ic3.gov/Home/Ransomware">page</a> where victims can submit samples of ransomware activity via their Internet Crimes Complaint Center (IC3).</p>
