					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Cyberattack on banking industry</div>
<p><b>Carbanak</b> is an <a href="/wiki/Advanced_Persistent_Threat" class="mw-redirect" title="Advanced Persistent Threat">APT</a>-style campaign targeting (but not limited to) financial institutions,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> that was discovered in 2014<sup id="cite_ref-Carbanak_APT_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Carbanak_APT-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> by the Russian <a href="/wiki/Cyber_security" class="mw-redirect" title="Cyber security">cyber security</a> company <a href="/wiki/Kaspersky_Lab" title="Kaspersky Lab">Kaspersky Lab</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SangerPerlroth20150214_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SangerPerlroth20150214-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> It utilizes <a href="/wiki/Malware" title="Malware">malware</a> that is introduced into systems running <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> using <a href="/wiki/Phishing" title="Phishing">phishing</a> emails,<sup id="cite_ref-SangerPerlroth20150214_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SangerPerlroth20150214-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> which is then used to steal money from banks via macros in documents. The <a href="/wiki/Hacker" title="Hacker">hacker</a> group is said to have stolen over 900 million dollars, from the banks as well as from over a thousand private customers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2024)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The criminals were able to manipulate their access to the respective banking networks in order to steal the money in a variety of ways. In some instances, <a href="/wiki/Automated_teller_machine" class="mw-redirect" title="Automated teller machine">ATMs</a> were instructed to dispense cash without having to locally interact with the terminal. <a href="/wiki/Money_mule" title="Money mule">Money mules</a> would collect the money and transfer it over the <a href="/wiki/Society_for_Worldwide_Interbank_Financial_Telecommunication" class="mw-redirect" title="Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication">SWIFT network</a> to the criminals’ accounts, Kaspersky said. The Carbanak group went so far as to alter <a href="/wiki/Database" title="Database">databases</a> and pump up balances on existing accounts and pocketing the difference unbeknownst to the user whose original balance is still intact.<sup id="cite_ref-Threatpost_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Threatpost-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Their intended targets were primarily in Russia, followed by the United States, Germany, China and Ukraine, according to Kaspersky Lab. One bank lost $7.3 million when its ATMs were programmed to spew cash at certain times that <a href="/wiki/Henchmen" class="mw-redirect" title="Henchmen">henchmen</a> would then collect, while a separate firm had $10 million taken via its online platform.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2024)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Kaspersky Lab is helping to assist in investigations and countermeasures that disrupt <a href="/wiki/Malware" title="Malware">malware</a> operations and <a href="/wiki/Cybercriminal" class="mw-redirect" title="Cybercriminal">cybercriminal</a> activity. During the investigations they provide technical expertise such as analyzing infection vectors, malicious programs, supported command and control infrastructure and exploitation methods.<sup id="cite_ref-Securelist_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Securelist-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/FireEye" class="mw-redirect" title="FireEye">FireEye</a> published research tracking further activities, referring to the group as <a href="/wiki/FIN7" title="FIN7">FIN7</a>, including an SEC-themed <a href="/wiki/Spear_phishing" class="mw-redirect" title="Spear phishing">spear phishing</a> campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Proofpoint,_Inc." title="Proofpoint, Inc.">Proofpoint</a> also published research linking the group to the Bateleur <a href="/wiki/Backdoor_(computing)" title="Backdoor (computing)">backdoor</a>, and expanded the list of targets to U.S.-based chain restaurants, hospitality organizations, retailers, merchant services, suppliers and others beyond their initial financial services focus.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On 26 October 2020, PRODAFT (Switzerland) started publishing internal details of the Fin7/Carbanak group and tools they use during their operation.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Published information is claimed to be originated from a single OPSEC failure on the threat actor's side.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>On March 26, 2018, <a href="/wiki/Europol" title="Europol">Europol</a> claimed to have arrested the "mastermind" of the Carbanak and associated Cobalt or Cobalt Strike group in <a href="/wiki/Alicante" title="Alicante">Alicante</a>, Spain, in an investigation led by the Spanish National Police with the cooperation of law enforcement in multiple countries as well as private <a href="/wiki/Cybersecurity" class="mw-redirect" title="Cybersecurity">cybersecurity</a> companies. The group's campaigns appear to have continued, however, with the <a href="/wiki/Hudson%27s_Bay_Company" title="Hudson&#39;s Bay Company">Hudson's Bay Company</a> breach using <a href="/wiki/Point_of_sale" title="Point of sale">point of sale</a> malware in 2018 being attributed to the group.<sup id="cite_ref-Wired_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Wired-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Controversy">Controversy</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbanak&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Controversy"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Some controversy exists around the Carbanak attacks, as they were seemingly described several months earlier in a report by the Internet security companies <a href="/wiki/Group-IB" title="Group-IB">Group-IB</a> (Singapore) and Fox-IT (The Netherlands) that dubbed the attack <a href="/wiki/Group-IB#Anunak/Carbanak" title="Group-IB">Anunak</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Anunak_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Anunak-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Anunak report shows also a greatly reduced amount of financial losses and according to a statement issued by Fox-IT after the release of <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> article, the compromise of banks outside Russia did not match their research.<sup id="cite_ref-Anunakupdate_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Anunakupdate-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Also in an interview conducted by Russian newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Kommersant" title="Kommersant">Kommersant</a></i> the controversy between the claims of Kaspersky Lab and Group-IB come to light where Group-IB claims no banks outside of Russia and Ukraine were hit, and the activity outside of that region was focused on <a href="/wiki/Point_of_Sale" class="mw-redirect" title="Point of Sale">Point of Sale</a> systems.<sup id="cite_ref-KommersantCarbanak_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KommersantCarbanak-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> issued a statement referencing a Private Industry Notification issued by the <a href="/wiki/FBI" class="mw-redirect" title="FBI">FBI</a> and USSS (<a href="/wiki/United_States_Secret_Service" title="United States Secret Service">United States Secret Service</a>) claiming they have not received any reports that Carbanak has affected the financial sector.<sup id="cite_ref-ReutersCarbanak_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReutersCarbanak-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Two representative groups of the US banking industry <a href="/wiki/Financial_Services_Information_Sharing_and_Analysis_Center" title="Financial Services Information Sharing and Analysis Center">FS-ISAC</a> and ABA (<a href="/wiki/American_Bankers_Association" title="American Bankers Association">American Bankers Association</a>) in an interview with <i>Bank Technology News</i> say no US banks have been affected.<sup id="cite_ref-BTNCarbanak_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BTNCarbanak-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
