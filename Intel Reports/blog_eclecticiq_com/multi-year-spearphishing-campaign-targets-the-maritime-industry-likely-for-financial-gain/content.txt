                <h1>Multi-Year Spearphishing Campaign Targets the Maritime Industry Likely for Financial Gain&nbsp;</h1>
                
                    <p class="c-intro mt-5">The EclecticIQ Intelligence and Research Team has discovered a single threat cluster almost certainly targeting the maritime industry in a multi-year campaign.&nbsp; The campaign has been active since at least October 2020 and uses spearphishing emails to target the maritime industry to deliver Agent Tesla and Formbook.</p>
                
                

                
                
                    <div class="flex flex-col sm:flex-row sm:items-center border-t pt-4 xs:pt-6 xl:pt-8 mt-6 xs:mt-8 xl:mt-10 text-secondary">

                            <span class="hidden sm:block mx-2">–</span>
                            <span>March 1, 2023</span>
                            
                        
                    </div>

<h2 style="text-align: left;">Executive summary</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">In May 2020 EclecticIQ Intelligence and Research Team published a report (<a href="#A1" rel="noopener">1</a>) on phishing lures impersonating the maritime industry. This research offers new insights and update on the topic. <span>The key </span><span>takeaways </span><span> </span><span>of this research </span><span>are</span>:</p>
<ul>
<li style="text-align: left;">A single threat cluster is conducting a campaign that is almost certainly targeting the maritime industry to deliver remote access trojans (RATs).</li>
<li style="text-align: left;">The campaign is likely financially motivated.</li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It is likely the maritime industry will continue to be targeted with more convincing spearphishing emails in the long -term due to the readily available information about maritime vessels and the nature of the industry.</li>
</ul>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">The Campaign is Likely Conducted by a Single Threat Cluster</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">EclecticIQ analysts assess that the campaign is likely conducted by a single related threat cluster. The campaign uses consistent maritime-related social engineering lures in spearphishing emails almost certainly targeting the maritime industry. &nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Some of the observed emails distributing Agent Tesla or Formbook include the IPv4 address 173[.]44[.]40[.]60 as the system the email originated from and use the email “‘fredyanni101@gmail.com”’ in the reply-to field. All the observed Formbook samples used the domain ‘www[.]ifair[.]ltd’ for command and control (C2).</p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">The Threat Cluster is Almost Certainly Targeting the Maritime Industry</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">EclecticIO analysts assess it is almost certain the tracked cluster targeted the maritime industry. Analysts observed the threat cluster sending emails to organizations in or related to the maritime industry. Some of the emails likely used spoofed email addresses to appear as though they were sent from people within the same organization or from other maritime-related organizations. &nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The email subject and filename attachments often used real maritime vessel names using the naming format ‘MV &lt;vessel name&gt;’. The email body often pretends that the ship is docking at a port and asks the target to click on the malicious attachment for more details. The email body contains shipping-specific terminology such as VSL (variable speed limit) or emails ending with “.amosconnect.com”, a maritime communication system, to make the emails appear more authentic. &nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">One email was observed using real vessel information; the email claimed to be from the company managing the ship and the email body contained correct last port information, however, there were discrepancies in the provided estimated time of arrival.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/maritime-article/maritime-fig-1.png?width=555&amp;height=377&amp;name=maritime-fig-1.png" alt="maritime-fig-1" width="555" height="377" loading="lazy" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 555px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/maritime-article/maritime-fig-1.png?width=278&amp;height=189&amp;name=maritime-fig-1.png 278w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/maritime-article/maritime-fig-1.png?width=555&amp;height=377&amp;name=maritime-fig-1.png 555w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/maritime-article/maritime-fig-1.png?width=833&amp;height=566&amp;name=maritime-fig-1.png 833w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/maritime-article/maritime-fig-1.png?width=1110&amp;height=754&amp;name=maritime-fig-1.png 1110w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/maritime-article/maritime-fig-1.png?width=1388&amp;height=943&amp;name=maritime-fig-1.png 1388w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/maritime-article/maritime-fig-1.png?width=1665&amp;height=1131&amp;name=maritime-fig-1.png 1665w" sizes="(max-width: 555px) 100vw, 555px"></p>

<h2 style="text-align: left;">Distribution of Agent Tesla</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">The campaign’s first spearphishing email was observed on the 21st &nbsp;of October 2020 against a shipping company headquartered in Norway. The spearphishing email had a CAB file attached using the name of a maritime vessel in its filename. The CAB file contained a Windows executable file, which contained the executable for the RAT Agent Tesla. Often, both the CAB file and executable used the file naming structure ‘MV &lt;vessel name&gt;.&lt;office document extension&gt;.&lt;real file extension&gt;’. User execution was required to launch and install the executable. &nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">EclecticIQ analysts observed 20 emails using CAB files to deliver Agent Tesla, targeting the maritime industry or related entities from 21 October 2020 until 2 May 2022. According to the email headers, the emails often came from the IPv4 address 173[.]44[.]40[.]60, and two of the emails contained the email address ‘fredyanni101@gmail.com’ in the reply-to field. The Agent Tesla payloads used SMTP to exfiltrate data. &nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The following Agent Tesla C2 domains were observed:</p>
<ul>
<li style="text-align: left;">smtp[.]hyshippingcn[.]com</li>
<li style="text-align: left;">smtp[.]bafaqroup[.]com</li>
<li style="text-align: left;">smtp[.]taizingshipping[.]com</li>
</ul>

<h2 style="text-align: left;">The Campaign Shifts from Delivering Agent Tesla to Delivering Formbook</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">On 29 July 2022, the campaign shifted away from distributing Agent Tesla using CAB file email attachments towards distributing Formbook. It is unknown why the campaign shifted tooling. EclecticIQ analysts were able to find this new activity due to the cluster’s consistent targeting of the maritime industry, its reuse of the IPv4 address 173[.]44[.]40[.]60 in the email header, and the re-use of the ‘fredyanni101@gmail.com’ in the email’s reply-to field.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The last email distributing Formbook was observed on 21 December 2022. The cluster used four different delivery techniques to distribute Formbook, each of which is described below. All the Formbook samples used the domain ‘www[.]ifair[.]ltd’ as its C2.</p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">1st Delivery Technique – Attached Word Document Exploits CVE-2017-0199</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">The first technique was first observed on 29 July &nbsp;2022 and starts with a spearphishing email to the target. The emails had an attached Microsoft Office Word document, and sometimes also an attached RAR file with Formbook inside. When clicked on, the Word document exploits CVE-2017-0199 automatically downloading an RTF document using the URL stored in the ‘word/_rels/settings.xml.rels’ file. &nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The following URLs have been observed hosting the RTF files:</p>

<li style="text-align: left;">https://myown[.]bio/jMB</li>
<li style="text-align: left;">http://trim[.]cfd/-d/jyR1PADO</li>
</ul>

<li style="text-align: left;">http://154[.]127[.]53[.]53/settings/wininit[.]exe</li>
<li style="text-align: left;">http://154[.]127[.]53[.]53/outlook/wininit[.]exe</li>
</ul>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">4th Delivery Technique – Attached RAR File Contains Formbook Executable</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">The fourth technique was first observed on 4 August 2022 and uses spearphishing with an attached RAR file. The RAR file contains a Windows executable. The Windows executable is Formbook. User execution is required for the Formbook sample to run. The campaign uses legitimate-looking file names for both the RAR and EXE file along with the spearphishing email to get the user to click on it. Often, both the RAR and the EXE have the same file name.</p>

<p style="text-align: left;">EclecticIQ analysts assess that the chances are about even the targeted cluster is related to a more generic spam cluster. The domain `trim[.]cfd` (discussed above in the first delivery technique) was reused by a Word document in a generic, non-maritime-related phishing lure to download an RTF document that delivers Formbook. The URL pattern ‘http://jmcglone.com@&lt;IPv4 / domain&gt;/&lt;file_path&gt;’ observed in this campaign has been observed embedded in a Word document attached to a generic phishing email. &nbsp;</p>

<h2 style="text-align: left;">The Campaign is Likely Financially Motivated</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">It is likely the campaign is financially motivated. The use of commodity RATs shows a focus on harvesting sensitive information like credentials, session tokens, and email lists. This information can be used for future Business Email Compromise (BEC) attacks or sold to provide initial access to other operators. &nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">EclecticIQ analysts are unable to confirm the financial crime occurring, but the maritime industry is particularly susceptible to BEC attacks because of the large amount of email communication due to the globally dispersed nature of the industry and the frequent charges that occur for ships, such as port charges (<a href="#A2" rel="noopener">2</a>). The industry has also been consistently targeted by ransomware groups (<a href="#A3" rel="noopener">3, 4</a>), groups that often use stolen legitimate credentials for initial access and lateral movement within a target’s environment (<a href="#A5" rel="noopener">5</a>).</p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">Outlook</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">It is likely that the maritime industry will continue to be targeted with more convincing spearphishing emails in the long -term. This campaign has implemented detailed, real-world shipping information to make their spearphishing lures more convincing. The easy accessibility of real-world shipping information from publicly accessible online sites (<a href="#A6" rel="noopener">6</a>) makes implementing this data in emails easy. The attractiveness of the industry to ransomware groups (<a href="#A3" rel="noopener">3, 4</a>) means there is a market for initial access brokers (IAB) to sell to.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Maritime companies need to focus on training their users to recognize phishing lures due to the increasingly convincing lures targeting the industry. &nbsp;</p>
