			<div class="dfd-blog-title">Masad Stealer: Exfiltrating using Telegram</div>
							<div class="entry-meta meta-bottom">
	<span class="entry-date">September 26, 2019</span>	<div class="meta-author">
	<span class="before-author">by</span>

	<span class="byline author vcard">
	<a href="https://blogs.juniper.net/author/paulkim" title="Posts by Paul Kimayong" class="author url fn" rel="author">Paul Kimayong</a>	</span>
	</div>

	
	</div>
			</div>
	<div class="entry-content">

		<p><strong><img loading="lazy" class="size-full wp-image-18680 aligncenter" src="https://blogs.juniper.net/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/IMAGE_1-20228i1C13C6ECA6852021.png" alt="" width="407" height="126" srcset="https://blogs.juniper.net/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/IMAGE_1-20228i1C13C6ECA6852021.png 407w, https://blogs.juniper.net/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/IMAGE_1-20228i1C13C6ECA6852021-300x93.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 407px) 100vw, 407px" /></strong></p>
<p>Juniper Threat Labs discovered a new Trojan-delivered spyware that uses Telegram to exfiltrate stolen information. Using Telegram as a Command and Control (C&amp;C) channel allows the malware some anonymity, as Telegram is a legitimate messaging application with 200 million monthly active users.</p>
<p>The malware is being advertised on black market forums as “Masad Clipper and Stealer.” It steals browser data, which might contain usernames, passwords and credit card information. Masad Stealer also automatically replaces cryptocurrency wallets from the clipboard with its own.</p>
<p>Masad Stealer sends all of the information it collects &#8211; and receive commands from &#8211; a Telegram bot controlled by the threat actor deploying that instance of Masad. Because Masad is being sold as off-the-shelf malware, it will be deployed by multiple threat actors who may or may not be the original malware writers.<br />
<strong>What it does</strong><br />
This malware is written using Autoit scripts and then compiled into a Windows executable. Most samples we have seen are about 1.5 MiB in size, however, Masad Stealer can be found in larger executables as it is sometimes bundled into other software.</p>
<p>When Masad Stealer is executed, it drops itself in %APPDATA%\folder_name}\{file_name}, where folder_name and file_name are defined in the binary. Examples include amd64_usbhub3.inf.resources and ws2_32.exe, respectively. As a persistence mechanism, mMasad Stealer creates a scheduled task that will start itself every one minute.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span class="lia-inline-image-display-wrapper lia-image-align-inline"><span class="lia-inline-image-caption"><img loading="lazy" class="size-full wp-image-18681 aligncenter" src="https://blogs.juniper.net/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/IMAGE_2-20229iC5E90EA3026111E9.png" alt="" width="640" height="234" srcset="https://blogs.juniper.net/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/IMAGE_2-20229iC5E90EA3026111E9.png 640w, https://blogs.juniper.net/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/IMAGE_2-20229iC5E90EA3026111E9-300x110.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" /><em>Masad stealer using scheduled task as persistence mechanism</em></span></span></p>
<p><strong><span style="font-size: x-large;">Stealing routine</span></strong><br />
After installing itself, Masad Stealer starts by collecting sensitive information from the system, such as:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cryptocurrency Wallets</li>
<li>PC and system information</li>
<li>Credit Card Browser Data</li>
<li>Browser passwords</li>
<li>Installed software and processes</li>
<li>Desktop Files</li>
<li>Screenshot of Desktop</li>
<li>Browser cookies</li>
<li>Steam files</li>
<li>AutoFill browser fields</li>
<li>Discord and Telegram data</li>
<li>FileZilla files</li>
</ul>
<p>It zips this information into a file using 7zip utility, which is bundled into the malware binary.</p>

<strong>Conclusion</strong><br />
Juniper Threat Labs believes that Masad Stealer represents an active and ongoing threat.  Command and Control bots are still alive and responding as of this writing, and the malware appears to still be available for purchase on the black market.</p>
<p>In order to protect your organization, make sure that you have a next generation firewall (NGFW) with Advanced Threat Protection. NGFWs have the ability to identify the Telegram protocol and block it, if there is no legitimate business use, while Advanced Threat Protection products offer other methods to detect and counteract this malware.</p>
<p>Juniper Sky ATP, in conjunction with our SRX firewall will block any client infected with Masad Stealer from reaching out to the Command and Control bot master. It will also block the download of the Masad Stealer malware files in the first place, offering both remediation and prevention capabilities.</p>
