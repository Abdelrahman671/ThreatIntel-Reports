	<div class="entry-content">
		<p>Readers of this blog will know that our investigations into APT3 and APT10 started with well-known intrusions and ended with the identities of the perpetrators and the identification of a front company connected to the Chinese Ministry of State Security (MSS).</p>
<p>As we pointed out on Twitter in December, there seems to be a pattern developing – a regional office of the MSS creates a company, hires a team of hackers and attacks Western targets. Why the MSS insists on using sloppy contracted hackers is beyond us here at Intrusion Truth, but the pattern is undeniable.</p>
<p><span id="more-382"></span></p>
<p>What <i>isn&#8217;t </i>clear is how widespread it is. As we asked in December, <b>what if <i>all</i> regional offices of the MSS have their own APT?</b> Could the trail of crumbs be followed in reverse?</p>
<p>Could we start an investigation with an MSS Cyber Officer and identify the APT they manage?</p>
<p><b>Starting with the MSS</b></p>
<p>We recently received a tip from a friendly source that prefers to remain anonymous, but whose identity we have independently verified. The source named an individual based in Jinan, a sprawling city famous for its many natural springs. The source claimed that the individual worked for the MSS on Cyber issues and might be involved in APT hacking.</p>
<p>This was the perfect opportunity to look for an APT and to test our hypothesis.</p>
<p>Experts working with this blog conducted an investigation.</p>
<p>We will bring you the results this week.</p>
<p>#weknowwherethisleads</p>
