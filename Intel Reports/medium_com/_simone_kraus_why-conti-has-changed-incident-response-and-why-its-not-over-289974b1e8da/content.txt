ne go bk">Psychological Effects in the Apparent Lawless Space of Cybercrime</p><h1 id="1cee" class="nf ng gv bf nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny nz oa ob oc bk">Cy-X Explorer 2024 — Justification of Actions</h1><p id="a17e" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">The following article about Conti is part 1 of 2. The second article will discuss what happened to CONTI affiliates, the re-victimization of victims since 2022 after they had re-branded into several Cy-X groups. Part one was published in our Orange Cyberdefense Security Navigator in 2023. Meanwhile with our latest <a class="af oi" href="https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7214178607517052928/" rel="noopener ugc nofollow" target="_blank">Cy-X Explorer 2024</a> we’ve got much more reliable data and research showing the twisted realities of these threat actors and their psychology. In previously published research, we revealed that threat actors actively apply a mechanism called “Neutralization Techniques” to justify their engagement in crime and denying victimhood and responsibility of their actions. In the following Security Navigator article I describe the consequences.</p><h1 id="8b33" class="nf ng gv bf nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny nz oa ob oc bk"><strong class="al">Introduction</strong></h1><p id="271c" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">CONTI has emerged as an extremely antisocial criminal gang. But what makes it stand out even in the ‘industry’ of digital extortion? It is worth investigating this question as the<strong class="mj gw"> lack of morality and integrity</strong> a<strong class="mj gw">re starting to be a threat to entire nations and national securities around the globe</strong>. In addition to operating in the apparent lawless space of cybercrime, there are psychological effects resulting from a ransomware attack by such groups.</p><h1 id="0c00" class="nf ng gv bf nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny nz oa ob oc bk"><strong class="al">1.</strong> <strong class="al">How does it change the way we think?</strong></h1><p id="6b08" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">In addition to the existential fears that arise from a successful ransomware attack on a company, the psychological consequences should not be underestimated.[1]</p><h2 id="fc92" class="oj ng gv bf nh ok ol dy nl om on ea np ms oo op oq mw or os ot na ou ov ow ox bk"><strong class="al">Blame &amp; shame</strong></h2><p id="f2a9" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">When engaging with their victims Conti uses elaborate psychological tricks to convince them to pay. Technically, encrypted files are not that much of a problem if they can be easily restored, so backups are encrypted or deleted too. But that’s not all. To increase the humiliation and pressure on the victims, data is stolen with the intention of leaking it if no payment is made. This double humiliation can leave psychological traces that undermine the morale not only of those IT employees directly affected, but the organization in general.[2]</p><h2 id="a311" class="oj ng gv bf nh ok ol dy nl om on ea np ms oo op oq mw or os ot na ou ov ow ox bk">Twisted Psychology</h2><p id="fad3" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">The next trick in the book is to turn the perception of what is going on. Victims are humiliated by the attacker, telling them that their inability led to the incident. Even worse: the operator dares to present it as a business relationship, which it clearly isn’t. Yet CONTI kindly offer their “services” as if they were not the main reason for the state of emergency. It’s a twisted psychology where the victim is to blame and the criminal the ‘helping hand’.[3]</p><h2 id="6dde" class="oj ng gv bf nh ok ol dy nl om on ea np ms oo op oq mw or os ot na ou ov ow ox bk"><strong class="al">They don’t just steal data; they take away the trust</strong></h2><p id="c2b9" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">Psychologically, a ransomware attack has massive consequences for the company’s employees, business partners and customers. It’s a breach of trust when the incident becomes known, and even worse if data is published. In the long term, this can lead to a break between all actors involved, damaging the company’s reputation and operations to such an extent that it does not recover from the incident.[4]</p><h1 id="c57f" class="nf ng gv bf nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny nz oa ob oc bk"><strong class="al">2.</strong> <strong class="al">CONTI: Criminals, Cyberterrorists and Mother Russia</strong></h1><p id="967b" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">Many of Conti’s activities could be traced back to Russia.[5] This is another source of self-justification the criminals use to explain their activities. Following the country’s agenda, ‘the West’ is the enemy that does not deserve any better. But the relationship goes deeper than that. [6] Through leaked chats by CONTI, it shows that the threat actor has former Russian soldiers in its ranks, who probably already fought against Ukraine in Crimea in 2014, the year Vladimir Putin officially declared his hybrid warfare against the West.[7]</p><h2 id="4181" class="oj ng gv bf nh ok ol dy nl om on ea np ms oo op oq mw or os ot na ou ov ow ox bk">Mind Games are Psychological Warfare</h2><p id="5946" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">It should not come as a surprise that CONTI’s mind games reflect psychological warfare tactics, like offering their victims a ‘business relationship’ that is in reality brutal extortion and not business. Conti as a threat actor is an organized, trans-national crime syndicate, waging operations close to plain <strong class="mj gw">terrorism</strong>. They do not even shy away from taking money out of the pockets of poor governments, as they have recently demonstrated terribly in COSTA RICA[8]. Attacking a country with ransomware that already has severe problems with poverty, starvation and corruption, is more than just a crime. In fact, Costa Rican authorities acted accordingly and declared the incident an <strong class="mj gw">act of war</strong> against the country. [9]</p><h1 id="4ae0" class="nf ng gv bf nh ni nj nk nl nm nn no np nq nr ns nt nu nv nw nx ny nz oa ob oc bk"><strong class="al">3.</strong> <strong class="al">What makes such groups so dangerous to our society?</strong></h1><p id="7dab" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">The absence of empathy, morality, integrity or even the awareness of guilt is psychologically seen a sociopathic behavior[10]. Sociopaths have a cognitive dissonant world view and see only themselves, exploiting and manipulating others. This quickly becomes a threat to society in an environment where consequences are unlikely. Ransomware is a very lucrative field, providing funds for terrorism and totalitarian systems.[11] It’s also harming our economic security. Not only individuals, but whole industries and countries suffer.[12]</p><p id="8240" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd ne go bk">Critical infrastructure attacks are a permanent threat now. Terroristic hacks on our grids, hospitals and water treatment facilities are a reality.[13] But not only that: cyberwarfare often precedes kinetic warfare, which adds to the pre-existing fear and uncertainty. In Ukraine Russian hackers attacked media and infrastructure, days before the first shot was fired.[14]</p><h2 id="c9d5" class="oj ng gv bf nh ok ol dy nl om on ea np ms oo op oq mw or os ot na ou ov ow ox bk">Not victim’s fault</h2><p id="7ebe" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mh mi gv mj b mk od mm mn mo oe mq mr ms of mu mv mw og my mz na oh nc nd ne go bk">According to the RAT, a factor that encourages crime is the absence of capable guardians.[15] Technically it could be a security control failure or insufficient patch management, but it can also refer to a shortage of cyber security experts, or to ordinary employee who lack the security knowledge and awareness to be considered ‘capable’. This doesn’t mean it’s the victim’s fault, though some of them could do more to avoid an attack. <strong class="mj gw">Yet we have seen even huge companies with the best practices implemented and modern environments getting critical assets encrypted.
