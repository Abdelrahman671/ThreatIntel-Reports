					<h2 class="entry-title">Keybase</h2>
				<div>
							<span>
					Posted by: 					<span class="text-color-dark font-weight-semibold">voidsec</span>
				</span>
			
			
			
			
			
			
												<span class="post-date-block m-l-lg">

					</span>

		<hr class="solid">
				<span class="vcard" style="display: none;"><span class="fn"><a href="https://voidsec.com/author/voidsec/" title="Posts by voidsec" rel="author">voidsec</a></span></span><span class="updated" style="display:none">2022-01-14T09:23:40+01:00</span>		<div class="entry-content">
			<span class="rt-reading-time" style="display: block;"><span class="rt-label rt-prefix"><b>Reading Time:</b> </span> <span class="rt-time">6</span> <span class="rt-label rt-postfix">minutes<br><br></span></span><p style="text-align: justify;">Recently, a <strong>malware</strong> known as <strong>KeyBase</strong>, is &#8220;<em>triggering</em>&#8221; some of my sensors. KeyBase was distributed in February 2015 and sold for about $ 50 (in its first version), It remained active until May and then disappear from internet. During November it is back up with thousands of infections (v1.5).</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Keybase is a malware with limited capabilities belonging to the families of <strong>keyloggers</strong> and <strong>info-</strong><strong>stealers</strong>.</p>
<div id="ez-toc-container" class="ez-toc-v2_0_69_1 counter-hierarchy ez-toc-counter ez-toc-custom ez-toc-container-direction">

<h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="malware-overview"></span>Malware Overview<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Keybase is written in C# and among its features we can find:</p>
<ul style="text-align: justify;">
<li><strong>Keylogging</strong></li>
<li>HotLogging(Keylogging ofspecific windows.ex. Paypal, bank accountsetc.)</li>
<li><strong>Password Stealer</strong>
<ul>
<li>Browsers (Chrome, FIrefox, Internet Explorer, Opera, Safari)</li>
<li>Client Email (Outlook, Thunderbird, Incredimail, NetScape, Eudora)</li>
<li>General Purpose Software (FileZilla, JDownloader, IDM, Imvu, PalTalk)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Taking <strong>Screenshot</strong> of the entire screen or specific windows</li>
<li>Steal the contents of the <strong>Clipboard</strong></li>
<li>Ability to block certain Web sites</li>
<li>Visit a website during the malware startup</li>
<li><strong>Self-</strong><strong>destruction</strong> upon reaching a specific date</li>
</ul>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/keybase.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2543"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2543" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x121.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/keybase.png" alt="keybase" width="470" height="569" /></a></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="code-analysis"></span>Code Analysis<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Code is not particularly difficult to read as it is <strong>not</strong><strong> obfuscated</strong>, it also contains the command and control server information &#8220;<em>hard</em>&#8211;<em>coded</em>&#8221; into the source code.<a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/embed.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2546"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2546" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x20.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/embed-1024x214.png" alt="embed" width="600" height="125" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">You can locate a key within resources of the program, once compiled, that appears <strong>unique to</strong><strong> each build</strong> performed on the same machine.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/key.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2545"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2545" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x11.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/key.png" alt="key" width="600" height="70" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The author has taken a number of simple <strong>obfuscation techniques</strong> to strings used in the code.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/encrypted.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2544"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2544" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x49.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/encrypted-1024x507.png" alt="encrypted" width="600" height="297" /></a>Mainly &#8220;<em>replace</em>&#8221; and &#8220;<em>reverse</em>&#8221; operations to characters and strings, in addition to these operations, all the Microsoft Windows API calls are encrypted.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Malware persistence is obtained by copying the executable in the Startup folder under the name of <strong>Important.exe</strong> (visible in the first block of code). This information cannot be changed by the builder and is statically set in the source code. It also set a key value in the registry as follow:</p>
<ul style="text-align: justify;">
<li>HKCU\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run {32 byte}:{exe path}</li>
</ul>
<h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="command-and-control-web-panel"></span>Command and Control &amp; Web Panel<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The most interesting thing, especially for the <strong>poor quality</strong><strong> of the code</strong>, is the web interface.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">All the communications with the remote server are performed via simple HTTP requests and they are not encrypted.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>KeyBase</strong> notification is issued to the remote server once it has been installed:<a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/kb_http.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2547"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2547" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x31.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/kb_http.png" alt="kb_http" width="600" height="191" /></a>Note the absence of certain HTTP headers.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The Web Control Panel gives you the control on all the information stolen from infected machines as seen in the following image:<a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/dashboard.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2548"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2548" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x56.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/dashboard-1024x576.png" alt="dashboard" width="600" height="338" /></a></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="exploit"></span>Exploit<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Default credentials for the web panels are: <em>Admin</em><em>:Admin, KeyBase:Logs123!</em></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Analyzing the source code of the Web panel, you can identify multiple vulnerabilities:<a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/post.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2549"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2549" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x45.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/post-1024x469.png" alt="post" width="600" height="275" /></a>Post.php is vulnerable to <strong>SQL</strong><strong> Injection</strong> (Error &amp; Blind Based) and <strong>Cross Site</strong><strong> Scripting</strong> (XSS) because the “<em>machinename</em><em>, windowtitle, keystrokestyped, machinetime” </em>parameters are not filtered in any way.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Sqlmap:</p>
<pre>Parameter: machinename (GET)
Type: error-based
Title: MySQL &gt;= 5.0 AND error-based - WHERE, HAVING, ORDER BY or GROUP BY clause
Payload: machinename=1' AND (SELECT 5650 FROM(SELECT COUNT(*),CONCAT(0x71706b6a71,(SELECT (ELT(5650=5650,1))),0x71706a7671,FLOOR(RAND(0)*2))x FROM INFORMATION_SCHEMA.CHARACTER_SETS GROUP BY x)a) AND 'oAZi'='oAZi&amp;windowtitle=a&amp;keystrokestyped=a&amp;machinetime=a&amp;type=keystrokes
Type: AND/OR time-based blind
Title: MySQL &gt;= 5.0.12 AND time-based blind (SELECT)
Payload: machinename=1' AND (SELECT * FROM (SELECT(SLEEP(5)))GRmL) AND 'mEfO'='mEfO&amp;windowtitle=a&amp;keystrokestyped=a&amp;machinetime=a&amp;type=keystrokes</pre>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/xss.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2550"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2550" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x48.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/xss-1024x501.png" alt="xss" width="600" height="293" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">XSS:</p>
<pre>GET /keybase/post.php?keystrokestyped=a'"&lt;script&gt;alert('VoidSec')&lt;/script&gt;&amp;machinename=1&amp;machinetime=a&amp;type=keystrokes&amp;windowtitle=a</pre>
<p style="text-align: justify;">The session cookie is set without the <em>&#8216;</em><em>HTTPOnly&#8217;</em> flag allowing the session hijacking through the previous <strong>XSS</strong> vulnerability.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">By default, the directory &#8220;<em>/image/Images</em>&#8220;, (destination of the screenshots) does not require authentication, allowing anyone to see the images stored on the server</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><em>Upload.php</em> does not perform any validation on the uploaded file, allowing a third party to upload a PHP script which can compromise the entire <strong>C&amp;C</strong><strong> server</strong>.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">You may also notice how the author forgot the &#8216;<em>re-</em><em>naming&#8217;</em> of the uploaded files, the function designed for this purpose is never used.<a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/upload.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2551"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2551" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x21.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/upload.png" alt="upload" width="600" height="128" /></a>By submitting this request, you can upload a PHP file that allows you to <strong>read username and password of the web interface.</strong></p>

<pre>POST /image/upload.php HTTP/1.1

<h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="malware-distribution-targets"></span>Malware Distribution &amp; Targets<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">KeyBase, seems to be used to infect devices <strong>in over</strong><strong> 41 countries</strong>, namely: Bosnia and Herzegovina, India, Iran, Jordan, Philippines, Serbia, Thailand, United States of America, Viet Nam.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Its incidence becomes relevant when we talk about the <strong>Iran</strong> that owns <strong>45</strong><strong>% of the infected devices</strong>..</p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/country-kb.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2552"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2552" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x82.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/country-kb.png" alt="country-kb" width="600" height="492" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Some installations include machines used in the following sectors: industrial, <strong>high tech</strong>, private medical offices, higher education, <strong>retail</strong><strong> industries</strong>, <strong>government agencies</strong> as well as to traditional computer households.<a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/court.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2553"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2553" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x62.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/court-1024x643.png" alt="court" width="600" height="377" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Among the bulk of the collected information, while hacking one of the web panels, I was able to discover some files regarding the creation of a new <strong>Ransomware</strong>. I &#8220;<em>dumped</em>&#8221; all the back-end source code and I am currently monitoring the server, trying to retrieve some samples or the executable source code.<a href="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/ransom.png" rel="attachment wp-att-2554"><img decoding="async" class="porto-lazyload aligncenter wp-image-2554" src="https://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/porto_placeholders/100x49.jpg" data-oi="http://voidsec.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/ransom.png" alt="ransom" width="600" height="298" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><strong>But that&#8217;s another story &#8230;.</strong></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span class="ez-toc-section" id="conclusions"></span>Conclusions<span class="ez-toc-section-end"></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;">Finally, despite <strong>KeyBase</strong>, lacks a few options (Download &amp; Execute, Update) in the current landscape of malware and it appears <strong>unsophisticated</strong> (lack of encrypted communication and obfuscation) its ease of use, <a href="https://www.virustotal.com/en/file/237d2c27eb8a1950d0daf070d015a01d8d1f0973073df8847234ba96e608cb57/analysis/1453587594/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><strong>low detection rate</strong></a> and its <strong>simplicity by design</strong>, makes it useful for <strong>further development</strong> and/or further steps in a crypter, allowing the evasion of the most common Anti-Virus products.</p>
