      <h1><span id="hs_cos_wrapper_name" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="text">Cybereason vs. NetWalker Ransomware</span></h1>
      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">
      <div id="hubspot-author_data" class="hubspot-editable cr-mln__post-meta" data-hubspot-form-id="author_data" data-hubspot-name="Blog Author">
        
          <span class="descriptor">Written By</span>

      </div>

        <div class="column is-7-fullhd is-7-desktop is-10-tablet is-10-mobile is-offset-1-fullhd is-offset-1-desktop is-offset-1-tablet is-offset-1-mobile">
          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p><span style="color: #000000;">The </span><a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.mailto"><span>NetWalker </span></a><span style="color: #000000;">ransomware has been one of the most notorious ransomware families over the course of the past year, targeting organizations in the US and Europe including several healthcare organizations, despite several known threat actors publicly </span><a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/ransomware-gangs-to-stop-attacking-health-orgs-during-pandemic/"><span>claiming</span></a><span style="color: #000000;"> to abstain from targeting such organizations due to COVID-19.</span></p>
<!--more-->
<h2 style="line-height: 1.75; font-size: 36px;"><span style="color: #000000;">Key Findings</span></h2>
<span style="font-size: 20px;"><strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">Worldwide Threat</span></strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">: NetWalker was employed in attacks across a variety of industries around the world, which caused great damage to many organizations.</span></span><br><span style="font-size: 20px;"><strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">Encrypting Mapped Drives</span></strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">: NetWalker encrypts shared network drives of adjacent machines on the network.</span></span><br><span style="font-size: 20px;"><strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">Double Extortion Operations</span></strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">: The threat actor behind NetWalker threatens to publicly reveal stolen data if payments are not made.</span></span><br><span style="font-size: 20px;"><strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">High Severity</span></strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">: The Cybereason Nocturnus Team assesses the threat level as HIGH given the destructive potential of the attacks.</span></span><br><span style="font-size: 20px;"><strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">Detected and Prevented</span></strong><span style="color: #1f2e33;">: </span><a href="https://www.cybereason.com/platform">The Cybereason Defense Platform</a><span style="color: #1f2e33;"> fully detects and prevents the NetWalker ransomware.&nbsp;</span></span><br>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<div class="hs-embed-wrapper" data-service="fast.wistia" data-script-embed="true" data-responsive="true" style="position: relative; overflow: hidden; width: 100%; height: auto; padding: 0px; max-width: 800px; min-width: 256px; display: block; margin: auto; max-height: 450px;"><div class="hs-embed-content-wrapper"><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/embed/medias/ivz7ky7kki.jsonp" async>&nbsp;</script><script src="https://fast.wistia.com/assets/external/E-v1.js" async></script><div class="wistia_responsive_padding" style="padding:56.25% 0 0 0;position:relative;"><div class="wistia_responsive_wrapper" style="height:100%;left:0;position:absolute;top:0;width:100%;"><span class="wistia_embed wistia_async_ivz7ky7kki popover=true popoverAnimateThumbnail=true videoFoam=true" style="display:inline-block;height:100%;position:relative;width:100%">&nbsp;</span></div></div></div></div>
<p style="line-height: 1;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 16px;"><em><span style="color: #859da6;">Cybereason Blocks NetWalker Ransomware</span></em></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">NetWalker ransomware first surfaced in August of 2019 (</span><a href="https://www.mcafee.com/enterprise/en-us/threat-center/threat-landscape-dashboard/ransomware-details.mailto-ransomware.html#:~:text=The%20ransomware%2C%20also%20known%20as,all%20Microsoft%20Windows%20systems%20found.&amp;text=The%20ransomware%20appends%20a%20random,files%20and%20uses%20Salsa20%20encryption."><span>first dubbed Mailto</span></a><span style="color: #000000;">). The group behind NetWalker operates a Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) business model, which means they provide their infrastructure, tools and support in exchange for affiliate payment.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">NetWalker operators have adopted the recent popular trend among ransomware purveyors:</span><a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-53214783"><span>double extortion</span></a><span style="color: #000000;">. In addition to demanding a ransom for the encrypted files, the group behind NetWalker steals sensitive data and files from its victims. The group extorts the victims by threatening to leak the stolen data unless ransom is paid. This technique renders the practice of data backups all but moot in combating the impact from ransomware attacks. Other known ransomware groups that leverage the double extortion paradigm are </span><a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.maze"><span>Maze</span></a><span style="color: #000000;">, </span><a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.revil"><span>REvil</span></a><span style="color: #000000;">, and </span><a href="https://malpedia.caad.fkie.fraunhofer.de/details/win.doppelpaymer"><span>DoppelPaymer</span></a><span style="color: #000000;">.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">The group behind NetWalker also maintains a blog on the Darknet where the group publishes information about its new victims alongside a countdown to the deadline for the ransom to be paid. If the time limit has expired and no ransom has been paid, the stolen data is published to this blog:</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;"><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/netwalker-blog-1.png?width=877&amp;name=netwalker-blog-1.png" alt="netwalker-blog-1" width="877" style="width: 877px; display: block; margin: 0px auto;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/netwalker-blog-1.png?width=439&amp;name=netwalker-blog-1.png 439w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/netwalker-blog-1.png?width=877&amp;name=netwalker-blog-1.png 877w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/netwalker-blog-1.png?width=1316&amp;name=netwalker-blog-1.png 1316w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/netwalker-blog-1.png?width=1754&amp;name=netwalker-blog-1.png 1754w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/netwalker-blog-1.png?width=2193&amp;name=netwalker-blog-1.png 2193w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/netwalker-blog-1.png?width=2631&amp;name=netwalker-blog-1.png 2631w" sizes="(max-width: 877px) 100vw, 877px"></span></p>
<p style="font-size: 16px; text-align: center;"><em><span style="color: #000000;">Netwalker Blog</span></em></p>
<p>The targets of NetWalker belong to various sectors, among them educational facilities, local government, healthcare providers, and private companies. In June of 2020, <a href="https://cointelegraph.com/news/ransomware-attack-kidnaps-austrian-city"><span>three US universities were&nbsp; targeted with the ransomware</span></a>: the University of California San Francisco, Michigan State University, and Columbia College of Chicago.&nbsp;<span style="color: #000000;"></span></p>
<p>Different government facilities were victims of NetWalker in <a href="https://cointelegraph.com/news/ransomware-attack-kidnaps-austrian-city"><span>Austria </span></a>and <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/technology-53214783"><span>Argentina</span></a> in the past year as well. The attackers behind NetWalker do not pass on healthcare facilities as well - it has been reported that NetWalker has attacked <a href="https://www.hipaajournal.com/more-than-114000-patients-affected-by-wilmington-surgical-associates-ransomware-attack/"><span>Wilmington Surgical Associates</span></a> and 13GB of data was stolen. <a href="https://siliconangle.com/2020/03/16/ransomware-targets-hospitals-medical-providers-coronavirus-pandemic/"><span>Other healthcare facilities</span></a> have been targeted as well, among them <a href="https://www.cyberscoop.com/crozer-keystone-cyber-attack-netwalker-ransomware/"><span>Crozer-Keystone Health System</span></a>.</p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">Other companies that fell victim include NameSouth, a US-based auto parts distributor, <a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/netwalker-ransomware-hits-pakistans-largest-private-power-utility/"><span>K-Electric</span></a>, an electricity provider in Pakistan, and <a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-ransomware-strain-halts-toll-group-deliveries/"><span>Toll Group Deliveries</span></a>, an Australian transportation and logistics company.</span></p>
<h2 style="font-size: 36px; line-height: 1.75;">Infection</h2>

<p>Next, the ransomware will start the encryption stage. NetWalker ransomware checks for valid drives in the system using <a href="https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/win32/api/fileapi/nf-fileapi-getlogicaldrivestringsw"><em><span>GetLogicalDriveStringsW</span></em></a><em>. </em>For network drives, the ransomware uses <a href="https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/win32/api/securitybaseapi/nf-securitybaseapi-impersonateloggedonuser"><em><span>ImpersonateLoggedOnUser</span></em></a> in an attempt to impersonate the context of the current user in order to access the remote drive. NetWalker then encrypts the files on the network and local drive using Salsa20 encryption. After the files are encrypted, the ransom note is placed.&nbsp;</p>
<p>On some variants, NetWalker also creates persistence via the run registry key and drops a copy of the ransomware to&nbsp; <em>C:\Program Files\random_generated_name\random_generated_name.exe</em>’ or ‘<em>C:\Program Files (x86)\random_generated_name\random_generated_name.exe’</em>.</p>
