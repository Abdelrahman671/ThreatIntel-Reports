<div class='post-body entry-content float-container' id='post-body-4621163286613257595'>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">Cerber has established itself as one of the most successful ransomware families to date. Distributed as Raas (Ransomware as a Service), the malware has retained popularity with over 6 known variants.</span><br />
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The malware is packed with Nullsoft PiMP (plugin Mini Packager) which hides much of the malware's true functionality.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEg2F7NpFGES_zvC0eZjXynoIpOe9MCwADjzommwmPZHIWTXPSUv9Q7iQSMoz19EaF16GdzOKoaxqvReUo0Tl4yS7xJh7nM0D2MdnxsrPy5T_62gbbe4lD2x4XjZ9jTiyveUR7zVBxhafgo/s1600/exeinfo.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="634" data-original-width="1292" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEg2F7NpFGES_zvC0eZjXynoIpOe9MCwADjzommwmPZHIWTXPSUv9Q7iQSMoz19EaF16GdzOKoaxqvReUo0Tl4yS7xJh7nM0D2MdnxsrPy5T_62gbbe4lD2x4XjZ9jTiyveUR7zVBxhafgo/s1600/exeinfo.PNG" /></a></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">As a whole, the malware's entropy is quite high (6.1), indicating packed code.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjRdlXNPHoCYJoZRa7ZcSpKY5k79VNfaE25nvYUurD6UyYmsG1sJ5KLJhuNkP0_odTPKiOsy4E6gOF-sEAZiqXZ3TB993mt5PO4uLBqBMEIeyYEXI5xsZOxmQTeO6m4xsRgNtDij9GrkLI/s1600/high+entropy.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="757" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjRdlXNPHoCYJoZRa7ZcSpKY5k79VNfaE25nvYUurD6UyYmsG1sJ5KLJhuNkP0_odTPKiOsy4E6gOF-sEAZiqXZ3TB993mt5PO4uLBqBMEIeyYEXI5xsZOxmQTeO6m4xsRgNtDij9GrkLI/s1600/high+entropy.PNG" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The malware contains an anomalous PE section ".ndata" which has a virtual size much higher than its physical/raw size.&nbsp; This indicates that there is a good chunk of code that won't present itself until runtime, once the malware is loaded in memory.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEg9LfPJGNGFGD_-5pt585mTIgNNxtgKqMl-iFxtP7UUsbOKOxUC3APJh9Sk34AcIdSfXn_EP31eJhshNDcKTynR3xN5CygKYqqlSNc96sFtg6AnDbIJxkm0z94Yhpk8kV5dlV1ntI9yZC0/s1600/pescanner.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="670" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEg9LfPJGNGFGD_-5pt585mTIgNNxtgKqMl-iFxtP7UUsbOKOxUC3APJh9Sk34AcIdSfXn_EP31eJhshNDcKTynR3xN5CygKYqqlSNc96sFtg6AnDbIJxkm0z94Yhpk8kV5dlV1ntI9yZC0/s1600/pescanner.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">pestudio marks the .text/.code section as highly entropic (6.4), this is where the actual code/instructions are stored in a PE.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhpdw5r600eQ8pdMeALIQf6zuOnsJVid30Y5E-2kLgVyZA7xk2He4l8gbKlWDBaI6arnX9rJvYN6oxvBYDYqced6-CCBEJgY7dXO3jrXU4VI-nQ49Wliw85-VH-UJCqvjBIHgkewdGrrFQ/s1600/pe+sections.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="678" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhpdw5r600eQ8pdMeALIQf6zuOnsJVid30Y5E-2kLgVyZA7xk2He4l8gbKlWDBaI6arnX9rJvYN6oxvBYDYqced6-CCBEJgY7dXO3jrXU4VI-nQ49Wliw85-VH-UJCqvjBIHgkewdGrrFQ/s1600/pe+sections.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">I conducted this analysis in a host-only virtual network with a Windows 10 VM routing its network traffic to a REMnux VM running fakedns, iNetSim, and Wireshark.&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The malware happily runs in the VM with security tools running, dropping several files to disk. The number of bytes written to the file 'collages.dll' is the same as the virtual size of the '.ndata' section.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEgVyrsOpl_YtNQxIgPP6PCrSd9cBaUuuec2ZubvHvhZ-HfLlDJz37XaikoUCH8kPGggmWlJ3CrrJp5alCw0SL2Y2AHw2TQg3F_4ricdnCeBCQtfpPT-i250jKE97XuxEdPMFor68iyWfJs/s1600/1stwritefile.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="431" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEgVyrsOpl_YtNQxIgPP6PCrSd9cBaUuuec2ZubvHvhZ-HfLlDJz37XaikoUCH8kPGggmWlJ3CrrJp5alCw0SL2Y2AHw2TQg3F_4ricdnCeBCQtfpPT-i250jKE97XuxEdPMFor68iyWfJs/s1600/1stwritefile.PNG" /></a></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The malware then makes a few modifications to the registry. A couple of these modifications have to do with what the user is presented with (Wallpaper), the rest have to do with network activity. This malware (interestingly enough) does not establish persistence, just encrypts and exits.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhUkozLS0mo55_pyy9YWafEA7oTJEfNG6iIOEO20UWnaXnmAVVMQiJcXnWN3L8-iyJgkQpqfKRAf9DVfl3ZKRgYny97xuXPNcbqJHQMnj7GdXOgflqamxFX2o1zzMTAl5VCBWcBILeez7c/s1600/regset.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="308" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhUkozLS0mo55_pyy9YWafEA7oTJEfNG6iIOEO20UWnaXnmAVVMQiJcXnWN3L8-iyJgkQpqfKRAf9DVfl3ZKRgYny97xuXPNcbqJHQMnj7GdXOgflqamxFX2o1zzMTAl5VCBWcBILeez7c/s1600/regset.PNG" /></a></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The malware spawns an instance of itself, which then opens the ransom note '_HELP_DECRYPT_N0BR8ST0_.hta'</span><span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">. The filename for this ransom note appears to be unique to the system, format is '</span><span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">_HELP_DECRYPT_[A-Z0-9]{8}_.hta' &lt;- simple regex for the 8 digit alphanumeric string.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhYPpNaulX2vMY_Nt7ZG5lZQQ1RR5fUx0OzYCoE3Kkcnv3XJLLudYVgYYbr0sogxvldyjAOYn6kEj4CU-wk-HGih4Z2BmI3vf2-s94oa-6DTGw-_Z5Uukt6vbxzunEfORN3pYktetDgHp8/s1600/processtree.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="484" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhYPpNaulX2vMY_Nt7ZG5lZQQ1RR5fUx0OzYCoE3Kkcnv3XJLLudYVgYYbr0sogxvldyjAOYn6kEj4CU-wk-HGih4Z2BmI3vf2-s94oa-6DTGw-_Z5Uukt6vbxzunEfORN3pYktetDgHp8/s1600/processtree.PNG" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">Like most ransomware, a ransom note is dropped to the desktop, the Wallpaper is changed, and encrypted files are tagged with a weird file extension '.94d4'.&nbsp; Another file extension found during analysis was '.bde6' - this value appears to be randomly generated.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEiRXUBQ7Lbj85nBNiJTVKS1Wwvelu9lZ879hAXeZFtLYA8QQqgxypFCzKg1RcLHhzj26pRIDbLYkZsuvPwjbj7LML-UiaIbmx_qBmdEjyCBgpZZTn8khbpxcBRuqsRyzrMhmranxkSj6OQ/s1600/desktop.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="1075" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEiRXUBQ7Lbj85nBNiJTVKS1Wwvelu9lZ879hAXeZFtLYA8QQqgxypFCzKg1RcLHhzj26pRIDbLYkZsuvPwjbj7LML-UiaIbmx_qBmdEjyCBgpZZTn8khbpxcBRuqsRyzrMhmranxkSj6OQ/s1600/desktop.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The malware contacts hundreds of hosts over port 6892.</span></div>

<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The same UDP packet is sent over and over.</span></div>

<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The process tree from behavioral analysis showed the original instance of cerber launching a new cerber, this turned out to be pretty interesting. Notice the sixth value pushed onto the stack for the CreateProcess API Call, the value 4 is passed for the dwCreationFlags argument. The 4 indicates that this process is created in a suspended state.&nbsp; Do I sense code injection?</span></div>

<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhn-q8sTxc1IhS8SkmeFExKYBC86d1PxCLzpqQeWu4Xf9K1U3IKahScRrwgLwNvw13-iplwN-RrUHwuUqz4b9_TtjW_TgA7qQGAL97qJmIVJS43_x89F8r8bMhauJXLkRZtVlsK3X2b9g8/s1600/createmutex.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="981" data-original-width="1307" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhn-q8sTxc1IhS8SkmeFExKYBC86d1PxCLzpqQeWu4Xf9K1U3IKahScRrwgLwNvw13-iplwN-RrUHwuUqz4b9_TtjW_TgA7qQGAL97qJmIVJS43_x89F8r8bMhauJXLkRZtVlsK3X2b9g8/s1600/createmutex.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">A UDP socket is created and will be used to blast that single string to hundreds of IPs.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhKoEEKWA_XHJE6ta48KfBpbciFHWDaCJOqqj8kPYpOppTJBx5CXAjYJgM4o0OG-zUDI49grQvCYdBbxozyVXiaekYMGEH_gzIciS13jR5HCP2uqpRPG0YscCqOw5yPPEXdK1sfy1UayHk/s1600/socket.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="457" data-original-width="1345" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEhKoEEKWA_XHJE6ta48KfBpbciFHWDaCJOqqj8kPYpOppTJBx5CXAjYJgM4o0OG-zUDI49grQvCYdBbxozyVXiaekYMGEH_gzIciS13jR5HCP2uqpRPG0YscCqOw5yPPEXdK1sfy1UayHk/s1600/socket.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">'sendto' function is included in a loop to contact the external hosts.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEivcQjLcsviCv6wuAbj3AsPfjqWjHOJR3un2szCRMaSl6nPx5diE7xuSq_vmO5eFD5TXsFcupMgB3fhS4V0VdhgoUEK3DwK18H5htaD26YS4brY87sbZpY36wNIVmuC795vgq-YUohVi-E/s1600/sendto+dbg.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="544" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEivcQjLcsviCv6wuAbj3AsPfjqWjHOJR3un2szCRMaSl6nPx5diE7xuSq_vmO5eFD5TXsFcupMgB3fhS4V0VdhgoUEK3DwK18H5htaD26YS4brY87sbZpY36wNIVmuC795vgq-YUohVi-E/s1600/sendto+dbg.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">Next, the encryption commences.&nbsp; Traversing directories via 'FindFirstFile' and 'FindNextFile'.&nbsp; The malware also looks for network resources to encrypt via 'WNetOpenEnum'.</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEh9SDMvDAkX5BtziIsk4_HjcdgZWsOjA1ySMxIr6IROZ_4akURSjvmSRNmFXuMSFwMpEnXW3quvh6Et8YZQag2BgaruLg0I__jLyPyLF_U2HP7z-p4B8JcubnBb3M0P-5xhzNDxH3Oi1Ws/s1600/find+first+file+a.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="516" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEh9SDMvDAkX5BtziIsk4_HjcdgZWsOjA1ySMxIr6IROZ_4akURSjvmSRNmFXuMSFwMpEnXW3quvh6Et8YZQag2BgaruLg0I__jLyPyLF_U2HP7z-p4B8JcubnBb3M0P-5xhzNDxH3Oi1Ws/s1600/find+first+file+a.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">Once encryption completes, the ransom note is opened via 'ShellExecute' with parameters&nbsp;'Open' and '_HELP_DECRYPT_N0BR8ST0_.hta'.&nbsp; This ransom note is far more robust than most. Most ransom notes are a simple text file, this is an .hta that has several functions, and is apparently quite universally accommodating.&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The ransom note even has a button to change the language.</span></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjG8SdQUWhSA0CbgSQw9IRqZuxVPVTBmKbeY328Yq3V-kGBuc1B4fym5LafOJmD7Gq6bK14AaO3ZiKUtU85t6rnoltVjUYtmTB2uZbMIvjE12Kh15x1HQ-EoD_R4OsLfKDFY5ORyvXWomQ/s1600/hta+lang.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="623" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjG8SdQUWhSA0CbgSQw9IRqZuxVPVTBmKbeY328Yq3V-kGBuc1B4fym5LafOJmD7Gq6bK14AaO3ZiKUtU85t6rnoltVjUYtmTB2uZbMIvjE12Kh15x1HQ-EoD_R4OsLfKDFY5ORyvXWomQ/s1600/hta+lang.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">Another interesting code segment is that the .hta file checks the victim's MAC address against a few MAC addresses associated with VMware and some popular network technology companies.&nbsp; If there is a match, the URL in the ransom note is updated to English. Interesting!</span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEh8s0seBmnQaFDqHO4EDniNre7NXKQ-p9-C288g6Fh9K9cGAN9WrSGlURJyABBRoTJylyi207vTzsxNbR1JKCmzhJZIC1EzYvAfgtkfNRhEQLpBQjl91tnR5ER8VcXv9IERA-JJci49XEo/s1600/hta+mac.PNG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" data-original-height="239" data-original-width="1600" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEh8s0seBmnQaFDqHO4EDniNre7NXKQ-p9-C288g6Fh9K9cGAN9WrSGlURJyABBRoTJylyi207vTzsxNbR1JKCmzhJZIC1EzYvAfgtkfNRhEQLpBQjl91tnR5ER8VcXv9IERA-JJci49XEo/s1600/hta+mac.PNG" /></a></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">The most interesting part of this malware to me was how it unpacked itself through code injection and process hollowing. This malware just performs the encryption and then exits, no persistence! Most filenames are randomized to evade signature based detection, so regex will be our friends when sweeping for/detecting these artifacts. A Snort rule may be plausible due to the port (6892), but UDP can be noisy. The rule would use PCRE to match the unique long string passed over 6892.</span></div>

<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;"><br /></span></div>

<span style="font-family: &quot;courier new&quot; , &quot;courier&quot; , monospace;">Key Takeaways:</span></div>
