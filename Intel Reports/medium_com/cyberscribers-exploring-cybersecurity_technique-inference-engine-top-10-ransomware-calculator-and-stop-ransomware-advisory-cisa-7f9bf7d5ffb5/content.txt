On the <a class="af pc" href="https://center-for-threat-informed-defense.github.io/technique-inference-engine/#/" rel="noopener ugc nofollow" target="_blank">project website</a> you can find a short description of the tool as well as on the <a class="af pc" rel="noopener" href="/mitre-engenuity/know-your-adversarys-next-move-with-tie-6eca0e082a31">Medium blog</a> written by Mike Cunningham.</p><p id="88c2" class="pw-post-body-paragraph nx ny gv nz b ht oa ob oc hw od oe of og oh oi oj ok ol om on oo op oq or os go bk"><strong class="nz gw">What is the next step an adversary could take?</strong></p><p id="9e4e" class="pw-post-body-paragraph nx ny gv nz b ht oa ob oc hw od oe of og oh oi oj ok ol om on oo op oq or os go bk">With the TIE, you can get the top 20 prioritized &amp; predicted techniques based on an observed MITRE ATTACK technique you choose, for example, to get top threat hunting TTPs or to use them for analysis in the incident response as triage. TIE will help analysts quickly understand what is likely to have happened next based on a broad corpus of threat intelligence.</p><p id="88f7" class="nx ny qe nz b ht oa ob oc hw od oe of og oh oi oj ok ol om on oo op oq or os go bk">Having the right dataset is critical to the predictive nature of the model. The model has sufficiently many TTP examples to discover trends in activity and avoid bias towards predicting the most common or popular techniques.</p><p id="e037" class="nx ny qe nz b ht oa ob oc hw od oe of og oh oi oj ok ol om on oo op oq or os go bk">We exclude contrived or speculative data.
