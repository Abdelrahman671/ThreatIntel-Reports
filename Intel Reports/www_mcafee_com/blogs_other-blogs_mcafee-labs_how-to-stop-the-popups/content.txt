		<p>McAfee is tracking an increase in the use of deceptive popups that mislead some users into taking action, while annoying many others.  A significant portion is attributed to browser-based push notifications, and while there are a couple of simple steps users can take to prevent and remediate the situation, there is also some confusion about how these should be handled.</p>
<h2>How does this happen?</h2>
<p>In many cases scammers use deception to trick users into Allowing push notifications to be delivered to their system.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1466" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/1-586x600.gif" alt="" width="586" height="600" /></p>
<p>In other cases, there is no deception involved.  Users willingly opt-in uncoerced.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1467" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/2-600x344.gif" alt="" width="600" height="344" /></p>
<h2>What happens next?</h2>
<p>After Allowing notifications, messages quickly start being received.  Some sites send notifications as often as every minute.</p>
<p>Many messages are deceptive in nature.  Consider this fake alert example.  Clicking the message leads to an imposter Windows Defender alert website, complete with MP3 audio and a phone number to call.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1468" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/3.gif" alt="" width="363" height="348" /></p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1469" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/5-600x514.gif" alt="" width="600" height="514" srcset="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/5-600x514.gif 600w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/5-768x657.gif 768w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>In several other examples, social engineering is crafted around the McAfee name and logo.  Clicking on the messages lead to various websites informing the user their subscription has expired, that McAfee has detected threats on their system, or providing direct links to purchase a <a href="https://www.mcafee.com/en-us/antivirus/mcafee-total-protection.html">McAfee subscription</a>.  Note that “Remove Ads” and similar notification buttons typically lead to the publishers chosen destination rather than anything that would help the user in disabling the popups.  Also note that many of the destination sites themselves prompt the user to Allow more notifications.  This can have a cascading effect where the user is soon flooded with many messages on a regular basis.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1470" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/6.gif" alt="" width="366" height="332" /></p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1481" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/7-300x272-1-1.gif" alt="" width="300" height="272" /></p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1475" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/8-300x272-1.gif" alt="" width="300" height="272" /> <img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1477" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/9-300x267-1.gif" alt="" width="300" height="267" /></p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1471" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/6-588x600.png" alt="" width="588" height="600" srcset="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/6-588x600.png 588w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/6-768x783.png 768w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/6.png 912w" sizes="(max-width: 588px) 100vw, 588px" /></p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1472" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/7-600x414.png" alt="" width="600" height="414" srcset="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/7-600x414.png 600w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/7-768x530.png 768w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/7.png 936w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1474" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/8-600x450.png" alt="" width="600" height="450" srcset="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/8-600x450.png 600w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/8-768x576.png 768w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/8.png 936w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>How can this be remediated?</h2>
<p>First, it’s important to understand that the representative images provided here are not indications of a virus infection.  It is not necessary to update or purchase software to resolve the matter.  There is a simple fix:</p>
<p>1. Note the name of the site sending the notification in the popup itself. It’s located next to the browser name, for example:</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-1476" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/9.png" alt="" width="450" height="410" /></p>
<p><em>Example popup with a link to a Popup remover</em></p>
<p>2. Go to your browser settings’ notification section</p>
<ul>
<li>For Chrome, go here: <a href="//settings/content/notifications">chrome://settings/content/notifications</a></li>
<li>For Edge, go here: <a href="//settings/content/notifications">edge://settings/content/notifications</a></li>
</ul>
<p>3. Search for the site name and click the 3 dotes next to the entry.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1478" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/10-600x399.png" alt="" width="600" height="399" srcset="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/10-600x399.png 600w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/10-768x510.png 768w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/10.png 936w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p>
<p><em>Chrome’s notification settings</em></p>
<p>4. Select Block</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1479" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/11-600x131.png" alt="" width="600" height="131" srcset="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/11-600x131.png 600w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/11-768x168.png 768w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/11.png 926w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p>
<h2>Great, but how can this be prevented in the future?</h2>
<p>The simplest way is to carefully read such authorization prompts and only click Allow on sites that you trust.  Alternatively, you can disable notification prompts altogether.</p>
<p><img decoding="async" class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-1480" src="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/12-600x194.png" alt="" width="600" height="194" srcset="https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/12-600x194.png 600w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/12-768x248.png 768w, https://www.mcafee.com/learn/wp-content/uploads/2022/12/12.png 936w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>As the saying goes, an ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure.</p>
