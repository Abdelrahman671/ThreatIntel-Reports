</td></tr></tbody></table><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Military unit</div>
<p><b>PLA Unit 61398</b> (also known as <b>APT1</b>, <b>Comment Crew</b>, <b>Comment Panda</b>, <b>GIF89a</b>, or <b>Byzantine Candor</b>; <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh">61398部队</span>, <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">Pinyin</a>: 61398 <i>bùduì</i>) is the <a href="/wiki/Military_Unit_Cover_Designator" class="mw-redirect" title="Military Unit Cover Designator">Military Unit Cover Designator</a> (MUCD)<sup id="cite_ref-MandiantAPT1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MandiantAPT1-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> of a <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army" title="People&#39;s Liberation Army">People's Liberation Army</a> <a href="/wiki/Advanced_persistent_threat" title="Advanced persistent threat">advanced persistent threat</a> unit that has been alleged to be a source of Chinese <a href="/wiki/Hacker_(computer_security)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hacker (computer security)">computer hacking</a> attacks.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_2013-02-18_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_2013-02-18-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The unit is stationed in <a href="/wiki/Pudong" title="Pudong">Pudong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and has been cited by US intelligence agencies since 2002.
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="History">History</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PLA_Unit_61398&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<figure class="mw-default-size mw-halign-left" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:FBI20140519.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/FBI20140519.jpg/220px-FBI20140519.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="77" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/FBI20140519.jpg/330px-FBI20140519.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/FBI20140519.jpg/440px-FBI20140519.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="175" /></a><figcaption>From left, Chinese military officers Gu Chunhui, Huang Zhenyu, Sun Kailiang, Wang Dong, and Wen Xinyu indicted on cyber espionage charges.</figcaption></figure><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Chinese_information_operations_and_information_warfare" title="Chinese information operations and information warfare">Chinese information operations and information warfare</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cyberwarfare_by_China" title="Cyberwarfare by China">Cyberwarfare by China</a></div>
<p>A report by the <a href="/wiki/Computer_security" title="Computer security">computer security</a> firm <a href="/wiki/Mandiant" title="Mandiant">Mandiant</a> stated that PLA Unit 61398 is believed to operate under the 2nd Bureau of the <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army_General_Staff_Department" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Liberation Army General Staff Department">People's Liberation Army General Staff Department</a> (GSD) <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army#Third_Department" title="People&#39;s Liberation Army">Third Department</a> (总参三部二局)<sup id="cite_ref-MandiantAPT1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MandiantAPT1-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> and that there is evidence that it contains, or is itself, an entity Mandiant calls <a href="/wiki/APT1" class="mw-redirect" title="APT1">APT1</a>, part of the advanced persistent threat that has attacked a broad range of corporations and government entities around the world since at least 2006. APT1 is described as comprising four large networks in Shanghai, two of which serve the Pudong New Area. It is one of more than 20 APT groups with origins in China.<sup id="cite_ref-MandiantAPT1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MandiantAPT1-1"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The Third and <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army#Fourth_Department" title="People&#39;s Liberation Army">Fourth Department</a>, responsible for <a href="/wiki/Electronic_warfare" title="Electronic warfare">electronic warfare</a>, are believed to comprise the PLA units mainly responsible for infiltrating and manipulating computer networks.<sup id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost_professional_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost_professional-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="2014_indictment">2014 indictment</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PLA_Unit_61398&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2014 indictment"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>On 19 May 2014, the <a href="/wiki/US_Department_of_Justice" class="mw-redirect" title="US Department of Justice">US Department of Justice</a> announced that a Federal <a href="/wiki/Grand_jury" title="Grand jury">grand jury</a> had returned an indictment of five 61398 officers on charges of theft of confidential business information and intellectual property from U.S. commercial firms and of planting <a href="/wiki/Malware" title="Malware">malware</a> on their computers.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The five are Huang Zhenyu (黄振宇), Wen Xinyu (文新宇), Sun Kailiang (孙凯亮), Gu Chunhui (顾春晖), and <a href="/wiki/Wang_Dong_(hacker)" title="Wang Dong (hacker)">Wang Dong</a> (王东).  Forensic evidence traces the base of operations to a 12-story building off Datong Road in a public, mixed-use area of <a href="/wiki/Pudong" title="Pudong">Pudong</a> in Shanghai.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_2013-02-18_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_2013-02-18-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The group is also known by various other names including "Advanced Persistent Threat 1" ("APT1"), "the Comment group" and "Byzantine Candor", a codename given by US intelligence agencies since 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CSMonitor_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSMonitor-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_EU_DC_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_EU_DC-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The group often compromises internal software "comment" features on legitimate web pages to infiltrate target computers that access the sites, leading it to be known as "the Comment Crew" or "Comment Group".<sup id="cite_ref-NYMag_Comment_Crew_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYMag_Comment_Crew-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> The collective has stolen <a href="/wiki/Trade_secret" title="Trade secret">trade secrets</a> and other confidential information from numerous foreign businesses and organizations over the course of seven years such as <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin" title="Lockheed Martin">Lockheed Martin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Telvent" title="Telvent">Telvent</a>, and other companies in the shipping, aeronautics, arms, energy, manufacturing, engineering, electronics, financial, and software sectors.<sup id="cite_ref-CSMonitor_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CSMonitor-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Dell_SecureWorks" class="mw-redirect" title="Dell SecureWorks">Dell SecureWorks</a> says it believed the group includes the same group of attackers behind <a href="/wiki/Operation_Shady_RAT" title="Operation Shady RAT">Operation Shady RAT</a>, an extensive computer espionage campaign uncovered in 2011 in which more than 70 organizations over a five-year period, including the United Nations, government agencies in the United States, Canada, South Korea, Taiwan and Vietnam, were targeted.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_2013-02-18_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_2013-02-18-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>The attacks documented in the summer of 2011 represent a fragment of the Comment group's attacks, which go back at least to 2002, according to incident reports and investigators. In 2012, <a href="/wiki/FireEye,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="FireEye, Inc.">FireEye, Inc.</a> stated that they had tracked hundreds of targets in the last three years and estimated the group had attacked more than 1,000 organizations.<sup id="cite_ref-Bloomberg_EU_DC_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bloomberg_EU_DC-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Most activity between <a href="/wiki/Malware" title="Malware">malware</a> embedded in a compromised system and the malware's controllers takes place during business hours in Beijing's time zone, suggesting that the group is professionally hired, rather than private hackers inspired by patriotic passions.<sup id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost_professional_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost_professional-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>A 2020 report in <a href="/wiki/Daily_News_and_Analysis" title="Daily News and Analysis">Daily News and Analysis</a> stated that the unit was eyeing information related to defense and research in India.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Public_position_of_the_Chinese_government">Public position of the Chinese government</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=PLA_Unit_61398&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Public position of the Chinese government"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Until 2013, the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_China" title="Government of China">Government of China</a> has consistently denied that it is involved in hacking.<sup id="cite_ref-MW_Feb_20_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW_Feb_20-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> In response to the <a href="/wiki/Mandiant" title="Mandiant">Mandiant</a> Corporation report about Unit 61398, <a href="/wiki/Hong_Lei_(politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hong Lei (politician)">Hong Lei</a>, a spokesperson for the <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People&#39;s Republic of China">Chinese foreign ministry</a>, said such allegations were "unprofessional".<sup id="cite_ref-MW_Feb_20_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MW_Feb_20-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
