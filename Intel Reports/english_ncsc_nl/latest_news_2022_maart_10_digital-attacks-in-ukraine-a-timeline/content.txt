  <h1 class="news">
      Digital attacks in Ukraine: A Timeline</h1>
<p class="article-meta">
  News item | 10-03-2022 | 11:55</p><div class="intro"><p>There has been frequent news reporting of digital attacks in Ukraine over the past month. This article provides a chronological overview of the reported cyber attacks and the subsequent publications.</p></div>
  <div class="timeline" data-expand="Expand overview" data-collapse="Collapse overview">
  <h2>Timeline</h2>
        <button class="timelineToggle">Expand overview</button>

            </a>

            <ol class="majorEvent" id="timeline-major-event-114550630-778963465" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true">
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">

                                            23 November</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-1145506321373830654" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550632-252394451">
                            <p>Around 16.20 a <a href="https://twitter.com/EP_President/status/1595443471518777345?cxt=HHwWgoC%2DsczYk6QsAAAA" class="external" aria-label="DDoS attack (opens external website)">DDoS attack</a> rendered the website of the European Parliament temporarily unavailable. According to the European Parliament President Roberta Metsola, a pro-Russian hackers’ collective claimed responsibility for the cyberattack.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-1145506341751317140" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-1145506341751317140" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550634-319169194">
                                            21 November</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-1145506341751317140" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550634-319169194">
                            <p><a href="https://twitter.com/ESETresearch/status/1596181925663760386" class="external" aria-label="ESET (opens external website)">ESET</a> tweeted that it had detected digital attacks on various Ukrainian organisations involving the use of RandomBoggs ransomware. According to ESET, the observed characteristics are in line with previous cyberattacks by the Russian actor Sandworm.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550637-906886965" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550637-906886965" aria-expanded="false">

                                            18 November</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550637-906886965" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-1145506371881178645">
                            <p>The Belarusian Cyber Partisans, an activist group, claimed on <a href="https://twitter.com/cpartisans/status/1593634926674706432" class="external" aria-label="Twitter (opens external website)">Twitter</a> to have access to the network of the Russian General Frequency Control Center (GRFC). They claimed to have encrypted the systems and obtained sensitive information.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-1145506391505111585" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-1145506391505111585" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550639535880352">
                                            16 November</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-1145506391505111585" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550639535880352">
                            <p>At the <a href="https://therecord.media/russias-cyber-personnel-have-underperformed-in-ukraine-u-s-defense-official/" class="external" aria-label="Aspen Cyber Summit (opens external website)">Aspen Cyber Summit</a>, Mieke Eoyang (Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defence for Cyber Policy) said that Russian cyber staff are under-performing. Part of the reason for this is that Moscow was ‘not prepared for the fact that this conflict would last as long as it has,’ Eoyang remarked. </p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550641-1217731579" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550641-1217731579" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-11455064176096044">
                                            10 November</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550641-1217731579" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-11455064176096044">
                            <p>The Microsoft Threat Intelligence Center (<a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2022/10/14/new-prestige-ransomware-impacts-organizations-in-ukraine-and-poland/" class="external" aria-label="MSTIC (opens external website)">MSTIC</a>) attributed the cyberattacks using ‘Prestige’ ransomware to the Russian actor IRIDIUM. The observed activity, as described on 14 October in this timeline, has overlapping characteristics with cyberattacks carried out by IRIDIUM, also known as Sandworm.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550644-1643591269" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550644-1643591269" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550644-1645680776">
                                            4 November</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550644-1643591269" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550644-1645680776">
                            <p>The FBI published <a href="https://www.ic3.gov/Media/News/2022/221104.pdf" class="external" aria-label="security recommendations (opens external website)">security recommendations</a> warning about activist, pro-Russian DDoS attacks. This guidance was issued following various observed DDoS attacks on vital infrastructure in the US. Although these attacks often have a limited impact, the FBI nevertheless recommend taking mitigating measures to reduce the likelihood that such attacks can occur.</p>

<p>The NCSC website contains more in-depth information (in Dutch) on how to ensure that online services are not rendered inaccessible by DDoS attack:</p>

<p>- <a href="https://www.ncsc.nl/onderwerpen/ddos/documenten/factsheets/2019/juni/01/factsheet-continuiteit-van-onlinediensten" class="external" aria-label="Factsheet (opens external website)">Factsheet</a> on ensuring the continuity of online services</p>

<p>- <a href="https://www.ncsc.nl/onderwerpen/ddos/documenten/factsheets/2019/juni/01/factsheet-technische-maatregelen-voor-continuiteit-van-online-diensten" class="external" aria-label="Factsheet (opens external website)">Factsheet</a> on technical measures to ensure the continuity of online services</p>

<p>- <a href="https://www.ncsc.nl/onderwerpen/ddos/het-nederlandse-anti-ddos-initiatief" class="external" aria-label="Anti-DDoS coalition (opens external website)">Anti-DDoS coalition</a></p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-1145506482135772391" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-1145506482135772391" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-1145506482027755167">
                                            1 November</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-1145506482135772391" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-1145506482027755167">
                            <p>Shortly after the Russian invasion, the <a href="https://www.gov.uk/government/news/uk-boosts-ukraines-cyber-defences-with-6-million-support-package" class="external" aria-label="British government (opens external website)">British government</a> announced that it had made a cyber support package of £6.35 million available to help Ukraine protect its critical infrastructure. This cyber support package came in the wake of an increased number of Russian cyber activities against Ukraine a day after the invasion.</p></div>
                    </li>
                </ol>

    <li class="timelineMajor">
            <a href="#timeline-major-event-114550650130612522" aria-controls="timeline-major-event-114550650130612522" aria-expanded="false">
                <h3 class="timelineMajorMarker">October 2022</h3>
            </a>

            <ol class="majorEvent" id="timeline-major-event-114550650130612522" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true">
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550652190418696" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550652190418696" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550652-1745890829">
                                            21 October</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550652190418696" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550652-1745890829">
                            <p><a href="https://cert.gov.ua/article/2394117" class="external" aria-label="CERT-UA (opens external website)">CERT-UA</a> observed that there are fraudulent emails in circulation claiming to be from the press office of the General Staff of the Ukraine&#39;s armed forces. The email contains a link to an external website, which instructs the user to perform a software update. This user interaction then launches the download of an executable file. When the file is run, the system will become infected with RomCom malware. Partly on account of the use of this particular type of malware, CERT-UA believes it is possible to attribute the activity to the actor behind the Cuba ransomware.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-1145506541937277633" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-1145506541937277633" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550655-231793289">
                                            14 October</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-1145506541937277633" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550655-231793289">
                            <p>Microsoft Threat Intelligence Center (MSTIC) <a href="https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2022/10/14/new-prestige-ransomware-impacts-organizations-in-ukraine-and-poland/" class="external" aria-label="flagged (opens external website)">flagged</a> a new ransomware campaign which targets transport and logistics organisations in Ukraine and Poland. The type of ransomware being used was referred to as ‘Prestige’ ransomware.</p>

<p>According to MSTIC there are a number of striking aspects to this campaign that distinguish it from other ransomware campaigns being monitored by Microsoft:</p>

<p>- There is a certain degree of overlap with the victims of the HermeticWiper malware.</p>

<p>- MSTIC could find no links between the characteristics of this new ransomware campaign and the 94 ransomware groups being actively monitored by Microsoft.</p>

<p>- Company-wide deployment of ransomware is not common in Ukraine.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550657-24291995" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550657-24291995" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550658740246888">
                                            11 October</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550657-24291995" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550658740246888">
                            <p>FortiGuard Labs <a href="https://www.fortinet.com/blog/threat-research/ukrainian-excel-file-delivers-multi-stage-cobalt-strike-loader?web%5Fview=true" class="external" aria-label="published (opens external website)">published</a> a technical analysis of the spread of a Cobalt Strike beacon malware. The malware is deployed by means of an Excel file designed to look like a tool for calculating the salaries of Ukrainian military personnel. This malware campaign shows how malicious parties are using the war in Ukraine to carry out cyberattacks.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550660-384208874" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550660-384208874" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550660743287117">
                                            10 October</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550660-384208874" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550660743287117">
                            <p>The websites of several American airports, including Atlanta (ATL), Los Angeles (LAX), Chicago (ORD) and Orlando (MCO), were <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2022/oct/10/cyberattacks-disrupt-us-airport-websites" class="external" aria-label="hit (opens external website)">hit</a> by a DDoS attacks.As a result they were temporarily inaccessible. The attacks, which have been attributed to the hackers’ group Killnet, apparently had no effect on flights to or from the airports.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550663312758019" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550663312758019" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-1145506631321620577">
                                            6 October</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550663312758019" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-1145506631321620577">
                            <p>The hackers group Killnet <a href="https://edition.cnn.com/2022/10/05/politics/russian-hackers-state-government-websites/index.html" class="external" aria-label="claimed (opens external website)">claimed</a> to have taken a number of US state government website offline (Colorado, Mississippi and Kentucky). The group had previously announced on Telegram that it was targeting US government sites. Most of the affected sites were back online the same day.</p></div>
                    </li>
                <li class="timelineMinor">
                        <div class="timelineEventHead">
                            <a href="#timeline-minor-event-114550665-344893040" aria-controls="timeline-minor-event-114550665-344893040" aria-expanded="false">
                                        <h4 id="timeline-minor-event-header-114550665-1951033349">
                                            2 October</h4>
                                        </a>
                                </div>
                        <div id="timeline-minor-event-114550665-344893040" role="region" tabindex="-1" aria-hidden="true" class="timelineEvent" aria-labelledby="timeline-minor-event-header-114550665-1951033349">
                            <p><a href="https://www.kyivpost.com/world/russian-citizens-wage-cyberwar-from-within.html" class="external" aria-label="According to (opens external website)">According to</a> <em>Kyiv Post</em>, a Ukrainian news platform, Russian hackers from the National Republican Army (NRA) carried out a ransomware attack on the Russian company Unisoftware. The platform claimed that customers’ personal data was stolen. The <em>Kyiv Post</em> writes that the Russian government is also a client of Unisoftware. The mobilisation of Russian citizens was allegedly the motivation for the digital attack.</p></div>
                    </li>
                </ol>

    <li class="timelineMajor">
            <a href="#timeline-major-event-1145506682071627686" aria-controls="timeline-major-event-1145506682071627686" aria-expanded="false">

            </a>

<h2>Perspective for action</h2>
        <p>The WhisperGate-attack or the other executed attacks in Ukraine have not led to so-called ‘spill-over effects’ in the Netherlands. However, it is recommended to act in accordance with the NCSC’s basis measures on cybersecurity, and to take note of the <a href="https://www.aivd.nl/documenten/publicaties/2021/06/28/cyberaanvallen-door-statelijke-actoren---zeven-momenten-om-een-aanval-te-stoppen" class="external" aria-label="AIVD and MIVD publication (opens external website)">AIVD and MIVD publication</a>, “Cyberattacks by statal actors, seven moments to stop an attack’’ [available in Dutch only]. In particular, the <a href="https://www.ncsc.nl/documenten/publicaties/2021/juni/28/handreiking-cybersecuritymaatregelen" class="external" aria-label="basic measure (opens external website)">basic measure</a> of ‘segmented networking’ can help to prevent that an attack on a coupled network could affect your organization.</p>

<p>The NCSC keeps monitoring the developments closely and shares relevant information wherever possible.</p></div>
