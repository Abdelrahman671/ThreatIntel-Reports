<h3 style="text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;">INTRODUCTION</span></h3>
<span style="font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;">It has been over 2 months since I <a href="https://sec0wn.blogspot.ae/2018/03/a-quick-dip-into-muddywaters-recent.html" target="_blank">last wrote</a> about MuddyWater or Temp.Zagros as named by <a href="https://www.fireeye.com/blog/threat-research/2018/03/iranian-threat-group-updates-ttps-in-spear-phishing-campaign.html" target="_blank">FireEye</a>. To be honest, I felt they were going quiet for a while; but boy was I wrong. Starting this week I have picked up some new interesting samples. Although these new samples have lots of similarities with the ones from earlier in the year, there are still some interesting aspects and additional, you guessed it, obfuscation used in the new samples. Their heavy focus on layered obfuscation and preference for PowerShell is still apparent. However, I will highlight what changed based on the samples that I have analyzed.</span><br />

<span style="font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;">You can see from the above screenshots that their targeting seem to continue to focus on the Middle East Region (Turkey and Iraq) and Pakistan. As mentioned in my <a href="https://sec0wn.blogspot.ae/2017/11/continued-activity-targeting-middle.html" target="_blank">previous blogs</a>, these lures can give us an idea of the organizations and industries that might have been targeted by this wave.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;">The timeline of these lures based on VT submission dates seems to be from Mid Feb all the way to the most recent sample dated May 6, 2018 which I will be focusing on. The sample has the name "<i>mofa.gov.iq.doc</i>" -&nbsp;94625dd8151814dd6186735a6a6a87b2a4c71c04b8402caf314fb6f98434eaad. MOFA of course stands for Ministry of Foreign Affairs.</span><br />
