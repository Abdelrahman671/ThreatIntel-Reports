The website owner had actually noticed the strange activity occurring and had us investigate further. One of our analysts, Conrado Torquato, investigated the case.</p><h2>Obscure Dessky Snippets plugin used to add malicious PHP</h2><p>It&#8217;s certainly very common for attackers to misuse WordPress plugins that give them the ability to add or manipulate code or files in victim websites.</p><p>Plugins such as WordPress File Manager, for example, are very commonly added to environments once access to the wp-admin administrator panel is established. It monitors POST data for the “<b>ccc</b>” parameters related to the injected form fields. Once these parameters detected, all the values from the billing form along with the credit card details are sent to a third-party URL <b>hxxps://2of[.]cc/wp-content/</b></p><h3>Evasion techniques</h3><p>The input of the fake checkout overlay contains a small quirk: The fields are set with <b>autocomplete=&#8221;off&#8221;. </b>The autocomplete feature in web browsers is of course intended to make the checkout experience more convenient for users and save people time from having to enter in their credit card details if they&#8217;ve done it previously and have the information saved in their browser.</p><p>By manually <i>disabling</i> this feature on the fake checkout form it reduces the likelihood that the browser will warn the user that sensitive information is being entered, and ensures that the fields stay blank until manually filled out by the user, reducing suspicion and making the fields appear as regular, necessary inputs for the transaction.</p><h2>Securing your ecommerce site from skimmers</h2><p>All website owners have a responsibility to keep their websites secure from attackers, but this is particularly true of ecommerce and other websites which handle sensitive information like payment details.</p><p>In essence, ecommerce sites are prime targets for hackers due to the valuable data they handle. Here&#8217;s a simple guide to protect your online store:</p><ol><li aria-level="1"><b>Keep your software patched</b>: Regularly update your CMS, plugins, themes, and any third-party components to patch vulnerabilities.</li><li aria-level="1"><b>Use strong passwords</b>: Ensure all accounts, including admin, sFTP, and database credentials, have <a href="https://blog.sucuri.net/2024/01/how-to-make-strong-password.html">strong and unique passwords</a>.</li><li aria-level="1"><b>Select trusted scripts</b>: Only integrate third-party JavaScript from reputable sources. Avoid unnecessary third-party scripts.</li><li aria-level="1"><b>Monitor for threats</b>: Regularly check your site for signs of malware, unauthorized changes, or any indicators of compromise.</li><li aria-level="1"><b>Implement a firewall</b>: Use a <a href="https://sucuri.net/website-firewall/">web application firewall</a> to block malicious bots, virtually patch known vulnerabilities, and filter harmful traffic.</li><li aria-level="1"><b>Set up a CSP</b>: <a href="https://blog.sucuri.net/2023/04/how-to-set-up-a-content-security-policy-csp-in-3-steps.html">Establish a Content Security Policy (CSP)</a> to protect against clickjacking, cross-site scripting (XSS), and other threats.</li></ol><p>For website visitors, consider using script-blocking extensions like NoScript and maintain robust antivirus software to detect any ongoing skimming attacks.</p><p>If you suspect your ecommerce site is compromised, our team is available 24/7 to help <a href="https://sucuri.net/website-malware-removal/">clean and secure your site</a> against malware and future threats.</p></div><div class="post-author"><div class="author-wrap"><div class="author"><div class="author-avatar"> <a href="https://blog.sucuri.net/author/benmartin" rel="author"> <img alt='' src='https://secure.gravatar.com/avatar/fcf2c7b3195ff9058d29af3b8a49fc43?s=120&#038;d=mm&#038;r=g' srcset='https://secure.gravatar.com/avatar/fcf2c7b3195ff9058d29af3b8a49fc43?s=240&#038;d=mm&#038;r=g 2x' class='avatar avatar-120 photo' height='120' width='120' loading='lazy' decoding='async'/> </a></div><div class="author-description"><h5 class="title-author"> <span class="fn"> <a href="https://blog.sucuri.net/author/benmartin" rel="author"> Ben Martin </a> </span></h5><p class="note">Ben Martin is a security analyst and researcher who joined the company in 2013.
