              <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><h1>Summary</h1>
<p>Watering hole attacks are an often underrated technique. On January 29th, 2023 <a href="https://www.chrisfarris.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Chris Farris</a> reported to members of the “Cloud Security Forum” slack channel that he saw a google ad delivering a malicious AWS console logon phishing page:</p>
<!--more--><img src="https://permiso.io/hs-fs/hubfs/Permiso_Security_June2023/images/Untitled.webp?width=954&amp;height=664&amp;name=Untitled.webp" alt="Charis Farris" width="954" height="664" loading="lazy" style="object-fit: cover; opacity: 1; height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 954px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://permiso.io/hs-fs/hubfs/Permiso_Security_June2023/images/Untitled.webp?width=477&amp;height=332&amp;name=Untitled.webp 477w, https://permiso.io/hs-fs/hubfs/Permiso_Security_June2023/images/Untitled.webp?width=954&amp;height=664&amp;name=Untitled.webp 954w, https://permiso.io/hs-fs/hubfs/Permiso_Security_June2023/images/Untitled.webp?width=1431&amp;height=996&amp;name=Untitled.webp 1431w, https://permiso.io/hs-fs/hubfs/Permiso_Security_June2023/images/Untitled.webp?width=1908&amp;height=1328&amp;name=Untitled.webp 1908w, https://permiso.io/hs-fs/hubfs/Permiso_Security_June2023/images/Untitled.webp?width=2385&amp;height=1660&amp;name=Untitled.webp 2385w, https://permiso.io/hs-fs/hubfs/Permiso_Security_June2023/images/Untitled.webp?width=2862&amp;height=1992&amp;name=Untitled.webp 2862w" sizes="(max-width: 954px) 100vw, 954px">
<p>While this is the latest report, there have been others mentioned recently too. The Permiso p0 labs team is always on the lookout for new attacks focused on cloud, and dove in on this.</p>
<h1>Attack Flow</h1>
<p>The way this attack works is:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>The attacker buys ad space for certain search terms in google.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The Victim searches for a term like “AWS Console Login” in google. Google returns a list of results that include the malicious Google ad at the top.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The Victim clicks on the ad, thinking it is the legitimate AWS Console Login site. The fake site looks exactly like the normal AWS Console Login page, so the victim enters their credentials to logon.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>These credentials are saved to the attacker infrastructure, and the victim is redirected to the the legitimate AWS Console Logon page.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Now at the legitimate AWS Console Logon page, the victim suspects they must have entered their username and password incorrectly, types in again, and now enters the legitimate AWS Console, not realizing they gave their credentials to attackers along the way.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>The attacker now has the username and password, and assuming the victim doesn’t have MFA enabled they can immediately use the credentials. Also, since often users reuse passwords, they may try to logon to other services with the victims credentials such as their email or VPN.</p>
</li>
</ol>

<h1>Attacker Infrastructure</h1>
<p>In this attack, the threat actor is using the domain <code>aws1-us-west.info</code> , to masquerade as a legitimate AWS website. The domain was registered on January 24, 2023, last updated on January 29. 2023 and was registered via the registrar <code>SAV</code>.</p>
<p>This domain currently resolves to a Cloudflare IP Address <code>104.21.39.144</code>. Before the Cloudflare IP Address though, it resolved to its true IP Address, <code>5.8.33.175</code> . The true address is hosted in the ASN <code>202422</code> (Ghost LU). The system it is hosted on is a Ubuntu 2.17 box running Apache/2.4.41 with PHP on TCP/80. The system also has TCP/22 (SSH) enabled and TCP/25 (STMP) returning a banner of <code>220 vm6.template ESMTP Postfix (Ubuntu)</code>.</p>
<p>The domain and this system were created recently. The domain on January 24, 2023 and the system <code>5.8.33.175</code> first had internet exposed services starting on January 26, 2023.</p>
