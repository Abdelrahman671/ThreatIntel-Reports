                <h1>Malware-as-a-Service: Redline Stealer Variants Demonstrate a Low-Barrier-to-Entry Threat</h1>
                
                    <p class="c-intro mt-5">EclecticIQ analysts collected samples from a RedLine stealer spam campaign between April and August 2023. The campaign has been successful through shifting command and control among recently created domains hosted on IP addresses with legitimate traffic. Redline developers provide minor iterations to previous variants. Disrupting the malware’s living off the land tactics is the best practice for security teams.</p>

<h2 style="text-align: left;">Multiple New Campaigns in 2023 Demonstrate The Malware Family Has Been Redeveloped to Remain a Popular And Prominent Threat</h2>
<p style="text-align: left;">EclecticIQ analysts observe the malware family targeting financial information to be used for immediate gain as well as reconnaissance functions to perform initial information gathering and establish persistence. RedLine stealer is almost always accompanied by other malware; either preceded by a loader to install it or succeeded by further malware.&nbsp;<br><br>In the last major iteration of RedLine stealer in 2022, variants were almost always configured to rely on exploit kits for infection. At some point in 2022 infections saw a relative break in traffic as developers retooled, but in 2023 the malware has re-emerged as a prominent threat and is now reliant on other malware to act as the loader. [<a href="#a1" rel="noopener">1</a>] Most recently, Trend Micro identified a campaign that leveraged trojanized large-language model software to trick users into installing RedLine. [<a href="#a2" rel="noopener">2</a>]<br><br>Campaign variants emerge in VirusTotal starting the last week of April. [<a href="#a3" rel="noopener">3</a>] Samples very likely undergo initial testing in late April. This is supported by evidence from command and control infrastructure, discussed below. A small initial cluster of RedLine peaks approximately mid-July before tapering off significantly by the beginning of August. Sample volume then resumes in higher volume the second week of August.</p>
<!--more--><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://www.eclecticiq.com/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png" rel="noopener"><img src="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png?width=1526&amp;height=584&amp;name=Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png" width="1526" height="584" loading="lazy" alt="Redline Stealer_Figure 1" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 1526px;" srcset="https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png?width=763&amp;height=292&amp;name=Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png 763w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png?width=1526&amp;height=584&amp;name=Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png 1526w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png?width=2289&amp;height=876&amp;name=Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png 2289w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png?width=3052&amp;height=1168&amp;name=Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png 3052w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png?width=3815&amp;height=1460&amp;name=Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png 3815w, https://blog.eclecticiq.com/hs-fs/hubfs/_blogs/corporate-blog/2023/Redline%20Stealer/Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png?width=4578&amp;height=1752&amp;name=Redline%20Stealer_Figure%201.png 4578w" sizes="(max-width: 1526px) 100vw, 1526px"></a><br>Figure 1 - Redline sample volumes collected through VirusTotal<br><em>(click on image to open in separate tab).</em></p>
<h2 style="text-align: left;">&nbsp;<br>WMI Abuse Continues to Provide Core Information Gathering Capabilities&nbsp;</h2>
<p><span>Notable capabilities within the Kill-Chain that are shared by these variants include:<br><br>- &nbsp; &nbsp;Code obfuscation using XOR and RC4 algorithms and future timestamps to bypass security systems.<br>- &nbsp; &nbsp;The use of registry keys via modification to establish persistence.&nbsp;<br>- &nbsp; &nbsp;WMI to drive local system queries and fingerprinting.<br>- &nbsp; &nbsp;The ability to delete files the malware creates to help conceal cyberattacks.<br><br>The first three capabilities were present in previous reporting by security researchers. [<a href="#a4" rel="noopener">4</a>] New variants use a new code obfuscation tactic; using XOR and RC4 encryption for payloads. PowerShell modules present in previous campaigns are absent in these versions of Redline stealer. [<a href="#a3" rel="noopener">3</a>, <a href="#a4" rel="noopener">4</a>]<br></span></p>

<h2><span>Samples Share Meta Data Information</span></h2>
<p><span>A notable feature shared by all variants identified in this campaign includes the same copyright, description, and comments within their metadata. The copyright is linked to an actual copyright associated to a Malaysian biotech company that recently joined a large consortium of regional biotech partners. <h2><span>Conclusion and Mitigation</span></h2>
<p><span>Redline stealer, a popular threat to a variety of organizations, continues to make minor changes to remain a successful and prominent low-barrier-to-entry threat. In lieu of major development changes, latest variants exclude PowerShell, possibly to reduce the malware’s footprint and automate via social media botnets. Command and control relies on new rapidly changing infrastructure that is difficult to mitigate by blocking indicators of compromise. Focusing on core malware development patterns - the use of WMI in this case - and successfully blocking malicious attempts through application whitelisting and process monitoring provide the best way to apply security resources to this and other malware.</span></p>
