  <p style="text-align: left;"><i>NOTE: The term &quot;Zerobot&quot; in this article refers to a specific malware variant. It is not in any way associated with zerobot.ai, an organization that offers a verbal chatbot service.</i></p>
<p style="text-align: left;">In November, <a href="https://www.fortinet.com/fortiguard/labs?utm_source=blog&amp;utm_campaign=fortiguardlabs">FortiGuard Labs</a> observed a unique botnet written in the Go language being distributed through <a title="IoT vulnerabilities" href="https://www.fortinet.com/resources/cyberglossary/iot-security?utm_source=blog&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=blog-iot-security">IoT vulnerabilities</a>. This <a href="https://www.fortinet.com/resources/cyberglossary/what-is-botnet?utm_source=pr&amp;utm_medium=pr&amp;utm_campaign=cyberglossary%2Fwhat-is-botnet">botnet</a>, known as Zerobot, contains several modules, including self-replication, attacks for different protocols, and self-propagation. It also communicates with its command-and-control server using the WebSocket protocol. Based on some IPS signatures trigger count (shown in Figure 1), this campaign started its distribution of the current version sometime after mid-November.</p>
<p style="text-align: left;margin-left: 40.0px;"><b>Affected platforms: </b>Linux<br />
<b>Impacted parties:</b> Any organization<br />
<b>Impact: </b>Remote attackers gain control of the vulnerable systems<br />
<b>Severity level: </b>Critical</p>
<p>This article details how this malware leverages vulnerabilities and examines its behavior once inside an infected device.</p>

  <h2>Conclusion</h2>
<p>Zerobot is a new botnet written in the Go programming language. It communicates via the WebSocket protocol. It first appeared on November 18 and is designed to target a variety of vulnerabilities. Within a very short time, it was updated with string obfuscation, a copy file module, and a propagation exploit module that make it harder to detect and gives it a higher capability to infect more devices. Users should be aware of this new threat, patch any affected systems listed in Figure 13 running on their network, and actively apply patches as they become available.</p>
