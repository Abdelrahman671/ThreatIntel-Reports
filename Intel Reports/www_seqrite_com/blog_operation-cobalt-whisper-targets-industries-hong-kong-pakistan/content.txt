<h2><strong><em>Introduction</em></strong></h2>
<p>SEQRITE Labs APT-Team has recently uncovered a campaign targeting various industries such as the Defense Sector in Pakistan and predominantly researchers from Hong Kong. Tracked as <strong>Operation Cobalt Whisper</strong>, the entire campaign heavily leverages the use of a post-exploitation tool Cobalt Strike, which is deployed using obfuscated VBScript. A total of 20 infection chains have been identified so far along with additional individual samples, where 18 of them target Hong Kong and two target Pakistan where over 30 decoy files have been identified.</p>
<p>In this blog, we will explore the technical details of one of the campaigns we encountered during our initial analysis and examine the various stages of the infection chain, starting with a deep dive into the decoy documents. We will then look into the common Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs), such as the use of malicious VBScript and LNK payloads employed by this threat actor across most campaigns. These methods facilitate the in-memory execution of the Cobalt Strike implant, which is delivered alongside these lures in an archive file.</p>
<h2><strong><em>Key Targets</em></strong></h2>
<h4><strong>Industries Affected</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Defense Industry</li>
<li>Electrotechnical Engineering</li>
<li>Energy (Hydropower, Renewable Energy)</li>
<li>Civil Aviation</li>
<li>Environmental Engineering</li>
<li>Academia and Research Institutions</li>
<li>Medical Science Institutions.</li>
<li>Cybersecurity Researchers.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Geographical Focus</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Hong Kong</li>
<li>Pakistan</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong><em>Initial Findings</em></strong></h2>
<p>Recently, on 9th of September 2024, our team found a malicious RAR archive, which surfaced both on various sources like <a href="https://www.virustotal.com/gui/file/3a00a211df2320b8a2f77e10700db5aa0e8e3a1fde93e0958901e532248dbd4d" data-wpel-link="external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">VirusTotal</a>, where the RAR has been used as preliminary source of infection, containing multiple decoys with PDF and LNK extensions and a final Cobalt Strike implant. This was also found by other <a href="https://x.com/strikereadylabs/status/1833171964636147850?s=46" data-wpel-link="external" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">threat researchers</a> as well.</p>
<p>The RAR archive contains a malicious LNK named, “<em>附件1</em><em>：《2024</em><em>年度中国</em><em>电工技术学会科学技术奖推荐提名书》（技术发明奖和科技进步奖）填报说明(2024</em><em>年8</em><em>月新版).pdf.lnk</em>”, which is responsible for execution of another malicious batch script named as <em>O365.vbs</em>. The VBScript is mostly responsible for decoding the Cobalt Strike beacon on disk, known as <em>cache.bak</em>, this is further executed, which connects back to the command-and-control server. Let us look into the two decoy documents.</p>
<h2><strong><em>Infection Chain</em></strong></h2>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-6459 size-large" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-1-1024x532.png" alt="Infection Chain" width="768" height="399" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-1-1024x532.png 1024w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-1-300x156.png 300w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-1-768x399.png 768w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-1.png 1144w" sizes="(max-width: 768px) 100vw, 768px" /></p>
<h3><strong><em>Looking into the decoy-document &#8211; I</em></strong></h3>
<p>Upon looking into the first decoy document known as subscription.db, it turns out that this lure is linked to the Electronic Society of China, focused on nominations for the award ceremony.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-6460 size-full" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-2.png" alt="Electronic Society of China" width="740" height="650" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-2.png 740w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-2-300x264.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 740px) 100vw, 740px" /></p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6461" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-3.png" alt="" width="628" height="852" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-3.png 628w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-3-221x300.png 221w" sizes="(max-width: 628px) 100vw, 628px" /></p>
<p>The contents and the entire decoy confirm that this PDF serves as a comprehensive guideline for the application and nomination process for the China Electrical Engineering Society Science and Technology Award. It outlines the necessary documentation, structure, and specific requirements for submitting a project, including details on technological innovations, evaluations, application promotion, and economic and social benefits.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6462" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-4.png" alt="" width="574" height="727" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-4.png 574w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-4-237x300.png 237w" sizes="(max-width: 574px) 100vw, 574px" /></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The decoy also mentions some interesting guidelines for the current project for nomination, in case it has received other awards too.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-6463" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-5.png" alt="" width="661" height="519" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-5.png 661w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-5-300x236.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 661px) 100vw, 661px" /></p>

<h2><strong><em>Technical Analysis</em></strong></h2>
<p>We will divide our analysis into two main sections. <strong>First</strong>, we will examine the malicious LNK and VBScript components utilized by the threat actor across the campaigns. <strong>Second</strong>, we will delve into the malicious Cobalt Strike implant and extract its configuration details.</p>
<p>Our research has uncovered more than <strong>18 distinct infection chains</strong> linked to this threat actor. In this blog, we will focus specifically on one of these campaigns that targets electrotechnical researchers in Hong Kong. This detailed exploration will shed light on the methodologies employed and provide insights into the threat actor&#8217;s tactics within this particular campaign.</p>
<h3><strong><em>Stage 1 &#8211; Malicious LNK Script &amp; VBScript</em></strong></h3>
<p>The RAR contains an LNK known as 附件1：《2024年度中国电工技术学会科学技术奖推荐提名书》（技术发明奖和科技进步奖）填报说明(2024年8月新版).pdf.lnk , upon exploring the it became quite evident that the sole purpose of the LNK is just to run the malicious VBScript O365.vbs using a Windows Utility known as wscript.exe .</p>

<h2><strong><em>Hunting and Infrastructure</em></strong></h2>
<p>In this section, we will discuss how we uncovered additional campaigns by leveraging a simple artifact: the threat actor&#8217;s consistent use of the name <strong>ImeBroker.exe</strong> for different Cobalt Strike implants across all campaigns. Originally, <strong>ImeBroker.exe</strong> is a legitimate Windows utility related to language input, specifically managing Input Method Editors (IME) that allow users to type in languages with complex scripts.</p>
<p>While reverse-engineering the implant, we discovered a suspicious code segment. Using this segment we identified a total of <strong>14 samples</strong> with similar names and identical binary sizes, all deployed by the threat actor as Cobalt Strike beacons with compilation timestamp “Compilation Timestamp: 2015-07-10 03:27:31“ and delivered via different lures. Additionally, going by configurations, we found <strong>21 more Cobalt Strike beacons</strong> with similar configurations. This pattern highlights the threat actor&#8217;s widespread use of consistent naming and configurations across multiple campaigns.</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-6474 size-large" src="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-16-1024x490.png" alt="" width="768" height="368" srcset="https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-16-1024x490.png 1024w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-16-300x143.png 300w, https://www.seqrite.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/image-16-768x367.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 768px) 100vw, 768px" /></p>
