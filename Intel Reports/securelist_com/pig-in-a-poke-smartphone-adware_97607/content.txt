													<p>Our support team continues to receive more and more requests from users complaining about intrusive ads on their smartphones from unknown sources. In some cases, the solution is <a href="https://securelist.com/in-app-advertising-in-android/97065/">quite</a> <a href="https://securelist.com/dropper-in-google-play/92496/">simple</a>. In others, the task is far harder: the <a href="https://encyclopedia.kaspersky.com/glossary/adware/?utm_source=securelist&amp;utm_medium=blog&amp;utm_campaign=termin-explanation">adware</a> plants itself in the system partition, and trying to get rid of it can lead to device failure. In addition, ads can be embedded in undeletable system apps and libraries at the code level. According to our data, 14.8% of all users attacked by malware or adware in the past year suffered an infection of the system partition.</p>
<p>Why is that? We observe two main strategies for introducing undeletable adware onto a device:</p>
<ul>
<li>The malware gains root access on the device and <a href="https://securelist.com/unkillable-xhelper-and-a-trojan-matryoshka/96487/">installs</a> adware in the system partition.</li>
<li>The code for displaying ads (or its loader) gets into the firmware of the device even before it ends up in the hands of the consumer.</li>
</ul>
<p>The Android security model assumes that an antivirus is a normal app, and according to this concept, it physically can not do anything with adware or malware in system directories. This makes adware a problem. The cybercriminals behind it stop at nothing that will earn them money from advertising (or rather, the forced installation of apps). As a result, malware can end up on the user&#8217;s device, such as <a href="https://securelist.com/cookiethief/96332/">CookieStealer</a>.</p>
<input type="hidden" class="category_for_banner" data-type="posts" value="encyclopedia-glossary" />
<p>As a rule, 1–5% of the total number of users of our security solutions encounter this adware (depending on the particular device brand). In the main, these are owners of smartphones and tablets of certain brands in the lower price segment. However, for some popular vendors offering low-cost devices, this figure can reach up to 27%.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30143828/sl_pre-installed_ads_01.png" class="magnificImage"><img decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97609" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30143828/sl_pre-installed_ads_01.png" alt="" width="1013" height="640" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30143828/sl_pre-installed_ads_01.png 1013w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30143828/sl_pre-installed_ads_01-300x190.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30143828/sl_pre-installed_ads_01-768x485.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1013px) 100vw, 1013px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Users who encountered malware or adware in the system partition as a percentage of the total number of Kaspersky users in the country, May 2019 — May 2020</em></strong></p>
<h2 id="whos-there">Who&#8217;s there?</h2>
<p>Among the most common types of malware installed in the system partition of smartphones are the <a href="https://android-developers.googleblog.com/2017/03/detecting-and-eliminating-chamois-fraud.html">Lezok</a> and <a href="https://news.drweb.com/show/?lng=en&amp;i=11749">Triada</a> Trojans. The latter is notable for its ad code embedded not just anywhere, but directly in libandroid_runtime — a key library used by almost all apps on the device. Although these threats were identified several years ago, users continue to run into them.</p>
<p>But Lezok and Triada are just the tip of the cyber iceberg. Below, we examine what else users face today and which system apps were found to contain &#8220;additional&#8221; code.</p>
<h3 id="trojan-dropper-androidos-agent-pe">Trojan-Dropper.AndroidOS.Agent.pe</h3>

<p>The second module is a backdoor allowing remote control of the smartphone. Its capabilities include installing, uninstalling, and running apps, which can be used to covertly install both legitimate and malicious apps, depending on the intruder&#8217;s goals.</p>

<p>Unlike the previous Trojans, Necro.d is a native library located in the system directory. Its launch mechanism is built into another system library, libandroid_servers.so, which handles the operation of Android services.</p>

<h3 id="trojan-dropper-androidos-virtualinst-c">Trojan-Dropper.AndroidOS.Virtualinst.c</h3>
<p>This dropper can take cover in the Theme app (com.nbc.willcloud.themestore). Its features are not original: downloading, installing, and running apps without the user&#8217;s knowledge.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145410/sl_pre-installed_ads_20.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97628" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145410/sl_pre-installed_ads_20.png" alt="" width="713" height="305" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145410/sl_pre-installed_ads_20.png 713w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145410/sl_pre-installed_ads_20-300x128.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 713px) 100vw, 713px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Trojan-Dropper.AndroidOS.Virtualinst.c installing apps</em></strong></p>
<h3 id="adware-androidos-secretad-c">AdWare.AndroidOS.Secretad.c</h3>
<p>Another piece of adware that we discovered was built into the wallpaper catalog app. The payload of Secretad.c is contained in the file kgallery.c1ass. It gets unpacked and launched, for example, when the device is unlocked on or apps are installed:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145448/sl_pre-installed_ads_21.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97629" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145448/sl_pre-installed_ads_21.png" alt="" width="608" height="218" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145448/sl_pre-installed_ads_21.png 608w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145448/sl_pre-installed_ads_21-300x108.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 608px) 100vw, 608px" /></a></p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145501/sl_pre-installed_ads_22.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97630" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145501/sl_pre-installed_ads_22.png" alt="" width="709" height="153" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145501/sl_pre-installed_ads_22.png 709w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145501/sl_pre-installed_ads_22-300x65.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 709px) 100vw, 709px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Unpacking the payload</em></strong></p>
<p>Secretad.c can display ads in full screen mode, open pages in the browser, or launch the advertised app itself. Like many other adware programs, Secretad.c can install apps without the user knowing about it.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145533/sl_pre-installed_ads_23.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97631" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145533/sl_pre-installed_ads_23.png" alt="" width="675" height="254" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145533/sl_pre-installed_ads_23.png 675w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145533/sl_pre-installed_ads_23-300x113.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 675px) 100vw, 675px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Secretly installing apps</em></strong></p>
<p>The app also has one more ad module:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145601/sl_pre-installed_ads_24.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97632" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145601/sl_pre-installed_ads_24.png" alt="" width="900" height="273" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145601/sl_pre-installed_ads_24.png 900w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145601/sl_pre-installed_ads_24-300x91.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145601/sl_pre-installed_ads_24-768x233.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 900px) 100vw, 900px" /></a></p>
<p>Its payload is encrypted in the file <strong>assets/1498203975110.dat</strong>. Among other things, it can cause the advertised app&#8217;s page on Google Play to unexpectedly open, installed apps to start, or the browser to open.</p>
<h2 id="adware-from-the-manufacturer">Adware from the manufacturer</h2>
<p>Some smartphones contain adware modules pre-installed by the manufacturers themselves. A few vendors openly admit to embedding adware under the hood of their smartphones; some allow it to be <a href="https://c.realme.com/in/post-details/1211549405472096256">disabled</a>, while others do not, describing it as part of their <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2018/9/19/17877970/xiaomi-ads-settings-menu-android-phones">business model</a> to reduce the cost of the device for the end user.</p>
<p>The user generally has no choice between buying the device at the full price, or a little cheaper with lifetime advertising. What&#8217;s more, we did not find any electronics store offering a clear warning to users that they would be forced to watch ads. In other words, buyers might not suspect that they are spending their cash on a pocket-sized billboard.</p>
<h3 id="meizu">Meizu</h3>
<p>Meizu devices make no secret that they display ads in apps. The advertising is fairly unobtrusive, and you can even turn it off in the settings. However, in the preinstalled AppStore app (c4296581148a1a1a008f233d75f71821), we uncovered hidden adware able to load under the radar and display itself in invisible windows (such method is usually used to boost the number of showings), which eats up data and battery power:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145627/sl_pre-installed_ads_25.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97633" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145627/sl_pre-installed_ads_25.png" alt="" width="623" height="249" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145627/sl_pre-installed_ads_25.png 623w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145627/sl_pre-installed_ads_25-300x120.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 623px) 100vw, 623px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Loading ads on the quiet</em></strong></p>
<p>But that&#8217;s not all. The app can download and execute third-party JavaScript code:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145706/sl_pre-installed_ads_26.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97634" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145706/sl_pre-installed_ads_26.png" alt="" width="460" height="318" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145706/sl_pre-installed_ads_26.png 460w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145706/sl_pre-installed_ads_26-300x207.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 460px) 100vw, 460px" /></a><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145739/sl_pre-installed_ads_27.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97635" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145739/sl_pre-installed_ads_27.png" alt="" width="599" height="199" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145739/sl_pre-installed_ads_27.png 599w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145739/sl_pre-installed_ads_27-300x100.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 599px) 100vw, 599px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Downloading and executing JS code</em></strong></p>
<p>Furthermore, the pre-installed AppStore app can mute the sound, access text messages, and cut and paste their contents into loaded pages.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145813/sl_pre-installed_ads_28.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97636" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145813/sl_pre-installed_ads_28.png" alt="" width="623" height="465" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145813/sl_pre-installed_ads_28.png 623w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145813/sl_pre-installed_ads_28-300x224.png 300w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145813/sl_pre-installed_ads_28-200x150.png 200w" sizes="(max-width: 623px) 100vw, 623px" /></a><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145849/sl_pre-installed_ads_29.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97637" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145849/sl_pre-installed_ads_29.png" alt="" width="623" height="402" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145849/sl_pre-installed_ads_29.png 623w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145849/sl_pre-installed_ads_29-300x194.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 623px) 100vw, 623px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Reading text messages and using their contents in a web page</em></strong></p>
<p>This approach is often used in outright malicious apps which, unbeknown to the user, sign up to paid subscriptions. One can only trust in the decency of the adware controllers, and hope that third parties do not gain access to it.</p>
<p>But AppStore is not the only suspicious app on Meizu devices. In Meizu Music (com.meizu.media.music 19e481d60c139af3d9881927a213ed88), we found an encrypted executable file used to download and execute a certain Ginkgo SDK:</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145921/sl_pre-installed_ads_30.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97638" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145921/sl_pre-installed_ads_30.png" alt="" width="624" height="117" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145921/sl_pre-installed_ads_30.png 624w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145921/sl_pre-installed_ads_30-300x56.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 624px) 100vw, 624px" /></a><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145954/sl_pre-installed_ads_31.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97639" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145954/sl_pre-installed_ads_31.png" alt="" width="623" height="426" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145954/sl_pre-installed_ads_31.png 623w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30145954/sl_pre-installed_ads_31-300x205.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 623px) 100vw, 623px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Downloading Ginkgo SDK</em></strong></p>
<p>What this SDK does can only be guessed at: not all Meizu devices download it, and we were unable to get hold of the latest version. However, the versions of Ginkgo SDK that we obtained from other sources display ads and install apps without the user&#8217;s knowledge.</p>
<p>The com.vlife.mxlock.wallpaper app (04fe069d7d638d55c796d7ec7ed794a6) also contains an encrypted executable file, and basically offers standard functions for gray-market adware modules, including the ability to install apps on the sly.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150033/sl_pre-installed_ads_32.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97640" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150033/sl_pre-installed_ads_32.png" alt="" width="623" height="137" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150033/sl_pre-installed_ads_32.png 623w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150033/sl_pre-installed_ads_32-300x66.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 623px) 100vw, 623px" /></a></p>
<p style="text-align: center"><strong><em>Secretly installing apps</em></strong></p>
<p>We contacted Meizu to report our findings, but did not receive a response.</p>
<h3 id="fotabinder">Fotabinder</h3>
<p>In addition to dubious files in devices from particular vendors, we found a problem affecting a huge number of smartphones. The memory of many devices contains the file /bin/fotabinder (<a href="https://opentip.kaspersky.com/3fdd84b7136d5871afd170ab6dfde6ca/">3fdd84b7136d5871afd170ab6dfde6ca</a>), which can download files to user devices and execute code on them received from one of the following remote servers: adsunflower[.]com, adfuture[.]cn, or mayitek[.]com.</p>
<p><a href="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150123/sl_pre-installed_ads_33.png" class="magnificImage"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-97641" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150123/sl_pre-installed_ads_33.png" alt="" width="367" height="529" srcset="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150123/sl_pre-installed_ads_33.png 367w, https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2020/06/30150123/sl_pre-installed_ads_33-208x300.png 208w" sizes="(max-width: 367px) 100vw, 367px" /></a></p>
<p>This file is most likely part of the update or testing system, but the encrypted C&amp;C addresses and functions providing remote access to the device raise a red flag.</p>
<h2 id="what-does-it-all-mean">What does it all mean?</h2>
<p>The examples in our investigation show that the focus of some mobile device suppliers is on maximizing profits through all kinds of advertising tools, even if those tools cause inconvenience to the device owners. If advertising networks are ready to pay for views, clicks, and installations regardless of their source, it makes sense to embed ad modules into devices to increase the profit from each device sold.</p>
<p>Unfortunately, if a user purchases a device with such pre-installed advertising, it is often impossible to remove it without risking damage to the system.</p>
<p>In this case, all hopes rest on enthusiasts who are busy creating alternative firmware for devices. But it&#8217;s important to understand that reflashing can void the warranty and even damage the device.</p>
<p>As for ad modules have not yet done anything malicious, the user can only hope that the developers do not tack on ads from a malicious partner network <a href="https://securelist.com/dropper-in-google-play/92496/">without even realizing it themselves</a>.</p>
