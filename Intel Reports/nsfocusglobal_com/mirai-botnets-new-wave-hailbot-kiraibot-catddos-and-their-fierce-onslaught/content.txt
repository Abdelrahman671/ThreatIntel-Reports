<h2><span id="I_Abstract">I. Abstract</span></h2>



<p>In September 2023, NSFOCUS global threat hunting system monitored several new botnet variant families developed based on Mirai, among which hailBot, kiraiBot and catDDoS are the most active, are accelerating their spread, and are widely deployed, which has constituted a considerable threat. Through this article, we will disclose the technical details of these three new Mirai variants and the data monitored by the global threat hunting system.<br>More and more botnet Trojan horses have been developed based on Mirai in recent years. A large number of attackers carry out secondary development based on Mirai source code. These newly developed Trojan horses either introduce new encryption algorithms to hide critical information or better hide themselves by modifying the go-live process and designing more covert communication methods. Although the open source code has gradually lowered the threshold for building a botnet, the attacker’s attempt to enhance invisibility has not been weakened.</p>



<h2><span id="II_Mirais_New_Variants_Are_Coming">II. Mirai’s New Variants Are Coming</span></h2>



<h3><span id="1_hailBot">(1) hailBot</span></h3>



<p>The hailBot is developed based on Mirai source code, and its name is derived from the string information “hail china mainland” output after running. This unusual expression cannot escape the suspicion of being planted and framed.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><a href="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.1-Information-output-after-hailBot-runs.png"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.1-Information-output-after-hailBot-runs.png" alt="" class="wp-image-26341" width="360" height="79" srcset="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.1-Information-output-after-hailBot-runs.png 255w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.1-Information-output-after-hailBot-runs-150x33.png 150w" sizes="(max-width: 360px) 100vw, 360px"></a><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Figure 2.1 &nbsp;Information output after hailBot runs</figcaption></figure></div>


<p></p>



<p>Regarding hailBot traffic, the go-live data packet of the original Mirai has been modified.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><a href="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.2-HailBot-go-live-data-packet.png"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.2-HailBot-go-live-data-packet.png" alt="" class="wp-image-26343" width="414" height="155" srcset="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.2-HailBot-go-live-data-packet.png 380w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.2-HailBot-go-live-data-packet-300x112.png 300w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.2-HailBot-go-live-data-packet-332x124.png 332w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.2-HailBot-go-live-data-packet-150x56.png 150w" sizes="(max-width: 414px) 100vw, 414px"></a><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Figure 2.2 &nbsp;HailBot go-live data packet</figcaption></figure></div>


<p></p>



<p>The flow generated is as follows:</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full"><a href="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.3-HailBot-go-live-traffic.png"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" width="599" height="171" src="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.3-HailBot-go-live-traffic.png" alt="" class="wp-image-26345" srcset="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.3-HailBot-go-live-traffic.png 599w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.3-HailBot-go-live-traffic-300x86.png 300w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.3-HailBot-go-live-traffic-332x95.png 332w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.3-HailBot-go-live-traffic-150x43.png 150w" sizes="(max-width: 599px) 100vw, 599px"></a><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Figure 2.3 &nbsp;HailBot go-live traffic</figcaption></figure></div>


<p></p>



<p>The current version of hailBot attack mode supports four DDoS attack methods based on TCP and UDP protocols.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">
<figure class="aligncenter size-full is-resized"><a href="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot.png"><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" src="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot.png" alt="" class="wp-image-26347" width="524" height="526" srcset="https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot.png 524w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot-300x300.png 300w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot-150x151.png 150w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot-50x50.png 50w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot-179x180.png 179w, https://nsfocusglobal.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Figure-2.4-Attack-vectors-with-hailBot-96x96.png 96w" sizes="(max-width: 524px) 100vw, 524px"></a><figcaption class="wp-element-caption">Figure 2.4 &nbsp;Attack methods with hailBot</figcaption></figure></div>


<p></p>



<p>The hailBot spreads through vulnerability exploitation and weak password scanning &amp; brute force. The current version has only one built-in vulnerability, CVE-2017-17215 , but it does not exclude the possibility of separating the vulnerability propagation module from the sample.</p>

<p></p>



<p>After further investigation into the asset information of the hailBot controller, we discovered that their C&amp;C infrastructure, with IP addresses 5.181.80.120 and 5.181.80.115, had previously disseminated multiple bait documents carrying the CVE-2017-11882 vulnerability. These bait documents bore file names such as ‘INVOICE.xlsx’, ‘Product_requetslist.xlsx’, ‘CIF WMS REF NO 451RFQ ARN-DT-2021-06-29.xlsx’, and others, intending to lure victims into opening the documents to trigger the vulnerability. As a result, they downloaded and executed various commercial espionage-oriented banking trojans, including Lokibot and Formbook.</p>



<p>Based on this, we believe that the hailBot controller is a systematically organized multi-faceted attacker. Initially, their primary targets were financial and trade institutions. However, in the latter half of 2022, they shifted their focus to IoT platforms.</p>

<h2><span id="III_Conclusion">III. Conclusion</span></h2>



<p>In recent years, we have observed the emergence of numerous new families of botnets, with most of them appearing briefly and then disappearing. Most of these botnets are derived from integrating Mirai and Gafgyt codes. Some attackers only slightly modified the go-live process of the open source Trojan horse and publicized the establishment of a new botnet family to attract buyers. However, some attackers insist on the “pursuit” of technology. Although they also reuse open-source code, they spend some time and energy on this basis.</p>



<p>Attackers’ redesign of open-source botnet trojans primarily focuses on two dimensions. First, they aim to enhance the stealthiness on the file side, such as introducing new encryption algorithms to conceal critical information or employing anti-analysis techniques. Second, they manipulate the communication aspect, employing tactics like binding malicious IPs to benign domains using ClouDNS or incorporating OpenNIC domains to evade detection. However, it’s noteworthy that the efforts on the communication side tend to be more intricate and intriguing.</p>
