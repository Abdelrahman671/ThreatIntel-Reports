    <p>Following last summer’s <a href="/blog/security/record-breaking-ddos-attack-in-europe" target="_self">record-setting attacks</a> on Europe, the distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) threat landscape continues to morph and intensify. </p>
<p>On February 23, 2023, at 10:22 UTC, Akamai mitigated the largest <a href="/glossary/what-is-ddos" target="_self">DDoS attack</a> ever launched against a <a href="/glossary/what-is-ransomware" target="_self">Prolexic</a> customer based in Asia-Pacific (APAC), with attack traffic peaking at 900.1 gigabits per second and 158.2 million packets per second. </p>
<p>In line with current trends, the attack was intense and short-lived, with most attack traffic bursting during the peak minute of the attack. Traffic patterns returned to normal after only a few minutes (Figure 1).</p>

    <p>The attack was distributed across our scrubbing network, but was most heavily sourced from APAC (Figure 2). No individual scrubbing center saw more than 12% of the total traffic, with the top locations being Hong Kong, Tokyo, São Paulo, Singapore, and Osaka. Forty-eight percent of traffic was in-region. All 26 of our <a href="/blog/news/akamais-prolexic-launches-sixth-generation-ddos-platform-upgrade" target="_self">ever-expanding fleet of scrubbing centers</a> saw some amount of attack traffic.</p>

</div>
<div class="text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12">
<div data-cmp-is="text" class="cmp-text" data-cmp-data-layer="{&#34;text-9b6c0bdf6a&#34;:{&#34;@type&#34;:&#34;akamai/components/text&#34;,&#34;repo:modifyDate&#34;:&#34;2023-03-08T17:21:53Z&#34;,&#34;xdm:text&#34;:&#34;&lt;ul>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>Immediately review and implement Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) recommendations.&amp;nbsp;&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>Review critical subnets and IP spaces, and ensure that they have mitigation controls in place.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>Deploy DDoS security controls in an always-on mitigation posture as a first layer of defense to avoid an emergency integration scenario and to reduce the burden on incident responders.     <ul>
<li><p>Immediately review and implement Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) recommendations. </p>
</li>
<li><p>Review critical subnets and IP spaces, and ensure that they have mitigation controls in place.</p>
</li>
<li><p>Deploy DDoS security controls in an always-on mitigation posture as a first layer of defense to avoid an emergency integration scenario and to reduce the burden on incident responders. If you don’t have a trusted and proven cloud-based provider, get one now. </p>
</li>
<li><p>Proactively pull together a crisis response team and ensure runbooks and incident response plans are up-to-date: </p>

</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
