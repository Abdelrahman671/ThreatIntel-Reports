														<h1 class="c-article__title">Android Trojan Found in Targeted Attack</h1>							<div class="c-article__info">
																<p class="c-article__headline u-hidden u-block@md">
									<a href="https://securelist.com/category/incidents/" class="c-tag c-tag--primary">Incidents</a>
								</p>
																<p class="u-uppercase"><time datetime="2013-03-26T16:14:19+00:00">26 Mar 2013</time></p>
								<p class="c-article__reading u-ml-auto@md">
									<svg class="o-icon o-svg-icon"><use xmlns:xlink="http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink" xlink:href="https://securelist.com/wp-content/themes/securelist2020/assets/sprite/icons.svg#icon-hourglass"></use></svg> <span class="js-reading-time"></span> minute read								</p>

									<div class="o-col c-article__content js-article-body">
										<div class="js-reading-wrapper">
											<figure class="c-article__figure u-hidden u-block@md">
												<img width="1200" height="600" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2018/11/11130521/securelist_abs_11-1200x600.jpg" class="attachment-securelist-2020-thumbnail-large size-securelist-2020-thumbnail-large wp-post-image" alt="" decoding="async" data-src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2018/11/11130521/securelist_abs_11-1200x600.jpg" data-srcset="" srcset="" />											</figure>

											<div class="c-article__authors u-hidden u-block@md">
												<p class="c-block__title">Authors</p>
												<ul class="c-list-authors">
																<li>
			<a  href="https://securelist.com/author/kurtb/" >
								<img alt='' src='https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2023/03/21155639/Kurt_Baumgartner_Securelist_2023-30x30.jpg' srcset='https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2023/03/21155639/Kurt_Baumgartner_Securelist_2023-60x60.jpg 2x' class='avatar avatar-30 photo' height='30' width='30' decoding='async'/>										<span>Kurt Baumgartner</span></a>
		</li>
			<li>
			<a  href="https://securelist.com/author/denis/" >
								<img alt='' src='https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/103/2021/06/10095238/avatar_def-30x30.png' srcset='https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/103/2021/06/10095238/avatar_def-60x60.png 2x' class='avatar avatar-30 photo' height='30' width='30' loading='lazy' decoding='async'/>										<span>Denis Maslennikov</span></a>

											<div class="js-reading-content">
												<div class="c-wysiwyg">
													<p>In the past, we&#8217;ve seen targeted attacks against Tibetan and Uyghur activists on Windows and Mac OS X platforms. We&#8217;ve documented several interesting attacks (<a href="https://securelist.com/a-gift-for-dalai-lamas-birthday/33351/">A Gift for Dalai Lamas Birthday</a> and <a href="https://securelist.com/cyber-attacks-against-uyghur-mac-os-x-users-intensify/64259/">Cyber Attacks Against Uyghur Mac OS X Users Intensify</a>) which used ZIP files as well as DOC, XLS and PDF documents rigged with exploits.</p>
<p>Several days ago, the e-mail account of a high-profile Tibetan activist was hacked and used to send targeted attacks to other activists and human rights advocates. Perhaps the most interesting part is that the attack e-mails had an APK attachment &#8211; a malicious program for Android.</p>
<p><strong>The attack</strong></p>
<p>On March 24th, 2013, the e-mail account of a high-profile Tibetan activist was hacked and used to send spear phishing e-mails to their contact list. This is what the spear phishing e-mail looked like:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214248/208194187.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>In regards to the message text above, multiple activist groups have recently organized a human rights conference event in Geneva. We&#8217;ve noticed an increase in the number of attacks using this event as a lure. Here&#8217;s another example of such an attack hitting Windows users:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214244/208194196.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>Going back to the Android Package (APK) file was attached to the e-mail, this is pushing an Android application named &#8220;WUC&#8217;s Conference.apk&#8221;.</p>
<p>This malicious APK is 334326 bytes file, MD5: 0b8806b38b52bebfe39ff585639e2ea2 and is detected by Kaspersky Lab products as &#8220;Backdoor.AndroidOS.Chuli.a&#8221;.</p>
<p>After the installation, an application named &#8220;Conference&#8221; appears on the desktop:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214239/208194194.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>If the victim launches this app, he will see text which &#8220;enlightens&#8221; the information about the upcoming event:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214235/208194195.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>The full text reads follows. Notice notice the use of the mistaken &#8220;Word&#8221; instead of &#8220;World&#8221;:</p>
<p><i>&#8220;On behalf of all at the Word Uyghur Congress (WUC), the Unrepresented Nations and Peoples Organization (UNPO) and the Society for Threatened Peoples (STP), Human Rights in China: Implications for East Turkestan, Tibet and Southern Mongolia</i></p>
<p>In what was an unprecedented coming-together of leading Uyghur, Mongolian, Tibetan and Chinese activists, as well as other leading international experts, we were greatly humbled by the great enthusiasm, contribution and desire from all in attendance to make this occasion something meaningful, the outcome of which produced some concrete, action-orientated solutions to our shared grievances. We are especially delighted about the platform and programme of work established in the declaration of the conference, upon which we sincerely hope will be built a strong and resolute working relationship on our shared goals for the future. With this in mind,we thoroughly look forward to working with you on these matters.</p>
<p>Dolkun lsa</p>
<p>Chairman of the Executive Committee</p>
<p>Word Uyghur Congress&#8221;</p>
<p>While the victim reads this fake message, the malware secretly reports the infection to a command-and-control server. After that, it begins to harvest information stored on the device. The stolen data includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>Contacts (stored both on the phone and the SIM card).</li>
<li>Call logs.</li>
<li>SMS messages.</li>
<li>Geo-location.</li>
<li>Phone data (phone number, OS version, phone model, SDK version).</li>
</ul>
<p>It is important to note that the data won&#8217;t be uploaded to C&amp;C server automatically. The Trojan waits for incoming SMS messages (the &#8220;alarmReceiver.class&#8221;) and checks whether these messages contain one of the following commands: &#8220;sms&#8221;, &#8220;contact&#8221;, &#8220;location&#8221;, &#8220;other&#8221;. If one these commands is found, then the malware will encode the stolen data with Base64 and upload it to the command and control server. The C2 URL is:</p>
<p align="center"><i>hxxp://64.78.161.133/*victims&#8217;s_cell_phone_number*/process.php</i></p>
<p>In addition to this, the malware also reports to another script, &#8220;hxxp://64.78.161.33/android.php&#8221;. First, it will get the &#8220;nativenumber&#8221; variable from the &#8220;telmark&#8221; value of &#8220;AndroidManifest.xml&#8221;. This is hardcoded and equals &#8220;phone&#8221;. Then, it will add the result of the public method localDate.getTime(), which simply gets the current date. An example of the string which is sent to the command-and-control would be &#8220;phone 26.03.2013&#8221;.</p>
<p>It is interesting that the attackers used Java Base64 library developed by Sauron Software. This software is free and distributed under LGPL license.</p>
<p>Also, command communications with the malware are parsed with a function named &#8220;chuli()&#8221; prior to POSTing stolen data to the command-and-control server. It appears that the attackers are somewhat familiar with the language and mountain-trekking culture of the targets &#8211; the meaning of &#8220;chuli&#8221; is &#8220;summit&#8221;:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214231/208194197.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>The command-and-control server and parameters can be easily seen in the decompiled source code:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214227/208194188.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p align="center"><i>Command and control server interaction code</i></p>
<p>Throughout the code, the attackers log all important actions, which include various messages in Chinese. This was probably done for debugging purposes, indicating the malware may be an early prototype version. Some actions include (with rough translations):</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214222/208194201.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p><strong>The command-and-control server</strong></p>
<p>The command-and-control server is located at IP <strong>64.78.161.133</strong>. This IP is located in Los Angeles, U.S.A., at a hosting company named &#8220;Emagine Concept Inc&#8221;.</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214217/208194189.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>Interestingly, there is a domain which used to point there, &#8220;DlmDocumentsExchange.com&#8221;. The domain was registered on March 8th, 2013:</p>
<p><i>Registration Service Provided By: SHANGHAI MEICHENG TECHNOLOGY INFORMATION DEVELOPMENT CO., LTD.</i></p>
<p>Domain Name: DLMDOCUMENTSEXCHANGE.COM</p>
<p>Registration Date: 08-Mar-2013</p>
<p>Expiration Date: 08-Mar-2014</p>
<p>Status:LOCKED</p>
<p>The domain registration data indicates the following owner:</p>
<p><i>Registrant Contact Details:</i></p>
<p>peng jia</p>
<p>peng jia (bdoufwke123010@gmail.com)</p>
<p>beijingshiahiidienquc.d</p>
<p>beijingshi</p>
<p>beijing,100000</p>
<p>CN</p>
<p>Tel. +86.01078456689</p>
<p>Fax. +86.01078456689</p>
<p>The command-and-control server is hosting an index page which also serves an APK file:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214213/208194190.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>The referenced &#8220;Document.apk&#8221; is 333583 bytes in size, MD5: c4c4077e9449147d754afd972e247efc. It has the same functionality as the one described above but contains different text. The new text (in Chinese, about relations between China, Japan and the disputed &#8220;Senkaku Islands / Diaoyudao Islands / Diaoyutai Islands&#8221;) is shown to the victims and reads as following:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214210/208194193.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>When opened in a browser, this is what the command-and-control index page looks like:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214206/208194191.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>The text on the top means &#8220;Title Title Title&#8221; in Chinese, while the other strings appear to be random characters typed from the keyboard.</p>
<p>Interestingly, the command and control server includes a publicly accessible interface to work with the victims:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214202/208194198.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>Some of the commands with rough translations:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214158/208194202.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>The command-and-control server is running Windows Server 2003 and has been configured for Chinese language:</p>
<p align="center"><img decoding="async" title="" src="https://media.kasperskycontenthub.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/43/2013/03/08214154/208194192.png" alt="" border="1" /></p>
<p>This, together with the logs, is a strong indicator that the attackers are Chinese-speaking.</p>
<p><strong>Conclusions</strong></p>
<p>Every day, there are hundreds if not thousands of targeted attacks against Tibetan and Uyghur supporters. The vast majority of these target Windows machines through Word documents exploiting known vulnerabilities such as CVE-2012-0158, CVE-2010-3333 and CVE-2009-3129.</p>
<p>In this case, the attackers hacked a Tibetan activist&#8217;s account and used it to attack Uyghur activists. It indicates perhaps an interesting trend which is exploiting the trust relationships between the two communities. This technique reminds us of a combination between ages old war strategies &#8220;Divide et impera&#8221; and &#8220;By way of deception&#8221;.</p>
<p>Until now, we haven&#8217;t seen targeted attacks against mobile phones, although we&#8217;ve seen <a href="http://www.darkreading.com/mobile-security/167901113/security/attacks-breaches/240004623/luckycat-apt-campaign-building-android-malware.html">indications</a> that these were in development.</p>
<p>The current attack took advantage of the compromise of a high-profile Tibetan activist. It is perhaps the first in a new wave of targeted attacks aimed at Android users. So far, the attackers relied entirely on social engineering to infect the targets. History has shown us that, in time, these attacks will use zero-day vulnerabilities, exploits or a combination of techniques.</p>
<p>For now, the best protection is to avoid any APK attachments that arrive on mobile phones via e-mail.</p>
<p>We detect the malware used in this attack as &#8220;Backdoor.AndroidOS.Chuli.a&#8221;.</p>
<p>MD5s:</p>
<p><i>c4c4077e9449147d754afd972e247efc Document.apk</i></p>
<p>0b8806b38b52bebfe39ff585639e2ea2 WUC&#8217;s Conference.apk</p>
