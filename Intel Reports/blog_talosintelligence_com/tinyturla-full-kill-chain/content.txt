            <p>Cisco Talos is providing an update on its <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/tinyturla-next-generation/"><u>two recent</u></a> reports on a new and ongoing campaign where Turla, a Russian espionage group, deployed their <a href="https://blog.talosintelligence.com/tinyturla-ng-tooling-and-c2/"><u>TinyTurla-NG (TTNG)</u></a> implant. We now have new information on the entire kill chain this actor uses, including the tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs) utilized to steal valuable information from their victims and propagate through their infected enterprises.&nbsp;</p><p></p><ul><li>Talos’ analysis, in coordination with <a href="https://cert.ngo/"><u>CERT.NGO</u></a>, reveals that Turla infected multiple systems in the compromised network of a European non-governmental organization (NGO).&nbsp;</li></ul><p></p><ul><li>The attackers compromised the first system, established persistence and added exclusions to anti-virus products running on these endpoints as part of their preliminary post-compromise actions.&nbsp;</li></ul><p></p><ul><li>Turla then opened additional channels of communication via Chisel for data exfiltration and to pivot to additional accessible systems in the network.</li></ul><p></p><h1 id="tracing-turla%E2%80%99s-steps-from-compromise-to-exfiltration">Tracing Turla’s steps from compromise to exfiltration</h1><p>Talos discovered that post-compromise activity carried out by Turla in this intrusion isn’t restricted to the sole deployment of their backdoors. Before deploying TinyTurla-NG, Turla will attempt to configure anti-virus software exclusions to evade detection of their backdoor. Once exclusions have been set up, TTNG is written to the disk, and persistence is established by creating a malicious service.</p><h2 id="preliminary-post-compromise-activity-and-tinyturla-ng-deployment">Preliminary post-compromise activity and TinyTurla-NG deployment</h2><p></p><p>After gaining initial access, Turla first adds exclusions in the anti-virus software, such as Microsoft Defender, to locations they will use to host the implant on the compromised systems.</p><p></p>
