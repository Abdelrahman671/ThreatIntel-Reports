<p>The world is moving towards digitalization from small to large, and every business has a website running to showcase its services. In addition to providing services, they keep user data in their databases, including cookies and personal information provided during registration.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Several technologies make a website more efficient and easy to use, increasing the opportunities to be vulnerable. Scanning is considered the second phase of ethical hacking, following reconnaissance. It aids in locating vulnerabilities in the target.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Website scanners are frequently used to test dynamic web applications; as a result, they are also sometimes referred to as dynamic application security tools (<strong><a href="https://cybersecuritynews.com/dynamic-application-security-testing/">DAST</a></strong>).</p>



<p>Website scanner tools enable analysts or testers to thoroughly scan a website and identify any vulnerabilities or weak points in the web application. The process can be manual or automated depending on the tool&#8217;s design.</p>



<p>Website scanner tools crawl through a web app&#8217;s pages and files to look for flaws, analyze them in depth, report them, and, if the scanner can do so, simultaneously fix them. For cybersecurity researchers, the recon process has been greatly facilitated by website scanner tools.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-what-does-a-web-scanner-do"><strong>What Does A Web Scanner Do?</strong></h2>



<p>The Website Scanner tool finds vulnerabilities on a website. <span style="box-sizing: border-box; margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">If available, it specifies their severity level</span> andÂ CVE IDs and can assign a <strong>CVSS</strong> score based on the findings.Â </p>



<p>Since some vulnerabilities and loopholes are complex and some can be found by connecting multiple vulnerabilities, manual scanning is also a best practice to increase security to the next level. This is because automated website scanner tools may be unable to find all types of vulnerabilities and loopholes.&nbsp;</p>



<h2 class="wp-block-heading" id="h-is-it-illegal-to-scan-a-website-for-vulnerabilities"><strong>Is It Illegal To Scan A Website For Vulnerabilities?</strong></h2>



<p>Yes, it is against the law to scan a website for vulnerabilities without the owner&#8217;s consent. Therefore, obtaining the website owner&#8217;s permission to monitor their infrastructure and ethically report the results to them is necessary.&nbsp;</p>



<p>The owner&#8217;s permission is required because otherwise, you risk getting into legal trouble if the company decides to sue you for scanning and accuses you of stealing intellectual property (IP) rights. Â </p>



<p>Website scanner tools may scan your website and find malware. However, the scanner&#8217;s design may determine whether it blocks and resolves the issue.&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Website scanner tools</strong> frequently include the ability to scan for malware. This detection may be based on anomaly-based or signature-based techniques. The tool will automatically report the results to the user.Â </p>
