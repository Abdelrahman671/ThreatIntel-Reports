    <p>DDoS attacks occur when malicious actors flood a network with traffic, overwhelming its resources and rendering its services unavailable. These attacks are sometimes used for <a href="/blog/news/the-rapid-resurgence-of-ddos-extortion-that-didnt-take-long" target="_self">extortion</a>; that is, attackers demand a ransom in exchange for stopping the flood of malicious traffic. In some cases, attackers use <a href="/blog/security/defeating-triple-extortion-ransomware" target="_self">DDoS as a smokescreen</a> to launch even more malicious activities, such as data breaches or malware deployments.</p>
<p>Although DDoS attacks have been around for years, they’ve recently grown in both size and complexity. There was a nearly <a href="/blog/security/a-retrospective-on-ddos-trends-in-2023" target="_self">50% increase in the number of large DDoS attacks between 2021 and 2023</a>, and 2024 is on track to break this record yet again. In the <a href="/security-research/the-state-of-the-internet" target="_self">State of the Internet (SOTI)</a> report <a href="/lp/soti/2024-emea-ddos-report" target="_self">Fighting the Heat: EMEA’S Rising DDoS Threats</a>, Akamai researchers observed that the number of DDoS attack events in Europe, the Middle East, and Africa (EMEA) has been rising, with higher peaks, since the beginning of 2019. </p>
<p>The SOTI researchers analyzed regional DDoS data and saw that EMEA’s DDoS attack event numbers are moving at a steadier incline than any other region’s numbers, including overall leader North America’s (Figure).</p>

    <p>According to <a href="/newsroom/press-release/ddos-attacks-on-financial-services-industry-up-154-according-to-new-fs-isac-akamai-report" target="_self">DDoS: Here to Stay</a>, a white paper published in early 2024 by <a href="https://www.fsisac.com/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">FS-ISAC</a> and Akamai, 35% of all DDoS attacks in 2023 were aimed at the financial services industry, which has surpassed the games sector as the most-attacked vertical. <div class="text aem-GridColumn aem-GridColumn--default--12">
<div data-cmp-is="text" class="cmp-text" data-cmp-data-layer="{&#34;text-4baa9916e8&#34;:{&#34;@type&#34;:&#34;akamai/components/text&#34;,&#34;repo:modifyDate&#34;:&#34;2024-12-11T17:49:22Z&#34;,&#34;xdm:text&#34;:&#34;&lt;p>If you’re under attack, time is of the essence. Here are seven key actions you can take to mitigate the damage and secure your infrastructure.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;ol>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>&lt;b>Assess your risk and current defense&lt;/b>s. Begin by evaluating your current DDoS mitigation capabilities. Are your existing defenses sufficient to handle the size and scope of the attack? Engage your DDoS mitigation provider to assess the ongoing threat and respond quickly to any vulnerabilities.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>&lt;b>Review your critical IP spaces and subnets&lt;/b>. Ensure that your most vital network resources, including critical subnets and IP spaces, are protected by mitigation controls. This will help to limit the areas of your infrastructure that can be compromised by the attack.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>&lt;b>Activate always-on DDoS security controls&lt;/b>. Deploy always-on security controls as a first layer of defense. This proactive approach minimizes the burden on incident responders and reduces the risk of emergency integration scenarios during a crisis.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>&lt;b>Implement an edge-based cloud firewall&lt;/b>. <p><b>Adaptive response to complex attacks</b>. DDoS attacks today are multilayered, and attackers often change tactics in the middle of an attack. Automated defenses may miss subtle shifts or new attack patterns, but Prolexic’s SOCC team actively monitors and adjusts strategies in real time, ensuring that defenses adapt as attacks evolve. This human oversight is critical to staying ahead of sophisticated attackers.</p>
<p><b>Reducing false positives</b>. Fully automated systems can mistakenly block legitimate traffic, sometimes causing more harm than the attack itself. Prolexic’s security engineers carefully analyze traffic to prevent false positives, ensuring the business continues to serve real customers even during an attack. This is especially important for industries like finance, games, and ecommerce, where uptime and user experience are paramount.</p>
<p><b>Real-time collaboration with clients</b>. Akamai’s engineers work directly with our customers’ in-house security teams before, during, and after an attack, offering hands-on support and making real-time adjustments. Whether it’s developing a proactive and positive security posture, prioritizing critical services, or refining key defenses and processes to save precious minutes during an attack, this collaboration ensures the attacked organization stays operational and responsive, with solutions tailored to their specific infrastructure.</p>
