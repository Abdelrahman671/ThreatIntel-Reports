<div class="section post-body">
<span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p><span>Since April, Morphisec Labs has been closely monitoring an active <a href="https://attack.mitre.org/software/S0561/">GuLoader</a> campaign that primarily focuses on law firms, along with healthcare and investment firms, specifically within the United States. GuLoader, also known as Cloudeye, has been active for over three years, continuously evolving over time. Its developers employ a range of anti-analysis techniques, making it challenging for security researchers to analyze.</span></p> 
<!--more--> 
<p><span>GuLoader has gained notoriety for its role in distributing numerous malware families, including NetWire, Lokibot, Xloader, Remcos, and others. In this context, it is likely employed by the threat actors to gain insights into the effectiveness of their malicious campaign.
