      <div class="cr-mln__post-author-share">

          <span id="hs_cos_wrapper_post_body" class="hs_cos_wrapper hs_cos_wrapper_meta_field hs_cos_wrapper_type_rich_text" style="" data-hs-cos-general-type="meta_field" data-hs-cos-type="rich_text"><p>Cybereason issues Threat Analysis reports to investigate emerging threats and provide practical recommendations for protecting against them. In this Threat Analysis report, Cybereason investigates the Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) known as Beast and how to defend against it through the <a href="https://www.cybereason.com/platform#graphic"><span>Cybereason Defense Platform</span></a>.</p>
<!--more--><h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #000000;">KEY POINTS</span></h3>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Expanding Marketplace:</strong> The Beast Ransomware group provides various tools&nbsp; with constant version updates. These updates are made to appeal to wider audiences across the underground cybercrime ecosystem.&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Binary Customizations:</strong> The Beast RaaS platform offers affiliates numerous options for building ransomware binaries that target Windows, Linux, and ESXi systems, enabling tailored configurations to suit different operational requirements.</li>
<li aria-level="1"><strong>Detection And Prevention:</strong> The Cybereason Defense Platform employs advanced Anti-Ransomware and Anti-Malware features, designed to detect and block ransomware payloads like Beast before they can execute.<br><br></li>
</ul>
<h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #000000;">INTRODUCTION</span></h3>
<p>The Beast Ransomware group has been active since 2022. Recently, a Beast Ransomware partnership program and new capabilities were promoted on the underground forums in June. The group has updated and created various versions to meet the market demand.&nbsp;</p>
<p><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-1.png?width=886&amp;height=217&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-1.png" width="886" height="217" loading="lazy" alt="beast-ransomware-blog-1" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 886px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-1.png?width=443&amp;height=109&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-1.png 443w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-1.png?width=886&amp;height=217&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-1.png 886w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-1.png?width=1329&amp;height=326&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-1.png 1329w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-1.png?width=1772&amp;height=434&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-1.png 1772w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-1.png?width=2215&amp;height=543&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-1.png 2215w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-1.png?width=2658&amp;height=651&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-1.png 2658w" sizes="(max-width: 886px) 100vw, 886px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center; font-size: 16px;"><em>Invitation to cooperate in Russian, English and Chinese languages.</em></p>
<p>Previous versions of the Beast Ransomware, also known as Monster, were developed using the Delphi programming language and offered as a Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) platform.</p>
<p><img src="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg?width=870&amp;height=363&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg" width="870" height="363" loading="lazy" alt="beast-ransomware-blog-2" style="height: auto; max-width: 100%; width: 870px; margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; display: block;" srcset="https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg?width=435&amp;height=182&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg 435w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg?width=870&amp;height=363&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg 870w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg?width=1305&amp;height=545&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg 1305w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg?width=1740&amp;height=726&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg 1740w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg?width=2175&amp;height=908&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg 2175w, https://www.cybereason.com/hs-fs/hubfs/dam/images/images-web/blog-images/beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg?width=2610&amp;height=1089&amp;name=beast-ransomware-blog-2.jpg 2610w" sizes="(max-width: 870px) 100vw, 870px"></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em>First Appearance Of Beast Ransomware On The Russian Anonymous Marketplace</em></span></p>
<h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #000000;">TECHNICAL ANALYSIS</span></h3>
<h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #000000;">Beast Operating System Support – Windows&nbsp;</span></h3>
<p>The current known Windows versions of Beast demonstrate the following capabilities:</p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1">Combination of Elliptic-curve and ChaCha20 encryption model&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
<li aria-level="1">Written in the C programming language</li>

<h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #000000;">Binary Analysis - BEAST HERE?&nbsp;</span></h3>
<p>Like most ransomware, the initial compromise often occurs through various infection vectors, such as phishing emails, or compromised remote desktop protocol (RDP) endpoints.</p>
<p>To prevent multiple instances of Beast running simultaneously on the same system, it creates a unique mutex with the string “BEAST HERE?”. This ensures efficient execution and enables the attacker to maintain control over the ransomware’s behavior on the infected system.&nbsp;</p>

<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-size: 16px;"><em>Beast Creates A Mutex Object With BEAST HERE? String</em></span></p>
<p>The latest version of Beast Ransomware specifically avoids encrypting data on devices located in Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) countries, such as Russia, Belarus, and Moldova. This is achieved through code that checks the system's default language settings, country code, and retrieves the target's IP address.&nbsp;</p>
<p>If the ransomware detects that the device is in a CIS country, it halts encryption activities. This strategic exclusion is likely a tactic to avoid drawing attention or repercussions from authorities in those regions.</p>

<h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #434343;">Load Of RstrtMgr DLL (Restart Manager)</span></h3>
<p><em>RstrtMgr.dll</em>, the Restart Manager, is a critical system component that safeguards open and unsaved files during system reboots. <h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #000000;">File Encryption</span></h3>
<p>Ransomware often employs multithreading to accelerate file encryption.</p>
<p>This technique involves the parent thread identifying and sending files for encryption to child threads.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The child threads then work concurrently, each encrypting a different file, significantly speeding up the overall encryption process. This approach leverages the system's hardware capabilities to encrypt files more efficiently.</p>

<h3 style="font-size: 30px; font-weight: normal;"><span style="color: #000000;">Cybereason Recommendations:</span></h3>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1">Follow and hunt Beast affiliate activity in order to identify pre-ransomware behaviors.&nbsp;</li>
<li aria-level="1">Promote cybersecurity best practices such as multifactor authentication and patch management.</li>
<li aria-level="1">For Cybereason customers on the Cybereason Defense Platform:
<ul>
<li aria-level="2">Enable Anti-Malware and set the Anti-Malware &gt; Signatures mode to Prevent, Quarantine, or Disinfect.</li>
<li aria-level="2">Enable Anti-Ransomware (PRP), set Anti-Ransomware to Quarantine mode and enable shadow copy protection.</li>
<li aria-level="2">Enable Application Control.</li>
<li aria-level="2">Keep systems fully patched: Make sure your systems are patched in order to mitigate vulnerabilities.</li>
<li aria-level="2">Regularly backup files and create a backup process and policy : Restoring your files from a backup is the fastest way to regain access to your data.</li>
<li aria-level="2">Enable Variant Payload Prevention with prevent mode on Cybereason Behavioral execution prevention.<br><br></li>
</ul>
</li>
