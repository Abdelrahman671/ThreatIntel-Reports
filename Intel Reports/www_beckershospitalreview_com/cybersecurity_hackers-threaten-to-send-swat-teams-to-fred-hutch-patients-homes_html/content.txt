					<h1 class="article-title">
		Hackers threaten to send SWAT teams to Fred Hutch patients&#039; homes</h1>

<div class="article-meta">
	<span class="author">Giles Bruce</span> - <span class='article-date'><time class="timeago" datetime="2024-01-04T15:59:23Z">Thursday, January 4th, 2024</time></span>

<div class='article-content'><div id='action-items' class='action-items'></div><div id='inner-article-content'><p>Hackers threatened to "swat" patients of Seattle-based Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center if the organization didn't meet its ransom demands, <a href="https://www.databreaches.net/recent-attacks-on-fred-hutch-and-integris-is-attempting-to-extort-patients-directly-becoming-the-new-normal/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">databreaches.net</a> reported.</p>
 
<p>Swatting is the act of making a bogus claim to law enforcement so that officers, such as SWAT teams, show up at the person's home. The threat actors reportedly made the demand of Fred Hutch after a <a href="https://www.beckershospitalreview.com/cybersecurity/fred-hutch-takes-it-systems-offline-following-cyberattack.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">November ransomware attack</a> on the organization. Fred Hutch got "upset when we threat to swat patients," the hackers told the website for the Dec. 29 story.</p>
<p>Databreaches.net reported that it was not aware of any such threat ever being made to a healthcare institution before, calling swatting cancer patients a "next level of evil." The hackers said they did not follow through with the warning, though they did <a href="https://www.beckershospitalreview.com/cybersecurity/fred-hutch-patients-get-blackmail-emails-after-cyberattack.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">email patients</a> demanding $50 in exchange for removing their personal data from the dark web.</p>
<p>"Fred Hutchinson Cancer Center was aware of cybercriminals issuing swatting threats and immediately notified the FBI and Seattle police, who notified the local police," a Fred Hutch spokesperson emailed <em>Becker's</em>. "The FBI, as part of its investigation into the cybersecurity incident, also investigated these threats."</p>
<p>A spokesperson for the FBI in Seattle told <em>Becker's</em> it is aware of the threats but that it has no information that a swatting event took place. "The FBI takes swatting very seriously because it can place innocent people at risk and takes responders away from real emergencies," the spokesperson said.</p><div id='topics-to-follow'></div><div id='latest-articles-outer'><div id='latest-articles'></div></div></div></div>
