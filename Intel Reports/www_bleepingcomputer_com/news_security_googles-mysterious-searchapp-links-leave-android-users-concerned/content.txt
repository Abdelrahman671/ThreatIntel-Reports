<article><div class="article_section">
          <h1>Google's mysterious 'search.app' links leave Android users concerned</h1>
          <div class="cz-news-story-title-section">
            <div class="cz-news-title-left-area">
                            By <h6><a rel="author" href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/author/ax-sharma/" class="author"><span itemprop="author" itemscope itemtype="https://schema.org/Person"><span itemprop="name">Ax Sharma</span></span></a></h6>
            </div>
	    <meta itemprop="articleSection" content="Security"><div class="cz-news-title-right-area">
              <ul><li class="cz-news-date">November 8, 2024</li>
                <li class="cz-news-time">07:56 AM</li>
                
                <li class="cz-news-comment"><a href="#comments">3</a></li>
              </ul></div>
          </div>
	  <div class="articleBody">
	   
<p style="text-align:center"><img alt="Google" height="900" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/content/hl-images/2023/12/29/Google_headpic.jpg" width="1600"></p>

<p>Google has left Android users puzzled after the most recent update to the Google mobile app causes links shared from the app to now be prepended with a mysterious "search.app" domain.</p>

<p>As the Google app is a popular portal for searching the web for Android users and delivers a personalized content news feed referred to as Google Discover, it has sparked concern among those who noticed the new links.</p>

<h2>What are these mysterious&nbsp;<em>search.app</em> links?</h2>

<p>On November 6, 2024, Google rolled out its an Android version 15.44.27.28.arm64 of its app. Around the same time, the company also rolled out&nbsp;Chrome (Android) updates [<a href="https://chromereleases.googleblog.com/2024/11/chrome-for-android-update_01166592258.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">1</a>, <a href="http://chromereleases.googleblog.com/2024/11/chrome-for-android-update.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">2</a>]&nbsp;"to a small percentage of users."</p>

<p>Ever since then, links viewed in Google's in-app Chromium browser, when shared externally, are being&nbsp;prepended with a "search.app" domain.</p>

<p>BleepingComputer noticed the behaviour shortly after updating our Google app and we admit, the sight of a mysterious domain left us alarmed at first. Was our device compromised by&nbsp;adware?</p>

<div style="text-align:center">
<figure class="image" style="display:inline-block"><img alt="Google app prepends links with the search.app domain" height="530" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/images/news/u/1164866/2024/Nov/google-search.app-domain/sharing-links-search-app.jpg" width="600"><figcaption><strong>Google in-app browser prepends links with search.app</strong><br>
(BleepingComputer)</figcaption></figure></div>

<p>Our concerns are echoed by other users on Reddit this week.</p>

<p>"Recently (few days ago), I noticed that each link shared from the Google in-app web browser uses the 'search.app'&nbsp;domain," <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/AndroidQuestions/comments/1ghhhxc/google_app_sharing_searchapp_links/?utm_source=share&amp;utm_medium=web3x&amp;utm_name=web3xcss&amp;utm_term=1&amp;utm_content=share_button" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">asked</a> Reddit user&nbsp;<em>danilopiazza</em>.</p>

<p>"For example, trying to share the link to the Reddit front Page, I get: <strong>https://search.app?link=</strong><em>https%3A%2F%2Fwww.reddit.com%2F&amp;utm_campaign=...&amp;utm_source=..."</em></p>

<p>"Is this a new feature from the Google app?"</p>

<p>A reader responded, "It seems like it. I'm getting this too. At first I thought I was somehow infected with some kind of malware, or somehow some setting unbeknownst to me got changed."</p>

<p>Similar posts have emerged from <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/GalaxyS24/comments/1gltx15/all_links_have_searchapplink_attached_when/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">others</a>.</p>

<p>BleepingComputer observed links being shared via social media posts on&nbsp;<a href="https://x.com/scottlincicome/status/1854568256956207322" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">X</a> and <a href="http://www.facebook.com/story.php?story_fbid=867785142193197&amp;id=100068851584731&amp;_rdr" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Facebook</a> via Google's Android app this week are bearing the "search.app" domain too:</p>

<div style="text-align:center">
<figure class="image" style="display:inline-block"><img alt="Social media posts bearing the search.app links" height="374" src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/images/news/u/1164866/2024/Nov/google-search.app-domain/fb-search-app.jpg" width="400"><figcaption><strong>Social media posts to external sites bearing search.app links</strong><br>
(BleepingComputer)</figcaption></figure></div>

<p>We observed some <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/357763210959917/posts/7760766810659483/?_rdr" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">older posts</a>&nbsp;(from June 2024) on social media with search.app links which had a rather different URL structure, e.g.&nbsp;<em>https://search[.]app/ddc8Ap3W8nySXJ7bA</em></p>

<h2>Is search.app safe?</h2>

<p>Put simply, <em>search.app</em> is a URL redirector domain, much like <em>t.co</em> used by X (formerly Twitter), Google's <em><a href="https://g.co/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">g.co</a></em>, or Meta's <a href="https://m.me/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener"><em>m.me</em></a>.</p>

<p>Prepending links with "https://search.app?link=" would give Google enhanced visibility into how links are being externally shared by the Google app&nbsp;users&nbsp;and <em>who</em> are clicking on these links (i.e. <a href="https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTTP/Headers/Referer" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">referrers</a>).</p>

<p>In addition to collecting&nbsp;analytics, by placing itself between users and external links by using&nbsp;the "search.app" domain,&nbsp;Google would now have&nbsp;the ability to block traffic to phishing or hacked domains, should a website go rogue, or in the event that users are mass-sharing questionable content with each other (such as a scam site).</p>

<p>In our tests, navigating to search.app directly took us to an "Invalid Dynamic Link" page with a Firebase logo.</p>

<div style="text-align:center">
<figure class="image" style="display:inline-block"><img alt="firebase landing page" height="285" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAAAAACH5BAEKAAEALAAAAAABAAEAAAICTAEAOw==" width="500" data-src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/images/news/u/1164866/2024/Nov/google-search.app-domain/google-firebase.jpg" class="b-lazy"><figcaption><strong>Navigating to search.app directly shows a Firebase landing page</strong><br>
(BleepingComputer)</figcaption></figure></div>

<p>Firebase was acquired in 2014 by Google and <a href="https://firebase.google.com/firebase-and-gcp#:~:text=Yes!,build%20and%20grow%20your%20app." target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">has since become</a> "Google's mobile development platform that empowers you to quickly build and grow your app."</p>

<p>We noticed a similar screen when navigating to Google's another domain: <em><strong>https://search.app.goo.gl/</strong></em></p>

<p>Ironically, Firebase Dynamic Links are <a href="https://firebase.google.com/docs/dynamic-links" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">deprecated</a> and set to be shut down&nbsp;by August 2025.</p>

<p>WHOIS records for both search.app and goo.gl show Google LLC as the registrant organization and MarkMonitor as the registrar.</p>

<h2>SSL certificate and hosting shared by over a hundred domains</h2>

<p>After publishing this piece, multiple BleepingComputer readers have pointed out an anomaly with the TLS&nbsp;certificate issued for the 'search.app' domain.</p>

<p>To add confusion, search.app's certificate has the Common Name (CN) set to&nbsp;<em>fallacni.com,&nbsp;</em>a French language website that claims to help you&nbsp;"find your national identity card."</p>

<div style="text-align:center">
<figure class="image" style="display:inline-block"><img alt="SSL certificate presented by search.app" height="393" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAAAAACH5BAEKAAEALAAAAAABAAEAAAICTAEAOw==" width="500" data-src="https://www.bleepstatic.com/images/news/u/1164866/2024/Nov/google-search.app-domain/fallacni-cert.jpg" class="b-lazy"><figcaption><strong>SSL certificate presented by search.app shows a different CN</strong><br>
(BleepingComputer)</figcaption></figure></div>

<p>It is possible that this due to the use of SSL/TLS technologies like&nbsp;<a href="https://https.cio.gov/sni/#:~:text=Server%20Name%20Indication%2C%20often%20abbreviated,from%20the%20same%20IP%20address." target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Server Name Indication (SNI)</a>&nbsp;which make&nbsp;it possible for multiple domains to be served over HTTPS on a shared hosting server, but&nbsp;certainly seems irregular.</p>

<p>For comparison, the certificate of Google's <em>search.app.goo.gl</em>&nbsp;has its&nbsp;CN set to 'misc-sni.google.com' and is hosted at IP address, 216.58.212.206.</p>

<p>While thus far, the <em>search.app</em> redirector URLs appears to be safe and officially operated by Google, the sheer lack of documentation surrounding the domain is odd, as is the&nbsp;lack of its mention&nbsp;in public changelogs of Google's&nbsp;open source projects, such as Android or&nbsp;Chromium.</p>

<p>The rollout of the app updates is bound to alarm even more users in the coming days who may wonder&nbsp;if their device is behaving erratically or&nbsp;has been compromised by malware.</p>

<p>Is this is Google's attempt at imitating Apple News which <a href="https://x.com/AppleNews/status/1854682748729819629?s=19" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">prepends links to external stories</a> with <em>https://apple.news</em>?</p>

<p>In the past, Google Chrome's&nbsp;use of <a href="https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/what-are-these-suspicious-google-gvt1com-urls/" target="_blank">strange GVT1.com domains</a>&nbsp;has drawn the scrutiny&nbsp;of&nbsp;even the most skilled researchers due to the lack of public documentation surrounding these domains.</p>

<p>BleepingComputer approached Google for comment in advance of publishing and we are awaiting a response.</p>

function cz_strip_tags(input, allowed) {
  allowed = (((allowed || '') + '')
    .toLowerCase()
    .match(/<[a-z][a-z0-9]*>/g) || [])
    .join(''); // making sure the allowed arg is a string containing only tags in lowercase (<a><b><c>)
  var tags = /<\/?([a-z][a-z0-9]*)\b[^>]*>/gi,
    commentsAndPhpTags = /<!--[\s\S]*?-->|<\?(?:php)?[\s\S]*?\?>/gi;
  return input.replace(commentsAndPhpTags, '')
    .replace(tags, function($0, $1) {
      return allowed.indexOf('<' + $1.toLowerCase() + '>') > -1 ? $0 : '';
    });
}
function cz_br2nl(str) {
	var regex = /<br\s*[\/]?>/gi;
    //var pure_str = str.replace(regex,"\n");
    var pure_str = str.replace(regex,"");
    return cz_strip_tags(pure_str,'');
}
