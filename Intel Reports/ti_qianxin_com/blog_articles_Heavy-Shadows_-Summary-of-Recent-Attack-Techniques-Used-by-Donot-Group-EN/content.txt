    Heavy Shadows: Summary of Recent Attack Techniques Used by Donot Group
  </div> <div class="author lh32" data-v-055d1340><p class="text-grey-12" data-v-055d1340> By çº¢é¨æ»´å¢é | äºä»¶è¿½è¸ª</p> <a title="ä¸è½½PDF" href="null" download="Heavy-Shadows:-Summary-of-Recent-Attack-Techniques-Used-by-Donot-Group-EN" class="dl-pdf" style="display:none" data-v-055d1340><i class="icon-dl-pdf icon-24" data-v-055d1340></i> <span class="dl-text" data-v-055d1340> PDF</span></a> <a title="ä¸è½½IOC" href="null" download="Heavy-Shadows:-Summary-of-Recent-Attack-Techniques-Used-by-Donot-Group-EN" class="dl-ioc" style="display:none" data-v-055d1340><i class="icon-dl-ioc icon-24" data-v-055d1340></i> <span class="dl-text" data-v-055d1340> IOC</span></a></div></div> <div class="main-body" data-v-055d1340><h3 class="line" data-line="0">Introduction</h3>
<p class="line" data-line="2">Donot group, also known as APT-Q-38 within QiAnXin, is believed to have a government background in a South Asian country. This group mainly conducts network espionage activities against government agencies, defense military departments, and important figures in the business field. Victims include China, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, and other South Asian countries.</p>
<p class="line" data-line="4">During the daily threat hunting process, the RedDrip Team of QiAnXin Threat Intelligence Center discovered that Donot's attack activities have maintained a high frequency since the end of last year, and this trend has continued until this year. At the end of January this year, we also captured attack samples<sup> [1]</sup> by this group using documents related to the Kashmir region as bait.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="6"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/b942c4b78395e7eae76b4de4e0113f4e.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<p class="line" data-line="8">After combing through the recent Donot samples we captured, we found that the group 's main attack process still maintains its usual style, but attackers are also trying different malicious code implantation methods and changing the code details of attack components. Therefore, this article will provide a brief summary of Donot's recent attack techniques.</p>
<p class="line" data-line="10">The Donot group often executes shellcode to download subsequent DLL components by carrying macros in documents. These DLLs can further download malicious components such as Trojan plugin managers and Trojan plugins. In attack samples using documents related to the Kashmir region as bait, attackers deliver downloader DLL components directly through self-extracting RAR archives. Additionally, in some attack campaigns, the Donot group also uses EXE components, which are released directly from compressed packages through macro documents, to download subsequent components.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="12"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/ba47ccd20961818e0a8575ec146e7b0a.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>

<h5 class="line" data-line="37">(1) VBA</h5>
<p class="line" data-line="39">Take the above sample (MD5: d98e2d7c8e91a9d8e87abe744f6d43f9) as an example for analysis</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="41"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/d8b2fdc9de99eb66822e74b6c43e1cf6.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<p class="line" data-line="43">VBA code uses a mixed case name generated with the letters ijl to obfuscate variable and function names. NtAllocateVirtualMemory is called to allocate memory. The encoded shellcode is decoded using the WideCharToMultiByte function according to the character encoding used in the document, and then the EnumUILanguages function is called to execute the shellcode. The specific shellcode executed depends on the system architecture.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="45"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/23e50b841a446dd02f9ac84b75af0aa9.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>

<h5 class="line" data-line="49">(2) Shellcode</h5>
<p class="line" data-line="51">In the case of the 64-bit version of the shellcode, self-decryption is performed by taking the byte inverse and then XOR.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="53"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/0f9dbb4a7367a95870f3221acd7c97c4.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<p class="line" data-line="55">APIs are imported using hash, and XOR with 0xBAADC0D3 is performed during import.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="57"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/fd32c9ec2b1ea0daaf66f7e75a1270b1.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<p class="line" data-line="59">The URLDownloadToCacheFileA function of the urlmon.dll module is called to download the payload, with the following URLs:</p>
<pre class="hljs"><code class="hljs">http://orangevisitorss\.buzz/QcM8y7FsH12BUbxY/XNJxFhZdMSJzq1tRyF47ZXLIdqNGRqiHQQHL6DJIjl2IoxUA.png
</code></pre>
<p class="line" data-line="65">The downloaded payload is loaded into memory in the same way as reading a file. The decryption operation is also performed by taking the byte inverse and then XOR, and the first byte of the decrypted data is checked for verification. If the verification passes, the decrypted data is executed.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="67"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/67efedc68d61f41b277d95900d18d5aa.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<p class="line" data-line="69">The first thing to do with the obtained second-stage shellcode is to self-decrypt by XORing the bytes, and then importing the relevant APIs. Wow64DisableWow64FsRedirection is called to disable file system redirection. GetLocalTime is used to obtain the system time. If the current time is greater than the hard-coded date, the shellcode ends its operation. As shown in the figure below, the hard-coded date is 0x7e70214, which can be decomposed into 0x07e7-0x02-0x14, and converted to decimal is 2023-02-20.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="71"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/8e0b771f2174cdee3ed96e0c18834059.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<p class="line" data-line="73">Each antivirus driver file is checked in turn to see if it exists in the "C:\Windows\System32\drivers" directory. If a certain antivirus driver exists, the value of the corresponding flag variable is determined based on whether the current date is greater than the corresponding built-in time data: if it is not later than the time data, it is assigned a value of 1, otherwise it is assigned a value of 2.</p>

<p class="line" data-line="87">If there is no antivirus software, URLDownloadToFileA is called to download the follow-up payload, which is saved as the Unincored.dll file in the current user's temp directory, i.e. "%tmp%\Unincored.dll". The URL for the follow-up is:</p>
<pre class="hljs"><code class="hljs">http://orangevisitorss.buzz/QcM8y7FsH12BUbxY/XNJxFhZdMSJzq1tRyF47ZXLIdqNGRqiHQQHL6DJIjl2IoxUA.mp4

<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="95"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/c56c7ba137a320c8b80939642df74a62.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<p class="line" data-line="97">In the presence of anti-virus software, if the corresponding flag variable is 2, the shellcode will be terminated by executing the int3 interrupt or other instructions to trigger an interrupt or exception. If the flag variable is 1, the execution of the shellcode corresponding to some anti-virus software is consistent with that when there is no anti-virus software; however, when other anti-virus software is present, current shellcode will load bcrypt.dll and copy the subsequent shellcode into the memory mapped by bcrypt.dll for execution.</p>
<p class="line" data-line="99">The 32-bit version of the shellcode launched by macro code is basically the same as the 64-bit version, but the URLs for downloading subsequent shellcode and DLL payloads are different.</p>

<h3 class="line" data-line="420">String Encryption</h3>
<p class="line" data-line="422">Donot frequently uses various encryption methods to encrypt critical strings in PE-based attack components. In addition to simple addition or subtraction of fixed numerical values and single 01 transformations, we found that DLL components that have recently appeared often use double 01 transformations and custom multi-layer encryption to hide critical strings. Besides, attackers use two encryption methods to process different strings sometimes.</p>
<br>
<h4 class="line" data-line="426">01 Transformation</h4>
<p class="line" data-line="428">In this type of encryption method, the ASCII code of the string is converted into binary and exists in the sample in the form of a 01 string. If this conversion is only performed once, it is a single 01 transformation. In the Donot sample that appeared at the end of 2022, double 01 transformations began to appear. The 01 string obtained after one round of transformation is transformed in the same way again, which means that each character of the original string needs to be represented by a 01 string with a length of 64 characters.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="430"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/018085502c0a738968942e9364786bd4.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<br>
<h4 class="line" data-line="434">Customized Multi-layer Encryption</h4>
<p class="line" data-line="436">In samples that use this type of encryption method, when restoring the original string, the decrypted data is decoded in base64 and decrypted in AES. Then, the decrypted data is processed in the following order: (1) subtracting 1 byte by byte, (2) swapping the positions of every two adjacent bytes, and (3) reversing the string.</p>
<p class="line img-box text-center" data-line="438"><img src="/uploads/2023/03/23/e80685cfeb1d48335394ceb70977449f.png" alt="" width="70%"></p>
<br>
<h3 class="line" data-line="442">Summary</h3>
<p class="line" data-line="444">Overall, the attack techniques of the Donot group are relatively fixed, and a commonly used tactics in their attack chain is linking sequential components with scheduled tasks. However, the group is also expanding its attack process, with more diverse methods of implanting malicious components and using various types of components.</p>
<p class="line" data-line="446">Although there have been no reports of related attacks affecting domestic users, QiAnXin's RedDrip Team would like to remind all users not to open links of unknown origin shared on social media, click on unknown email attachments, run unknown files with exaggerated titles, or install apps from non-official sources. It is also important to regularly back up important files and update software patches.</p>
<p class="line" data-line="448">If there is a need to install an app of unknown origin, it can be first analyzed through QiAnXin Threat Intelligence Center's deep analysis platform (https://sandbox.ti.qianxin.com/sandbox/page). Currently, the deep analysis platform supports the analysis of various file formats, including those for Windows and Android platforms.</p>
