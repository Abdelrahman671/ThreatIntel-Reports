class="pw-post-body-paragraph mg mh gu mi b mj pb ml mm mn pc mp mq mr pd mt mu mv pe mx my mz pf nb nc nd gn bk">The <strong class="mi gv">SmokeLoader</strong> backdoor with a range of capabilities which depend on the modules included in any given build of the malware. The malware is delivered in a variety of ways and is notorious for its use of deception and self-protection, using random API function calls and a multi-stage decryption process. The malware frequently tries to hide its C2 activity by generating requests to legitimate sites such as microsoft.com, bing.com, adobe.com, and others. Typically the actual Download returns an HTTP 404 but still contains data in the Response Body.</p><p id="689c" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mg mh gu mi b mj mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd gn bk"><strong class="mi gv">SystemBC</strong> is primarily used as a SOCKS5 proxy allowing for interaction between victim machines and attacker infrastructure to execute commands, deploy additional payloads or exfiltrate data. SystemBC has reportedly been associated with loaders such as Smokeloader as well as Gootloader and ModernLoader. It has risen in popularity in recent years with usage linked to a variety of capable ransomware groups such as Cuba, BlackBasta, Play (Now BlackSuit), Rhysida, 8Base and more.</p><p id="bcc8" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mg mh gu mi b mj mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd gn bk">For an excellent in-depth report on SystemBC:</p><p id="8bb2" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mg mh gu mi b mj mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd gn bk"><a class="af ni" href="https://github.com/vc0RExor/Malware-Threat-Reports/blob/main/The%20Swiss%20Knife%20-%20SystemBC%20%7C%20Coroxy/The%20Swiss%20Knife-SystemBC_EN.pdf" rel="noopener ugc nofollow" target="_blank">https://github.com/vc0RExor/Malware-Threat-Reports/blob/main/The%20Swiss%20Knife%20-%20SystemBC%20%7C%20Coroxy/The%20Swiss%20Knife-SystemBC_EN.pdf</a></p><p id="b4c4" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mg mh gu mi b mj mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd gn bk">As reported by VMware’s Carbon Black, 8Base use <strong class="mi gv">Smokeloader</strong> and<strong class="mi gv"> SystemBC</strong> in their intrusions:</p><blockquote class="ne nf ng"><p id="1f05" class="mg mh nh mi b mj mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd gn bk">“8base uses SystemBC to encrypt command and control traffic and Smokeloader, which provided initial obfuscation of the ransomware on ingress, unpacking, and loading of the Phobos ransomware.”</p></blockquote><p id="9a0a" class="pw-post-body-paragraph mg mh gu mi b mj mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd gn bk">Cisco Talos follows this description with:</p><blockquote class="ne nf ng"><p id="889b" class="mg mh nh mi b mj mk ml mm mn mo mp mq mr ms mt mu mv mw mx my mz na nb nc nd gn bk">“8base sample had been downloaded from the domain <strong class="mi gv">admlogs25[.]xyz</strong> — which appears to be associated with SystemBC, a proxy and remote administration tool.
