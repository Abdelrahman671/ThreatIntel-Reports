<div data-cmp-is="text" class="cmp-text" data-cmp-data-layer="{&#34;text-8f7c859dbd&#34;:{&#34;@type&#34;:&#34;akamai/components/text&#34;,&#34;repo:modifyDate&#34;:&#34;2024-07-26T16:41:49Z&#34;,&#34;xdm:text&#34;:&#34;&lt;ul>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>Following the July 19, 2024, CrowdStrike incident, Akamai researchers uncovered more than 180 newly created malicious domains purporting to assist with navigating the incident, and that number continues to grow.&amp;nbsp;&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>We identified the top trafficked malicious domains associated with the CrowdStrike incident and have developed a list of &lt;a href=\&#34;https://github.com/akamai/esg-secops/blob/main/IOCs/crowdstrike-day2-ioc.txt\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34; rel=\&#34;nofollow noreferrer\&#34;>indicators of compromise&lt;/a> (IOCs) for block list consideration or to analyze further on your own.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>One of the malicious domains observed here was ranked in the top 200,000 sites for associated keywords.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>Nonprofit and education were among the top industries affected, collectively composing more than 20% of the observed attack traffic.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;li>&lt;p>In this blog post, we explore some of the types of scams that are currently active and look into who is being targeted, and we provide mitigation tips for both organizations and individuals affected by this incident.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;/li>\r\n&lt;/ul>\r\n&#34;}}">
    <ul>
<li><p>Following the July 19, 2024, CrowdStrike incident, Akamai researchers uncovered more than 180 newly created malicious domains purporting to assist with navigating the incident, and that number continues to grow. </p>
</li>
<li><p>We identified the top trafficked malicious domains associated with the CrowdStrike incident and have developed a list of <a href="https://github.com/akamai/esg-secops/blob/main/IOCs/crowdstrike-day2-ioc.txt" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">indicators of compromise</a> (IOCs) for block list consideration or to analyze further on your own.</p>
</li>
<li><p>One of the malicious domains observed here was ranked in the top 200,000 sites for associated keywords.</p>
</li>
<li><p>Nonprofit and education were among the top industries affected, collectively composing more than 20% of the observed attack traffic.</p>
</li>
<li><p>In this blog post, we explore some of the types of scams that are currently active and look into who is being targeted, and we provide mitigation tips for both organizations and individuals affected by this incident.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<div data-cmp-is="text" class="cmp-text" data-cmp-data-layer="{&#34;text-431a702477&#34;:{&#34;@type&#34;:&#34;akamai/components/text&#34;,&#34;repo:modifyDate&#34;:&#34;2024-07-26T17:22:01Z&#34;,&#34;xdm:text&#34;:&#34;&lt;p>On Friday, July 19, 2024, the world buzzed with the manifestation of blue screens across a majority of devices after a recent &lt;a href=\&#34;https://www.crowdstrike.com/falcon-content-update-remediation-and-guidance-hub/\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34; rel=\&#34;nofollow noreferrer\&#34;>CrowdStrike Falcon&lt;/a> content update.     <p>On Friday, July 19, 2024, the world buzzed with the manifestation of blue screens across a majority of devices after a recent <a href="https://www.crowdstrike.com/falcon-content-update-remediation-and-guidance-hub/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">CrowdStrike Falcon</a> content update. The update triggered bug checks on Windows hosts that induced a global sea of blue screens of death (BSODs) — causing billions of system outages across various locations.</p>
<p>With <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cpe3zgznwjno" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">8.5 million devices</a> affected globally, just about every industry was hit by this outage, including critical services such as <a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/news/microsoft-crowdstrike-outage-airlines-government-businesses-rush-to-get-back-on-track/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">aviation</a>, <a href="https://fedscoop.com/federal-government-agencies-affected-by-worldwide-it-outage/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">government</a>, and <a href="https://www.healthcareitnews.com/news/worldwide-it-disruption-disrupts-healthcare-delivery" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">healthcare</a>, which produced real-world impacts, some continuing even days after the incident.</p>
<p>As is often the case with newsworthy events, threat actors immediately attempted to exploit the situation by piggybacking on the widespread confusion and disruption caused by the update. The extent of the outage, tied with the extent of the news coverage surrounding it, led to a number of disconcerted users searching for answers wherever they could find them.</p>

<div data-cmp-is="text" class="cmp-text" data-cmp-data-layer="{&#34;text-ea1ffe9ec1&#34;:{&#34;@type&#34;:&#34;akamai/components/text&#34;,&#34;repo:modifyDate&#34;:&#34;2024-07-29T17:29:07Z&#34;,&#34;xdm:text&#34;:&#34;&lt;p>Threat actors saw this incident as a prime opportunity for social engineering — they rapidly set up &lt;a rel=\&#34;nofollow noreferrer\&#34; href=\&#34;https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366599253/Scam-CrowdStrike-domains-growing-in-volume\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34;>scam sites that targeted impacted CrowdStrike customers&lt;/a> to steal information and spread malware.&lt;/p>\r\n&lt;p>&lt;b>By analyzing the data we saw on our worldwide edge network, we identified the top malicious domains being used for scams surrounding this incident&lt;/b> (Figure 1). The threats we’ve already seen span disciplines, including &lt;a href=\&#34;https://x.com/NicoleFishi19/status/1815446499372785748\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34; rel=\&#34;nofollow noreferrer\&#34;>wipers&lt;/a>, &lt;a href=\&#34;https://x.com/G60930953/status/1815811815722439090\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34; rel=\&#34;nofollow noreferrer\&#34;>stealers&lt;/a>, and &lt;a href=\&#34;https://x.com/blackstormsecbr/status/1815878866743021742\&#34; target=\&#34;_blank\&#34; rel=\&#34;nofollow noreferrer\&#34;>remote access tools&lt;/a> (RATs).&lt;/p>\r\n&#34;}}">
    <p>Threat actors saw this incident as a prime opportunity for social engineering — they rapidly set up <a rel="nofollow noreferrer" href="https://www.computerweekly.com/news/366599253/Scam-CrowdStrike-domains-growing-in-volume" target="_blank">scam sites that targeted impacted CrowdStrike customers</a> to steal information and spread malware.</p>
<p><b>By analyzing the data we saw on our worldwide edge network, we identified the top malicious domains being used for scams surrounding this incident</b> (Figure 1). The threats we’ve already seen span disciplines, including <a href="https://x.com/NicoleFishi19/status/1815446499372785748" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">wipers</a>, <a href="https://x.com/G60930953/status/1815811815722439090" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">stealers</a>, and <a href="https://x.com/blackstormsecbr/status/1815878866743021742" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">remote access tools</a> (RATs).</p>

    <p>Security professionals who are dealing with the effects of this incident also have a few ways to help remediate and limit further exposure.</p>
<ul>
<li><p><b>Perform a lateral movement gap analysis or adversary emulation</b>. Threat actors with financial aspirations will find more opportunities to drop ransomware into an environment. Although this wasn’t a CVE to be exploited, there are potential technical impacts from a threat actor who knows a critical piece of your security stack. We recommend a gap analysis or even an <a href="/infectionmonkey" target="_self">adversary emulation</a> to get a current view of your threat landscape.</p>
</li>
<li><p><b>Block known and related IOCs</b>. There are a number of credible intel sources of known IOCs associated with this campaign, including <a href="https://github.com/akamai/esg-secops/blob/main/IOCs/crowdstrike-day2-ioc.txt" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noreferrer">this list we built</a>.     <p>It is likely we will see more phishing attempts associated with this issue beyond the time when every device is remediated. A simple scroll through social media can provide  an attacker with a sense of which brands generate the most heightened emotions and which are ripe to impersonate for malevolent gain.</p>
<p>This is an attacker’s job, and it’s important to remember that. Malicious campaign operations function just as we do in legitimate corporations: The victims are their “customers,” and the varied tactics presented in this post show how “plugged in” to their customers they are.
