<h5 id="viewer-56cml"><span class="vkIF2 public-DraftStyleDefault-ltr"><strong>Background</strong></span></h5> 
<p> 
 <!-- /wp:heading --></p> 
<p> 
 <!-- wp:paragraph {"className":"XzvDs _208Ie _1atvN _2QAo- _25MYV _2WrB- public-DraftStyleDefault-block-depth0 public-DraftStyleDefault-text-ltr"} --></p> 
<p class="XzvDs _208Ie _1atvN _2QAo- _25MYV _2WrB- public-DraftStyleDefault-block-depth0 public-DraftStyleDefault-text-ltr" id="viewer-vetr"><span class="vkIF2 public-DraftStyleDefault-ltr">Reviewing the backdoored Orion installers, they match what appears to be SolarWind's normal build process. It is likely the attackers have compromised both the SolarWind source code, and their build process to deliver backdoored updates through their normal release process.</span></p> 
<p>
