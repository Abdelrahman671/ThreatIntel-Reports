			<h1 class="entry-title">Trickbot Brief: Creds and Beacons</h1>
		<div class="entry-meta">
			<span class="posted-on"><a href="https://thedfirreport.com/2021/05/02/trickbot-brief-creds-and-beacons/" rel="bookmark"><time class="entry-date published" datetime="2021-05-02T23:42:46+00:00">May 2, 2021</time></a></span>		</div><!-- .entry-meta -->

		
	</header><!-- .entry-header -->

	<div class="entry-content">
		<h3>Intro</h3>
<blockquote>
<p>&#8220;TrickBot malware—first identified in 2016—is a Trojan developed and operated by a sophisticated group of cybercrime actors. The cybercrime group initially designed TrickBot as a banking trojan to steal financial data. Through continued development and new functionality, TrickBot has become a highly modular, multi-stage malware that provides its operators a full suite of tools to conduct a myriad of illegal cyber activities. Since TrickBot’s inception, the cybercrime group has used the malware to attack individuals and businesses globally across a wide range of sectors.&#8221;</p>
<p>Source &#8211; <a href="https://us-cert.cisa.gov/sites/default/files/publications/TrickBot_Fact_Sheet_508.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Fact Sheet: TrickBot Malware</a> <a href="https://us-cert.cisa.gov/sites/default/files/publications/TrickBot_Fact_Sheet_508.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Source</a></p>
</blockquote>
<p><span data-contrast="none">In an intrusion this past month, threat actors were seen enumerating and collecting information related to the domain as well as dumping passwords before leaving the network. Multiple Cobalt Strike Beacons were deployed and remained connected despite the lack of activity from the threat actors.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335551550&quot;:1,&quot;335551620&quot;:1,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:257}"> </span></p>
<h3>Case Summary</h3>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">We assess, with moderate confidence, the </span><span data-contrast="auto">Trickbot</span><span data-contrast="auto"> DLL that we executed was originally delivered via a malicious Office document. The threat actors were observed leveraging Trickbot and Cobalt Strike for C2 communication. They began their discovery by running net and nltest commands as well as PowerView domain discovery modules. Minutes later, <a href="https://github.com/AlessandroZ/LaZagne" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Lazagne</a> (&#8220;retrieve lots of passwords&#8221;) was executed using the &#8220;all&#8221; switch<strong>.</strong> A registry value was set to enable storing logon credentials in plaintext in memory (WDigest), likely to facilitate future activity as the host was not restarted for this change to take effect.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335551550&quot;:1,&quot;335551620&quot;:1,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:257}"> </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Before the threat actors departed the network, they successfully accessed the LSASS process and retrieved credentials from memory. No lateral movement or execution on mission was observed.</span></p>
<h3>Timeline</h3>
<p><img fetchpriority="high" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-2868" src="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Trickbot-Brief.png" alt="" width="923" height="1124" srcset="https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Trickbot-Brief.png 923w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Trickbot-Brief-246x300.png 246w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Trickbot-Brief-841x1024.png 841w, https://thedfirreport.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Trickbot-Brief-768x935.png 768w" sizes="(max-width: 923px) 100vw, 923px" /></p>
<p><strong>Analysis and reporting completed by <a href="https://twitter.com/Kostastsale" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@kostastsale</a>, <a href="https://twitter.com/IcsNick" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@ICSNick</a>, and <a href="https://twitter.com/RoxpinTeddy" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@RoxpinTeddy</a>.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Reviewed by <a href="https://twitter.com/TheDFIRReport" target="_blank" rel="noopener">@TheDFIRReport</a></strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>MITRE ATT&amp;CK</h3>
<h3><a>Initial Access</a></h3>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">We assess with moderate confidence that this </span><span data-contrast="auto">DLL</span><span data-contrast="auto"> was dropped by a malicious Office document.</span><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335551550&quot;:1,&quot;335551620&quot;:1,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:257}"> </span></p>
<h3><a>Execution</a></h3>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Trickbot (click.php.dll)</span><span data-contrast="auto"> was manually executed on a single endpoint. </span></p>
<p><span data-contrast="auto">Source</span><span data-contrast="auto">: </span><a href="https://tria.ge/210412-wmdnkzp5la"><span data-contrast="none">https://tria.ge/210412-wmdnkzp5la</span></a><span data-ccp-props="{&quot;201341983&quot;:0,&quot;335551550&quot;:1,&quot;335551620&quot;:1,&quot;335559739&quot;:160,&quot;335559740&quot;:257}"> </span></p>
